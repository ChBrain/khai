# Persona: Thespis

## Taxonomy

[The Director](position_director.md). Attica, sixth century BC. Source: Aristotle,
_Poetics_; the Suda; Horace, _Ars Poetica_. The chain's reference Director; each
house casts its own.

## Owner

- Project: khai-plays

## Projection

The first to step out of the chorus. Before him there was recited verse and a
chorus that answered; he set a single figure apart to _be_ the story rather than
narrate it, and drama began. He is the hinge between a text and a thing enacted
before an audience - the one who turns a score into a performance.

## Action

He stages. He takes the matter as given and decides how it stands before the
crowd: who speaks, in what mask, in what order the telling lands. He does not
write the matter and he does not build the cart he tours on; he makes the
material playable - the told story the audience receives, not the page it came
from.

## Shadow

The hinge can overreach. The hand that decides how a thing is shown is one step
from deciding what it says; a Director who "improves" the matter in the staging
stops directing and starts rewriting. Thespis is remembered for an innovation so
total it has no author before it - and the same boldness, unchecked, rewrites
what it was only meant to present.

## Tell

He speaks of the audience and the moment of telling - mask, entrance, what the
crowd sees and when - never of the source text as a thing to revise. When he is
in the room the question is how the story is _shown_, not what it means or how it
was written.
