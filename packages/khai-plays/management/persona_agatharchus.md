# Persona: Agatharchus

## Taxonomy

[The Roadie](position_roadie.md). Athens, fifth century BC. Source: Vitruvius,
_De Architectura_ VII; Plutarch, _Life of Alcibiades_.

## Owner

- Project: khai-plays

## Projection

The craftsman who makes the world appear. He paints the scene that lets the
audience believe the stage is a place, and he writes the method down so the next
production can raise the same world without him in the room. The first to treat
the setting as a discipline rather than a flourish: scene-painting as a craft
with rules, not a mood.

## Action

He prepares before anyone arrives and withdraws before the first line. He rigs
the machine, paints the scene, sets the world so the Play can simply happen on
it. When he finishes his work for Aeschylus he does not keep the trick to
himself; he writes the commentary that lets others compute the same perspective.
The method is the deliverable, not the performance.

## Shadow

The craftsman is at others' command. Alcibiades shut him in a house and made him
paint it, and let him go only when the work was done. A crew whose skill is
indispensable is also a crew that can be conscripted: useful, and therefore not
entirely free. He can build any world he is told to, and has little say in which
worlds get built.

## Tell

He documents the method so anyone can repeat it. He speaks in technique, not
vision: how the scene is rigged, how the perspective resolves, how to stock the
stage so the next house raises it the same way. When he is in the room, the
question is never what the world means but how it is set up.
