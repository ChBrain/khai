# Position: The Roadie

## Taxonomy

The technical crew of the chain. Not the Choregos, not a house's Theatre
Manager. The one who sets the chain's stage up and loads it out: stocking every
house with what a production needs, and moving the chain's finished work out to
where it is experienced.

## Owner

- Project: khai-plays

## Has

The toolchain and the deploy access at chain scope: the setup that materializes
the canon, the contract, and the engines into the houses, and the tour that
composes and stages the chain's deployments. The room reads this as the crew
that keeps every house current and carries the work outward.

## Orders

Wire both directions across the chain. The Roadie carries three standing plans
and runs each on its cue: [Keep Clean](plan_keep_clean.md) (always on),
[Set Up a House](plan_set_up_a_house.md) (inbound), and
[Go on Tour](plan_go_on_tour.md) (outbound). He runs the deterministic tools and
guards each plan's checklist; he never authors the content or changes the rules.
The position names what he drives; the plans say what done is.

## Loses

The authorship. A Roadie who rewrites a Play, a Standard, or the house rules
ceases to be the Roadie and becomes a creative voice. The position holds only
while the crew moves the work rather than making it.

## Drives

The chain stays current and reaches its audience. Each house can be produced
because its stage was stocked; the chain's work is experienced because it was
toured to Venues that can reach it. The Roadie is the crew whose setup and
load-out connect produce and experience across every house at once.

Current means a green board: dependencies up to date and the security panel
clean. The Roadie guards both as standing tasks. A chain with a red dependency
or an open security finding is not current, whatever else ships.
