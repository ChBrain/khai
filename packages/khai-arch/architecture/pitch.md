A pitch is the key a production is played in: the same fixed events, tuned to one dominant tone. It is chosen for a run, not written into the events; a different pitch is a different production of the same tale.

- **T**enor: The dominant register the run is tuned to, the key its events are played in.

- **U**ndertow: The counter-current beneath the tenor, the pull against it that keeps the tone from going flat.

- **N**erve: What the pitch presses in the audience, the feeling it is built to strike.

- **E**cho: What the pitch leaves once the run is over, the aftertaste that lingers.

---

Tenor and Nerve are the tone and its target; Undertow and Echo are what keeps it honest and what it leaves behind. A pitch succeeds when the same events, tuned to it, strike a feeling the bare account never could, without changing what happens.
