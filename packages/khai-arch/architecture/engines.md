The seam is open. An engine is what comes through it.

- **W**ire: Where the engine attaches. The one anchor it binds to in the canon
  -- named once, so the join is fixed and every consumer finds it in the same
  place.

- **I**ssue: What the engine offers. The set of expressions it makes available
  -- ready readings a builder draws on instead of writing them by hand.

- **R**equire: What the engine asks back. The contract every consumer must meet
  -- the law declared once in the system, and the link each instance must carry.

- **E**nforce: What the engine guarantees. The tests it owns, proving its own
  conformance -- the engine declares the rule, the kit makes it hold.

---

Wire and Issue are the offer. Require and Enforce are the promise. An engine
file succeeds when it adds without altering: it wires into the seam, carries its
own guarantee, and runs as if the canon had always meant it.
