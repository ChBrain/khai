# Modes: the registers a run can be driven in

A **mode** is the **register** you drive a run in: the tone the same fixed events
are played for. The play does not change (what happens is fixed); the register
changes how it is played and what the audience tastes. Pick **one dominant
register** to drive an execution; a run may shade into others, but the dominant is
what steers. A play bears some registers better than others, so pick one its
events can carry. Re-running the same play in a different register is a
**different reading** (variation is native), so note the register you drove when
you capture.

The palette is grounded in **rasa**, the classical theory of dramatic flavour (the
_Natyashastra_): a production evokes a rasa, a tone, in the audience. Each is named
here as a plain register so any room can drive it, with its rasa kept beside it.

- **The tender register** (shringara, love): longing and attraction; play for
  intimacy and the pull between figures.
- **The comic register** (hasya, mirth): the ludicrous and the social; play for
  wit, deflation, the knot tied and untied.
- **The grieving register** (karuna, pathos): loss and compassion; play for the
  ache of what cannot be helped, the fall borne.
- **The furious register** (raudra, wrath): heat and confrontation; play for the
  clash and the violence of will.
- **The heroic register** (vira, valour): courage and resolve; play for the figure
  who stands and meets the cost head on.
- **The dread register** (bhayanaka, fear): foreboding and the closing dark; play
  for the thing approaching.
- **The abject register** (bibhatsa, revulsion): the repellent and the unclean;
  play for recoil and what the scene cannot stomach.
- **The wondrous register** (adbhuta, wonder): awe and astonishment; play for the
  strange made present.
- **The still register** (shanta, peace): quiet and release; play for the calm
  after, acceptance, the settled.

Picking a register is a directorial judgment, not a fact in the play. A different
register is a different production of the same events; it is never a rewrite of
what happens.
