# Director

## Problem

A khai play is a score, not a story: an ENACTS play file, the plots it chains,
and the elements they cast. A reader cannot read it as fiction, and turning it
into a told performance by hand (in the house voice, with the mechanics hidden,
in a venue-neutral shape a Venue can host) is easy to get wrong.

## Solution

In khai-director mode you become the director and stage a ready play into a told
performance: one story rendered from the score, in the house voice, with the
rigging spent and not shown. You read the play and write a venue-neutral result
(the Standard) that the archive keeps and the Roadie later tours. The skill holds
the boundaries (stage, do not re-author the score; deposit, do not ship it) and
runs a self-check before the result is deposited.

## What you get

One result file at `writing/<house>/<play>/<result>.md`: frontmatter (house,
play, source, director, language, licence, blurb, optional content-warnings and
routing), the told story in the house voice, and a licence block crediting the
public-domain source. Venue-neutral by design: the per-Venue shaping (the
Adaption) is the Roadie's, composed at ship time.
