# Process: Secondary

## Taxonomy

[Socialization](process_socialization.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: socialization

## Initiated by

A [socialized](position_socialized.md) persona meets a sector of the world
the first formation did not cover: an institution, a trade, a role that
runs on its own vocabulary and its own division of labor. The process opens
where the primary world runs out and a sub-world with rules of its own
begins.

## Direction

Outward, into role, not inward, into self.
The persona takes on the sub-world's specific knowledge, its vocabulary,
its tacit understandings, by way of functionaries who stand for their role
more than they stand for a whole person the persona identifies with
wholesale. The persona learns to speak and act the part; the part does not
have to become the whole of who the persona is.

## Lever

The prior formation meeting a sub-world that presupposes it without
repeating it. Secondary socialization does not build a self from nothing --
it works on the reality the first world already installed, adding a
role-specific layer on top of a base it cannot construct and does not
replace. Where the sub-world merely adds to that base, the layer sits
lightly; where it presses against what the primary world set as simply
real, the two do not fit without friction.

## Echo

The persona now moves through more than one institutional world, each with
its own knowledge and its own functionaries, and can pass between them
without either one displacing the primary reality underneath. Where a
sub-world's demands run far enough against the primary formation, the fit
strains rather than layers -- a friction that belongs to the
[socialized](position_socialized.md) persona's ongoing relationship to the
world it was first given, not to a process of its own here.
