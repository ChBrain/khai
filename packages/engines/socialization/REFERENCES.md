# Socialization: Reference

## Line of Work

Socialization as **process**: how the objective social world -- built by
prior generations and handed to a newcomer ready-made -- becomes the
persona's own taken-for-granted reality, first in childhood and then, in a
different form, across the institutional sub-worlds a person is inducted
into afterward. The domain does not model the durable residue a formation
leaves (that is heritage's), the self-story built on the formation (that is
identity's), the moment-to-moment pull of others' presence (that is
conformity's), or the content of any particular norm (that is the
culture's) -- it models the mechanism itself: how a socially constructed
order comes to feel simply real, and how the induction continues into
adult institutional life.

The spine is Berger and Luckmann's account of the social construction of
reality: society is a human product (externalization), society is an
objective reality (objectivation), and man is a social product
(internalization). Socialization is the third moment read from the
inside -- primary socialization inducts the child into society as such,
making the world of significant others THE world; secondary socialization
later inducts the already-socialized person into institutional sub-worlds
the first formation did not cover.

## Restrictions

What the engine refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **The durable residue a formation leaves**: The engine models the
  mechanism by which a world becomes taken-for-granted, not the
  class-marked dispositions and capital that mechanism deposits over a
  lifetime. The habitus a formation leaves behind, and what it transmits
  across generations, is the heritage engine's.
- **The self-story built on the formation**: The engine models the world
  becoming real to the persona, not the narrative the persona builds about
  who they are on top of it. The account of self assembled from the
  formation is identity's.
- **The moment-to-moment pull of others' presence**: The engine models a
  standing, structural process laid down over years, not the live pull to
  align with whoever is in the room right now. That immediate social
  pressure is conformity's.
- **The norms' content**: The engine ships the mechanism that makes an
  order feel real, not what any given order actually requires or forbids.
  Which norms a culture installs through this mechanism is the culture's
  to supply.

## Encoding

Source to constraint. A process anchor (the internalizing movement) with
one process naming the mechanism, one position naming the stance it leaves
behind, and one process naming how the formation continues past childhood.

- **[socialization](process_socialization.md)** (the anchor): The read
  itself -- an objective world built by others is handed to a newcomer and
  becomes, through the process, reality itself rather than one
  construction among others. Anchored by Berger & Luckmann (internalization
  as the third moment of the dialectic).
- **[internalization](process_internalization.md)**: The mechanism --
  identification with significant others, crystallizing into the
  generalized other, through which the world stops being read as report
  and starts being apprehended directly as real. Anchored by Berger &
  Luckmann (primary socialization) and Mead (the generalized other).
- **[socialized](position_socialized.md)**: The stance the process leaves
  the persona holding -- a member who inhabits the given world rather than
  inspecting it, with no vantage before the formation to return to.
  Anchored by Berger & Luckmann (the taken-for-granted world).
- **[secondary](process_secondary.md)**: How the formation continues --
  later induction into institutional sub-worlds and role-specific
  knowledge, built on the self primary socialization already installed.
  Anchored by Berger & Luckmann (secondary socialization).
