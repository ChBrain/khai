# Process: Internalization

## Taxonomy

[Socialization](process_socialization.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: socialization

## Initiated by

An objective event -- a parent's face, a rule enforced, a word repeated --
is read as more than itself: as the expression of another's meaning, and
through that significant other's meaning, as a piece of a world that holds
together. The process opens the moment the child stops merely reacting to
the event and starts reading through it to the shared meaning behind it.

## Direction

From the significant other's world into the child's own.
The child first identifies with the significant other -- takes on their
roles and attitudes -- before it can take on the world those roles and
attitudes belong to. Role by role, attitude by attitude, the significant
others' world becomes the child's world, not a report about a world held
elsewhere.

## Lever

The child's total dependence on significant others, set against the
near-total absence of a competing account. There is no second set of
parents offering a rival world to weigh against the first; the significant
others reach the child before comparison is possible. What they hand over
is not filtered for plausibility against an alternative -- it arrives
simply as the shape of things.

## Echo

The particular others coalesce into what Berger and Luckmann name the
generalized other: a general capacity to take the attitude of "anyone" in
the persona's world, not just this parent or that sibling. Once the
generalized other is in place, the world is no longer received from a
person; it is apprehended directly as objective, self-evident reality. The
[socialized](position_socialized.md) stance follows from that
crystallization.
