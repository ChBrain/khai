- f9585e6: Add the socialization engine: socialization as process -- how the objective social world, built by prior generations and handed to a newcomer ready-made, is internalized until it is no longer one possible order among others but reality itself, and how that induction continues past childhood into institutional sub-worlds. A process anchor (socialization) with one process naming the mechanism (internalization: identification with significant others crystallizing into the generalized other), one position naming the stance it leaves behind (socialized: a member who inhabits the given world rather than inspecting it), and one process naming how the formation continues (secondary: later induction into institutional sub-worlds built on the self primary socialization already installed). Grounded in Berger & Luckmann's _The Social Construction of Reality_ (1966), with Mead's generalized other (1934). The durable residue a formation leaves is heritage's; the self-story built on it is identity's; the moment-to-moment pull of others' presence is conformity's; the norms' content is the culture's.
