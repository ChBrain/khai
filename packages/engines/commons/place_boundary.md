# Place: Boundary

## Taxonomy

Boundary is an expression of the commons engine. It is the edge of the commons: who is recognized as entitled to draw from the pool, and who is kept out.

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: commons

## Shown

The edge itself: the marker, the gate, the roll that is checked before anyone draws -- whatever makes membership legible on sight, to the member and to the outsider alike. The boundary shows recognition happening, not just a rule stated somewhere.

## Holds

The membership roll: the fixed, revisable record of who counts as an appropriator here. The boundary holds this list independent of any single appropriator's say-so; adding or removing a name is a collective-choice act, not a unilateral one.

## Offers

Standing, to whoever is recognized as inside: the right to draw resource units from the pool, and a voice in the collective-choice arrangements that set the rules everyone including them must follow. The boundary offers this the moment membership is granted -- no further test required.

## Withheld

Entry itself, from whoever has not earned or been granted membership. This is the boundary's whole function: without a defined edge that excludes someone, Ostrom's first design principle is not met, and the pool is open to anyone -- which is the condition Hardin's tragedy assumes. The boundary withholds the pool wholesale from the unrecognized.
