# Place: Commons

## Taxonomy

Commons is a place engine (TO SHOW). It models a common-pool resource governed by its own users through crafted institutions -- not by an owner, and not by a distant regulator. The commons is a shared place whose sustainability depends on the rules its users hold and enforce for themselves.

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: commons

## Shown

The shared resource in plain view: the herd on the pasture, the water in the channel, the fish coming up in the nets, the users drawing from the same stock side by side. What is shown is finitude made visible -- the pool is not abstract; its level, its yield, its crowding can be seen by everyone who draws from it.

## Holds

The crafted institutions that outlast any single user's visit: the boundary rules for who may draw, the collective-choice arrangements that set and revise those rules, the monitoring and graduated sanctions that hold compliance. These do not belong to any one appropriator; they are fixed to the commons itself, carried forward whether or not the person who set them is still present.

## Offers

What membership in good standing makes available: the standing to draw resource units up to the agreed share, a voice in the collective-choice process that sets and revises the rules, the benefit of monitoring that most others also submit to. The commons offers what no single user could sustain alone -- a resource that survives repeated use precisely because the rules are shared.

## Withheld

The sustainable yield, withheld from whoever would take past their share: the reserve that regeneration depends on does not release just because someone wants it. Withheld too is continued access itself, from whoever breaks the rules and exhausts the graduated sanctions built to hold them. Take without limit or defect without consequence, and what the commons withholds is the very thing Hardin predicted the tragedy would take: the resource's future.
