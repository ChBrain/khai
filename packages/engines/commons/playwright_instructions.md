# Instructions: Commons

How a Playwright wires the commons engine into a play. You wire by linking the
play's own content to the engine's files; the engine files stay untouched, they
are canon. The Roadie plumbs the engine's law into Instructions Knowledge on
deploy. Authoring guidance, not runtime content, and it does not go on tour.

## Human

- The human defines the commons: which expression (pool, boundary, or sanction) is in play, what the shared resource is, who is entitled to draw from it, and what happens to a member who breaks the rules.

## Agent

- From the play's own content, a persona links the commons expression it is standing in relation to under its Projection. The expression is the institutional pressure the persona faces, not what they feel about it. You link to the engine, you never edit it.

## Collaboration

- The group and conformity engines hold what cooperating or defecting feels like from inside; this engine holds the institution itself -- the pool, the boundary, the sanction -- that cooperation and defection act on. The desire engine holds the appetite that tempts an appropriator past their share. The hierarchy engine holds where a member ranks once inside the boundary; this engine holds only whether they are inside it.

## Knowledge

- Commons is a common-pool resource governed by its own users through crafted institutions. It is a place: the pool of what is shared and finite, the boundary of who may draw from it, the sanction that holds the rules in force. It shows the resource in visible use; it holds the institutions that outlast any one appropriator's visit; it offers standing and a share to whoever is recognized inside the boundary; it withholds the resource's sustainable yield from whoever would take past it, and continued access from whoever exhausts the graduated sanctions. The engine models three expressions: pool (the resource stock itself, subtractable and finite), boundary (who is entitled to draw and who is excluded), and sanction (the monitoring and graduated response that keeps the rules enforced).

## System

- Do not model the felt trust or the pull to free-ride here; that belongs to the group and conformity engines. This engine models the institution, not the felt charge of keeping or breaking its rules.
- Do not collapse sanction into simple punishment. It is graduated: the harshest response is held in reserve, not issued first.
- Do not model the individual appetite that tempts overuse; that belongs to the desire engine.
- Do not edit the engine's files; wire only from the play's side.
