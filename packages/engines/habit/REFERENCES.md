# Habit: Reference

## Line of Work

Habit as **process**: how a response, repeated in a stable context, comes to run from the context without intention. The domain models four passes -- cueing (a stable context fires the response), repetition (the pairing in a stable context lays the groove), automaticity (the response runs without intention or goal), and friction (a change of context interrupts the loop and returns the act to deliberation) -- and what each demands of the persona whose behaviour is moving through it.

The spine is the cue-response account of habit: a habit is not a strong intention but a learned association between a stable context and a response, built by repetition and run automatically when the cue recurs. Wood and Neal supply the architecture (habit as a context-response association at the habit-goal interface, distinct from goal-directed action); Lally et al. supply the formation curve (automaticity grows with repetition in a consistent context, steeply then flattening). Graybiel supplies the mechanism (the basal ganglia chunk a learned sequence into a single retrieved unit); Verplanken supplies the measure (habit strength as automaticity, not frequency); Duhigg supplies the popular synthesis (the cue-routine-reward loop).

## Restrictions

What the engine refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **The valuation of the outcome**: The engine models how a response becomes automatic, not what makes its outcome worth pursuing. The weighing of payoff and the discounting of delay belong to the **reward** engine; habit runs whether or not the reward is still valued.
- **The deliberate plan that starts a behaviour**: The engine models the groove that forms from repetition, not the conscious if-then plan a persona forms to initiate an action in the first place. That intentional staging belongs to the **implementation-intention** engine; habit begins where the plan stops being needed.
- **The goal that seeds the pursuit**: The engine models the response that survives its purpose. The goal that first motivated the behaviour -- and the goal-directed control that competes with habit -- belongs to the **goal** engine.
- **How the habit changes across the play**: The engine models the persona's current pass at entry. Movement across passes -- deepening through repetition, breaking through friction -- belongs to the play's arc.

Source to constraint, per file.

- **[cueing](process_cueing.md)** (pass): A stable context fires the response. Anchored by Wood & Neal (2007) -- the cue, not the goal, retrieves the response; Duhigg (2012) -- the cue that opens the loop.
- **[repetition](process_repetition.md)** (pass): The pairing in a stable context lays the groove. Anchored by Lally et al. (2010) -- the formation curve under consistent repetition; Verplanken (2006) -- strength as accrued automaticity, not frequency alone.
- **[automaticity](process_automaticity.md)** (pass): The response runs without intention or goal. Anchored by Graybiel (2008) -- the chunked sequence retrieved whole from the cue; Wood & Neal (2007) -- the response decoupled from the outcome that built it.
- **[friction](process_friction.md)** (pass): A change of context interrupts the loop and returns the act to deliberation. Anchored by Wood & Neal (2007) -- the cue removed, the response un-triggered; Duhigg (2012) -- changing the routine by attacking the loop.
