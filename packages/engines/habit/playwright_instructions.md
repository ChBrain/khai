# Instructions: Habit

How a Playwright wires the habit engine into a play. You wire by linking the
play's own content to the engine's files; the engine files stay untouched, they
are canon. The Roadie plumbs the engine's law into Instructions Knowledge on
deploy. Authoring guidance, not runtime content, and it does not go on tour.

## Human

- The human defines the habit: the response, the stable context that cues it, how deeply the repetition has cut the groove, and whether the context is currently holding or breaking. The human also sets whether the original goal is still live or has faded.

## Agent

- From the play's own content, a persona links the habit pass the moment runs (cueing, repetition, automaticity, or friction) under its Projection. Let the pass shift as the context holds and the groove deepens, or as the context changes and the act returns to choice. You link to the engine, you never edit it.

## Collaboration

- The reward engine holds the valuation of the outcome the habit may once have served; the habit can run after that value has gone. The implementation-intention engine holds the deliberate if-then plan that starts a behaviour before any groove exists. The goal engine holds the purpose that seeded the pursuit and the goal-directed control that competes with the habit. The emotion and mood engines hold the felt charge around the act -- the pull of the cue, the unease of the broken routine.

## Knowledge

- A habit is a learned association between a stable context and a response, built by repetition and run from the cue without intention. The process moves through cueing (a stable context -- time, place, preceding act, state -- fires the response), repetition (the pairing in a consistent context lays the groove, steeply at first and flattening), automaticity (the response runs chunked and unbidden, decoupled from the goal that built it), and friction (a change of context removes the cue and returns the act to deliberation). The passes are not a clean sequence; a habit can be deepened by more repetition or undone by a change of context.

## System

- Do let the habit run after its goal has faded; automaticity is decoupled from the value of the outcome, and a persona may act without being able to say why.
- Do attack the cue, not the will, to break a habit; friction works by removing or changing the context, not by stronger intention against it.
- Do not conflate habit with goal-directed action; a habit is fired by the context, a goal-directed act is chosen for its outcome, and the two can pull against each other.
- Do not edit the engine's files; wire only from the play's side.
