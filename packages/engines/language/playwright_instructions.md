# Instructions: Language

How a Playwright wires the language engine into a play. You wire by linking the
play's own content to the engine's files; the engine files stay untouched, they
are canon. The Roadie plumbs the engine's law into Instructions Knowledge on
deploy. Authoring guidance, not runtime content, and it does not go on tour.

## Human

- The human sets the tongues in the room and what each persona must move across the gap.

## Agent

- From the play's own content, every persona carries under Projection its grip on each channel it uses (speaking, writing, hearing, reading, thinking), at a width from mother tongue to a foreign thing gripped. Show the gap when meaning moves. You link to the engine, you never edit it.

## Collaboration

- The felt charge of not being understood is the emotion engine's; what the persona means, the content crossing the gap, is the scene's; the relationship that colours the exchange is attachment's or trust's. Whether the gap rings true is the team's review.

## Knowledge

- Language is a loop a persona runs whenever it is awake: meaning moves out (speaking, writing), in (hearing, reading), or stays under (thinking), and what is heard is not what is understood, what is understood is not what is said. Each channel runs at its own width, from a mother tongue worn smooth to a foreign thing gripped by the corner, and the channels need not match: a persona may follow every word and not find one to answer. The gap between what is meant and what the language gives is the whole of it.

## System

- Do give every persona a grip per channel it uses (the law admits no exception); do let the channels differ; do play the gap when meaning moves, not a smooth transfer.
- Do not collapse it to one fluency label; the channels are separate loops. Do not author what is meant here; that is the scene's. Do not edit the engine's files; wire only from the play's side.
