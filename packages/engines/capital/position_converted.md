# Position: Converted Capital

## Taxonomy

Converted capital is an expression of the capital engine. The persona holds high cultural or social capital that compensates for lower economic capital; position is maintained through conversion and the legitimacy that non-economic forms confer.

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: capital

## Has

The persona is rich in one or both of the non-economic forms -- credentials, specialised knowledge, legitimate cultural taste, or dense and well-placed networks -- and uses these to occupy a field position that economic capital alone would not purchase. The conversion rate is favourable enough that the deficit in economic capital does not foreclose access to the field's central positions.

## Orders

The position demands the active maintenance of the forms that substitute for economic capital. Credentials must be kept current, cultural capital must be performed, networks must be tended. Conversion is not passive: the persona must work to ensure the field continues to recognise the forms they hold as equivalent to what they lack.

## Loses

The position forecloses economic leverage in negotiations where money is the primary language. When the field shifts its conversion rates -- devaluing credentials, delegitimating cultural forms, or dissolving the networks that generate social capital -- the persona's position can erode faster than economic capital would.

## Drives

When conversion rates are challenged or non-economic forms are delegitimated, the persona invests more deeply in the forms they hold, seeks new conversion routes, or moves to adjacent fields where those forms are more highly valued.
