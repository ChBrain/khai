# Capital: Reference

## Line of Work

Capital as **position**: the persona's location in a field as determined by the volume and composition of capital they hold. The domain models five expressions -- dominant (high total capital; field position secure), converted (high cultural or social capital compensating low economic), rising (high economic capital, low cultural or social recognition), marginalized (low across all forms), and mismatched (high capital in an adjacent field, low here) -- and what each demands of the persona who holds it.

The spine is Bourdieu's field theory of capital: economic, cultural, and social capital as distinct but inter-convertible resources that determine position in a field; the field as a structured space of positions in which capital determines what strategies are available. The five expressions model the most dramaturgically useful configurations of capital composition within a field.

## Origin

Bourdieu's sociology of capital, fields, and social reproduction.

## Restrictions

What the engine refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Capital as inherited pieces**: The engine models field position by capital configuration, not the pieces a persona has accumulated and carries. What the persona has inherited -- objects, knowledge, practices, connections -- as portable assets belongs to the **heritage** engine.
- **Status as deference rank**: The engine models field position, not rank in the scene's interactional deference order. The expectation-states logic of deference belongs to the **status** engine.
- **The habitus and dispositions that capital produces**: The engine models the structural position, not the embodied dispositions, tastes, and automatic strategies that living in a capital configuration produces. Those belong to the persona's own files and the play's context.
- **How the capital configuration shifts across the play**: The engine models the persona's configuration at entry. How capital is accumulated, lost, or converted across the run belongs to the play's arc.

## Encoding

Source to constraint, per file.

- **[capital](position_capital.md)** (the anchor): Capital as position: field position by configuration of economic, cultural, and social capital. Anchored by Bourdieu (1986) -- the three forms and their convertibility; (1984) -- the space of positions.
- **[dominant](position_dominant.md)** (expression): High total capital; field position secure; the persona acts from accumulated advantage. Anchored by Bourdieu (1984) -- dominant configurations and the performance of distinction.
- **[converted](position_converted.md)** (expression): High cultural or social capital compensating low economic capital; position maintained through conversion. Anchored by Bourdieu (1986) -- capital convertibility; (1984) -- converted configurations.
- **[rising](position_rising.md)** (expression): High economic capital, low cultural or social recognition; position advancing but not yet secured. Anchored by Bourdieu (1984) -- the nouveaux riches and the limits of economic capital in fields that prize cultural legitimacy.
- **[marginalized](position_marginalized.md)** (expression): Low capital across all forms; field position precarious. Anchored by Bourdieu (1984) -- dominated configurations; Savage et al. (2013) -- precarious configurations in contemporary class mapping.
- **[mismatched](position_mismatched.md)** (expression): High capital in an adjacent field, low here; position precarious from irrelevance, not absence. Anchored by Bourdieu & Wacquant (1992) -- field-specificity of capital; capital that is valuable in one field may be worthless in another.
