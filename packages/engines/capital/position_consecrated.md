# Position: Consecrated Capital

## Taxonomy

Consecrated capital is an expression of the capital engine. The persona's capital has been converted into symbolic capital by an act of institutional recognition -- a title, an honour, a credential from the field's consecrating institution -- and the rank is held without being demonstrated.

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: capital

## Has

The persona holds recognition already banked. Bourdieu's consecrating institutions -- the elite school, the academy, the honours list, the old name -- perform the act that converts accumulated capital into legitimacy itself: the persona is no longer someone with resources but someone entitled, and the field misrecognises the arbitrariness of that conversion as natural order. What the persona has is symbolic capital in its purest state: the form the other capitals assume when they are perceived and recognised as legitimate, guaranteed by the institution the way a bank guarantees a note.

## Orders

The position demands that rank be embodied, never argued. Consecration means the case has been made once, institutionally, and is not to be made again; visible effort, open competition, or self-justification would concede that the rank still needs earning and thereby undo it. The persona is expected to perform ease -- the effortlessness that marks distinction -- and to act as the consecrating institution's living warrant, upholding the rites that made them.

## Loses

The position forecloses striving. The consecrated persona cannot openly compete with the unconsecrated without descending to a contest their rank claims to be above, and cannot re-demonstrate what the title certifies without suggesting the title has stopped working. It also binds the persona's fate to the consecrating institution: recognition held in an institution's currency devalues with it, and the persona has no independent denomination to fall back on.

## Drives

When the rank is questioned, the persona mobilises the consecration rather than the substance: the title is cited, the institution invoked, the challenger's standing to judge disputed. The defence of a consecrated position is a defence of the bank, not of the deed -- which is why a field-wide crisis of the consecrating institution reaches every persona it ever stamped at once.
