# Position: Rising Capital

## Taxonomy

Rising capital is an expression of the capital engine. The persona holds substantial economic capital but lacks the cultural or social recognition the field grants to its established occupants; position is advancing but not yet secured.

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: capital

## Has

The persona commands economic resources and is using them to purchase access to a field whose cultural and social capital they do not yet fully hold. Money opens doors but does not confer legitimacy; the persona occupies a liminal position in which their economic power is recognised but their right to the field's central positions is not yet conceded by those who hold the cultural and social forms.

## Orders

The position demands the active acquisition of the forms the field requires: credentials, relationships, the embodied dispositions that mark a legitimate field occupant. The persona must spend economic capital to accumulate what it cannot directly purchase, and must do so in ways the field will eventually recognise.

## Loses

The position forecloses the assumption of belonging. A rising persona who acts as though their economic capital already confers the legitimacy associated with cultural and social capital is read as presumptuous and triggers the gatekeeping mechanisms of those who hold the forms being sought. Money must be converted, not simply spent.

## Drives

When the field resists the conversion -- when gatekeepers deny access regardless of economic resources, or when the cultural forms prove harder to acquire than expected -- the persona either redoubles investment in conversion or applies economic pressure to bypass the gatekeeping mechanisms.
