# Position: Downclassed Capital

## Taxonomy

Downclassed capital is an expression of the capital engine. The persona is on Bourdieu's declining trajectory: symbolic standing -- the name, the title, the accustomed regard -- no longer matched by the economic or social substance that once backed it, with the conversion rates thinning season by season.

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: capital

## Has

The persona holds recognition that arrived from a richer past. Trajectory is a constitutive dimension of position alongside volume and composition, and this persona's runs downward: the title has devalued, the assets have drained or the network has aged out of power, yet the symbolic capital -- being known, being owed regard, knowing how things are done -- still opens some doors. What the persona has is a standing that works on credit, honoured by those who remember and invisible to those who count.

## Orders

The position demands the husbanding of a recognition the substance no longer backs. Bourdieu's downclassed accept declining material advantage precisely to avoid losing symbolic capital: keep the front, keep the memberships, keep the vocabulary of ease, and spend from the shrinking store only where the return is symbolic too. Every expenditure is triage -- which appearances are load-bearing, which can be quietly let go before anyone prices them.

## Loses

The position forecloses naming the slide. To say it aloud is to complete it: the credit the standing runs on is exactly the field's assumption that nothing has changed, and an admission reprices everything at once. It equally forecloses the rising persona's open striving -- visible hustle would announce the need -- so the downclassed persona is barred from the very strategies that could rebuild the substance, which is the trajectory's trap.

## Drives

When the gap between standing and substance threatens to become visible, the persona reaches for symbolic strategies: gatekeeping the field's rites so that what they still hold stays the currency, nostalgia that revalues the past their capital was minted in, alliances that marry the name to someone else's substance. The scenes this position drives are pricing scenes -- the moment a room quietly recalculates what the old name is now worth, and who at the table already has.
