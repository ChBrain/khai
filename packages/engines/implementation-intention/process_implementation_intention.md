# Process: Implementation Intention

## Taxonomy

A process (TO IDLE). How a persona bridges the gap between intending to act and actually acting, by forming a specific if-then plan that delegates the initiation of action to a situational cue. Root of the implementation-intention process tree.

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: implementation-intention

## Initiated by

The gap between a goal intention the persona genuinely holds and reliable execution of the actions that would advance it. The implementation intention is not needed when a persona's behavior is already under strong environmental control or strong habitual control; it is needed when the intention exists but consistently fails to translate into action. Gollwitzer's research identifies the mechanisms of the gap: failing to remember the intention at the opportune moment, failing to get started when the moment arrives, and being derailed mid-execution by distractions or competing responses.

## Direction

From goal intention through the if-then plan to automatic action initiation. The implementation intention does not add motivation; it adds a specific mechanism for action initiation by linking the when (a situational cue the persona specifies in advance) to the what (the action they will perform when that cue occurs). The direction of the process is from deliberate planning to delegated execution.

## Lever

The specificity of the if-then format. Gollwitzer's experimental finding is that the effect of implementation intentions on behavior is robust across a wide range of goal domains specifically because the if-then link pre-commits the action to the cue: when X occurs, the persona is pre-decided. The lever is the format itself: "When situation X arises, I will initiate response Y." The more specific the cue and the more specific the response, the more reliably the link holds.

## Echo

The automaticity that forms once the link has been triggered. Gollwitzer documents that repeated triggering of an implementation intention produces a strategic automaticity: the cue comes to initiate the action without deliberate effort, as if habitually. The echo is not a habit -- it remains under the persona's intentional control -- but it resembles one in its ease of initiation. What the implementation intention leaves behind is a pre-decided response that no longer requires deliberation at the point of execution.
