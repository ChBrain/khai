# Process: Planning

## Taxonomy

[Implementation Intention](process_implementation_intention.md), the phase in which the persona forms the specific if-then plan. Planning is the deliberate construction of the link: identifying the critical situational cue and specifying the action response to it.

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: implementation-intention

## Initiated by

The persona's recognition that the goal intention alone is insufficient: they intend to act but have not been acting. Planning begins when the persona makes the commitment to specify the when and the what -- to move from "I intend to do X" to "when Y happens, I will do X." The recognition may be prompted by repeated failure to act on the intention, by external instruction, or by the persona's own deliberate choice to strengthen their plan.

## Direction

From the general intention to the specific link. The planning phase moves the persona from holding a goal intention (I intend to exercise more, I intend to speak up in meetings, I intend to call my mother) to holding a specific if-then plan (when my Monday alarm goes off, I will put on my shoes immediately; when the meeting chair opens the floor, I will say the first thing I prepared). The direction is from vague commitment to specified link.

## Lever

The identification of the critical cue. The planning phase is productive to the degree that the persona identifies a cue that is highly likely to occur, is unambiguous when it occurs, and is genuinely the opportune moment for the intended action. An implementation intention built on a rare, ambiguous, or non-opportune cue will not bridge the gap; the link cannot fire if the cue never occurs or is not recognized.

## Echo

The mental representation of the link. Once the planning phase is complete, the if-then plan is encoded: the persona has not just an intention but a specific pre-decision. The echo of the planning phase is the encoded link that will produce automatic initiation when the cue occurs.
