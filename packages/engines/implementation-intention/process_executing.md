# Process: Executing

## Taxonomy

[Implementation Intention](process_implementation_intention.md), the phase in which the action is carried out. Executing is the completion of the link: the response that the plan specified is being performed.

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: implementation-intention

## Initiated by

The triggered action response. Executing begins at the moment of initiation and continues through the completion of the specified action. Unlike the triggering phase, which is brief and automatic, the executing phase may involve sustained effort, navigation of obstacles, and the ongoing direction of the persona's attention and resources.

## Direction

Through the action to its completion. The executing phase moves the persona from action initiation to action completion. The implementation intention has solved the initiation problem; the executing phase is governed by the goal itself and by whatever competence, resources, and attention the action requires. Implementation intentions do not guarantee successful execution -- they guarantee initiated execution.

## Lever

The pre-committed response resisting deflection. Gollwitzer's research shows that implementation intentions make the action more resistant to competing impulses, distractions, and temptations because the action is already pre-decided: the persona is not weighing the action against alternatives at the point of execution. The lever is this pre-committed quality, which keeps the action going in the face of interference that would derail a merely intended action.

## Echo

The completed action and its effect on the intention-behavior link. Repeated successful execution strengthens the if-then link and moves it toward strategic automaticity: the cue-response association becomes easier to initiate, less effortful to sustain, and more resilient to interference. The echo is the strengthened link that makes the next cycle of triggering and executing more reliable.
