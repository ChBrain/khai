# Implementation Intention: Reference

## Line of Work

Implementation intention as **process**: the if-then plan that bridges a goal intention to its execution. The domain models the root (what an implementation intention is; how it is initiated, what it moves toward, what levers it, and what it echoes) and three phases -- planning (forming the specific if-then link: when X, I will do Y), triggering (the cue X occurs and initiates the pre-decided response), and executing (the response Y is carried through to completion) -- and what each phase requires and forecloses.

The spine is Gollwitzer's theory of implementation intentions and the distinction between goal intentions and implementation intentions. The central finding is that the intention-behavior gap -- the consistent failure of people to act on their own intentions -- is largely addressed by a single structural modification: specifying in advance the when and the where and the how of the intended action in an if-then format. Meta-analytic evidence across dozens of domains shows that the effect size is large and robust. The mechanism is what Gollwitzer calls strategic automaticity: the if-then link delegates action initiation from deliberate intention to the situational cue, so the persona does not have to notice the opportunity, retrieve the intention, and decide to act in the moment -- the cue does the remembering and the deciding. Gollwitzer and Brandstatter supply the experimental foundation; Oettingen's WOOP (Wish, Outcome, Obstacle, Plan) model extends implementation intentions into mental contrasting, showing that the implementation intention is most effective when preceded by realistic appraisal of obstacles.

## Restrictions

What the engine refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Goal intentions**: The goal itself -- what the persona wants to achieve and why -- belongs to the **goal** engine. The implementation intention presupposes a goal intention; it does not generate one.
- **Habit**: An implementation intention produces strategic automaticity, not a habit. Habits are acquired through repetition without deliberate specification of the cue; implementation intentions are deliberately specified. The distinction matters: habits are harder to extinguish; implementation intentions remain under the persona's intentional control.
- **The persona's motivation to pursue the goal**: Whether the persona wants to achieve the goal belongs to the **motivation** engine. The implementation intention does not add motivation; it adds a specific mechanism for action initiation.
- **Planning in the general sense**: Implementation intentions are specifically structured as if-then plans. General planning (developing strategies, allocating resources, sequencing tasks) is not an implementation intention; it does not have the automatic-initiation effect.

## Encoding

Source to constraint, per file.

- **[implementation-intention](process_implementation_intention.md)** (the root): The if-then plan as a process: what initiates it, its direction, its lever, and its echo. Anchored by Gollwitzer (1999) -- the if-then format and the mechanism of strategic automaticity; Gollwitzer & Sheeran (2006) -- the robust effect across domains.
- **[planning](process_planning.md)** (phase): Forming the specific if-then link; identifying the critical cue and specifying the response. Anchored by Gollwitzer (1999) -- the deliberate pre-commitment as the source of the effect; Oettingen (2014) -- obstacle identification as the precondition for effective planning.
- **[triggering](process_triggering.md)** (phase): The cue occurs and initiates the pre-decided response; the environment does the remembering. Anchored by Gollwitzer (1999) -- delegating control to the environment; Gollwitzer & Sheeran (2006) -- cue detection as the primary mechanism.
- **[executing](process_executing.md)** (phase): The action is carried through to completion; the pre-committed response resists deflection. Anchored by Gollwitzer & Sheeran (2006) -- resistance to competing impulses and distractions; repeated execution strengthens the if-then link toward strategic automaticity.
