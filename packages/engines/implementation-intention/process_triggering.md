# Process: Triggering

## Taxonomy

[Implementation Intention](process_implementation_intention.md), the phase in which the situational cue specified in the plan occurs and activates the pre-decided response. Triggering is the moment the if fires the then.

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: implementation-intention

## Initiated by

The occurrence of the cue specified in the planning phase. Triggering is initiated not by the persona's deliberate decision to act but by the environment: when the alarm sounds, when the meeting opens, when the critical moment in the situation arrives. The persona does not have to remember to act; the cue does the work of remembering. Gollwitzer calls this delegating control to the environment.

## Direction

From the cue's occurrence to the initiation of the action response. The triggering phase is rapid: unlike deliberate action initiation, which requires the persona to notice the opportunity, retrieve the intention, evaluate the situation, and decide to act, the pre-decided link bypasses much of this chain. The direction is: cue detected, action initiated.

## Lever

The salience of the cue. The triggering phase is more reliable when the specified cue is distinctive, clear, and not easily confused with other situational features. A cue that is genuinely distinct in the environment is more likely to be detected and to fire the link; an ambiguous cue may pass unnoticed. The pre-decided quality of the link is only as strong as the cue's reliable detection.

## Echo

The action's initiation. Triggering leaves the persona in the action -- or the action has begun. If the cue fires reliably, the action initiates reliably; the persona finds themselves doing the thing they intended without having had to deliberate at the point of execution. The echo of triggering is action started.
