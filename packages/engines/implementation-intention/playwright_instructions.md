# Instructions: Implementation Intention

How a Playwright wires the implementation-intention engine into a play. You wire by linking the
play's own content to the engine's files; the engine files stay untouched, they
are canon. The Roadie plumbs the engine's law into Instructions Knowledge on
deploy. Authoring guidance, not runtime content, and it does not go on tour.

## Human

- The human names the goal intention the persona holds (what they intend to do), the if-then plan they have formed (when X, I will do Y), the specific situational cue (what X is and when it is expected to occur), and the specific action response (what Y is). The human also names what has previously prevented the persona from acting on the intention, since the implementation intention is designed to address that specific gap.

## Agent

- From the play's own content, a persona links the implementation-intention phase they are in (planning, triggering, or executing) to their Projection. The phase marks where the persona is in the process of bridging the intention-behavior gap, not what the persona intends in a general sense.

## Collaboration

- The goal engine holds the goal intention that the implementation intention serves. The motivation engine holds the persona's broader motivational state. The executive-function engine holds the self-regulatory capacities (inhibition, working memory, planning) that support the executing phase. The habit engine holds the distinction between strategic automaticity and genuine habit.

## Knowledge

- An implementation intention is a specific if-then plan: "When situation X occurs, I will perform response Y." The specificity is the mechanism. The engine models three phases: planning (the persona forms the if-then link; the critical cue is identified; the action response is specified; the plan is mentally committed), triggering (the situational cue X occurs in the environment; the pre-decided link activates the action response automatically, without the persona having to notice the opportunity and decide to act; the environment does the remembering), and executing (the action Y is carried through to completion; the pre-committed response is resistant to competing impulses and distractions; repeated successful execution strengthens the if-then link toward strategic automaticity). The effect of implementation intentions on behavior is large and robust across domains because the if-then format delegates action initiation to the situational cue.

## System

- An implementation intention is not a general plan. It must have the specific if-then format: a specific cue (when, where, under what circumstances) and a specific action response. General plans ("I will try harder," "I will work on this soon") do not have the automatic-initiation effect.
- The implementation intention does not add motivation; it adds a mechanism for action initiation. A persona who does not want the goal should not be given an implementation intention to achieve it; the goal engine holds whether the persona genuinely wants the goal.
- Triggering is automatic, not deliberate. The persona does not decide to act when the cue occurs; the cue initiates the action. If the persona is deliberating about whether to act when the cue occurs, they are not in the triggering phase; the if-then link has not fired.
- Do not edit the engine's files; wire only from the play's side.
