# Motivation: Reference

## Line of Work

Motivation as **process**: what moves a persona to act, and where the drive springs from. The domain does not model the content of what is wanted -- the specific goal or value -- nor whether the persona is able to perform the act; it models the quality of the drive behind the move, set by whether the reason for acting lives inside the act, inside the persona, or outside in the world.

The spine is the self-determination continuum: motivation is not one quantity that is high or low, but a set of qualitatively different regulations, ranging from the freely chosen to the externally pressed to the absent. The same act can be driven by any of them, and the source decides how the effort is shaped and how long it lasts.

## Origin

Self-determination theory and the psychology of motivation, applied directly as process constraints.

| Source          | Key Work                                                                          | Scope                                                                                                                 |
| :-------------- | :-------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | :-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Deci & Ryan** | _Intrinsic Motivation and Self-Determination in Human Behavior_ (1985)            | The intrinsic/extrinsic distinction and the basic psychological needs underwriting self-determined action.            |
| **Ryan & Deci** | _"Self-Determination Theory and the Facilitation of Intrinsic Motivation"_ (2000) | Organismic Integration Theory: the continuum of extrinsic regulation from external through introjected to identified. |
| **Edward Deci** | _"Effects of externally mediated rewards on intrinsic motivation"_ (1971)         | The overjustification effect: an external reward can overwrite an intrinsic reason and crowd it out.                  |

## Restrictions

What the engine refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **The want itself**: The engine models the quality of the drive -- where the reason springs from -- not the content of what is wanted. The specific goal, need, or value is owned by the persona's own Projection and the scene's stakes, not the engine.
- **Capability**: The engine models whether and how the persona is moved to act, not whether it can perform the act. Skill, competence, and the means to succeed are out of scope; the engine assumes the act is available.
- **The feeling**: The engine models the drive, not the emotion that rides it -- the eagerness, dread, or apathy a persona feels while moving is **delegated to the emotion engine**.
- **The world's incentives**: The engine models how a persona takes up a reward or a pressure, not the design of the reward or pressure itself. The contingencies of the scene -- who pays what, who threatens what -- are owned by the place and the plot, not the engine.

## Encoding

Source to constraint, per file.

- **[motivation](process_motivation.md)** (the root): The drive where a need or value meets an available act, with the source of the pull shaping the effort. Anchored by Deci & Ryan (the self-determination continuum).
- **[intrinsic](process_intrinsic.md)** (expression): The act is its own reward; effort is freely given and self-sustaining. Anchored by Deci & Ryan (intrinsic motivation).
- **[identified](process_identified.md)** (expression): The act serves a value the persona owns; effort is willing and durable. Anchored by Ryan & Deci (identified regulation).
- **[introjected](process_introjected.md)** (expression): The act runs on internal pressure -- guilt, shame, ego; effort is brittle and anxious. Anchored by Ryan & Deci (introjected regulation).
- **[external](process_external.md)** (expression): The act runs on outside reward or punishment; effort lasts only while the contingency holds. Anchored by Ryan & Deci (external regulation).
- **[amotivated](process_amotivated.md)** (expression): The link between act and outcome is broken; no drive forms. Anchored by Ryan & Deci (amotivation).
