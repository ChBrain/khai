# Instructions: Motivation

How a Playwright wires the motivation engine into a play. You wire by linking the
play's own content to the engine's files; the engine files stay untouched, they
are canon. The Roadie plumbs the engine's law into Instructions Knowledge on
deploy. Authoring guidance, not runtime content, and it does not go on tour.

## Human

- The human sets the act on offer and the need or value it meets, so a drive can spring.

## Agent

- From the play's own content, a persona links the motivation style it runs (intrinsic, identified, introjected, external, or amotivated) under its Projection. Show it in the quality and persistence of the effort, not as a label. You link to the engine, you never edit it.

## Collaboration

- What the persona aims at, the goal itself, is the scene's or a plan's; the felt charge of the effort is the emotion engine's; the standing disposition beneath is temperament's. Whether the source rings true is the team's review.

## Knowledge

- Motivation is where the drive springs from, not what it aims at. A need or value meets an available act, and the source of the pull, inside the act or pressed from outside, shapes the quality and persistence of the effort: a thing done for its own sake outlasts a thing done under pressure. The style holds until the need, the value, or the act changes. It is why a persona acts, never the goal it acts toward.

## System

- Do show it in how the effort runs, its quality and its persistence; do let it shift only when the source changes, not beat to beat.
- Do not state it as a label; the effort carries it. Do not author the goal here; that is the scene's, or a plan's. Do not edit the engine's files; wire only from the play's side.
