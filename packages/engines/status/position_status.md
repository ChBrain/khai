# Position: Status

## Taxonomy

Status is a position engine (TO HOLD). It models the rank a persona holds in the scene's deference order: who is expected to initiate, who is expected to defer, and what burden of proof a contribution must meet before it is credited.

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: status

## Has

The persona holds a rank assigned by the scene's status order. Rank is conferred, not claimed; it rests on one or more status characteristics -- diffuse (age, gender, race, education level) or specific (task-relevant skill or credential) -- that the scene treats as relevant. Multiple characteristics combine non-additively: the highest-ranking characteristic tends to dominate, but which characteristic the scene foregrounds shifts that combination.

## Orders

The rank demands a commensurate performance. A high-rank persona is expected to initiate, to be heard without interruption, and to be credited with competence until evidence overrides. A low-rank persona is expected to defer, to wait, and to meet a higher burden of proof before contributions are accepted. Acting above or below rank triggers correction.

## Loses

The position forecloses the opposite register. A high-rank persona cannot easily accept correction, ask for help, or express uncertainty without risking rank. A low-rank persona cannot easily assert, initiate, or claim competence without triggering sanction. The position narrows the behavioural range the scene permits.

## Drives

When rank is challenged, the high-rank persona reasserts standing -- through initiative, through correction of the challenger, or through appeal to the characteristic that confers rank. The low-rank persona defers; or, if the cost of deference is high enough, mounts a focused case on one specific, deniable point rather than a direct claim to rank.
