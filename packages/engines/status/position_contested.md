# Position: Contested Status

## Taxonomy

Contested status is an expression of the status engine. Rank is disputed; the burden of proof is active on both sides and the scene has not settled who defers to whom.

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: status

## Has

The persona holds a rank that at least one other participant refuses to concede. The dispute may rest on competing characteristics (each party ranks higher on a different dimension), on ambiguity about which characteristic the scene treats as relevant, or on a direct challenge to the persona's claim. The scene is in a status negotiation it has not resolved.

## Orders

The position demands that the persona establish rank without being seen to merely assert it. Unilateral claims are available but costly; the more effective path is to perform the characteristic that confers rank so the scene confirms it, rather than demanding confirmation.

## Loses

The position forecloses resolution by fiat. A persona who simply insists on their rank without performing the underlying characteristic deepens the contest rather than settling it. Backing down is also costly: it concedes the point and risks a lasting rank drop.

## Drives

When challenged directly, the persona performs the characteristic rather than arguing about rank -- demonstrates expertise, invokes the credential, or acts from the role -- and waits for the scene to confirm. If the scene does not confirm, the persona escalates the performance or reframes which characteristic is at stake.
