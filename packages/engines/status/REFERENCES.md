# Status: Reference

## Line of Work

Status as **position**: the rank a persona holds in the scene's deference order. The domain models four expressions -- high (deferred to), low (defers), contested (rank disputed), and token (visibly solo on a relevant characteristic) -- and what each demands of the persona who holds it.

The spine is Berger et al.'s Status Characteristics Theory (SCT): rank is conferred by status characteristics (diffuse or specific), expectations for competence track rank, and the burden of proof for contributions scales inversely with rank. The non-additive combination rule (the highest-ranking characteristic tends to dominate) and the token effect (isolation amplifies status dynamics) are drawn from the same line of work and its extensions by Ridgeway.

## Restrictions

What the engine refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Power as coercive or reward-based capacity**: The engine models the deference order, not the capacity to impose costs or provide benefits. Coercive and reward power belong to the **power** engine.
- **Role expectations and role congruity**: The engine models rank, not the behavioural script the role specifies. Role expectations and the penalties for violating them belong to the **role** engine.
- **The felt experience of rank**: The emotional charge of being high or low in the deference order -- pride, shame, resentment, entitlement -- belongs to the **emotion** and **mood** engines.
- **Status moves during the scene**: The engine models the persona's entry rank. How rank shifts across the scene belongs to the play's arc and the **status-move** engine.

## Encoding

Source to constraint, per file.

- **[status](position_status.md)** (the anchor): Status as position: the rank a persona holds in the scene's deference order. Anchored by Berger et al. (1972, 1977) -- diffuse and specific characteristics; expectation states; burden of proof scales with rank.
- **[high](position_high.md)** (expression): The persona is deferred to; competence expectations are high and burden of proof is low. Anchored by Berger et al. (1977) -- high-rank expectations; initiative and evaluation as rank performances.
- **[low](position_low.md)** (expression): The persona defers; competence expectations are low and burden of proof is high. Anchored by Berger et al. (1977) -- low-rank expectations; justification demanded before contributions are credited.
- **[contested](position_contested.md)** (expression): Rank is disputed; burden of proof is active on both sides. Anchored by Ridgeway (1982) -- rank as interactional achievement; performance of the underlying characteristic rather than assertion of rank.
- **[token](position_token.md)** (expression): The persona is visibly solo on a relevant characteristic; status effects are amplified. Anchored by Ridgeway (2011) -- the token effect; categorical visibility overdetermines how contributions are received.
