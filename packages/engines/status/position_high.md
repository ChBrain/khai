# Position: High Status

## Taxonomy

High status is an expression of the status engine. The persona is deferred to; competence expectations are high and burden of proof is low.

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: status

## Has

The persona holds a rank the scene treats as conferring authority to initiate, evaluate, and allocate. Others wait for the persona to speak first, credit the persona's contributions more readily, and require less justification from them than from lower-rank peers.

## Orders

The position demands initiative and visible confidence. The persona is expected to set direction, to evaluate the contributions of others, and to perform competence without qualification. Hedging, asking for help, or deferring to a lower-rank peer costs rank.

## Loses

The position forecloses visible uncertainty and ready deference. A high-status persona who regularly defers, apologises, or asks for basic help erodes the rank the scene has conferred. The position makes dependence costly.

## Drives

When rank is challenged, the persona reasserts it -- by claiming the floor, by correcting the challenger, or by invoking the characteristic (expertise, credential, role) that places them above. Ignoring the challenge is also available; acknowledgement is not required.
