# Instructions: Status

How a Playwright wires the status engine into a play. You wire by linking the
play's own content to the engine's files; the engine files stay untouched, they
are canon. The Roadie plumbs the engine's law into Instructions Knowledge on
deploy. Authoring guidance, not runtime content, and it does not go on tour.

## Human

- The human sets the status characteristics the scene treats as relevant: which diffuse characteristics (age, gender, race, background) and which specific characteristics (expertise, credential, role title) confer rank, and what the deference order looks like before the play begins.

## Agent

- From the play's own content, each persona links under Projection the status expression it holds: high, low, contested, or token. Show rank in who initiates, who waits, whose contributions require justification, and who is interrupted -- not in statements about status.

## Collaboration

- The power engine holds the capacity to impose costs or provide benefits; a high-status persona may be powerless, and a powerful persona may be low in rank. The role engine holds the behavioural script the position specifies; status and role are separate axes. The status-move engine holds how rank shifts during the scene.

## Knowledge

- Status is the rank a persona holds in the scene's deference order. It rests on status characteristics -- diffuse (age, gender, race, education) or specific (task skill, credential) -- that the scene treats as relevant. Rank sets who is expected to initiate, who is expected to defer, and how much justification a contribution requires before it is credited. High rank lowers the burden of proof; low rank raises it. Contested means rank is not settled. Token means the persona is visibly solo on a relevant characteristic, which amplifies the status effects already in play.

## System

- Do read status in who initiates, who waits, whose interruptions go unchallenged, and what burden of proof a contribution faces -- not in explicit statements of rank.
- Do not collapse status into power: a high-status persona may have no coercive or reward capacity; a powerful persona may be low in the deference order.
- Do not collapse status into role: a persona can hold a high-status position while violating role expectations, or hold a congruent role at low rank.
- Do not model the emotional register of rank here; that belongs to the emotion and mood engines.
