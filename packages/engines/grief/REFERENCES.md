# Grief: Reference

## Line of Work

Grief as **process**: how a persona adapts to an irreversible loss -- not by returning to what was, but by building what comes after. The domain does not model sadness (that is the emotion engine's expression of irrevocable loss); it models the sustained adaptation work that runs after the fact of loss has been established, through oscillating between confronting it and rebuilding around it.

The spine is the Dual Process Model of coping with bereavement: grief is not a sequence of stages but an oscillation between loss-orientation (confronting the absence) and restoration-orientation (attending to the changed world), neither of which can be held without the other.

## Origin

Bereavement research and loss-adaptation theory, applied directly as process constraints.

| Source                                                   | Key Work                                                     | Scope                                                                                                                                            |
| :------------------------------------------------------- | :----------------------------------------------------------- | :----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Margaret Stroebe &amp; Henk Schut**                    | _"The Dual Process Model of Coping with Bereavement"_ (1999) | The oscillation model: grief as movement between loss-orientation and restoration-orientation, neither held permanently.                         |
| **William Worden**                                       | _Grief Counselling and Grief Therapy_ (1982)                 | Tasks of mourning: accept reality, work through pain, adjust to changed world, find continuing bond -- grief as active work, not passive stages. |
| **Colin Murray Parkes**                                  | _Bereavement: Studies of Grief in Adult Life_ (1972)         | Yearning and searching as the acute phase: the involuntary reach toward the absent.                                                              |
| **Dennis Klass, Phyllis Silverman &amp; Steven Nickman** | _Continuing Bonds_ (1996)                                    | The endpoint of grief is not detachment but a transformed relationship with the lost -- a continuing bond.                                       |

## Restrictions

What the engine refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Sadness**: The engine models the sustained adaptation process after loss, not the discrete emotion of sadness. The moment-read of irrevocable loss is **delegated to the emotion engine** (process_sadness.md). A persona may run both -- the emotion fires in the moment; this engine governs the longer arc of adaptation.
- **Trauma**: The engine models loss adaptation, not traumatic disruption that reorganises the nervous system. Where the loss itself was violent, sudden, or deliberately inflicted and the response is structural, that territory begins to overlap with the stress engine; grief governs the relational adaptation, stress governs the autonomic disruption.
- **Attachment strategy**: The engine models how the persona adapts to loss, not the strategy it uses to seek or give up on closeness to a secure base. The attachment engine governs the proximity-seeking system; grief governs what happens when the object of attachment is permanently gone.
- **The specific object of loss**: The engine models the process, not the nature of what was lost. What the persona lost -- a person, a role, a future -- is owned by the play's arc and the persona's Projection, not by this engine.

## Encoding

Source to constraint, per file.

- **[grief](process_grief.md)** (the root): The adaptation work -- the persona oscillates between confronting the loss and rebuilding around it; neither pole can be held. Anchored by Stroebe &amp; Schut (Dual Process Model).
- **[shock](process_shock.md)** (expression): The loss has not yet become real; the persona moves through the world with the old expectations still intact. Anchored by Parkes (the initial phase of numbness and disbelief).
- **[yearning](process_yearning.md)** (expression): The loss is real and the persona reaches for what is gone; the reach fails, and the failing is the grief. Anchored by Parkes (yearning and searching as the acute phase).
- **[restoration](process_restoration.md)** (expression): The persona turns from the loss to the changed world, picking up tasks and trying new arrangements. Anchored by Stroebe &amp; Schut (restoration orientation) and Worden (task three: adjusting to a world without).
- **[continuing](process_continuing.md)** (expression): The persona finds a way to carry the lost thing forward -- a transformed relationship, not an ending. Anchored by Klass, Silverman &amp; Nickman (continuing bonds) and Worden (task four).
