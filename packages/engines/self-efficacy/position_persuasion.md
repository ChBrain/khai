# Position: Persuasion

## Taxonomy

[Self-Efficacy](position_self_efficacy.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: self-efficacy

## Has

A specific [self-efficacy](position_self_efficacy.md): a capability belief
lent by another's credible word, not proven by the persona's own hand or
read off anyone else's attempt.
The read that takes on a task because someone whose judgment is trusted
said it could be done, borrowed at one remove further than watching a
model try it.
The most fragile source, propped on the credibility of the speaker and apt
to fold at the first real setback.

## Orders

Take the word.
The read does not ask for proof before it commits, only whether the
speaker is trusted, and spends that trust as if it were the persona's own
appraisal.
The room reads the borrowed nerve in how quickly it was handed over, and
how quickly it can be taken back.

## Loses

The option of a belief with its own record behind it.
The persona discovers the read has no attempt of its own underneath the
word that granted it, and cannot defend it past the first setback the way
a proven or witnessed belief could.

## Drives

On the loss of that backing:
rides the lent word into the task and commits on trust in the speaker
alone,
or works against a setback that outruns the persuasion and lets the
propped-up read collapse at a cost, faster than a belief grounded in the
persona's own hand or eye ever would.
The lent-by-a-credible-word baseline holds underneath either way.
