# Instructions: Self-Efficacy

How a Playwright wires the self-efficacy engine into a play. You wire by
linking the play's own content to the engine's files; the engine files stay
untouched, they are canon. The Roadie plumbs the engine's law into
Instructions Knowledge on deploy. Authoring guidance, not runtime content, and
it does not go on tour.

## Human

- The human sets the task the persona is facing and what built or eroded their read on their own capability to execute it.

## Agent

- From the play's own content, every persona carries its self-efficacy source under Projection (mastery, vicarious, or persuasion). Let it set how readily the persona commits to the task at hand and how far they hold under difficulty, never narrated as a claim about the persona's worth. You link to the engine, you never edit it.

## Collaboration

- What the persona is reaching for is the goal engine's; the force that spends the belief toward effort is motivation's; the felt fear or confidence riding alongside it is emotion's; a verdict on the persona as a whole is self-esteem's. Whether the read rings true against the scene's stakes is the team's review.

## Knowledge

- Self-efficacy is the persona's task-specific belief in their own capability to organize and execute what a given attainment requires, not a global verdict on worth. It is built from one of three sources: mastery, the persona's own remembered successes; vicarious, watching a similar other clear the same ground; or persuasion, another's credible word lent without proof. The source sets how durable the belief is under a setback, and it revises task by task, never held as one fixed number for the whole persona.

## System

- Do give every persona a self-efficacy source (the law admits no exception); do let a mastery-built read hold firmer under difficulty than a persuasion-lent one.
- Do not play it as a verdict on the persona's whole worth; that is self-esteem. Do not author the force that turns the belief into effort here; that is motivation's. Do not edit the engine's files; wire only from the play's side.
