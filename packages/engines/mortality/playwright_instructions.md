# Instructions: Mortality

How a Playwright wires the mortality engine into a play. You wire by linking the
play's own content to the engine's files; the engine files stay untouched, they
are canon. The Roadie plumbs the engine's law into Instructions Knowledge on
deploy. Authoring guidance, not runtime content, and it does not go on tour.

## Human

- The human sets the play's brush with death -- the moment finitude turns real for the persona, and what is at stake when it does.

## Agent

- From the play's own content, every persona carries its mortality stance under Projection (worldview, esteem, legacy, denial, or acceptance). Let it organise what the persona reaches for the instant death is made salient, holding under the bravado rather than narrated. You link to the engine, you never edit it.

## Collaboration

- The loss of another is the grief engine's (mortality is one's own end); whether the life mattered is meaning's; the doctrine of the afterlife a persona reaches into is cultures'; the cold spike of fear in the moment is emotion's. Whether the stance rings true is the team's review.

## Knowledge

- Mortality is the standing posture a persona holds toward their own finitude once death is made salient, a resting answer carried before it is asked, holding as one of five stances. No persona meets the fact of their end on neutral ground: the stance decides what they reach for when the floor drops, and the room reads it beneath the bravado before the fear is named.

## System

- Do give every persona a mortality stance (the law admits no exception); do let it organise the reach the moment death turns real, and hold steady beneath the surface.
- Do not play it as the felt fear of a moment; that is emotion. Do not author the loss of another here; that is grief. Do not edit the engine's files; wire only from the play's side.
