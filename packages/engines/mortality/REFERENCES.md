# Mortality: Reference

## Line of Work

Mortality as **position**: the standing posture a persona holds toward their own
finitude once death is made salient. The domain does not model the loss of
another, nor the felt fear of a single moment, nor the question of whether life
means anything; it models the resting answer a persona carries to the one fact
they cannot revise -- that they end -- and how that answer organises the reach
the instant the fact turns real.

## Origin

Existential and terror-management work on death awareness, applied as code
constraints.

| Source                              | Key Work                                                         | Scope                                                                                                |
| ----------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Ernest Becker**                   | _The Denial of Death_ (1973)                                     | Awareness of death as the spring of human action; culture and heroism as the denial that buries it.  |
| **Greenberg, Pyszczynski, Solomon** | _Terror Management Theory_ (1986); _The Worm at the Core_ (2015) | Mortality salience drives worldview defence and self-esteem striving as buffers against death dread. |
| **Irvin Yalom**                     | _Existential Psychotherapy_ (1980)                               | Death as the first of the four givens; the confrontation that can be fled or integrated.             |
| **Martin Heidegger**                | _Being and Time_ (1927)                                          | Being-toward-death; authentic existence as life owned in the face of its own end.                    |

## Restrictions

What the engine refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **The loss of another**: This engine is one's _own_ finitude only. Mourning a
  death that is not the self's, the work of bereavement, **delegates to the grief
  engine**. Mortality is the posture toward my end; grief is the wound of yours.
- **Whether life matters** (the mattering question): The engine models the stance
  toward the end, not the verdict on the worth of the span. Whether a life
  signifies, and against what, **delegates to the meaning engine**. Mortality
  asks how the persona meets the full stop; meaning asks whether the sentence
  was worth writing.
- **The religious or cultural content of the afterlife**: The engine models the
  reach for continuance as a posture, never the doctrine it reaches into. What
  the afterlife _is_, the heaven or rebirth or ancestor-line a culture supplies,
  **delegates to cultures**. Mortality holds the wager; cultures hold its terms.
- **The felt fear of the moment** (the spike): The acute dread that fires and
  fades, the cold drop at a near miss, **delegates to the emotion engine**.
  Mortality is the standing posture under that fear, not the strike of it.
- **The empirical mechanism**: The engine encodes the stances, not the lab. The
  measured effects of mortality salience on judgement and behaviour are
  **the research's**, drawn on here as grounding, not reproduced as claims.

## Encoding

Source to constraint, per file.

- **[mortality](position_mortality.md)** (the anchor): finitude as a standing
  posture held ready for the moment death turns real, a resting answer to a
  terror that has no answer, carried before it is asked rather than run as a
  process. Anchored by Becker (death awareness as the spring of action) and Yalom
  (death as a given that is fled or faced).
- **[worldview](position_worldview.md)** (expression): cling to the cultural
  frame, its values gripped tighter and rivals read as threats to life itself.
  Anchored by Terror Management Theory's worldview defence under mortality
  salience (Greenberg, Pyszczynski & Solomon).
- **[esteem](position_esteem.md)** (expression): earn significance, be a hero on
  the culture's scale, buy a stay against the end with standing. Anchored by
  Terror Management Theory's self-esteem striving and Becker's hero project.
- **[legacy](position_legacy.md)** (expression): outlast the body through
  children, works, or faith, the wager that something of the self continues.
  Anchored by Becker's symbolic immortality and the literal-immortality strand of
  Terror Management Theory.
- **[denial](position_denial.md)** (expression): suppress and distract, push the
  fact out of awareness, the lid kept on what will not stay down. Anchored by
  Becker (the denial of death) and the proximal-defence strand of Terror
  Management Theory.
- **[acceptance](position_acceptance.md)** (expression): face finitude whole and
  live by it, the end let stand as a horizon that sharpens the life. Anchored by
  Yalom (integration of the given) and Heidegger (authentic being-toward-death).
