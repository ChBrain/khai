# Adoption: Reference

## Line of Work

Adoption as **process**: how a new idea travels through a social system over time, and where each persona stands relative to the spread. The domain does not model the idea itself or the system's content; it models the universal shape of diffusion -- the five adopter positions a persona may hold and the four places an idea passes through as it spreads or stalls.

The spine is Rogers's diffusion of innovations, with Moore's chasm: an idea moves from the few who reach for the new before it is proven, across a gap where most adoptions die, through a tipping point into the majority, and finally over the ones who hold the old ground. A persona's adopter position is relative to the idea in question -- an innovator with one idea is a laggard with another.

## Origin

Diffusion theory, applied directly as process constraints.

| Source             | Key Work                                          | Scope                                                                                                                                                    |
| :----------------- | :------------------------------------------------ | :------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Everett Rogers** | _Diffusion of Innovations_ (1962 / 2003, 5th ed.) | The five adopter categories -- innovator, early adopter, early majority, late majority, laggard -- and the S-curve of diffusion through a social system. |
| **Geoffrey Moore** | _Crossing the Chasm_ (1991)                       | The chasm between the early adopters and the early majority -- the gap where most adoptions die before reaching the mainstream.                          |

## Restrictions

What the engine refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **The idea itself**: The engine models the spread, not the innovation that spreads. What the idea is, and why it is taken up or refused, is the scene's, materialised in its own pieces and dialogue, not carried in the engine.
- **Why a persona adopts**: The drive behind reaching for the new or refusing it -- intrinsic interest, social pressure, reward -- is the motivation engine's. Adoption names where the persona stands in the spread; motivation names why.
- **Rank and influence**: How much weight a persona's adoption carries in the system -- the opinion leader the early majority watches -- shades into the hierarchy engine; adoption models the position in the curve, not the standing that amplifies it.
- **The system's culture**: How fast a particular social system adopts, and which ideas it rewards, is the consuming culture's; the engine ships the universal curve, not the local rate.
