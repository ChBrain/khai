# Instructions: Adoption

How a Playwright wires the adoption engine into a play. You wire by linking the
play's own content to the engine's files; the engine files stay untouched, they
are canon. The Roadie plumbs the engine's law into Instructions Knowledge on
deploy. Authoring guidance, not runtime content, and it does not go on tour.

## Human

- The human names the idea and the social system whose uptake the run tracks.

## Agent

- From the play's own content, link to adoption where it fits: a persona links the adopter position it holds (a position), a plot links the spread (the process), a scene names the place the idea has reached (a place), and the links advance over the plots. You link to the engine; you never edit it.

## Collaboration

- The feeling of being passed by or first is the emotion engine's; who the other is and the live tie are the scene's; whether the movement rings true is the team's review.

## Knowledge

- Adoption is how an idea crosses a social system over time. A persona's relation to the idea is a position on a curve, never a trait: the same persona is an innovator with one idea and a laggard with another. A system takes a thing up unevenly, and the idea passes through stages as it spreads. The engine is that curve, so any stance is a moment in a movement.

## System

- Do read it across the plots: let the links from persona and scene advance over the run, so the curve shows.
- Do not pin a persona to one position; freeze the curve and you keep a label but lose the engine. Do not model the other or the relationship here; that is the scene's.
- Do not edit the engine's files; wire only from the play's side.
