# Position: Legitimate Power

## Taxonomy

Legitimate power is an expression of the power engine. The persona holds authority by role, norm, or office; compliance rests on the shared belief that the persona has the right to direct.

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: power

## Has

The persona holds a position -- a formal role, an institutional office, a socially recognised right -- that the scene treats as conferring the authority to direct others within a specified domain. Others comply not because they are rewarded or threatened but because they believe the persona's direction is sanctioned: it is what one does in response to that role.

## Orders

The position demands that the persona act within the scope of the role. Authority exercised outside its domain is experienced as overreach and triggers resistance rather than compliance. The persona must also enact the role convincingly: legitimate power requires visible alignment between the person and the office.

## Loses

The position forecloses authority beyond its sanctioned domain. A persona who directs others on matters outside the role's scope loses the deference the role provides. It also forecloses disregard for the norms that define the role: a persona who violates the expectations attached to the office undermines the basis on which others comply.

## Drives

When authority is questioned, the persona invokes the role explicitly -- references the norm, the title, the institutional frame -- rather than arguing on substance. When directed outside the role's scope, the persona either clarifies the boundary or accepts the constraint.
