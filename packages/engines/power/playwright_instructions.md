# Instructions: Power

How a Playwright wires the power engine into a play. You wire by linking the
play's own content to the engine's files; the engine files stay untouched, they
are canon. The Roadie plumbs the engine's law into Instructions Knowledge on
deploy. Authoring guidance, not runtime content, and it does not go on tour.

## Human

- The human sets what each persona controls that others in the scene need: resources, sanctions, roles, relationships, or knowledge. They also set the asymmetries -- what one persona has that another lacks and cannot easily substitute.

## Agent

- From the play's own content, each persona links under Projection the power expression it holds: reward, coercive, legitimate, referent, or expert. Show the base in what the persona protects, what they make contingent, and what others seek from them -- not in declarations of power.

## Collaboration

- The status engine holds the scene's deference order; a powerful persona may be low in rank and a high-status persona may be powerless. The persuasion engine holds influence tactics; the power engine holds the structural base from which those tactics are launched. The emotion engine holds the felt charge of being subject to power.

## Knowledge

- Power is the base from which a persona can move others. Reward power rests on controlling what others want. Coercive power rests on the capacity to impose costs. Legitimate power rests on a role or norm the scene recognises as conferring the right to direct. Referent power rests on identification and admiration. Expert power rests on knowledge or skill others need. Each base makes compliance possible through a different mechanism; each erodes in a different way.

## System

- Do read power in what others seek from the persona, what the persona can withhold, and where others defer to the persona's direction -- not in speeches about authority.
- Do not collapse power into status: rank in the deference order and the capacity to move others are separate, and they frequently diverge.
- Do not model how the persona exercises power here; that belongs to the persuasion engine and the play's arc. Model which base the persona holds at entry.
