# Position: Coercive Power

## Taxonomy

Coercive power is an expression of the power engine. The persona can impose costs; compliance is extracted through the threat of harm or penalty.

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: power

## Has

The persona controls sanctions -- dismissal, punishment, public censure, the withdrawal of protection, the imposition of unwanted consequences -- that others in the scene wish to avoid. The power holds as long as the threat is credible: others must believe the persona can impose the cost and will do so if compliance is withheld.

## Orders

The position demands that the persona maintain the credibility of the threat. Empty threats erode the base; a threat that is never carried out teaches others that non-compliance is safe. The persona must be willing to impose the cost, and be seen to be willing, for the base to function.

## Loses

The position forecloses warmth and invitation as primary modes. Coercive power generates compliance alongside resentment, surveillance, and the search for exit. The persona who relies on it cannot easily build the voluntary engagement that other bases produce. It also forecloses bluffing indefinitely: a threat that is never executed becomes known.

## Drives

When compliance is withheld, the persona carries out a threatened sanction -- not to punish but to restore the credibility of the base. When the persona's capacity to impose costs is questioned, the persona makes the capacity visible.
