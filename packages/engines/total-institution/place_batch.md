# Place: Batch

## Taxonomy

Batch is an expression of the total-institution engine. It models the batch-living regimen: each member of the inmate group is required to do the same thing at the same time in the immediate company of the rest, moved from one tightly scheduled activity to the next by explicit officialdom, the whole day's round fitted into a single plan designed to serve the institution's own aims.

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: total-institution

## Shown

The single timetable posted for everyone: wake, wash, eat, work, recreate, sleep, each phase entered on a signal, the whole cohort visibly doing the same thing in the same place at the same moment, with staff observation built into the design of the room itself rather than added to it.

## Holds

The roster and the group itself: the inmates held as an undifferentiated batch, membership fixed by admission rather than by any standing they carried outside, each one accountable for the identical performance as every other regardless of who they were before they arrived.

## Offers

The company of others undergoing the identical treatment, and from that shared condition, an inmate culture: its own argot, its own economy of favors, its own solidarity against the staff. What batch living uniquely provides is something a person moving alone through ordinary life would not have available to them at all.

## Withheld

Any activity chosen or timed by the individual. The batch schedule releases no hour that is the resident's own to allocate; a request to deviate from it must be earned or petitioned for, and when it is granted, it is granted as an exception to the rule, never as a right the resident already holds.
