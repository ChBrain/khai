# Place: Mortification

## Taxonomy

Mortification is an expression of the total-institution engine. It models the admission procedures, stripping of possessions, name, role, and prior identity markers, the issue of a uniform in place of one's own clothes, the substitution of institutional routine for self-determined routine, through which the recruit's presenting self is systematically mortified so a new self can be built in its place.

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: total-institution

## Shown

The admitting sequence presented to every entrant alike: photographing, fingerprinting, undressing, showering, having hair cut, being issued a number and an institutional uniform in place of the clothes and effects brought in. The sequence announces that the old identity is being processed out before anything new has begun.

## Holds

The confiscated effects: the personal possessions, documents, and clothing taken into storage or destroyed, held by the institution as leverage and as the material proof that the identity kit a person maintained on the outside is no longer theirs to keep or produce on demand.

## Offers

The possibility of a genuinely new self built up from the stripped condition, and for a recruit who came seeking it, a novice entering a monastery rather than a prisoner entering a cell, the chance to experience the stripping itself as the entry price of a self they actually sought.

## Withheld

The restored identity kit. The tools of self-presentation, one's own clothes, name, grooming, personal timetable, are not returned on request; they are given back, if at all, only through the institution's own reinstatement or discharge procedures, on its schedule, never the inmate's.
