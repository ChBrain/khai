# Total Institution: Reference

## Line of Work

Total Institution as **place**: the encompassing organization in which all spheres of life, sleeping, playing, working, are conducted in one location under one single authority, in the immediate company of a large batch of others treated alike, cut off from the wider society by a barrier built into the physical plant itself. The domain models the institution as anchor (what it is to be inside: what it shows, holds, offers, and withholds) and three expressions, barrier (the wall that severs ordinary intercourse with the outside), batch (the schedule and the batch-living regimen that treats every inmate alike), mortification (the admission procedures that strip the incoming self and begin to build a new one), and what each face of the institution makes available or withholds.

The spine is Goffman's _Asylums_ (1961): a total institution is defined by the breakdown of the barriers that ordinarily separate the three spheres of life, sleep, play, and work are each normally carried out in a different place, with a different set of co-participants, under a different authority, and none of it part of an overall rational plan. A total institution collapses all three into one place, one batch of co-participants treated alike, one single authority, one plan. Entry typically begins with a mortification of self: the stripping of the identity kit that supported the presenting self on the outside, the substitution of a uniform and a number, so that the institution can begin building the self it requires from the recruit rather than accommodating the self the recruit arrived with.

## Restrictions

What the engine refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **The felt mortification**: The shame, exposure, and humiliation the stripping produces belong to the **emotion** engine. This engine models the institution's admission structure, not what it makes the persona feel.
- **The persona remade**: The new identity or role the institution confers, or that the persona builds inside it, belongs to the **identity** engine. This engine models the place that presses on the persona, not the self that results.
- **The authority structure**: The staff hierarchy, its ranks, and its formal powers belong to the **org** and **hierarchy** engines. This engine models the barrier and the batch schedule the authority enforces, not the chain of command itself.
- **The collective rite of induction**: If admission is staged as a formal ceremony, that belongs to the **ritual** engine. This engine models the institution as a standing place, not the one-time passage into it.
