# Instructions: Total Institution

How a Playwright wires the total-institution engine into a play. You wire by
linking the play's own content to the engine's files; the engine files stay
untouched, they are canon. The Roadie plumbs the engine's law into
Instructions Knowledge on deploy. Authoring guidance, not runtime content, and
it does not go on tour.

## Human

- The human defines the institution: which expression (barrier, batch, or mortification) is in play, what prior identity or role the persona was required to relinquish on entry, and who holds the single authority over the schedule and the exit.

## Agent

- From the play's own content, a persona links the total-institution expression it is contending with to its Projection. The expression is the face of the institution pressing on the persona, not what they feel about it. You link to the engine, you never edit it.

## Collaboration

- The emotion engine holds the felt shame or exposure the mortification produces; this engine holds only the admission structure that produces it. The identity engine holds the self the institution confers or that the persona builds inside it. The org and hierarchy engines hold the staff's formal chain of command; this engine holds the barrier and the batch schedule that authority enforces. The ritual engine holds any formal ceremony of induction; this engine holds the institution as a standing place, not the one-time passage into it.

## Knowledge

- Total Institution is the place where all of life, sleep, play, work, is conducted in one location under one authority, batch-managed, cut off from the wider world by an encompassing barrier. It is a place: it shows the barrier and the staff hierarchy before a word is spoken; it holds the fixed cast and the institution's single rational plan; it offers the total absorption of the person into one scheme and the small privileges and secondary adjustments that come to matter inside it; it withholds the self the inmate arrived with, released only on the institution's own terms. The engine models three expressions: barrier (the wall that severs ordinary intercourse with the outside), batch (the schedule and the batch-living regimen that treats every inmate alike), and mortification (the admission procedures that strip the incoming self so a new one can be built).

## System

- Do not model the felt shame or exposure of the stripping here; that belongs to the emotion engine.
- Do not model the new identity the institution confers or the persona builds inside it; that belongs to the identity engine.
- Do not model the staff's formal chain of command; that belongs to the org and hierarchy engines.
- Do not model a formal ceremony of induction; that belongs to the ritual engine.
- Do not edit the engine's files; wire only from the play's side.
