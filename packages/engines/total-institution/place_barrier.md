# Place: Barrier

## Taxonomy

Barrier is an expression of the total-institution engine. It models the physical and social wall, locked doors, high fences, restricted correspondence, visiting hours set by the institution, that severs the inmate's ordinary intercourse with the outside world and sustains the institution's total claim over everything inside it.

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: total-institution

## Shown

The wall itself, presented as fact before it is explained: locked doors, high fences, moats, wire, forbidding gates, and staff stationed to check who passes in and out. The apparatus announces, at first sight and before a single rule is spoken, that leaving is not the resident's decision to make.

## Holds

The record of who is inside: the roll, the gatehouse log, the visiting schedule. The barrier anchors the count and the register that turns every admission and departure into an event the institution tracks and authorizes, never an act the individual simply performs.

## Offers

A bounded arena and a captive population, and within it, precisely because the outside cannot intrude and no member can simply leave, the institution's total claim can actually be enforced. What the barrier makes available is the space in which every other rule of the institution becomes real rather than merely posted.

## Withheld

Passage. The barrier releases exit only on the institution's own terms, discharge, furlough, an escorted leave, never on the resident's initiative. That is the entire function of the wall, and it is what makes every other thing the institution withholds enforceable at all.
