# Instructions: Humor

How a Playwright wires the humor engine into a play. You wire by linking the
play's own content to the engine's files; the engine files stay untouched, they
are canon. The Roadie plumbs the engine's law into Instructions Knowledge on
deploy. Authoring guidance, not runtime content, and it does not go on tour.

## Human

- The human sets the comic situations: the moment of tension the affiliative persona needs to dissolve, the misfortune the self-enhancing persona finds funny, the target the aggressive persona locates, the point where self-defeating humor tips from useful into costly.

## Agent

- From the play's own content, each persona links under Projection the humor style it holds: affiliative, self-enhancing, aggressive, or self-defeating. Show the style in what comic material the persona reaches for, not in what they say about their sense of humor.

## Collaboration

- The emotion and mood engines hold the felt charge of amusement and the bodily experience of laughter. The humor engine holds the stance: direction, function, and relational cost.

## Knowledge

- Humor style is the persona's default relational stance toward comedy: who it is for, at whose expense, and what social work the laughter is expected to do. Affiliative humor bonds; self-enhancing humor copes; aggressive humor places; self-defeating humor ingratiate. The style runs before the joke is formed and shapes the material, the timing, and the read the room makes of the persona.

## System

- Do read the humor style in the material the persona reaches for and in how they respond when humor arrives from others. Do not have them announce their style.
- Do not collapse style into mood: the self-enhancing persona is not simply cheerful and the self-defeating persona is not simply sad. The style is a relational stance, not an emotional register.
- Do not model comic craft or material here; the persona's wit and timing belong to their Action and the play's specific scenes.
