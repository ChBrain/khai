# Instructions: Meaning

How a Playwright wires the meaning engine into a play. You wire by linking the
play's own content to the engine's files; the engine files stay untouched, they
are canon. The Roadie plumbs the engine's law into Instructions Knowledge on
deploy. Authoring guidance, not runtime content, and it does not go on tour.

## Human

- The human sets what presses on the persona's stance: the loss that threatens a present centre, the failed candidate for a searching persona, the disruption that re-opens the question for a persona adrift, the threat that tests a renewed centre.

## Agent

- From the play's own content, each persona links under Projection the meaning expression it holds: present, searching, adrift, or renewed. Show the stance in how the persona orients to what the scene offers, not in explicit statements about purpose.

## Collaboration

- The emotion and mood engines hold the felt charge of meaninglessness or purpose. The play's arc holds the narrative of how the sense of meaning shifts across the run. The meaning engine holds the structural stance at entry.

## Knowledge

- Meaning in life is the stance a persona holds toward the question of whether their life matters. The stance has two underlying dimensions: whether the sense of mattering is present, and whether it is actively sought. Present means the sense is there and not questioned. Searching means it is sought because it is not yet found. Adrift means it is absent and not sought; the question is suppressed or bypassed. Renewed means it was lost and rebuilt; the centre holds but is known to be held, not merely assumed.

## System

- Do read the stance in how the persona orients, what they attend to and what they dismiss, not in speeches about purpose or meaning.
- Do not collapse the stance into mood: adrift is not sad, present is not happy. The stance is structural, not emotional.
- Do not model the specific content of what the persona finds meaningful here; that belongs to their own Projection and the play's context.
