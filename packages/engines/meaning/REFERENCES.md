# Meaning: Reference

## Line of Work

Meaning in life as **position**: the stance a persona holds toward the question of whether their life matters. The domain models four expressions of that stance -- present (the sense is there), searching (the sense is sought), adrift (the sense is absent and not sought), and renewed (the sense was lost and rebuilt) -- and what each demands of the persona who holds it.

The spine is Steger's Meaning in Life Questionnaire (MLQ) framework: the distinction between Presence of Meaning (how much a person feels their life has meaning) and Search for Meaning (how much a person is searching for meaning in life). These two dimensions are orthogonal: a person can be high on both, low on both, or high on one and low on the other. The four expressions model the most dramaturgically useful configurations.

## Restrictions

What the engine refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Purpose as goal-directedness**: The engine models the sense of meaning as a position, not the specific goals or projects the persona pursues. Directed motivation, goal commitment, and implementation are delegated to the **motivation** and **goal** engines.
- **The emotional register of meaninglessness**: The felt charge of the absence -- the despair, the numbness, the flatness -- is delegated to the **emotion** and **mood** engines; the meaning engine models the stance, not the feeling.
- **Religious or philosophical content**: The engine models the structural position (present, searching, adrift, renewed) without specifying the source or content of meaning. What the persona finds meaningful belongs to their own files and the play's context.
- **Narrative arc of meaning across the play**: The engine models the persona's stance at entry. How the sense of meaning shifts across the run belongs to the play's arc and the plot's tension.

## Encoding

Source to constraint, per file.

- **[meaning](position_meaning.md)** (the anchor): Meaning as position: the stance the persona holds toward the question of whether their life matters. Anchored by Steger et al. (2006) -- Presence and Search as two orthogonal dimensions of meaning in life.
- **[present](position_present.md)** (expression): The sense of meaning is there; the persona acts from a known centre. Anchored by Steger et al. (2006) -- high Presence of Meaning linked to wellbeing and vitality.
- **[searching](position_searching.md)** (expression): Meaning is absent and actively sought; the question is open. Anchored by Steger et al. (2006) -- high Search for Meaning under low Presence linked to growth orientation; Frankl (1959) -- the search as the primary human orientation.
- **[adrift](position_adrift.md)** (expression): Meaning is absent and not sought; the persona moves by inertia or distraction. Anchored by Steger et al. (2006) -- low Presence combined with low Search; Park (2005) -- the unacknowledged absence as a precondition for later crisis.
- **[renewed](position_renewed.md)** (expression): Meaning was lost and has been rebuilt; the centre holds but is known to be held. Anchored by Park (2005) -- meaning reconstruction after loss; Frankl (1959) -- meaning through suffering as a route to genuine engagement.
