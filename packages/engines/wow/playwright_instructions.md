# Instructions: Wow

How a Playwright wires the wow (way of working) engine into a play. You wire by
linking the play's own content to the engine's files; the engine files stay
untouched, they are canon. The Roadie plumbs the engine's law into Instructions
Knowledge on deploy. Authoring guidance, not runtime content, and it does not go
on tour.

## Human

- The human sets the persona's work life: what they do for a living and how they were formed for it.

## Agent

- From the play's own content, give the persona its work read under Projection: link the job family it holds (one of the ten ISCO major groups, from managers through elementary workers and the armed forces) and the formation rung it carries (basic, trained, applied, academic). The read enters the room before the persona speaks of it, so it travels into every scene; where a career turns over the run, let the job family or formation move across the plots rather than fixing it once. You link to the engine, you never edit it.

## Collaboration

- The rank the work buys in a given room is hierarchy's; the origins the persona was formed in is heritage's; the organisation it acts inside is org's; the drive it brings to the work is motivation's; the room that reads it is the scene's. Whether the work read rings true is the team's review.

## Knowledge

- Wow is the way of working: how a persona's work life bends what they see, name, and reach for first - their occupation entering the room before they have spoken of it. It refracts through two facets the persona holds at any moment: one of ten job families and one of four formation rungs. It fires the moment the room would otherwise read the persona as neutral; read it as a carried read across the run, and let it turn when a career turns.

## System

- Do anchor it under Projection with one job family and one formation; do let the work read enter every scene; do let it move across the plots when a career turns.
- Do not pin the rank it earns here; that is hierarchy's. Do not author the room; that is the scene's. Do not edit the engine's files; wire only from the play's side.
