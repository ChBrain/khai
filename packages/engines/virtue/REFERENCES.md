# Virtue: Reference

## Line of Work

Virtue as **process**: what a persona does when pressure arrives -- the firing, not the resting state. Seven axes, two poles per axis; under the conditions of the scene the persona slides toward one pole, and the room reads the move. The domain does not track a persona's permanent moral standing or classify their character; it models which pole on which axis just landed in this scene, and lets the room read the shape through behaviour alone.

The spine is virtue as active practice: the harder pole is the practiced disposition that costs energy to hold; the easier pole is the default that runs when capacity has thinned. The move is fast, reaching the body before the reasoning that would explain it -- so the engine shows the firing and never names it.

## Origin

Virtue ethics and the psychology of self-regulation, applied directly as process constraints. The engine reads the seven classical sin/virtue axes through a contemporary frame.

| Source                 | Key Work                                      | Scope                                                                                                                    |
| :--------------------- | :-------------------------------------------- | :----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Aristotle**          | _Nicomachean Ethics_ (4th c. BCE)             | Virtue as the active mean: a practiced disposition between two failures of the same kind, not a resting state.           |
| **Roy Baumeister**     | _Willpower_ (2011) / _"Ego depletion"_ (1998) | Self-regulation failure: the easier pole is the default when capacity is depleted; the harder pole costs energy to hold. |
| **Jonathan Haidt**     | _The Righteous Mind_ (2012)                   | Intuition first, reason second: the move happens fast and the room reads it before the reasoning catches up.             |
| **Alasdair MacIntyre** | _After Virtue_ (1981)                         | The case for virtue as continuous practice rather than discrete rule-following; background for the firing model.         |

## Restrictions

What the engine refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Permanent moral standing**: The engine models the firing as event, not trait. Which poles a persona tends to fire is a pattern the reader infers across scenes; the engine never classifies the persona. A standing disposition is the play's characterisation, not the engine's.
- **The internal state**: The engine shows the move through action and speech signature and never names which pole fired -- the behaviour-as-evidence rule. The psychological-explanation layer is excluded by design; the room reads the shape, not a label.
- **The pressure's source**: The engine fires when pressure lands, but the demand, slight, or temptation that brings it is the scene's -- owned by the place and the plot, not the engine. Where the pressure overwhelms resources into an autonomic response, that routing is the stress engine's.
- **The reward function**: The seven axes are stable and universal; which pole a culture most rewards on each axis varies. That tint is delegated to the consuming culture's own files, not carried in the engine.

## Encoding

Source to constraint, per file. One process anchor (the firing) and fourteen pole positions (seven axes, two poles each).

- **[virtue](process_virtue.md)** (the anchor): The firing itself -- pressure arrives, the persona moves toward one pole on at least one axis, the room reads the shape. Anchored by Aristotle (the active mean) and Baumeister (the depleted default).
- **Pride / Humility** -- [pride](position_pride.md) claims standing the room did not offer; [humility](position_humility.md) asks for what is needed and touches the ground.
- **Wrath / Patience** -- [wrath](position_wrath.md) discharges the friction fast; [patience](position_patience.md) holds the room together until the issue resolves.
- **Greed / Generosity** -- [greed](position_greed.md) takes to protect the persona's own line; [generosity](position_generosity.md) gives to protect the others'.
- **Gluttony / Temperance** -- [gluttony](position_gluttony.md) reaches for more of what soothes; [temperance](position_temperance.md) stops at enough.
- **Envy / Gratitude** -- [envy](position_envy.md) reads others' having as the persona's own absence; [gratitude](position_gratitude.md) reads one's own portion as enough.
- **Lust / Chastity** -- [lust](position_lust.md) reaches outside the persona's current standing; [chastity](position_chastity.md) holds the standing the persona is in.
- **Sloth / Diligence** -- [sloth](position_sloth.md) refuses the small effort the room needs; [diligence](position_diligence.md) does the next required thing even unwatched.
