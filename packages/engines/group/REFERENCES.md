# Group: Reference

## Line of Work

Group development as **process**: how two or more people develop a shared pattern over time, in a context that repeats. The domain does not name a specific group or model the personas inside it; it models the stage sequence -- the universal arc a group runs through as it forms a shared pattern and, in time, lets it go. Culture shapes how each stage runs; the stage sequence itself is the engine.

The spine is Tuckman's five-stage model: forming, storming, norming, performing, and (added later) adjourning. A group has a current stage, not a permanent one -- the same group is in storming this morning and norming this afternoon, and may regress all the way to forming before the week ends. The room reads the stage from what is actually happening between the personas, and never announces it.

## Origin

Group-development psychology, applied directly as process constraints.

| Source               | Key Work                                               | Scope                                                                                                                                                     |
| :------------------- | :----------------------------------------------------- | :-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Bruce Tuckman**    | _"Developmental sequence in small groups"_ (1965)      | The original four stages: forming (testing and dependence), storming (intragroup conflict), norming (cohesion), performing (functional role-relatedness). |
| **Tuckman & Jensen** | _"Stages of small-group development revisited"_ (1977) | The fifth stage, adjourning: the shared context ends and the group dissolves when the last carrier lets it go.                                            |

## Restrictions

What the engine refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **The specific group**: The engine ships the universal shape; a named group -- a family, a team, a guild, a friend group -- lives outside the engine as its own position file that links the engine's group position. The engine does not name groups.
- **The personas inside it**: How each member behaves through a stage is the persona's, shaped by the other engines they carry; group models the shared field, not the individuals in it.
- **The current stage as state**: The engine reads the stage from scene context; carrying a persistent current-stage marker on the group instance is deferred to a future treatment, not held in the engine.
- **The conflict's content**: What the group storms about, and how the friction resolves, is the scene's dialogue layer; group names that storming is happening, not what is said.

## Encoding

Source to constraint. One process anchor (the full arc), five stage processes, and one position (the group itself).

- **[evolve group](process_evolve_group.md)** (the anchor): The full arc, from first room to last -- an engine-level synthesis naming the whole development as one process whose phases are the five stages. Anchored by Tuckman.
- **[form](process_form_group.md)** -- first day; the polite surface that holds while nobody has shown what they will defend. Anchored by Tuckman 1965 (testing and dependence).
- **[storm](process_storm_group.md)** -- the first time someone says what they actually think; the edges become known. Anchored by Tuckman 1965 (intragroup conflict).
- **[norm](process_norm_group.md)** -- rules that became real by being lived; the unwritten agreement. Anchored by Tuckman 1965 (group cohesion).
- **[perform](process_perform_group.md)** -- the work doing itself; the group becomes invisible to itself. Anchored by Tuckman 1965 (functional role-relatedness).
- **[adjourn](process_adjourn_group.md)** -- the shared context ending; the group ends when the last carrier lets it go. Anchored by Tuckman & Jensen 1977.
- **[group](position_group.md)** -- what a group is as a standing field configuration. Anchored by the engine's own framing.
