# Instructions: Group

How a Playwright wires the group engine into a play. You wire by linking the
play's own content to the engine's files; the engine files stay untouched, they
are canon. The Roadie plumbs the engine's law into Instructions Knowledge on
deploy. Authoring guidance, not runtime content, and it does not go on tour.

## Human

- The human sets the people who keep meeting and the repeated context that makes them a group.

## Agent

- From the play's own content, cast the group as a group and link the stage it is running now (forming, storming, norming, performing, or adjourning), and let that stage advance across the plots. The arc belongs to the group, not to any one persona in it. You link to the engine, you never edit it.

## Collaboration

- A single persona's feeling or bet inside the group is the persona engines' (emotion, trust); the standing and roles within it are hierarchy's and org's; what the group is for, its task, is the scene's. Whether the arc rings true is the team's review.

## Knowledge

- Group development is how two or more people grow a shared pattern over time: forming, storming, norming, performing, and the close, adjourning. The group is an entity in its own right, and its stage is where the pattern stands now, the same group storming one morning and norming by the afternoon. It is collective and temporal: read it on the group across the run, not as a trait of the people in it.

## System

- Do read it at the group level and let the stage advance across the plots; do let the same group sit at different stages over the run.
- Do not pin it to a single persona; the arc is the group's. Do not author the task here; that is the scene's. Do not edit the engine's files; wire only from the play's side.
