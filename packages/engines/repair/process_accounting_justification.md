# Process: Accounting, Justification

## Taxonomy

[Accounting](process_accounting.md), the form this account arrives in.

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: repair

## Initiated by

The account accepts the authorship and disputes the injury: "I did it, and it was right, or it cost nothing."
Scott and Lyman's justifications supply the moves - denial of injury (no real harm was done), denial of victim (the target had it coming, or was not really a victim), and appeal to loyalties (a higher claim licensed the act) - each leaving the "I did it" intact while contesting what the act cost, or who it was owed to.

## Direction

Inward, at the harmed party's own reading of what happened.
Where excuse moves the causal line off the responsible party, justification leaves that line exactly where the harmed party put it and argues the endpoint instead: not who did this, but whether this was a wrong at all, or a wrong done to this particular party.

## Lever

The responsible party's own standard, turned to certify the act rather than answer for it.
Justification borrows the vocabulary of the very standard it crossed - proportion, desert, loyalty - so the account reads as principled disagreement rather than dodge.
The lever needs the harmed party's own account to be the thing in question; it cannot be run against a fact the harmed party holds beyond dispute.

## Echo

Justification is the form most likely to reopen the rupture rather than close it, because it disputes the harmed party's own reading of the injury - the one thing an account cannot contest and still be received as accounting.
Where the harmed party's version holds, the accounting phase does not advance; it returns to [rupture](process_rupture.md) carrying a second grievance layered on the first, the act, and the dispute over whether it counted.
