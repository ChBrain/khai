# Process: Restitution

## Taxonomy

[Repair](process_repair.md), the relational process this phase is one movement of.

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: repair

## Initiated by

The account has been given and received; something concrete is now done to address the harm.
Restitution is not payment; it is action that demonstrates the accounting was real: a change in behaviour, an amends, a making-good that is visible and material.
The responsible party does something; the doing matters alongside the saying.

## Direction

Toward a tangible demonstration that the accounting was not merely words.
Restitution closes the gap between what was said and what was done; it shows the harmed party that the responsible party's understanding of the harm has produced a change in the world, not only in the narrative.

## Lever

The action meets the adequacy question.
What counts as sufficient restitution is not set in advance; the harmed party holds the judgment of adequacy, and the responsible party must offer what they can while the harmed party determines whether it is enough.
Restitution that is too small is an insult; restitution that exceeds the harm in scale but not in kind misses the point.

## Echo

Restitution may take time; some harms cannot be addressed in a single act.
The phase is complete not when the responsible party has done everything they intended but when the harmed party has received enough to be willing to move into integration.
When the restitution is received as adequate -- not perfect, but sufficient -- the move into [integration](process_integration.md) becomes possible.
