# Instructions: Repair

How a Playwright wires the repair engine into a play. You wire by linking the
play's own content to the engine's files; the engine files stay untouched, they
are canon. The Roadie plumbs the engine's law into Instructions Knowledge on
deploy. Authoring guidance, not runtime content, and it does not go on tour.

## Human

- The human defines the rupture: what happened, who was responsible, what was harmed, and why both parties are still in the relationship. The human also sets how far along the repair is at entry and what the obstacles are.

## Agent

- From the play's own content, a persona links the repair phase the moment runs (rupture, accounting, restitution, or integration) under its Projection. Let the phase shift as accounting is given and received, as restitution is offered, or as the integration either holds or reopens. You link to the engine, you never edit it.

## Collaboration

- The betrayal engine holds the harmed party's internal reckoning with the violation before the decision to repair. The emotion and mood engines hold the felt charge of each phase: the shame of accounting, the uncertainty of restitution, the fragile relief of integration. The trust engine holds the baseline disposition toward trust that shapes how the repair is received and what it can restore.

## Knowledge

- Repair is what a relationship undergoes when a rupture is addressed rather than abandoned. It moves through rupture (the break is acknowledged; both parties know something is damaged), accounting (the responsible party names what happened and why; the account must hold their full agency without explaining the harm away), restitution (concrete action addresses the harm; adequacy is the harmed party's judgment), and integration (the repair is received; the relationship is remade to include what happened). The phases do not run once and stop; earlier phases may need to be revisited if the accounting proves incomplete or the restitution proves insufficient. The repaired relationship is not the prior relationship restored; it is new.

## System

- Do distinguish accounting (naming and accepting) from explanation (reducing the harm to something the harmed party should not hold against the responsible party). Accounting holds full agency; explanation dissolves it.
- Do not collapse repair into forgiveness; forgiveness is the harmed party's unilateral change of stance. Repair requires both parties and produces reconciliation, not merely forgiveness.
- Do not model the harmed party's internal reckoning here; that belongs to the betrayal engine. This engine models what the relationship undergoes, not what either party feels.
- Do not edit the engine's files; wire only from the play's side.
