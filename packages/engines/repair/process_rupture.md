# Process: Rupture

## Taxonomy

[Repair](process_repair.md), the relational process this phase is one movement of.

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: repair

## Initiated by

Both parties know that something has been damaged.
The break may have been named explicitly or felt without naming; what defines the phase is that the rupture is acknowledged between the parties, not only held by one.
The relationship is suspended between what it was and what it might become.

## Direction

Toward acknowledgement on both sides.
The harmed party holds the fact of harm; the responsible party holds the fact of having caused it.
The phase is not complete until both holdings are visible to each other -- the harm named, the responsibility located.

## Lever

The break meets the reluctance to hold it.
Acknowledgement costs both parties: the harmed party must hold the harm as something real that happened in the relationship rather than suppressing or dismissing it; the responsible party must hold their role rather than explaining it away.
The cost of acknowledgement is lower than the cost of repair failing because the rupture was never fully named.

## Echo

The rupture phase is not yet repair; it is the condition that makes repair possible.
Without full acknowledgement of the break -- on both sides -- the accounting phase cannot proceed honestly.
When both parties are holding the same fact, the move into [accounting](process_accounting.md) becomes possible.
