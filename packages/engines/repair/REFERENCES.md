# Repair: Reference

## Line of Work

Repair as **process**: what a relationship undergoes when rupture is addressed rather than abandoned. The domain models four phases -- rupture (the break is acknowledged; both parties know something is damaged), accounting (the responsible party names what happened and why), restitution (concrete action addresses the harm), integration (the repair is received; the relationship is remade on new terms) -- and what each demands of the parties moving through it.

The spine is the sociology of apology and the psychology of relational repair: repair is not restoration but reconstruction; the repaired relationship includes the rupture in its history rather than erasing it. Tavuchis supplies the irreducible structure of accounting (the apology that names, accepts, and does not merely explain away); Gottman supplies the relational dynamics (what repair attempts look like in practice and why they succeed or fail); Worthington supplies the distinction between forgiveness (a change in the harmed party's stance toward the responsible party) and reconciliation (the rebuilding of the relationship, which requires both parties and which this engine models).

## Restrictions

What the engine refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Betrayal reckoning (the harmed party's process)**: The engine models the repair process jointly, with emphasis on what the responsible party must do. The harmed party's internal reckoning with the violation -- discovery, disorientation, and the decision to repair rather than withdraw -- belongs to the **betrayal** engine.
- **Forgiveness as a unilateral act**: Forgiveness (the harmed party's change of stance toward the responsible party) does not require repair and is not modelled here. This engine models reconciliation -- the rebuilding of the relationship -- which requires both parties.
- **The emotional register of repair**: The felt charge of each phase -- the shame of accounting, the relief of integration, the uncertainty of restitution -- belongs to the **emotion** and **mood** engines.
- **How the repair process shifts across the play**: The engine models the persona's current phase at entry. Movement across phases belongs to the play's arc.
