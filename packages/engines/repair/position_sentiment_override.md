# Position: Sentiment Override

## Taxonomy

[Repair](process_repair.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: repair

## Has

The dyad's accumulated regard, sentiment override in Gottman's terms: the reservoir of goodwill built from the ordinary traffic of the relationship, checked at the moment any repair attempt lands.
It is not held by either party alone; it is a joint balance the relationship carries into every rupture, read fresh each time one partner reaches for the other.

## Orders

Read every repair attempt through the reservoir, not through the attempt's own skill.
Gottman's finding (repair attempts land roughly eighty percent of the time in happy couples) traced the success not to how well the attempt was executed but to how much regard the couple had banked beforehand; the position orders every attempt, clumsy or polished, to be graded against the balance available at the moment it is made.

## Loses

The freedom to read a partner's reach in isolation.
While the position holds, the dyad cannot evaluate a repair attempt purely on its own terms: a joke, a touch, an apology offered mid-conflict is never received as bare content; it is received against everything the reservoir already contains, for better and worse.

## Drives

A high override reads a clumsy repair generously: the joke that badly times an apology still lands, because the reservoir supplies the goodwill the attempt itself lacked.
A depleted override reads a skilled repair as manipulation: the same words, offered by a low-reserve dyad, are met with suspicion rather than relief.
The position drives the dyad toward one of two spirals: attempts that succeed and refill the reservoir, or attempts that fail and draw it further down; every scene this engine runs plays out against whichever spiral the dyad is already in.
