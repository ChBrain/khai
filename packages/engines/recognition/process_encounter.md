# Process: Encounter

## Taxonomy

[Recognition](process_recognition.md), the phase in which the recognizing other responds to the claim. The encounter is the moment the bid meets its answer: confirmation, withholding, or distortion.

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: recognition

## Initiated by

The recognizing other's response to the persona's claim. The encounter phase begins when the social response occurs -- whether in a formal act (an award, a verdict, a declaration) or in an informal one (acknowledgment, dismissal, silence). What initiates the encounter is not the response itself but the moment the response becomes available to the persona.

## Direction

Toward the determination of the claim. The encounter phase resolves the openness of the claim phase: the recognizing other grants the acknowledgment (recognition), withholds it (non-recognition), or acknowledges something other than what was claimed (misrecognition). The direction is toward the outcome that will change the persona's standing.

## Lever

The quality of the recognition offered. Taylor distinguishes recognition that confirms authentic identity from recognition that imposes a distorted image; Honneth distinguishes recognition that fortifies self-relation from misrecognition that injures it. The lever is whether the encounter offers genuine acknowledgment or a distortion that the persona must accept, contest, or resist.

## Echo

The felt impact of the response. The encounter leaves a specific residue: the warmth of confirmation, the sting of dismissal, the disorientation of misrecognition. The echo is not the changed standing itself but the immediate experience of the response -- what it is like for the persona to receive what the encounter offers.
