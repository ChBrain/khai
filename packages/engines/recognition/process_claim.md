# Process: Claim

## Taxonomy

[Recognition](process_recognition.md), the phase in which the persona holds and puts forward a claim to acknowledgment. The claim is the bid: who the persona is, what they have done, or what they are owed that the social world has not yet confirmed.

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: recognition

## Initiated by

The persona's awareness of a gap between their self-understanding and their social standing. The claim phase begins when the persona holds something -- an identity, a contribution, a right -- that they know they have not yet received acknowledgment for. The bid may be explicit (a demand, a disclosure, a demonstration) or implicit (behavior that invites the recognizing other to respond).

## Direction

Toward the recognizing other. The claim phase moves the persona toward the social encounter that will determine whether the claim is confirmed. The direction is outward: the persona is making something about themselves available for acknowledgment.

## Lever

The form the bid takes. A claim may be made through self-disclosure, demonstration of contribution, assertion of right, or simple presence in the relevant sphere. The form of the bid influences what kind of recognition becomes possible; an implicit bid invites a different response than an explicit demand.

## Echo

The vulnerability of the bid. The claim phase leaves the persona exposed: having put forward a claim, the persona is now susceptible to the recognizing other's response. The echo of the claim phase is the stake -- the weight of what it would mean for the claim to be confirmed or denied.
