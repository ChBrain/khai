# Process: Recognition

## Taxonomy

A process (TO IDLE). How a persona moves through the cycle of seeking, encountering, and being changed by social acknowledgment. Root of the recognition process tree.

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: recognition

## Initiated by

The gap between how the persona understands themselves and how they are acknowledged by others. Recognition is initiated when a persona holds a claim -- to an identity, a contribution, a standing -- that the social world has not yet confirmed. The gap between claimed and received acknowledgment is what starts the process.

## Direction

From claim through encounter to changed standing. The recognition process moves the persona from holding an unconfirmed claim, through the moment of social response, to a revised standing in which the claim has been granted, withheld, or distorted. The direction is not toward approval; it is toward settled standing, which may be positive, negative, or contested.

## Lever

The response of the recognizing other. Honneth identifies three spheres of recognition -- love (who you are in intimate relation), rights (what you are owed as a legal subject), solidarity (what your particular contribution is worth to the group) -- and each requires a different recognizing other. The lever is the specific response in the relevant sphere: does the other confirm, withhold, or distort the claim?

## Echo

The persona's revised self-relation. Honneth's fundamental insight is that recognition is constitutive: it is not merely pleasant but necessary for the self-confidence, self-respect, and self-esteem that make autonomous action possible. The echo of recognition granted is the fortification of the relevant self-relation; the echo of recognition withheld or distorted is its injury.
