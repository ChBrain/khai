# Instructions: Recognition

How a Playwright wires the recognition engine into a play. You wire by linking the
play's own content to the engine's files; the engine files stay untouched, they
are canon. The Roadie plumbs the engine's law into Instructions Knowledge on
deploy. Authoring guidance, not runtime content, and it does not go on tour.

## Human

- The human names what the persona claims (an identity, a contribution, a right), who the recognizing other is and what sphere they represent, and what the outcome of recognition or misrecognition would mean for the persona's standing. The human also specifies whether the bid is explicit or implicit.

## Agent

- From the play's own content, a persona links the recognition phase they are in (claim, encounter, or standing) to their Projection. The phase is where the persona currently stands in the process, not what the persona feels about the acknowledgment they are seeking.

## Collaboration

- The status engine holds how recognized standing maps to status rank. The emotion and mood engines hold what the persona feels during the claim and encounter phases. The repair engine holds what happens after a misrecognition injures the persona. The power engine holds the structural conditions that determine whose claims are heard and whose are dismissed.

## Knowledge

- Recognition is the social acknowledgment of who a persona is or what they have done. It is constitutive: granted recognition fortifies the self-confidence, self-respect, or self-esteem required for autonomous action; withheld or distorted recognition injures these. The engine models three phases: claim (the persona holds an unconfirmed claim and puts it forward, explicitly or implicitly; the bid exposes the persona to the recognizing other's response), encounter (the recognizing other responds; the claim is confirmed, withheld, or distorted; misrecognition is not merely disappointing but a moral injury), and standing (the persona settles into the revised self-relation the outcome creates; the standing phase is marked by the persona's orientation within the result rather than toward it). Honneth identifies three spheres: love (self-confidence, granted in intimate relationships), rights (self-respect, granted in legal and civic acknowledgment), and solidarity (self-esteem, granted when a particular contribution is valued by the group).

## System

- Do not conflate recognition with praise. Recognition is a social determination that changes standing; praise is a positive response. Recognition can be withheld or distorted without any hostile intent; a well-meaning misrecognition is still a misrecognition.
- Do not model recognition as a one-time event. The standing phase opens the persona to future claims; recognition is a recurring process, not a final determination.
- Do not model the persona's felt response as the recognition itself. What the persona feels belongs to the emotion and mood engines; the recognition engine models the structural encounter and its outcome.
- Do not edit the engine's files; wire only from the play's side.
