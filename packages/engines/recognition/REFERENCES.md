# Recognition: Reference

## Line of Work

Recognition as **process**: the social acknowledgment of who a persona is or what they have done. The domain models the root (what recognition is; how it is initiated, what it moves toward, what levers it, and what it echoes) and three phases -- claim (the persona's bid for acknowledgment), encounter (the recognizing other's response), and standing (the revised self-relation the outcome creates) -- and what each phase requires and forecloses.

The spine is Honneth's theory of recognition as constitutive of self-relation: recognition is not merely pleasant but necessary for the self-confidence, self-respect, and self-esteem that make autonomous action possible. Honneth identifies three spheres -- love (intimate acknowledgment that grounds self-confidence), rights (legal acknowledgment that grounds self-respect), solidarity (social esteem that grounds self-esteem as a particular contributor) -- and argues that misrecognition in any sphere is a moral injury, not merely an inconvenience. Taylor supplies the cultural and political dimension: recognition of authentic identity is a fundamental human need, and the failure to recognize -- or the imposition of a diminishing image -- is a form of oppression. Hegel's master-slave dialectic is the deep structure: self-consciousness requires the recognition of another self-consciousness, and the struggle for recognition is the engine of social development.

## Restrictions

What the engine refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Praise and positive feedback**: Recognition is the acknowledgment of who the persona is or what they have done; it is not praise or reward. Praise is a response; recognition is a social determination that changes standing. Positive affective response belongs to the **emotion** engine.
- **Status rank**: How high the persona stands in a status hierarchy belongs to the **status** engine. Recognition may affect status, but the recognition engine models the process of acknowledgment, not the rank outcome.
- **The persona's felt experience of the encounter**: What the persona feels when the encounter occurs belongs to the **emotion** and **mood** engines. The recognition engine models the structure of the encounter; the experience is held elsewhere.
- **Repair of misrecognition**: What the persona and the recognizing other do to address a misrecognition or an injury belongs to the **repair** engine. The recognition engine models the process through standing; repair is a subsequent process.

## Encoding

Source to constraint, per file.

- **[recognition](process_recognition.md)** (the root): The social acknowledgment process: what initiates it, its direction, its lever, and its echo. Anchored by Honneth (1995) -- recognition as constitutive of self-relation; Taylor (1994) -- recognition as a fundamental human need.
- **[claim](process_claim.md)** (phase): The persona's bid for acknowledgment; the vulnerability of the exposed claim. Anchored by Honneth (1995) -- the struggle for recognition begins with the claim; Taylor (1994) -- authentic identity as the content of the claim.
- **[encounter](process_encounter.md)** (phase): The recognizing other's response; the determination of the claim as confirmed, withheld, or distorted. Anchored by Honneth (1995) -- recognition, non-recognition, and misrecognition as distinct outcomes; Taylor (1994) -- the distorting image as oppression.
- **[standing](process_standing.md)** (phase): The revised self-relation; the persona's orientation within the outcome. Anchored by Honneth (1995) -- self-confidence, self-respect, and self-esteem as the products of recognition; the injury of misrecognition as moral harm.
