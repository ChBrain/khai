# Process: Standing

## Taxonomy

[Recognition](process_recognition.md), the phase in which the persona's position in the social world has been revised by the encounter's outcome. Standing is the post-recognition condition: the claim has been confirmed, withheld, or distorted, and the persona now occupies a different position than before.

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: recognition

## Initiated by

The persona's settlement into the outcome of the encounter. The standing phase begins when the persona has absorbed the response and stopped treating the claim as open. Whether the outcome was confirmation, denial, or misrecognition, the standing phase is marked by the persona's orientation within the result rather than toward it.

## Direction

Into the revised self-relation. Honneth's analysis is that recognition is constitutive: granted recognition fortifies the self-confidence, self-respect, or self-esteem required for autonomous action; withheld or distorted recognition injures these. The standing phase moves the persona into the condition that the encounter has created.

## Lever

What the persona does with the outcome. A contested misrecognition opens resistance; a confirmed claim opens new action; a withheld recognition may intensify the claim or redirect it. The lever is the persona's active response to their revised standing -- not the passive receipt of the outcome but their first deliberate move within it.

## Echo

The revised horizon of self-understanding. The echo of the standing phase is what the persona now knows about where they are acknowledged, by whom, and in what sphere. This revised horizon shapes how the persona approaches future claims; it is the lasting mark of the recognition process on the persona's social self.
