# Process: Inscription

## Taxonomy

[Document](piece_document.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: document

## Initiated by

Someone with standing writes the claim down in a form built to last
and to be checked -- signing, sealing, filing, registering. The
threshold is the crossing from spoken and mutable into written and
set: an utterance becomes an artifact that can be reread, compared,
and audited rather than merely remembered and retold.

## Direction

Toward fixity. Inscription does not just record a claim, it commits
it, closing off future renegotiation of what was actually said or
agreed. Where speech can be walked back as "not what I meant," an
inscription is what was written, and stays what was written.

## Lever

The act of marking meeting the convention that assigns the mark its
force -- the form, the signature, the seal, the register it is filed
in. Remove the convention and the marks are just marks; remove the
marking and the convention has nothing yet to fix. The two together
are what turn ink into a claim that outlives the hand that wrote it.

## Echo

Once inscribed, the claim exists apart from the person who fixed it
and is available to be transported, copied, and checked against. The
next process inherits an object built to travel -- and everyone who
later relies on it is relying on the inscription, not on the
inscriber's continued presence or memory.
