# Process: Circulation

## Taxonomy

[Document](piece_document.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: document

## Initiated by

The document leaves the hands of its author -- carried, filed,
copied, cited, mailed onward. The threshold is the moment it starts
acting somewhere its author is not present to explain, defend, or
update it: the inscription is now working alone.

## Direction

Outward, into contexts the author never occupies, unchanged. Its
claim reads the same in the office where it was made as in a
courtroom a decade later or a filing cabinet in another country --
its authority rides entirely on that sameness holding.

## Lever

Portability meeting the presumption that the copy carries the same
force as the original. A document circulates because everyone
downstream treats a faithful reproduction as equivalent to the
inscribed original; remove that presumption and it is just paper in
transit; remove the portability and it is a plaque bolted to one
wall, never leaving the room it was fixed in.

## Echo

The more places it travels to and is compared against, the more
authority it accrues -- combined with other documents, cross-checked,
cited by later ones in turn. But the whole arrangement depends on the
one thing circulation cannot itself guarantee: that what arrived is
what was inscribed. Where that presumption breaks -- an altered copy,
a forged duplicate -- the document's authority breaks with it, and
the next reader inherits a claim that must be re-verified, not
trusted.
