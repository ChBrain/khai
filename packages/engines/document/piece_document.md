# Piece: Document

## Taxonomy

Inscription and the fixing of knowledge / immutable mobiles (no khai file
of its own yet).

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: document

## Place

Nowhere in particular, and that is what makes it a document rather than
a conversation. It is made once, at inscription, and after that it simply
presents itself -- the same object -- wherever it is opened: an office, a
courtroom, a mailbox, a screen a decade later. Its place is anywhere it is
produced or consulted, never a room it is re-made in.

## Load Bearing

Remove it and the claim has nothing to stand on beyond someone's word,
repeated. A document carries a decision across time and space so that no
living witness has to keep repeating it -- a debt owed, a birth
registered, a sale agreed, a title held. What it bears is trust
displaced from persons onto the object: believe the paper, not the
person, and the claim survives the person's absence, memory, or death.

## Apparent

What shows is the mark of fixing as much as the content: a seal, a
signature, a letterhead, a registration number, a form that announces
"this is settled" before it is read for what it says. A document argues
for its own authority through the conventions of its form, and a forged
form is often caught before a forged fact is.

## Yearbook

Every document carries the history of one act of fixing and every act
of consultation since. It is made once and then ages as an original
that copies are checked against; the longer it stands unchallenged and
uncontradicted, the more weight later readers assume it can bear, until
it is cited not because anyone re-verified it but because it has always
been cited.
