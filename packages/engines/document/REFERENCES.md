# Document: Reference

## Line of Work

Document as **piece**: an inscription that fixes a claim into durable
form and travels unchanged, carrying its fixed authority into contexts
its author never occupies. The domain does not model writing in
general, nor any marked surface; it models the artifact whose force is
that it says the same thing everywhere it goes and can be checked
against a fixed original -- the record, the contract, the certificate,
the file.

The spine is Latour's account of the **immutable mobile**: an
inscription engineered to move without deformation (mobile), to hold
its form across the move (immutable), and to be combined, superimposed,
and compared with other inscriptions at a distance -- so that power
accrues wherever many such traces can be gathered and read together.
Goody supplies the deeper mechanism underneath the mobility: literacy
fixes an utterance into an object that can be reread, laid side by
side, and cross-examined in ways speech cannot be, which is the
technical basis of the durable record in the first place.

## Origin

The sociology and anthropology of inscription, applied directly as one
piece with its bearer and its arc.

| Source            | Key Work                                                        | Scope                                                                                                                                                                          |
| :---------------- | :-------------------------------------------------------------- | :----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Bruno Latour**  | _"Visualisation and Cognition: Drawing Things Together"_ (1986) | The immutable mobile: an inscription that travels without distortion and can be combined with others at a distance, concentrating authority wherever such traces are gathered. |
| **Jack Goody**    | _The Domestication of the Savage Mind_ (1977)                   | Literacy's fixing effect: writing turns an utterance into an artifact that can be reread, compared, and audited -- the technology underneath the durable record.               |
| **Lisa Gitelman** | _Paper Knowledge: Toward a Media History of Documents_ (2014)   | The document as a recognized genre: form -- letterhead, seal, signature, the "documentary" mode of address -- as itself a claim to evidentiary authority.                      |

## Restrictions

What the engine refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **The claim's truth**: Whether what the document states is actually
  true or false is not this engine's question; belief, and the field
  the claim is about, carry that. Document models the fixing and
  carrying of a claim, not its veracity.
- **The felt weight**: The gravity of signing, the anxiety of custody,
  the relief of finally producing the paper -- those are emotion's and
  stress's. Document names what the object is and does; what it feels
  like to bear it is delegated.
- **The place it is kept**: A filing cabinet, an archive, a vault, a
  server -- where a document is physically or digitally lodged is a
  piece or place in the scene, not carried here. The engine ships the
  fixed inscription, not the container it sits in.
- **The institution behind it**: Who has the standing to issue a
  document that will be honoured -- a court, a registrar, a bank -- is
  org's and hierarchy's. Document assumes some inscription has already
  been made; it does not model the authority that licenses the making.
- **What it is worth**: A financial instrument's value, and the
  accounting done with it, are money's and capital's. Document ships
  the claim fixed and carried, not the value that claim happens to
  represent.

## Encoding

Source to constraint. A piece anchor (the fixed inscription) with one
position (who bears it) and two processes (how it is fixed and how it
travels).

- **[document](piece_document.md)** (the anchor): The inscription
  itself -- fixed once, load-bearing because later readers trust the
  object rather than re-verifying the person. Anchored by Latour (the
  immutable mobile) and Goody (writing as fixing).
- **[bearer](position_bearer.md)** (position): The stance of holding
  and producing the document, speaking with its authority rather than
  one's own. Anchored by Goody (the artifact that stands in for the
  speaker) and Latour (the one who can produce the trace).
- **[inscription](process_inscription.md)** (process): The act of
  fixing a claim into a form built to last and be checked, closing off
  its future renegotiation. Anchored by Goody (the fixing effect of
  literacy) and Latour (the making of the inscription).
- **[circulation](process_circulation.md)** (process): The document's
  unchanged movement into contexts its author never occupies, gaining
  authority as it is combined and compared. Anchored by Latour
  (mobile, immutable, combinable inscriptions) and, optionally,
  Gitelman (the document's own claim to evidentiary form).
