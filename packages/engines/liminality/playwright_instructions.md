# Instructions: Liminality

How a Playwright wires the liminality engine into a play. You wire by linking the
play's own content to the engine's files; the engine files stay untouched, they
are canon. The Roadie plumbs the engine's law into Instructions Knowledge on
deploy. Authoring guidance, not runtime content, and it does not go on tour.

## Human

- The human defines the liminal state: which expression (threshold, suspension, or emergence) the persona is in, what prior identity or status was left behind, how long they have been in the between, and what the stakes are of what comes next.

## Agent

- From the play's own content, a persona links the liminality expression to its Projection. The expression is the threshold space the persona occupies, not what they feel about it. You link to the engine, you never edit it.

## Collaboration

- The ritual engine holds the collective rite of passage that may have produced the liminality; this engine holds what the individual experiences in the between. The status and role engines hold what was left behind and what will be entered. The emotion and mood engines hold the felt charge of the liminal state. The identity engine holds the prior and emerging self-concept that frames the crossing.

## Knowledge

- Liminality is the individual threshold state between prior identity and what comes next. It is a place: the persona is neither what they were nor what they will be. It shows the absent markers of the prior status; it holds the suspended self; it offers what the between-state uniquely provides (openness, freedom from structure, the possibility of radical change); it withholds the ground that only comes from completing the crossing. The engine models three expressions: threshold (the crossing has just occurred; the prior status is freshly absent; the new is not yet visible), suspension (held deep in the between; time passes differently; the prior self is fading but the new self is not yet legible), and emergence (the new identity is beginning to take shape at the edges; the crossing is nearing completion).

## System

- Do not model the ritual that produced the liminality; that belongs to the ritual engine. This engine models the individual between-state, not the ceremony.
- Do not collapse the suspension into mere waiting. The suspension is where the most radical change can occur; it is productive, not merely uncomfortable.
- Do not model what the persona feels in the liminal state; that belongs to the emotion and mood engines.
- Do not edit the engine's files; wire only from the play's side.
