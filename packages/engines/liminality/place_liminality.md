# Place: Liminality

## Taxonomy

Liminality is a place engine (TO SHOW). It models the individual threshold state a persona occupies when prior identity or status has been genuinely relinquished and the new identity or status has not yet been conferred or claimed. The persona is between: neither what they were nor what they will be.

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: liminality

## Shown

The absent markers: what the persona no longer has the right to claim, the roles and identities that no longer apply, the ground that has been left behind. The liminal state presents as absence before it presents as possibility.

## Holds

What has been suspended but not released: the prior self's habits, reflexes, and claims, which no longer apply but have not yet been replaced. The persona carries these into the liminal space and they remain as the texture of the in-between -- the self that is still here, deactivated but not gone.

## Offers

What the between-state makes available that no fixed position can: the freedom from prior structure; openness to new possibility; the experience of undifferentiation; the chance to be remade. The liminal space offers what it does precisely because the ordinary markers do not apply here.

## Withheld

The ground to stand on: the new identity, the new status, the new markers that would end the in-between. These are not available from within the liminal state; they can only be received from outside -- from the community, the rite, the event that completes the crossing.
