# Plot: Rising Action

## Taxonomy

[The Drama](play_drama.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: dramatic-arc

## Cue

The exciting force has landed; the stasis of the exposition is broken. What
was a single event now has to become a climb -- Freytag's Steigerung, the
rise in intensity that carries the play from its opening toward its peak.

## Action

The complication builds. Each beat raises the stakes set by the exciting
force, laying obstacle on obstacle so the tension mounts rather than merely
continues. The rising action is not one complication but a sequence of
them, each one steeper than the last.

## Stage

The protagonist, now in motion, meeting the antagonist's resistance in a
widening field. The forces of the rising action -- the obstacles, the
counter-plays -- accumulate here, each adding to what the protagonist must
overcome before the peak.

## Tension

Mounting tension (Steigerung) with no relief: the defining feature of this
act is that it only climbs. Its close is the peak itself -- the moment the
climb can rise no further and must turn, which is the climax's cue.
