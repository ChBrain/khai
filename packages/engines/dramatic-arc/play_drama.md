# Play: The Drama

## Estate

Project khai holds this run: the dramatic-arc engine, staged as a play. It
is not one production but the abstracted structure any production borrows --
Gustav Freytag's pyramid, offered as the shape a five-act drama runs on. The
specific play's cast, setting, and story are delegated outward; what lives
here is the pyramid itself.

## Name

The Drama: the five-act arc named for Freytag's pyramid, _Die Technik des
Dramas_ (1863) -- exposition, rising action, climax, falling action,
catastrophe. Every plot inside runs beneath this name, and the name is the
shape, not the story.

## Arc

The pyramid: a rise from the exciting force (erregendes Moment) through
mounting tension to the climax (Höhepunkt), then a fall through the tragic
force to the catastrophe (Katastrophe). The rise and the fall are not
symmetric in content -- the rise complicates, the fall unwinds -- but they
are symmetric in shape, meeting at a single peak. Reorder the acts and the
pyramid collapses: the climax cannot precede the rise that earns it, and the
catastrophe cannot precede the fall that spends it.

## Company

The figures a drama fields, cast by function rather than by name:

- The protagonist, whose fortune the pyramid tracks -- rising to the climax,
  falling from it.
- The antagonist, or the counter-play: the resistance the rising action
  builds against.
- The exciting force (erregendes Moment): the piece that fires the rising
  action out of the exposition's stasis.
- The tragic force: the piece that turns the fall from the climax into
  motion, carrying the action toward the catastrophe.
- The forces of the rising and falling action: the mounting complications on
  the way up, the unwinding consequences on the way down.

## Triggers

The five acts chain in order; each act's close cues the next.

- [Exposition](plot_exposition.md) fires when the play opens; it runs until
  the exciting force sets the action going.
- [Rising action](plot_rising_action.md) fires on that exciting force; it
  runs until the mounting complication reaches its peak.
- [Climax](plot_climax.md) fires at that peak; it runs until the
  protagonist's fortune has turned.
- [Falling action](plot_falling_action.md) fires on that turn; it runs until
  the tragic force has driven the fall to its final suspense.
- [Catastrophe](plot_catastrophe.md) fires on that final suspense; it closes
  the run by resolving the protagonist's fortune, in tragedy or in comedy.

## Stakes

Whether the protagonist's fortune, having turned at the climax, ends in the
fall (tragedy) or the restoration (comedy). Every act must move the tension:
an act that leaves the pyramid exactly as it found it -- no rise, no turn, no
fall -- has not earned its place in the run.
