# Plot: Exposition

## Taxonomy

[The Drama](play_drama.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: dramatic-arc

## Cue

The play opens on stasis: no rise has begun yet. Freytag's exposition lays
the ground the rest of the pyramid stands on -- it has no tension of its own
to inherit, only the tension it is about to introduce.

## Action

The setting is placed, the characters are introduced, and the initial
situation is established well enough that a departure from it will register.
Nothing complicates yet; the exposition's work is to make the coming
complication legible when it arrives.

## Stage

The protagonist and the field they stand in before anything moves them off
it. The antagonist, or the shape of the resistance to come, may be named
here or held back for the rising action -- Freytag allows either, so long as
the ground is set.

## Tension

The exciting force (erregendes Moment): the single event that breaks the
stasis and fires the rising action. Exposition's tension is not yet
complication -- it is anticipation, the held breath before the exciting
force lands. Its close is that landing.
