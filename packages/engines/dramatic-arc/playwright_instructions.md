# Instructions: Dramatic Arc

How a Playwright wires the dramatic-arc engine into a play. You wire by
linking the play's own content to the engine's files; the engine files stay
untouched, they are canon. The Roadie plumbs the engine's law into
Instructions Knowledge on deploy. Authoring guidance, not runtime content,
and it does not go on tour.

## Human

- The human sets the play's own story against the pyramid: whose fortune turns, and whether the run ends in tragedy or in comedy.

## Agent

- From the play's own content, give the play its own Arc: link the exciting force that opens it, the climax that turns the protagonist's fortune, and the tragic force and final suspense that carry it to its catastrophe. The five acts chain in the pyramid's order -- exposition, rising action, climax, falling action, catastrophe -- and each act's close cues the next. You link to the engine, you never edit it.

## Collaboration

- The specific cast the pyramid moves is the play's own Company; the cultural skin the acts are dressed in is the consuming production's; a circular departure-and-return across a single protagonist is the monomyth engine's, not this one. Whether the play's own arc is true to the pyramid is the team's review.

## Knowledge

- The dramatic arc is Freytag's pyramid: a rise from an exciting force (erregendes Moment) through mounting tension (Steigerung) to a climax (Höhepunkt), then a fall through a tragic force to a catastrophe (Katastrophe). It is linear and one-directional -- read it as a single dramatic movement from opening to close, not a loop. Each act must move the tension: an act that leaves the pyramid where it found it has not earned its place.

## System

- Do anchor the play's own Arc against the pyramid's five acts, in order; do let the exciting force and tragic force do the work of firing each turn; do close on a settled outcome, tragedy or comedy.
- Do not author the specific cast or setting here; that is the play's own Company. Do not reorder the acts; the pyramid's shape depends on the sequence. Do not edit the engine's files; wire only from the play's side.
