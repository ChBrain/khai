# Plot: Catastrophe

## Taxonomy

[The Drama](play_drama.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: dramatic-arc

## Cue

The final suspense has fixed the outcome the falling action was carrying.
Freytag's Katastrophe closes the pyramid: the last act, where the fall
spends the last of its momentum and the protagonist's fortune settles.

## Action

The resolution lands. In tragedy, this is the protagonist's fall completed
-- the outcome the tragic force was driving toward. In comedy, it is the
restoration -- the disorder the rising action introduced is set right, and
the protagonist's fortune is repaired rather than spent. Either way, the
pyramid closes on a settled state, not an open one.

## Stage

The protagonist at the bottom of the arc the pyramid traced, meeting
whatever the fall was carrying them toward -- ruin or restoration -- with no
further act to carry the tension onward.

## Tension

Resolution, not suspense: the catastrophe's tension is that it must actually
resolve, not merely stop. Its close is the play's close -- the pyramid has
run its full rise and fall, and nothing in the arc remains unspent.
