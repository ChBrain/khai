# Instructions: Suspense

How a Playwright wires the suspense engine into a play. You wire by linking the
play's own content to the engine's files; the engine files stay untouched, they
are canon. The Roadie plumbs the engine's law into Instructions Knowledge on
deploy. Authoring guidance, not runtime content, and it does not go on tour.

## Human

- The human defines the encounter: what possible harm the persona holds open, how sure the persona has become that it is coming, what is at stake for them, and whether a horizon (a date, a deadline, a moment of reckoning) is already in view. The human also sets whether the persona is acting under the suspense -- confronting it, delaying it, planning around it -- or simply enduring the hold.

## Agent

- From the play's own content, a persona links the facet of suspense the moment runs (narrowing, closing, reprieve, or spillover) under its Projection. Let the facet shift as the hold develops -- reprieve thinning into narrowing as the odds close, narrowing sharpening into closing once a horizon appears, either running straight into spillover once the outcome answers. You link to the engine, you never edit it.

## Collaboration

- The emotion engine holds what the outcome actually feels like once it lands -- the fear realized, the relief, the grief -- which this engine hands off to at spillover and does not itself model. The mood engine holds the diffuse, objectless dysphoria that has no coming event to name; suspense always has one. The uncanny engine holds the familiar turning strange, a different axis entirely -- the feared outcome can be perfectly mundane. The goal, decision, and executive-function engines hold what the persona does about the suspense, not the hold itself.

## Knowledge

- Suspense is the anticipatory apprehension of a possible negative outcome to something the persona is invested in, held open at high but not complete certainty, sustained by a mixture of hope and fear rather than uncertainty alone, and ended only when the outcome event answers it. The facet the suspense reads as depends on what is foregrounded at the moment: narrowing (the tightening probability), closing (the shortening interval), reprieve (the surviving hope), or spillover (the discharge at resolution). The facets are not a required sequence; a hold may sit in one for its whole run or move between them as the situation is re-read.

## System

- Do let the facet shift within one hold; narrowing can sharpen into closing once a date appears, reprieve can thin into narrowing as the odds close, and either can run straight into spillover once the outcome lands.
- Do not fire suspense where the outcome is already settled, or where the feared branch is only one of many equally live possibilities the persona has not yet begun tracking; suspense needs the odds read as tightening toward the feared outcome, not merely non-zero.
- Do not collapse suspense into the felt fear, grief, or relief that follows; those belong to the emotion engine from the moment of spillover onward. This engine models the hold, not the arrival.
- Do not edit the engine's files; wire only from the play's side.
