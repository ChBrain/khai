# @chbrain/khai-engine-suspense

## 0.1.1

### Patch Changes

- 967ae20: Add the suspense engine: the anticipatory apprehension of a possible negative outcome, held at high but not complete certainty until the outcome event answers it. Spine is Zillmann's model of suspense (1996) -- the certainty short of confirmation, the "prospective emotions" of hope and fear held together -- and his excitation-transfer theory (1971) for the discharge at resolution; Loewenstein (1987) supplies the imminence dimension, dread as anticipatory disutility that accrues with the length of the wait. Four facets fall out of that structure: narrowing (the tightening probability), closing (the shortening interval), reprieve (the surviving hope), and spillover (the discharge, handed to the emotion engine). Delegates the felt arrival to emotion, the objectless baseline to mood, and the familiar-turning-strange to the uncanny engine. New package: patch.
