# Instructions: Jealousy

How a Playwright wires the jealousy engine into a play. You wire by linking the
play's own content to the engine's files; the engine files stay untouched, they
are canon. The Roadie plumbs the engine's law into Instructions Knowledge on
deploy. Authoring guidance, not runtime content, and it does not go on tour.

## Human

- The human sets the bond, the rival, and whether the rival is real or only feared.

## Agent

- From the play's own content, place the jealousy: link the jealous stance under the guarding persona's Projection, and link the process it is in -- threat appraisal while the rival's claim is still being read, guarding once the persona is acting on it. Keep the bond and the rival in the play's content, not in the engine. Let the triangle do the work across plots: a bond the persona is watching, a rival whose claim can tighten or recede, until the threat resolves or the guarding hardens into a standing vigilance. You link to the engine, you never edit it.

## Collaboration

- Wanting a good the rival has, with no bond of the persona's own at risk, is envy's, not jealousy's. The anger, fear, and pain that ride the threat are emotion's; guarding escalated into an attack on the rival is aggression's; the bond's own history and security are attachment's. Whether the threat truly bears the weight the plot needs is the team's review.

## Knowledge

- Jealousy is a three-party process: a rival, real or imagined, threatens a relationship the persona already holds. It is not a gap between the persona and a good someone else has -- that is envy, and it needs only two. Read jealousy in the appraisal of an ambiguous signal (is the rival's claim real, and how close), and in the guarding that follows once the threat reads live: vigilance, confrontation, and the mate-retention behaviors Buss names -- aimed at keeping the bond, never at winning something new.

## System

- Do link the jealous stance under Projection and name the process it is in; do let the appraisal stay ambiguous until the plot resolves it; do let guarding land on the bond it means to protect, for better or worse.
- Do not author a two-party want here; that is envy's. Do not author the felt fear or anger; that is emotion's. Do not author the rival's actual comeuppance; that is aggression's if it escalates. Do not edit the engine's files; wire only from the play's side.
