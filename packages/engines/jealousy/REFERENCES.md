# Jealousy: Reference

## Line of Work

Jealousy as **process**: a rival, real or imagined, threatens a valued
relationship the persona already holds, driving the persona to appraise the
threat and guard the bond against it. The domain does not model wanting a good
someone else has -- that is a two-party comparison, and it belongs to the
**envy** engine. Jealousy needs three: the persona, the bond, and the rival who
endangers it.

The spine is Parrott and Smith's experiential distinction between envy and
jealousy: envy arises from a lack, comparing the self to another who has a good
the self does not; jealousy arises from a threatened possession, and requires a
third party who might take a valued relationship away. Buss supplies the
function underneath the guarding -- mate-guarding as an evolved strategy for
deterring rivals and retaining a partner, read here as the drive that fires once
the threat is appraised.

## Restrictions

What the engine refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **The two-party comparison**: Wanting a good someone else has, with no bond
  of the persona's own at stake, is envy, not jealousy. That comparison and its
  branches (benign, malicious, resentment) belong to the **envy** engine.
- **The felt charge**: The anger, fear, and pain that ride the threat and the
  guarding belong to the **emotion** engine. Jealousy names the appraisal and
  the guarding; what it feels like to run them is delegated.
- **Aggression toward the rival**: Where guarding turns into an attack on the
  rival -- confrontation escalated past vigilance -- the aggression itself is
  the **aggression** engine's. Jealousy names the drive to guard; the act of
  striking out is delegated.
- **The bond itself**: The valued relationship jealousy threatens -- its
  history, its security, its style of attachment -- is the **attachment**
  engine's. Jealousy takes the bond as given and models the threat to it, not
  the bond's own structure.

## Encoding

Source to constraint, per file.

- **[jealousy](process_jealousy.md)** (the root): The three-party threat itself
  -- a rival inside a bond the persona already holds. Anchored by Parrott &
  Smith (1993) -- jealousy as threatened possession, distinct from envy's lack.
- **[threat appraisal](process_threat_appraisal.md)** (process): Reading how
  real and how close the rival's claim is, under ambiguous evidence. Anchored
  by Parrott & Smith (1993) -- the appraisal that distinguishes jealousy's
  fear of loss from envy's wanting.
- **[jealous](position_jealous.md)** (position): The standing of a persona who
  has read the threat and is now watching the triangle. Anchored by Buss
  (2000) -- the vigilant stance that precedes and sustains guarding behavior.
- **[guarding](process_guarding.md)** (process): The vigilance, confrontation,
  and mate-retention behavior the appraised threat drives. Anchored by Buss
  (2000) -- mate-guarding as the evolved function of jealousy in action.
