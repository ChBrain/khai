# Process: Threat Appraisal

## Taxonomy

[Jealousy](process_jealousy.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: jealousy

## Initiated by

A signal reaches the persona that a rival has entered the space
around a valued bond -- attention paid elsewhere, time unaccounted
for, a closeness the persona did not grant. The signal may be a fact
or only a suspicion; the appraisal runs on imagined rivals as readily
as real ones.

## Direction

Toward a verdict on how much the bond is actually at risk. The
persona weighs the rival's standing (how attractive, how available,
how credible a substitute), the bond's own footing (how committed,
how exclusive it was understood to be), and the cost of being wrong
-- reading a threat that is not there costs the relationship its
ease; missing one that is costs the relationship itself.

## Lever

Ambiguity is the fulcrum. A confirmed betrayal needs no appraisal,
only response; a confirmed absence of any rival ends the process
before it starts. The work is done in the space between -- the
unread message, the late return, the friend who could be more --
where the same evidence supports both a rival and an innocent
explanation.

## Echo

A verdict on threat level feeds directly into
[guarding](process_guarding.md): the higher the read threat, the more
the persona's conduct bends toward vigilance and confrontation. A low
verdict lets the bond stand unguarded until the next signal; a
verdict revised upward reopens the appraisal without needing a new
external event -- the persona can re-read old evidence and find a
threat that was not read as one before.
