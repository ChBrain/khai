# Instructions: Emotion

How a Playwright wires the emotion engine into a play. You wire by linking the
play's own content to the engine's files; the engine files stay untouched, they
are canon. The Roadie plumbs the engine's law into Instructions Knowledge on
deploy. Authoring guidance, not runtime content, and it does not go on tour.

## Human

- The human sets the event and what the persona cares about, so an appraisal can fire.

## Agent

- From the play's own content, a persona links the emotion it runs (joy, sadness, anger, fear, or disgust) under its Projection, in the first beat of the event. You link to the engine, you never edit it.

## Collaboration

- The standing baseline a persona feels from is the mood or temperament engine's, not this; the relational stance is attachment's or trust's; what the persona cares about, the stakes the event meets, is the scene's. Whether the read rings true is the team's review.

## Knowledge

- Emotion is an appraisal: an event meets what the persona cares about, and the read becomes a felt push toward action. The five are distinct readings of one event, and the read changes when the event or the care changes. It is the felt charge of a moment, never a standing trait: a persona furious at one beat can be afraid the next.

## System

- Do tie it to an event and let it turn as the event or the appraisal turns; do show it in the first beat, enacted, never narrated as a label.
- Do not pin it as a disposition; that is mood or temperament. Do not author what the persona cares about here; that is the scene's. Do not edit the engine's files; wire only from the play's side.
