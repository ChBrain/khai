# Emotion: Reference

## Line of Work

Emotion as **process**: how a persona appraises an event against what it cares about, and the appraisal becomes a felt push toward a particular action. The domain does not model mood, temperament, or the autonomic survival surge under threat (that is the stress engine's); it models the discrete, appraisal-driven feeling that reads what an event did to a stake and aims the persona at a response.

The spine is appraisal theory: an emotion is not the event and not the body alone, but the persona's reading of the event against what it wants and can do. Each discrete emotion is a different reading -- a core relational theme -- and each reading carries its own action tendency, the direction the body is already taking before the mind explains it.

## Origin

Appraisal theory and the psychology of discrete emotion, applied directly as process constraints.

| Source              | Key Work                                         | Scope                                                                                                         |
| :------------------ | :----------------------------------------------- | :------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **Richard Lazarus** | _Emotion and Adaptation_ (1991)                  | Appraisal and the core relational theme: each emotion is a distinct reading of an event against a stake.      |
| **Nico Frijda**     | _The Emotions_ (1986)                            | Action tendencies: an emotion is a state of readiness to act on a relation in a particular way.               |
| **Paul Ekman**      | _"An Argument for Basic Emotions"_ (1992)        | The discrete basic-emotion set; each emotion a distinct, recognisable program rather than a point on a scale. |
| **Robert Plutchik** | _Emotion: A Psychoevolutionary Synthesis_ (1980) | Emotions as adaptive action programs, and blends as combinations of the primaries.                            |

## Restrictions

What the engine refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Autonomic survival**: The engine models the felt, appraised emotion, not the involuntary nervous-system surge under acute threat. Fight, flight, freeze, and fawn mobilisation are **delegated to the stress engine**. A persona may run both at once -- the stress engine routes the survival response, this engine names what is felt.
- **Mood and temperament**: The engine models the discrete emotion an event generates, not the persisting background mood or the stable trait disposition; those are delegated to the persona's own Projection and to the play's arc design.
- **Regulation and display**: The engine models the emotion generated, not how the persona suppresses, amplifies, or masks it for the room. The gap between felt and shown is delegated to the persona (Action, Tell) and to the Cultures display rules.
- **Blends and gradients**: The engine models discrete emotions as separate, **structurally unlinked** files for stability. Mixed feeling (Plutchik's dyads) and intensity gradients emerge at the persona level -- through which expression is linked and how strongly -- not as files of their own.

## Encoding

Source to constraint, per file.

- **[emotion](process_emotion.md)** (the root): The appraisal where an event meets a stake and becomes a felt push toward action. Anchored by Richard Lazarus (appraisal, the core relational theme) and Nico Frijda (the action tendency).
- **[joy](process_joy.md)** (expression): An unmet want is met; the read is progress, and the tendency is to approach and stay in contact. Anchored by Lazarus (making reasonable progress toward a goal).
- **[sadness](process_sadness.md)** (expression): An irrevocable loss; the read is gone-for-good, and the tendency is to withdraw, conserve, and signal for care. Anchored by Lazarus (having experienced an irrevocable loss).
- **[anger](process_anger.md)** (expression): A demeaning offence against the persona or its own; the read is wrong-and-someone's-fault, and the tendency is to move against and remove the obstacle. Anchored by Lazarus (a demeaning offence against me and mine).
- **[fear](process_fear.md)** (expression): An uncertain existential threat; the read is open-danger, and the tendency is to move away, cover, and keep the exit open. Anchored by Lazarus (facing an uncertain, existential threat).
- **[disgust](process_disgust.md)** (expression): A contaminating object too close to the boundary of the self; the read is not-to-be-taken-in, and the tendency is to reject and expel. Anchored by Paul Ekman (disgust as a basic emotion of rejection).
