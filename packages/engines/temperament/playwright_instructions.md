# Instructions: Temperament

How a Playwright wires the temperament engine into a play. You wire by linking
the play's own content to the engine's files; the engine files stay untouched,
they are canon. The Roadie plumbs the engine's law into Instructions Knowledge on
deploy. Authoring guidance, not runtime content, and it does not go on tour.

## Human

- The human sets who the persona is at rest, before any pressure bends it.

## Agent

- From the play's own content, every persona carries its poles across the five axes under Projection. Let the baseline tilt every act, and show the persona return to it once the pressure passes. You link to the engine, you never edit it.

## Collaboration

- The current weather the persona is under is the mood engine's; the sharp feeling of a moment is emotion's; what the persona does with the tilt is the scene's and the other engines'. Whether the disposition rings true is the team's review.

## Knowledge

- Temperament is the stable disposition a persona arrives with and returns to: five axes, two adaptive poles each, the resting baseline the room reads when nothing is pushing. It tilts every act without deciding any one of them. It is the climate beneath mood's weather and emotion's lightning: it does not turn across the run, it is the thing departures are measured against and the thing the persona settles back into.

## System

- Do give every persona a baseline (the law admits no exception); do let it tilt every act and pull the persona back after a departure.
- Do not drift it across the run; it is the resting state, not the weather (that is mood). Do not author the act here; that is the scene's. Do not edit the engine's files; wire only from the play's side.
