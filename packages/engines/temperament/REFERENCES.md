# Temperament: Reference

## Line of Work

Temperament as **position**: the stable disposition a persona arrives with and returns to -- the resting baseline that tilts every act without deciding any one of them, and that the room reads when nothing in particular is happening. The domain does not model what a persona does in a given moment; it models where they start from, what costs them effort, and what they fall back to when the pressure lifts. Held, not run.

The engine is original khai framing built on the established trait literature, not lifted from the Cultures engine catalogue. It is the deliberate complement to the virtue engine: virtue _fires_ under pressure, temperament _sits_ underneath it. A steady persona can still fire wrath; a warm one can still fire greed -- the firing is the event, the temperament is the ground it returns to. Five axes, two adaptive poles each; each pole has its own gift and its own standing cost.

## Origin

Trait psychology, applied directly as position constraints. The five axes are the Five-Factor Model.

| Source                | Key Work                                                  | Scope                                                                                                                                 |
| :-------------------- | :-------------------------------------------------------- | :------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **Costa & McCrae**    | _Revised NEO Personality Inventory_ (1992)                | The Five-Factor Model: extraversion, neuroticism, openness, conscientiousness, agreeableness as the canonical trait axes.             |
| **Lewis Goldberg**    | _"The structure of phenotypic personality traits"_ (1993) | The lexical Big Five: the five factors recovered from the language people use to describe one another.                                |
| **Hans Eysenck**      | _The Biological Basis of Personality_ (1967)              | The biological roots of extraversion and neuroticism -- the arousal and reactivity baselines the engine treats as resting tendencies. |
| **John & Srivastava** | _"The Big Five trait taxonomy"_ (1999)                    | The integration of the lexical and questionnaire traditions into one Big Five taxonomy; the poles as bidirectional and both adaptive. |

## Restrictions

What the engine refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **The situational firing**: Temperament is the resting baseline; what a persona actually _does_ under pressure -- the move that fires on the moral axes -- is **delegated to the virtue engine**. Temperament sets the odds and the cost of each firing, never the firing itself.
- **The present feeling**: What a persona feels in the moment, as an appraisal of an event, is **delegated to the emotion engine**. A reactive baseline makes strong feeling likelier; the feeling itself is emotion's, not temperament's.
- **The drive to act**: Why a persona pursues a thing, and where the drive springs from, is **delegated to the motivation engine**. Temperament colours how the pursuit looks; it does not supply the want.
- **How the baseline was set**: The formation of disposition -- heritable temperament and the habitus absorbed in childhood -- is **delegated to the heritage engine** and the play's arc. Temperament reads the baseline as it sits now, not the history that laid it down.
- **The clinical extreme**: The engine models the normal range of disposition, not personality disorder or pathology. The poles are adaptive variants, not diagnoses.

## Encoding

Source to constraint. One position anchor (the baseline) and ten pole positions across the five Big Five axes -- both poles of each axis adaptive, each with its own cost.

- **[temperament](position_temperament.md)** (the anchor): The resting baseline a persona arrives with and returns to. Anchored by Costa & McCrae (the Five-Factor Model).
- **Extraversion** -- [extraverted](position_extraverted.md) (energy rises in company) / [reserved](position_reserved.md) (energy holds and spends down in it). Anchored by Eysenck and Costa & McCrae.
- **Neuroticism** -- [reactive](position_reactive.md) (a system quick to register threat) / [steady](position_steady.md) (slow to spike, quick to settle). Anchored by Eysenck and Costa & McCrae.
- **Openness** -- [open](position_open.md) (pull toward the new) / [grounded](position_grounded.md) (pull toward the proven). Anchored by Costa & McCrae and Goldberg.
- **Conscientiousness** -- [driven](position_driven.md) (pull to order and finish) / [easygoing](position_easygoing.md) (low background pressure, held lightly). Anchored by Costa & McCrae and Goldberg.
- **Agreeableness** -- [warm](position_warm.md) (pull toward the others' comfort) / [forthright](position_forthright.md) (pull to the true thing and the held line). Anchored by Costa & McCrae and Goldberg.
