# Process: Aggression

## Taxonomy

Mobilization of hostile force.

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: aggression

## Initiated by

A persona moves to harm another who is motivated to avoid that harm.
The trigger is not the harm itself but the readiness to inflict it: a provocation, a goal that another body stands in the way of, a felt threat, a standing intent.
What enters the process is force aimed at a target who does not consent to receive it.

## Direction

Toward the delivery of harm, or its credible threat.
The persona does not merely feel hostile; they organize the feeling into an act that lands on another -- a blow, a word, a withholding, a manoeuvre through the group.
The form the force takes is chosen, even when the choosing is fast and unfelt.

## Lever

Inputs meet a present state, and the state decides what the body does with them.
Provocation, frustration, pain, and intent raise arousal and prime hostile thought; appraisal then routes the charge -- toward a target or away, now or later, hot or cold.
The same provocation yields a different act in a different state; the lever is not the spark alone but the state that the spark meets.

## Echo

The force takes one of five forms: [hostile](process_hostile.md) (provoked, retaliatory, hot), [instrumental](process_instrumental.md) (harm as a means to an end), [displaced](process_displaced.md) (redirected onto a safer target), [relational](process_relational.md) (harm through the social bond), or [passive](process_passive.md) (harm through withholding).
The forms are not stages and not exclusive; a single act may begin hot and turn instrumental, or wear a relational face over an instrumental aim.
What ends the process is not the spending of the charge but the act's arrival on a target -- or the persona's choice to route the force somewhere it cannot land.
