# Place: Discharge

## Taxonomy

Discharge is an expression of the crowd engine. It isolates the moment Canetti names as the crowd's own goal: when the distinctions that ordinarily separate one person from another fall away and, for as long as the crowd holds together, all who belong to it feel equal.

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: crowd

## Shown

The instant made visible: a mass that a moment ago was made of separately dressed, separately ranked, separately postured people now moves, sounds, or acts as one, and the differences that would ordinarily mark rank or role are, in that instant, not on display.

## Holds

The equality itself, precisely as long as the crowd holds and no longer. Canetti is exact about this: discharge is the crowd's most treasured moment and its most fragile one -- it is what people join a crowd to reach, and it lasts only as long as the crowd itself does.

## Offers

The one thing no other condition of the crowd offers: the felt abolition of standing. Not the absence of standing, which is anonymity, but the positive sensation of the differences no longer applying, however briefly, to anyone present.

## Withheld

Permanence. Discharge is not a state the crowd sustains; it is the peak the crowd's other conditions exist to make possible, and it withholds itself again the moment the crowd disperses or its direction is lost -- the equality does not travel home with anyone.
