# Crowd: Reference

## Line of Work

Crowd as **place**: the massed environment that presses on everyone inside it before anyone speaks. The domain models the crowd as anchor (what the crowd is as a pressure environment -- what it shows, holds, offers, and withholds simply by being a crowd) and three expressions -- density (the physical closeness that abolishes individual distance), discharge (the moment internal distinctions fall away and the mass feels equal), direction (the shared goal that keeps the mass moving as one rather than dispersing) -- and what each condition makes available or withholds.

The spine is Canetti's phenomenology of the crowd, read as environment rather than as any one member's experience: a crowd has its own drive to grow, needs density to exist as a crowd at all, reaches for discharge as its most treasured and most fragile moment, and requires direction to remain a crowd once formed rather than dissolving back into separate people. Le Bon supplies the earlier, harder-edged account of what massing does to the individual submerged in it: the loss of personal restraint, the contagion of gesture and feeling, and the heightened suggestibility of anyone inside the mass -- all read here as what the place offers to whoever enters it, not as claims about crowds being inherently irrational or dangerous.

## Origin

Crowd psychology and the sociology of collective behavior.

| Source             | Key Work                                                             | Scope                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                             |
| :----------------- | :------------------------------------------------------------------- | :-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Elias Canetti**  | _Crowds and Power_ (1960; English trans. 1962)                       | The crowd's own drive to grow; density as the abolition of individual distance (the reversal of the fear of being touched); discharge as the moment all distinctions fall away and the crowd feels equal; direction as what a crowd needs to remain a crowd rather than disperse. |
| **Gustave Le Bon** | _The Crowd: A Study of the Popular Mind_ (1895; English trans. 1896) | The submergence of individual personality inside the crowd; anonymity and the loss of personal restraint; the contagion of ideas and emotion through the mass; the heightened suggestibility of anyone submerged in it.                                                           |

## Restrictions

What the engine refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **The individual's felt loss-of-self and its contagion of affect**: What any one persona feels as their personality submerges into the crowd, and how that feeling or emotion spreads from person to person, belong to the **contagion** and **emotion** engines. This engine models the massed environment that makes submergence and contagion possible, not the felt experience or its transmission.
- **Conformity to the group's standard**: Once a persona adjusts their behavior to match what the crowd or group expects, that adjustment belongs to the **conformity** and **group** engines. This engine models the pressure environment prior to any one persona's conforming response to it.
- **The individual's standing**: What rank, role, or status a persona holds -- inside the crowd or outside it -- belongs to the **hierarchy** engine. This engine withholds any accounting of standing; discharge is precisely the moment such accounting stops applying, but the engine does not model what standing was erased.
