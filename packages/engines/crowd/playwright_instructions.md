# Instructions: Crowd

How a Playwright wires the crowd engine into a play. You wire by linking the
play's own content to the engine's files; the engine files stay untouched, they
are canon. The Roadie plumbs the engine's law into Instructions Knowledge on
deploy. Authoring guidance, not runtime content, and it does not go on tour.

## Human

- The human defines the crowd's condition: which expression (density, discharge, or direction) the crowd is in, how close its bodies are pressed, whether it has reached discharge, and what shared goal or target holds it together and moving.

## Agent

- From the play's own content, a persona links the crowd's current expression to its Projection. The expression is the pressure environment the persona stands inside, not what the persona feels about being there. You link to the engine, you never edit it.

## Collaboration

- The contagion and emotion engines hold what any one persona feels as their personality submerges into the crowd, and how that feeling spreads person to person. The conformity and group engines hold a persona's adjustment to the crowd's standard. The hierarchy engine holds the persona's own standing, inside the crowd or outside it -- standing that discharge erases for as long as the crowd holds, without this engine accounting for what was erased.

## Knowledge

- Crowd is the massed environment that presses on everyone inside it before anyone speaks. It is a place: the crowd acts on its members, not the reverse. It shows the visible mass arriving before any word; it holds the crowd's drive to grow and the density that makes it a crowd at all; it offers the relief of the submerged individual, the contagion of gesture and emotion, and heightened suggestibility; it withholds the discharge (the moment internal distinctions fall away and the mass feels equal) and the direction (the shared goal that keeps the crowd a crowd) until the mass itself supplies them. The engine models three expressions: density (bodies pressed close enough that individual distance is abolished), discharge (the crowd's own most treasured and most fragile moment, when distinctions drop and all feel equal), and direction (the shared goal or target that keeps the mass moving as one rather than dispersing).

## System

- Do not model the individual's felt loss-of-self or its contagion of affect; that belongs to the contagion and emotion engines.
- Do not model conformity to the crowd's standard; that belongs to the conformity and group engines.
- Do not model any one persona's own standing, inside the crowd or outside it; that belongs to the hierarchy engine.
- Do not edit the engine's files; wire only from the play's side.
