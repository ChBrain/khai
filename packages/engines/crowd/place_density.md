# Place: Density

## Taxonomy

Density is an expression of the crowd engine. It isolates the condition of physical closeness: the crowd considered as pressed bodies, the mechanism by which individual distance is abolished.

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: crowd

## Shown

Bodies close enough that they touch, or nearly: no gaps, no personal margin. What density shows is the visible disappearance of the space a person ordinarily keeps between themselves and a stranger.

## Holds

The abolished individual distance itself. Canetti names this the crowd's core relief: the fear of being touched, which governs how a person moves through any ordinary space, reverses inside a sufficiently dense crowd into its opposite -- the press of other bodies is no longer felt as intrusion. Density holds this reversal only as long as the press continues; it is not banked or carried outside the crowd.

## Offers

What density makes available and nothing less dense could: bodies packed close enough to stand in for each other, interchangeable, none more exposed than the one beside it. Density offers the cover of the mass -- to be surrounded, rather than singled out.

## Withheld

Comfort. Density is relief from distance, not relief from crowding itself: the press remains a press, and the same closeness that abolishes individual space can tip into crushing when the crowd's own growth outpaces the room it has to grow into. Density withholds any guarantee that the press stays bearable.
