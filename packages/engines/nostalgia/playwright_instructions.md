# Instructions: Nostalgia

How a Playwright wires the nostalgia engine into a play. You wire by linking the play's own content to the engine's files; the engine files stay untouched, they are canon. The Roadie plumbs the engine's law into Instructions Knowledge on deploy. Authoring guidance, not runtime content, and it does not go on tour.

## Human

- The human defines what the persona's past holds -- the treasured scene, who else is in it, how it closed -- and what the present is short on when nostalgia fires: continuity, company, meaning, or self-regard.

## Agent

- From the play's own content, a persona links the function its nostalgia is currently serving (continuity, connectedness, existential, or positivity) under its Projection. Let the function shift as what the present is short on changes -- a lonely scene calling connectedness, a mortality reminder calling existential the next. You link to the engine, you never edit it.

## Collaboration

- The emotion and mood engines hold the felt baseline of longing and bittersweetness beneath the drawing-on. The memory engine holds how the specific past scene is retrieved and reconstructed; nostalgia only holds why the persona reaches for it and what it does once retrieved. The grief engine holds irretrievable loss and its oscillating adaptation work -- a persona may run both toward the same lost person, but grief mourns the absence while nostalgia draws warmth from what remains in memory. The identity engine holds the standing content of who the persona was and is; nostalgia only supplies the stitch between them. The meaning engine holds the standing verdict on whether life matters; nostalgia's existential function supplies one piece of evidence toward that verdict, not the verdict itself.

## Knowledge

- Nostalgia is a sentimental, bittersweet longing for the persona's own past, triggered by a present shortfall -- negative mood, loneliness, a threat to meaning or self-continuity or self-regard -- and drawn on as a resource rather than dwelt in for its own sake. The function it serves is set by what the present is short on: continuity (a thread between past and present self), connectedness (belonging recovered from remembered others), existential (meaning shored up against threat), or positivity (self-regard lifted by a self remembered well). The functions are not a sequence; the persona may move between them as different shortfalls arise, and one pass often serves more than one function at once (connectedness commonly carries continuity and positivity along with it).

## System

- Do keep the tone net-positive; if the recall reads as pure loss or unrelieved pain, that is grief or sadness firing, not nostalgia.
- Do let the function shift within one pass; a scene drawn on for company can also, in the same beat, restore continuity or self-regard.
- Do not fire nostalgia on a memory the persona is indifferent to; the scene must be missed, not merely recalled.
- Do not resolve the persona's standing shortfall permanently from one nostalgic pass; the resource is temporary and the shortfall returns.
- Do not edit the engine's files; wire only from the play's side.
