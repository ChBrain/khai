# Nostalgia: Reference

## Line of Work

Nostalgia as **process**: a sentimental, bittersweet longing for one's own past, drawn on by the persona as a psychological resource rather than dwelt in for its own sake. The domain does not model the felt emotion in the moment (that is the emotion and mood engines' territory) or the retrieval mechanism that reconstructs a specific memory (that is the memory engine's); it models the orientation toward the past and the work that orientation does once fired -- self-continuity, social connectedness, existential meaning, and self-positivity, the four functions the empirical literature documents and replicates.

The spine is Sedikides and Wildschut's programme: nostalgia is triggered chiefly by dysphoric states (negative mood, loneliness) and by threats to meaning, self-continuity, or self-regard; its content typically features the self as protagonist among close others at a momentous, now-closed moment; and it is net-positive in affective tone even though it is bittersweet -- the "sweet" of a good past outweighs the "bitter" of its being over.

## Restrictions

What the engine refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **The felt affective baseline**: The engine models the orientation toward the past and the functions it serves, not the raw emotion of longing or bittersweetness in the moment. The persona's baseline feeling-tone belongs to the **emotion** and **mood** engines; nostalgia holds the specific bittersweet-longing structure and what it is for, not the general feeling.
- **The reconstruction of the memory itself**: The engine models why the persona reaches for the past and what it does once it arrives, not how a specific past event is retrieved, reconsolidated, or distorted. The retrieval mechanism belongs to the **memory** engine; nostalgia is the orientation, not the recall process.
- **Nostalgia is not grief.** This is the sharpest line the engine draws. Grief is adaptation to an irreversible loss that the persona did not choose and cannot make peace with quickly -- it is oscillation between confronting an absence and rebuilding around it, and its material is net-negative until adaptation is well underway. Nostalgia is a bittersweet-but-net-positive longing for a past self or time that the persona draws on as a resource, on demand, without needing the past to still be reachable or the loss to be the point. A persona missing someone who died is grieving; a persona warmed by a memory of that person, drawing comfort or continuity from it, is nostalgic -- the two can run in the same persona but are not the same process, and irretrievable-loss mourning is delegated wholesale to the **grief** engine.
- **How the past self relates to present identity across the play**: The engine models the resource-drawing pass, not the standing content of who the persona is or was. What the persona's past self actually contained -- the specific history -- belongs to the play's arc and the persona's Projection, not to this engine.

## Encoding

Source to constraint, per file.

- **[nostalgia](process_nostalgia.md)** (the root): Nostalgia as process: bittersweet longing for one's own past, triggered by dysphoric states or a threat the present cannot answer, drawn on as a resource with net-positive tone. Anchored by Sedikides, Wildschut, Arndt & Routledge (2008) -- the overview, triggers, and net-positive tone; Wildschut, Sedikides, Arndt & Routledge (2006) -- the content of nostalgic narratives.
- **[continuity](process_continuity.md)** (function): The persona is short on a felt thread between its past and present self; nostalgia stitches the two together. Anchored by Sedikides, Wildschut, Routledge & Arndt (2015) -- self-discontinuity as trigger, self-continuity as restored resource.
- **[connectedness](process_connectedness.md)** (function): The persona is short on belonging; nostalgia recovers a felt sense of being loved and accompanied from a remembered scene. Anchored by Wildschut, Sedikides, Arndt & Routledge (2006) -- loneliness as trigger, social bonds as function; the self-as-protagonist-among-others content finding.
- **[existential](process_existential.md)** (function): The persona is short on meaning under an existential threat; nostalgia supplies a treasured scene as evidence that life has mattered. Anchored by Routledge, Arndt, Sedikides & Wildschut (2008) -- the terror management function, buffering death-thought accessibility.
- **[positivity](process_positivity.md)** (function): The persona's self-regard is running low; nostalgia lifts it by recalling a self remembered favourably. Anchored by Wildschut, Sedikides, Arndt & Routledge (2006) and Sedikides, Wildschut, Arndt & Routledge (2008) -- the self-esteem and net-positive-affect functions.
