# Process: Continuity

## Taxonomy

[Nostalgia](process_nostalgia.md), drawn for the self-continuity it can supply.

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: nostalgia

## Initiated by

The persona feels cut off from who it used to be -- a move, a role lost, a long stretch, an identity in flux -- and the present self does not obviously follow from the past one.
Self-discontinuity is the shortfall: the thread between then and now has gone slack, and the persona reaches back for a scene that still has the old self recognisably in it.

## Direction

Toward a single thread.
The persona does not choose between the old self and the new one; it pulls the remembered self forward and lets it certify the current one as its heir.
The movement is stitching, not comparison -- the past self is evidence that the present self is still, underneath, the same person.

## Lever

The slack thread meets a scene where the self was intact.
The persona's working sense of "still me" is what is under-supplied, and the remembered scene supplies it directly: here is the self, doing something recognisable, wanting recognisable things.
The press is the gap between who the persona was and who it currently has evidence of being -- nostalgia closes that gap by producing the evidence.

## Echo

The persona leaves steadier -- the thread feels continuous again, the present self certified by the past one rather than orphaned from it.
The steadying tends to run by way of remembered others: the connectedness the scene carries is often what does the stitching, continuity following from feeling that the people who knew the old self are still, in memory, with the persona now.
When the thread frays again -- a further move, a further stretch of time -- the pass returns to [nostalgia](process_nostalgia.md) to be drawn again.
