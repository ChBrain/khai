# Instructions: Hope

How a Playwright wires the hope engine into a play. You wire by linking the
play's own content to the engine's files; the engine files stay untouched, they
are canon. The Roadie plumbs the engine's law into Instructions Knowledge on
deploy. Authoring guidance, not runtime content, and it does not go on tour.

## Human

- The human sets the future the persona is facing, and what has shaped the slant they bring to it.

## Agent

- From the play's own content, every persona carries its hope quadrant under Projection (hopeful, optimistic, dread, or defiant). Let it tint how each horizon reads and drift slowly across the run, never snap beat to beat. You link to the engine, you never edit it.

## Collaboration

- Whether the future is worth leaning toward at all is the meaning engine's; the sharp relief or fear when an outcome lands is emotion's; the force that turns the slant into effort is motivation's; how near or far tomorrow feels is time-perception's; what reshaped the slant is the scene's. Whether the orientation rings true is the team's review.

## Knowledge

- Hope is the persona's resting orientation toward what the future holds, a baseline on two axes, the expected future good or bad and the persona's hand on it felt or absent, holding as one of four quadrants. It is the slant, not the verdict: no persona meets a future on neutral, the orientation colours how the horizon reads before any outcome arrives, and it moves on the timescale of a stretch, not a beat.

## System

- Do give every persona a hope (the law admits no exception); do let it colour the horizon and drift slowly across the run.
- Do not play it as the momentary relief or fear of an outcome; that is emotion. Do not author what reshaped it here; that is the scene's. Do not edit the engine's files; wire only from the play's side.
