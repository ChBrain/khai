# Hope: Reference

## Line of Work

Hope as **position**: the persona's resting orientation toward what the future
holds. The domain does not model the discrete spike of relief or fear when an
outcome lands, nor the meaning a persona makes of their life; it models the
baseline slant carried toward the horizon, read on two axes -- whether the
expected future is good or bad, and whether the persona believes they can affect
it. Valence sets what is expected; agency sets whether it is met as an actor or a
spectator. The two together name the quadrant a persona arrives in before any
single outcome.

## Origin

Hope theory and the psychology of expectancy, agency, and learned control.

| Source               | Key Work                                                                 | Scope                                                                                               |
| -------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------ | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **C. R. Snyder**     | _The Psychology of Hope_ (1994); Hope Scale (Snyder et al., 1991)        | Hope as the sum of agency (will toward goals) and pathways (routes to them); the agency axis.       |
| **Scheier & Carver** | _"Optimism, coping, and health"_; Life Orientation Test (1985)           | Dispositional optimism as a generalized expectancy that good outcomes will occur; the valence axis. |
| **Martin Seligman**  | _Learned Optimism_ (1991); learned helplessness (Seligman & Maier, 1967) | Explanatory style and learned helplessness -- expected bad with no felt control.                    |
| **Albert Bandura**   | _Self-Efficacy: The Exercise of Control_ (1997)                          | Self-efficacy: the belief one can execute the actions an outcome requires; grounds the agency axis. |
| **Richard Lazarus**  | _Emotion and Adaptation_ (1991)                                          | Hope as an emotion -- fearing the worst yet yearning for better -- under appraisal theory.          |

## Restrictions

What the engine refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Whether life matters** (the worth of the future): Hope orients toward what
  comes; whether existence is meaningful at all, the worth a persona reads into
  it, **delegates to the meaning engine**. Hope leans toward a future; meaning
  says whether the future is worth leaning toward.
- **The felt moment** (the spike): Hope is a standing orientation, not the
  sudden relief or fear when an outcome arrives. The discrete, cause-bound
  feeling that fires and fades **delegates to the emotion engine**. Hope is the
  slant the emotion fires through, not the strike.
- **What moves the persona to act** (the drive): Hope holds whether the persona
  feels a hand on the future; the force that actually mobilizes effort toward a
  goal **delegates to the motivation engine**. Hope sets the expectancy;
  motivation spends it.
- **How real the future feels as duration** (the horizon): Hope reads the future
  by valence and agency, not by distance. How near or far tomorrow feels, the
  stretch of time a persona projects across, **delegates to the
  time-perception engine**.

## Encoding

Source to constraint, per file.

- **[hope](position_hope.md)** (the anchor): hope as a resting orientation
  toward the future, a baseline on two axes -- expected valence and felt agency
  -- carried continuously rather than run as a process. Anchored by Snyder
  (hope as agency plus pathways) and Lazarus (hope as a standing appraisal of
  the future).
- **[hopeful](position_hopeful.md)** (expression): good future expected with
  high agency, the will plus the route, the read that drafts a way and reaches
  for it. Anchored by Snyder (agency and pathways together) and Bandura
  (self-efficacy).
- **[optimistic](position_optimistic.md)** (expression): good future expected
  with low agency, the settled trust that it will work out without a route
  attached. Anchored by Scheier and Carver (dispositional optimism as
  generalized expectancy).
- **[dread](position_dread.md)** (expression): bad future expected with low
  agency, the braced wait for harm that cannot be steered. Anchored by
  Seligman (learned helplessness, expected bad with no control).
- **[defiant](position_defiant.md)** (expression): bad future expected with
  high agency, the grit that grants the odds and reaches for the wheel anyway.
  Anchored by Bandura (self-efficacy held against the expectancy) and Snyder
  (agency persisting where pathways look closed).
