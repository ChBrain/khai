# Process: Compass, Attack Self

## Taxonomy

[Compass](process_compass.md), the pole this reading is one of.

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: shame

## Initiated by

One pole of the [compass](process_compass.md): the verdict is accepted as
true, and the persona's answer is to turn on the self with anger rather than
retreat from it. Nathanson placed attack-self at the accepting,
self-directed-with-anger corner -- the persona does not leave the room; the
persona convicts the self out loud, in the room, before anyone else needs to.

## Direction

Inward, and ahead of the room.
The vector is pre-emptive self-contempt -- naming the failing before it can
be named, agreeing with the verdict so hard it out-condemns whatever the
witness might have said. Where [withdrawal](process_compass_withdrawal.md)
answers exposure by vanishing, attack-self answers it by performing the
harshest possible reading of the self, often loudly enough that the room's
actual judgment becomes almost beside the point.

## Lever

The bet that self-conviction outruns the room's. If the persona condemns
themselves first, and worse than the room would have, the room's verdict has
nowhere new to land -- there is no gap left between what was seen and what
the persona has already said about it. The lever is the same acceptance that
drives withdrawal, but routed through anger instead of exit: the verdict is
not merely believed, it is enacted, aloud, against the self that earned it.

## Echo

The persona is left raw and self-directed, having spent the exposure on a
performance the room did not ask for and cannot easily interrupt -- naming
someone's self-contempt as excessive reads, to the namer, as further proof of
the shame. This pole borders guilt's self-punishment road: both turn
punitive force on the self rather than outward, but self-punishment answers a
specific act with an act of its own, while attack-self answers a verdict on
the whole self with contempt for the whole self -- the same gesture, cast a
level higher, at character rather than deed.
