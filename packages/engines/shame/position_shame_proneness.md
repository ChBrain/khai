# Position: Shame-Proneness

## Taxonomy

[Shame](process_shame.md), the appraisal this dial calibrates.

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: shame

## Has

The persona's standing disposition toward the whole engine: how readily
their [shame](process_shame.md) appraisal fires, how easily the verdict lands
on the whole self rather than staying act-sized, and how far the [compass](process_compass.md)
swings once it does. Tangney and Dearing measured it with the same instrument
that measures guilt-proneness, the TOSCA, and found the two travel as
distinct traits: shame-proneness is not guilt-proneness turned up, and a
persona can score high on one while scoring low on the other -- a persona
who repairs readily but rarely collapses, or one who collapses readily and
rarely repairs, which is castable gold.

## Orders

Set the threshold.
The position calibrates every exposure the engine reads: a high-prone
persona finds the gap between self and standard in scenes others would walk
through unmarked, and the appraisal widens from act to whole self with
little resistance; a low-prone persona needs the exposure sharp, public, and
undeniable before the verdict returns as shame rather than staying, if it
returns at all, on the act alone. Neither is the neutral setting -- the
position denies the persona any unset dial.

## Loses

High proneness loses resilience under a witness: ordinary missteps read as
character verdicts, and the persona enters rooms already braced for
exposure. Low proneness loses the self-correcting function shame is built to
serve -- gaps between self and standard go unregistered until they are large
enough that avoiding them no longer works, and the persona meets the verdict
late and all at once rather than early and in installments.

## Drives

On those losses: the high-prone persona is driven toward whichever
[compass](process_compass.md) pole is their habitual one, reached sooner and
harder than the scene alone would predict; the low-prone persona is driven
toward scenes that would shame another persona passing through them
unmarked, the appraisal simply not firing where a higher setting would. Cast
the dial once per persona, distinct from their guilt-proneness where the play
needs the two dispositions to diverge, and let every exposure and every pole
in this engine read against it.
