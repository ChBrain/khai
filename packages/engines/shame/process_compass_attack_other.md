# Process: Compass, Attack Other

## Taxonomy

[Compass](process_compass.md), the pole this reading is one of.

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: shame

## Initiated by

One pole of the [compass](process_compass.md): the verdict is minimized, not
accepted, and the persona's answer is to turn the anger it carries outward
rather than inward. Nathanson placed attack-other at the minimizing,
other-directed corner -- the persona does not absorb the verdict at all; the
verdict, or the anger that came with it, is handed to someone else before it
can settle.

## Direction

Outward, at whoever is lowest in reach.
The vector is transfer -- the exposure is answered by exposing someone else,
often someone with less standing to resist it: the humiliated humiliating,
the contempt passed downward rather than metabolized. The room's verdict on
the persona is displaced by a new verdict the persona hands out, restoring,
in the persona's own read, the balance of who is found wanting.

## Lever

The bet that the verdict can be relocated rather than answered. If someone
else can be made to hold the exposure -- caught out, mocked, blamed -- the
persona's own gap closes by comparison rather than by repair; the lever
presses hardest where a lower-standing target is available and least where
none is. This is the pole that most resembles [humiliation](process_humiliation.md)'s
rage, but the direction of authorship differs: humiliation answers a verdict
imposed by another's power, while attack-other is the persona's own exit from
a verdict it minimizes and redirects.

## Echo

The relocated verdict lands on someone who did not open the account, and the
persona exits feeling the balance restored rather than the gap closed. This
pole borders guilt's refusal by condemnation: both discount a verdict by
attacking the standing of whoever holds it, but condemnation argues the
judges are unfit to rule on the act, while attack-other simply moves the
verdict onto a new target -- the same discounting gesture, cast outward at a
person rather than argued against a court. Run as a standing habit, it
teaches the room to answer exposure with exposure, and the persona's account
with the room grows, even as the felt account with the self stays unpaid.
