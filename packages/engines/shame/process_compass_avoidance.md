# Process: Compass, Avoidance

## Taxonomy

[Compass](process_compass.md), the pole this reading is one of.

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: shame

## Initiated by

One pole of the [compass](process_compass.md): the verdict is minimized, not
accepted, and the persona's answer is to keep the feeling itself off the
record. Nathanson placed avoidance at the minimizing, self-directed corner --
the persona does not dispute the room aloud and does not leave; instead the
exposure is buried under noise: distraction, thrill-seeking, drink,
performance, a grandiosity pitched loud enough to drown its own premise.

## Direction

Away from the feeling, not the room.
The vector is submersion -- the persona stays present, sometimes more
present than before, but reroutes attention (the room's and their own) onto
anything that is not the exposed gap. Where [withdrawal](process_compass_withdrawal.md)
shrinks the self the room can see, avoidance inflates it, or simply floods
the channel so the verdict has no quiet moment to land in.

## Lever

The bet that the feeling can be outrun by volume. Nathanson called this the
pole that looks least like shame and is running it hardest: the drink, the
joke that will not stop, the sudden grandiosity are all working to keep the
exposure unprocessed rather than resolved, so the lever never gets the chance
to press cleanly. It differs from denial's edit of the facts -- avoidance
does not argue the exposure away, it drowns it out.

## Echo

The persona exits the scene looking, often, like the least affected person in
it -- which is the tell, not the disproof. The verdict is deferred rather
than answered, and deferred shame tends to resurface later, in a quieter
room, once the noise the persona built around it has nowhere left to run.
Held as a standing habit rather than one scene's cover, avoidance is the pole
most likely to be misread by everyone in the room, including, some of the
time, the persona running it.
