# Process: Compass, Withdrawal

## Taxonomy

[Compass](process_compass.md), the pole this reading is one of.

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: shame

## Initiated by

One pole of the [compass](process_compass.md): the verdict is accepted as
true, and the persona's answer is to shrink the self the verdict landed on.
Nathanson placed withdrawal at the accepting, self-directed corner -- the
persona does not dispute what the room saw, does not turn the anger anywhere;
it simply removes the exposed self from further view.

## Direction

Out of the room.
The vector is exit, bodily where the room allows it -- the door found, the
gathering left early -- and postural where it does not: eyes down, voice
lowered, the body made smaller and quieter without moving at all. Silence is
withdrawal's version of leaving when leaving is not an option. The persona is
not performing contrition for an audience; the audience is precisely what
withdrawal is trying to stop being in front of.

## Lever

The verdict, once accepted, has nowhere left to press but on visibility
itself. If the exposed self is wrong, the least-cost move is to be seen less
-- the logic is not "I will change" but "I will disappear," which is why
withdrawal, unlike guilt's reparative pull, does nothing to the standard or
the harm. It only removes the target the gaze was resting on.

## Echo

The persona is smaller in the room's memory than before, and the exposure is
answered without being addressed -- nothing about the gap the standard found
has changed, only how much of the persona is left in view of it. Held once,
withdrawal passes with the scene; held as the persona's standing answer to
exposure, it narrows the rooms the persona will enter at all, which is the
compass's most costly pole to run as a habit rather than a single retreat.
