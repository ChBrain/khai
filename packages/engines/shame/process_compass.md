# Process: Compass

## Taxonomy

[Shame](process_shame.md), the appraisal this channel runs after.

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: shame

## Initiated by

The appraisal has already landed -- the self is exposed, the gap is seen, the
verdict is "you are wrong." The compass does not decide whether shame fires;
it opens once shame has, at the moment the persona must do something with a
verdict already in hand. Nathanson mapped four recurring scripts personas
reach for at this moment, arranged around two axes: whether the verdict is
accepted or minimized, and whether the response turns on the self or on
another. The compass is what the persona does with shame, not what shame is.

## Direction

Toward one of four poles.
The vector forks on contact: [withdrawal](process_compass_withdrawal.md) and
[attack self](process_compass_attack_self.md) both accept the verdict, one by
leaving the field, one by turning inward with anger; [avoidance](process_compass_avoidance.md)
and [attack other](process_compass_attack_other.md) both minimize it, one by
distraction, one by transferring the verdict onto someone lower. A persona
reaching reliably for the same pole across scenes is not choosing it fresh
each time -- the pole has become the script.

## Lever

Which of the two questions the persona answers first: is the verdict true,
and does it stay on me. Accepting the verdict presses toward the self;
refusing it presses outward or away from it. Elison, Lennon and Pulos found
the four poles hold up as a real structure under measurement, not just
Nathanson's clinical taxonomy -- attack-self and attack-other share anger,
directed at different targets; withdrawal and attack-self share acceptance,
directed at different intensities; avoidance and attack-other share
minimization, directed at different objects.

## Echo

Whichever pole is reached, the exposure is answered but the standard is not
necessarily met -- the room's verdict may still stand unaddressed underneath
the response. A persona who runs the same pole repeatedly is showing the
compass's other job: it is not just a reaction to one scene but a readable
signature, the same needle finding the same direction across many rooms. How
readily the whole engine fires in the first place, and how far the needle
swings once it does, is the persona's own
[shame-proneness](position_shame_proneness.md) -- the compass reads the poles,
the dial sets how hard it needs to be pushed to move at all.
