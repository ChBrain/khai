# Process: Betrayal

## Taxonomy

Trust violation reckoning.

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: betrayal

## Initiated by

Someone the persona trusted has acted against the persona's interests, expectations, or well-being -- and done so in a way that could not happen without access granted by trust.
The betrayal is not a mistake by a stranger; it is a violation by someone whose ability to harm depended on closeness.
The persona now holds the knowledge that the trust was used against them.

## Direction

Toward a settled relationship with the violation.
The persona does not return to the prior trust; they renegotiate what the relationship is and what can be held within it, or they leave the relationship behind.
The process is not a sequence; passes may recur as new information arrives or as earlier passes remain incomplete.

## Lever

The trust and the violation are simultaneous facts.
The persona cannot hold the two without the structure of the relationship becoming incoherent: the person who violated the trust is also the person the trust was in.
What the persona built on the trustworthy relationship must now be rebuilt on a different foundation.

## Echo

The process runs through discovery (learning what happened), disorientation (losing the prior map of the relationship), reckoning (deciding what to do with the knowledge), and resolution (establishing a new relationship to the person and the fact).
The passes do not resolve in a straight line; the persona may move back to disorientation from reckoning as the reckoning makes the full extent of the violation visible.
What ends the process is not forgetting but a stable account: [discovery](process_discovery.md), [disorientation](process_disorientation.md), [reckoning](process_reckoning.md), [resolution](process_resolution.md).
