# Process: Betrayal, Institutional

## Taxonomy

[Betrayal](process_betrayal.md), the violation this is one kind of -- on a different axis from the other three.

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: betrayal

## Initiated by

The wrongdoer is not a person the persona loved but an institution the persona depended on -- an employer, a school, the military, a church, a healthcare system -- and it acted against those who depend on it, or failed to prevent foreseeable harm, or failed to respond supportively once harm inside it was reported.
Smith and Freyd (2014) name this institutional betrayal: the same violated-dependence structure as any betrayal, with the institution standing where a trusted person would stand.

## Direction

Across the who-axis, not the what-axis.
The other three kinds of this violation -- infidelity, confidence, neglect -- differ in what was betrayed.
This one differs in who did the betraying: the depended-upon body rather than a depended-upon person.
The dependence the institution violates is the same dependence the [blindness](position_blindness.md) dial reads -- an institution the persona cannot afford to distrust produces the identical unawareness a trusted intimate does.

## Lever

The institution's own structural power under its own failure to use it protectively.
An institution can betray by direct wrongdoing (harassment, discrimination, exploitation enabled by the institution's own structure) or by institutional betrayal proper (failing to prevent foreseeable harm, or failing to respond supportively once harm was reported) -- both press the same lever: the body the persona had no choice but to depend on used, or squandered, the power that dependence gave it.

## Echo

Because the wrongdoer is diffuse -- a policy, a culture, a committee, rarely a single named actor -- reckoning runs differently than against a person: there is no one face to confront, and the institution the persona must still depend on (the job, the school, the care system) is frequently the same institution that failed them, which forecloses the ordinary exit of simply leaving.
Discovery here often lands hardest of all four kinds, against a blindness built not from love but from need.
