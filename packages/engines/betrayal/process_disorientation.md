# Process: Disorientation

## Taxonomy

[Betrayal](process_betrayal.md), the reckoning this pass is one movement of.

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: betrayal

## Initiated by

The violation is now held as fact and the prior map of the relationship is visibly wrong.
If the betrayer could do this, what else about the relationship was not what it appeared to be?
The persona cannot locate a stable account of who the betrayer is, what the relationship was, or which of their own memories can be trusted.

## Direction

Toward a revised account of the relationship and the person.
The persona must replace the prior map with one that can hold the betrayal as part of the picture -- not by excusing the violation but by integrating it into a coherent understanding of what happened and who did it.

## Lever

The revised fact meets every memory of the relationship.
The persona re-reads the past through the new information; encounters that seemed innocent are re-examined; the betrayer's motives are re-attributed.
Each re-reading shifts the account, and the account must be rebuilt from a foundation the persona did not know was unreliable.

## Echo

The persona is revising the past under conditions of uncertainty: they do not know how far the revision must go, and each new re-reading may require another.
The disorientation may persist until the persona reaches a revised account stable enough to act on; or the persona may choose to limit the re-reading rather than pursue the full revision.
When a working account is achieved, the move into [reckoning](process_reckoning.md) begins.
