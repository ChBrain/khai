# Process: Betrayal, Infidelity

## Taxonomy

[Betrayal](process_betrayal.md), the violation this is one kind of.

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: betrayal

## Initiated by

The bond promised exclusivity, and the exclusivity itself is what was given to another.
A kind of [betrayal](process_betrayal.md): among Jones and Burdette's collected betrayal narratives, infidelity is the most cited type on record -- the tie's own defining claim, broken at its center rather than at its edge.
What is violated here is not a rule adjacent to the bond but the bond's own terms.

## Direction

Outward, toward the third party.
The vector runs from what was promised only to the persona -- attention, desire, a particular kind of access -- through its extension to someone the promise excluded.
The violation completes the moment the exclusivity is spent elsewhere, whether or not the persona yet knows it.

## Lever

The bond's own defining term under the betrayer's own choice.
What makes infidelity betrayal rather than mere disappointment is that the exclusivity was never incidental to the relationship; it was close to definitional.
Remove the promise of exclusivity and the same act may still wound, but it no longer violates a term the bond was built on.

## Echo

The persona is left holding a relationship whose central promise has already been broken elsewhere, often for some length of time before discovery arrives.
What was exclusive to the bond is now known to have been shared, and the sharing cannot be undone even where the relationship continues.
This kind tends to detonate hardest against a persona whose own [blindness](position_blindness.md) had been running high before discovery forced the account open.
