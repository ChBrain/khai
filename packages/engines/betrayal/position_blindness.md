# Position: Blindness

## Taxonomy

[Betrayal](process_betrayal.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: betrayal

## Has

The persona's standing unawareness of a betrayal already underway or already available to be seen -- not a failure of intelligence or attention but, per Freyd, a protective mechanism: the awareness that would force the persona to act against a bond they cannot afford to lose stays unprocessed, so the bond survives.
Freyd and Birrell's later work names the correlate on the other side, blind trust: a readiness to extend the exact trust that made the blindness necessary in the first place.

## Orders

Scale to the dependence.
The position sets how much of an available signal the persona can register: the higher the dependence on the relationship or institution for survival, resources, or attachment, the stronger the blindness runs, and the more evidence it takes before the persona's own perception is permitted to reach them.
A low-dependence bond affords the persona the safety of noticing early; a high-dependence one does not.

## Loses

Timely knowledge.
What the position costs the persona is not the capacity to eventually see -- the blindness is not blindness of another kind, permanent -- but the years or the incidents it takes to reach the seeing.
The information was available; the persona's own protective architecture kept it from landing.

## Drives

On that loss: the persona under high blindness stays in the position of dependence past the point an outside observer would call obvious, is often read by others as willfully naive, and pays for the delay in a [discovery](process_discovery.md) that lands all at once rather than in installments -- the accumulated unseen evidence returning as a single detonation instead of a gradual dawning.
A low-blindness persona pays the opposite cost: they see early, at the price of a dependence they can no longer fully inhabit while watching it.
This is the most castable position in the engine: two personas at opposite ends of this dial live in different realities about the same facts, right up until discovery closes the gap for one of them by force.
