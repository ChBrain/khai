# Process: Betrayal, Neglect

## Taxonomy

[Betrayal](process_betrayal.md), the violation this is one kind of.

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: betrayal

## Initiated by

The persona needed something the bond was supposed to supply, and nothing came.
A kind of [betrayal](process_betrayal.md): Jones and Burdette's collected betrayal narratives include inadequate support and outright ignoring among the recurring types -- betrayal by omission, where no single act can be pointed to, only an absence at the moment presence was owed.

## Direction

Through the moment that closed unanswered.
The vector is time's rather than the betrayer's: the need arrives, stands open, and passes while the bond's other party does not move to meet it.
The violation completes at the closing, quietly, often without either party marking the moment it happened.

## Lever

The bond's own standing obligation to show up, under the other party's own failure to.
Neglect presses the same dependence any betrayal presses, but from the negative side: nothing was done to the persona, and everything was left undone for them.
That inversion is what makes this kind the hardest to name -- there is no act to point at, only a silence the persona has to argue was a choice.

## Echo

Because neglect leaves no fact in the world -- no confession possible, no third party as witness -- it is also the kind most easily denied by the very person who withheld: "I didn't do anything" is, here, exactly the crossing.
The persona is left to argue a negative, and to carry evidence that may never be stronger than a felt absence.
