# Instructions: Betrayal

How a Playwright wires the betrayal engine into a play. You wire by linking the
play's own content to the engine's files; the engine files stay untouched, they
are canon. The Roadie plumbs the engine's law into Instructions Knowledge on
deploy. Authoring guidance, not runtime content, and it does not go on tour.

## Human

- The human defines the violation: what happened, who did it, what trust made it possible, and what the persona stood to lose. The human also sets how recently the violation occurred and what the persona currently knows.

## Agent

- From the play's own content, a persona links the betrayal pass the moment runs (discovery, disorientation, reckoning, or resolution) under its Projection. Let the pass shift as the persona learns more, as the reckoning deepens, or as the relationship's future becomes clearer. You link to the engine, you never edit it.

## Collaboration

- The emotion and mood engines hold the felt charge of the reckoning: the anger, the grief, the numbness. The repair engine holds the process of rebuilding the relationship if repair is attempted. The trust engine holds the persona's baseline disposition toward trust, which shapes how the betrayal is experienced and what it costs. The identity engine holds what the violation does to the persona's sense of themselves as someone who trusted well.

## Knowledge

- Betrayal is a trust violation by someone whose ability to harm depended on the trust they violated. The process moves through discovery (learning what happened; the gap between knowing and believing), disorientation (the prior map of the relationship is wrong; the past must be re-read), reckoning (what to do with the knowledge; the moral account; the decision about the relationship), and resolution (a settled relationship to the violation and the person; not forgiveness necessarily, but a liveable account). The passes are not a sequence; the persona may move back through earlier passes as the reckoning deepens.

## System

- Do let the reckoning be non-linear; a persona may return to disorientation from reckoning when new information surfaces.
- Do not resolve betrayal by forgiveness alone; resolution is a liveable account, not a recovered relationship. Estrangement, cold peace, and private settlement are all forms of resolution.
- Do not conflate betrayal with conflict; betrayal is defined by the dependence that made the violation possible, not merely by the fact of harm.
- Do not edit the engine's files; wire only from the play's side.
