# Process: Betrayal, Confidence

## Taxonomy

[Betrayal](process_betrayal.md), the violation this is one kind of.

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: betrayal

## Initiated by

Something was said inside the bond on the understanding that it would stay there, and it did not.
A kind of [betrayal](process_betrayal.md): Jones and Burdette's collected betrayal narratives name betrayed confidences among the recurring types -- the secret disclosed, the private admission repeated, the vulnerability offered in trust and later produced as currency outside the room it was given in.

## Direction

Outward, past the room's boundary.
The vector runs from what was said in confidence through its passage to an audience the persona never authorized.
The violation completes at the telling, not at the persona's discovery of it -- the confidence is already spent by the time the persona learns it was not kept.

## Lever

The room's own boundary under the confidant's own choice.
Confidence-keeping is what makes disclosure to a particular person survivable at all; the persona said what they said because the room was supposed to end at the confidant.
Remove that boundary and there was no confidence to begin with, only an announcement.

## Echo

The persona now edits, retroactively, what they believe was ever safe to say -- not only to this confidant but to the category of person the confidant belonged to.
What was entrusted cannot be un-disclosed, and the damage often widens past the original secret, since the persona cannot know how far the telling traveled or to whom.
