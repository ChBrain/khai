# Loyalty: Reference

## Line of Work

Loyalty as **position**: a standing allegiance to a person, group, or cause,
held before any single decline is felt. The domain does not model trust as a
process of being extended, tested, and betrayed, nor the felt bond behind
the allegiance, nor a violation of it, nor the group's own arc of change; it
models the standing stake itself -- the accumulated investment that raises
the cost of leaving and buys a declining relationship time. Read against
Hirschman's own pair, exit and voice, it is the switch that decides which
one fires, and for how long it is delayed, when quality drops.

## Origin

The economics and social psychology of response to organizational and
relational decline.

| **Albert O. ## Restrictions

What the engine refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Trust as a process** (extended, tested, betrayed): Loyalty is the standing
  stake once allegiance is held; whether that allegiance was ever warranted,
  and how it is built or eroded case by case, **delegates to the trust
  engine**. Loyalty spends the stake trust has accumulated; it does not
  account for how the stake was earned.
- **The felt bond** (the attachment): Loyalty is the cost-raising mechanism,
  not the warmth behind it. The felt closeness a persona carries toward the
  person, group, or cause **delegates to the attachment engine**. Loyalty is
  what the bond buys against decline, not the bond itself.
- **A violation of the allegiance** (the break): Loyalty holds while decline
  is tolerated; the sharp, named breach that ends tolerance outright
  **delegates to the betrayal engine**. Loyalty measures a slow drop against
  a stake; betrayal is the single cut that voids the stake at once.
- **The group's own development** (the arc): Loyalty reads a member's
  standing response to decline; the group's own trajectory -- forming,
  norming, fracturing -- that the decline is a symptom of **delegates to the
  group engine**. Loyalty answers what a member does about the drop, not
  what the group is going through.

## Encoding

Source to constraint, per file.

- **[loyalty](position_loyalty.md)** (the anchor): loyalty as a standing
  allegiance, the accumulated stake that raises the cost of leaving and
  buys a declining relationship time before a member acts. Anchored by
  Hirschman (loyalty holding exit at bay) and Rusbult (investment size
  raising commitment independent of current satisfaction).
- **[exit](position_exit.md)** (expression): the quiet pull to leave once
  the stake no longer covers the drop -- Hirschman's market response, an
  anonymous defection rather than a named complaint.
- **[voice](position_voice.md)** (expression): loyalty's active form,
  staying to press for repair from within -- Hirschman's political
  response, spending the stake as leverage rather than cashing it out.
