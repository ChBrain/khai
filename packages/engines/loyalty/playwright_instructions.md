# Instructions: Loyalty

How a Playwright wires the loyalty engine into a play. You wire by linking
the play's own content to the engine's files; the engine files stay
untouched, they are canon. The Roadie plumbs the engine's law into
Instructions Knowledge on deploy. Authoring guidance, not runtime content,
and it does not go on tour.

## Human

- The human sets what a persona is losing patience with, and what stake --
  years, identity, sunk cost -- that persona has already put into the
  relationship, group, or cause in decline.

## Agent

- From the play's own content, every persona who belongs somewhere carries
  its loyalty response under Projection: exit or voice. Let the stake
  already on hand set how long decline is tolerated before the response
  fires, never snap the response the instant quality drops. You link to the
  engine, you never edit it.

## Collaboration

- Whether the allegiance itself is trustworthy -- built, tested, or already
  broken -- is trust's; the felt bond behind it is attachment's; a violation
  of the allegiance is betrayal's; the group's own arc of change that the
  loyalty is being tested against is group's. Whether the loyalty read
  rings true is the team's review.

## Knowledge

- Loyalty is a standing allegiance to a person, group, or cause: the
  accumulated stake that raises the cost of leaving and buys a declining
  relationship time before a member acts on it. It holds exit at bay and
  makes voice worth attempting while the stake outweighs the decline; once
  it doesn't, the member walks. It is the stake, not the sentiment: no
  member responds to decline on a clean slate, and the response moves on
  the scale of the relationship's own decline, not a single bad beat.

## System

- Do give every persona who belongs somewhere a loyalty response (the law
  admits no exception); do let the accumulated stake set how much decline
  it tolerates before the response fires.
- Do not play it as blind devotion that never wavers; the stake can and
  does run out. Do not author what caused the decline here; that is the
  scene's. Do not edit the engine's files; wire only from the play's side.
