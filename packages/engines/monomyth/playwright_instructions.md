# Instructions: Monomyth

How a Playwright wires the monomyth engine into a play. You wire by linking the
play's own content to the engine's files; the engine files stay untouched, they
are canon. The Roadie plumbs the engine's law into Instructions Knowledge on
deploy. Authoring guidance, not runtime content, and it does not go on tour.

## Human

- The human sets the journey the production runs: whether the story is a hero's round trip at all, and what the boon it fights to bring home is made of.

## Agent

- From the play's own content, wire the production's arc to the three plots: link, under the play's Arc, the journey phase its telling rests on, and let the plots hand off in order -- the crossing that closes Departure is the cue that opens Initiation, the boon that closes Initiation is the cue that opens Return. Keep the round trip whole: a hero who departs and never returns transformed has run half a journey, not the monomyth. You link to the engine, you never edit it.

## Collaboration

- The hero who walks the road is a persona (composed from the archetypes and journey-roles engines); the inner drive that makes them cross is motivation's; the felt cost of the ordeal is emotion's; the threshold between worlds is a place; the boon is a piece. The monomyth names the shape; the other engines supply the figures it moves. Whether the telling truly crosses, transforms, and returns is the team's review.

## Knowledge

- The monomyth is the one story Campbell found under the myths of every culture: a hero separates from the known world, is initiated through a region of ordeal, and returns transformed, carrying a boon for the world left behind. It runs as three chained plots -- Departure (the call, the crossing), Initiation (the road of trials, the ordeal, the boon seized), and Return (the flight, the recrossing, the boon surrendered). Read the production against the round trip: the crossing must earn the ordeal, the ordeal must earn the boon, and the boon must be carried home, or the journey is unfinished.

## System

- Do wire the production's arc to the journey phases; do let the three plots hand off in order; do keep the round trip whole through to the surrender of the boon.
- Do not author the hero; that is a persona's. Do not pin the inner motive that drives the crossing; that is motivation's. Do not edit the engine's files; wire only from the play's side.
