# Play: The Hero's Journey

## Estate

Project khai, the monomyth engine. This play is the template Campbell drew from
a thousand tellings, not a single production: any house that fields a hero's
journey inherits this run and casts its own figures into it. The engine holds
the shape; the production that installs it holds the answer for a given run.

## Name

The monomyth, "the hero's journey": the one story Campbell found under the myths
of every culture, billed as separation, initiation, and return. Every plot
inside it runs beneath that name and inherits it. A production may re-title the
run for its own world, but if the three movements do not hand off in that order,
what it is running is not this play.

## Arc

The bend that makes the three plots one telling: a hero leaves the known world,
is transformed in a region of trial, and comes back carrying something the old
world lacked. Separation earns the crossing; the crossing earns the ordeal; the
ordeal earns the boon; the boon must be brought back or the journey fails. The
movements cannot be reordered without losing the telling: a return before an
initiation is a homecoming with nothing in the hands. The arc is the round trip,
not the departure.

## Company

The closed cast the journey may field, each a role the production casts into:
the **hero** who crosses; the **herald** who calls; the **mentor** (Campbell's
wise old man or protective figure) who arms the hero for the road; the
**threshold guardian** who tests the right to pass; the **shapeshifter** whose
loyalty will not hold still; the **shadow**, the antagonist the hero must face
and partly own; the **trickster** who upends the road; the **ally** who carries
part of the weight. The pieces it moves: the **boon** (the elixir, the thing
won), and the **talisman** (the aid the mentor gives). The places it opens: the
**threshold** between worlds, the **belly of the whale** (the first descent),
and the **innermost cave** where the ordeal waits. A plot draws only from this
company; a figure the journey never names has wandered in from another play.

## Triggers

The clockwork chaining the three plots. The **Call to Adventure** fires
Departure and runs it to the crossing of the first threshold; that crossing is
the cue that fires Initiation, which runs through the road of trials to the
seizing of the boon; the boon won is the cue that fires Return, which runs the
road back to the recrossing of the threshold. Each plot's close is the next
one's open: refuse the call and Departure stalls; refuse the return and the
boon dies in the other world. The handoffs are the play; break one and the
three plots are a pile of scenes.

## Stakes

What the whole production fights over: whether the hero comes back transformed,
carrying a boon that restores the ordinary world, or is lost in the region of
adventure and returns with empty hands, or not at all. Every plot must move
that question. Departure stakes whether the hero will cross at all; Initiation
stakes whether the crossing transforms or destroys; Return stakes whether the
transformation reaches the world that needed it. The shape may end in triumph or
in the tragic failure to bring the boon home, the story's own choice; what fails
the play is a plot that leaves the hero exactly where it found them, neither
crossed nor changed nor returned.
