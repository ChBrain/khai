# Monomyth: Reference

## Line of Work

The monomyth as **play**: the single narrative arc Joseph Campbell drew out from
the myths, legends, and folktales of cultures across the world -- a hero
separates from the known world, is initiated through a region of ordeal, and
returns transformed, carrying a boon for the world left behind. The domain does
not model a character's inner motive (that is motivation's read) or a scene's
local collision (that is a plot's own work); it models the whole run, the shape
that makes a sequence of plots one journey and not a list of adventures.

The spine is Campbell's _The Hero with a Thousand Faces_: the "nuclear unit of
the monomyth" is separation, initiation, return, and the seventeen stages he
groups beneath those three movements. The play names the round trip and chains
its three phases so that the crossing earns the ordeal and the ordeal earns the
boon that must be brought home.

## Origin

Comparative mythology and depth psychology, applied directly as a play arc and
its chained plots: Campbell's monomyth, standing on Jung's archetypes and van
Gennep's rites of passage.

| Source                | Key Work                                               | Scope                                                                                                          |
| :-------------------- | :----------------------------------------------------- | :------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Joseph Campbell**   | _The Hero with a Thousand Faces_ (1949)                | The monomyth: separation, initiation, return, and the seventeen stages that compose the hero's journey.        |
| **Arnold van Gennep** | _The Rites of Passage_ (1909)                          | The tripartite structure of passage -- separation, liminal margin, reincorporation -- Campbell's arc rests on. |
| **Carl G. Jung**      | _The Archetypes and the Collective Unconscious_ (1959) | The archetypal figures (hero, shadow, wise old man) the journey casts and the transformation it dramatizes.    |

## Restrictions

What the engine refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **The specific hero**: The engine ships the journey's shape, not a named
  protagonist. A hero with a face, a history, and a wound is a persona composed
  in the consuming production; the monomyth supplies the road they walk, not who
  they are.
- **The archetypal cast**: The engine names the roles the journey may field
  (hero, herald, mentor, shadow), but the personas that fill them are the
  archetypes and journey-roles engines' and the production's. The monomyth
  deploys the seats; it does not author who sits in them.
- **The single scene**: The engine models the arc across the three plots, not
  the beat-by-beat of any one scene. How a given trial plays moment to moment is
  that plot's own work; the monomyth names which movement the trial belongs to.
- **The cultural skin**: Which trials a particular myth stages, and what the boon
  is made of, is the consuming culture's. The engine ships the universal round
  trip; the culture supplies the local dragon and the local elixir.

## Encoding

Source to constraint. A play anchor (the journey) with three plots (its
movements), chained so each hands off to the next.

- **[monomyth](play_monomyth.md)** (the anchor): the hero's journey as a whole
  run -- separation, initiation, return -- the round trip that makes the plots
  one telling. Anchored by Campbell (the nuclear unit of the monomyth).
- **[departure](plot_departure.md)**: the separation phase -- the call, the
  refusal, the mentor's aid, and the crossing of the first threshold into the
  belly of the whale. Anchored by Campbell (Departure) on van Gennep
  (separation).
- **[initiation](plot_initiation.md)**: the ordeal phase -- the road of trials,
  the innermost cave, the atonement and apotheosis, and the seizing of the
  ultimate boon. Anchored by Campbell (Initiation) on van Gennep (the liminal
  margin).
- **[return](plot_return.md)**: the homecoming phase -- the flight, the rescue
  from without, the recrossing of the threshold, and the surrender of the boon
  as master of two worlds. Anchored by Campbell (Return) on van Gennep
  (reincorporation).
