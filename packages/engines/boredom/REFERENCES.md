# Boredom: Reference

## Line of Work

Boredom as **process**: the aversive state of an unengaged mind, in which mobilized attention seeks a target the situation will not supply. The domain models four resolutions of the same gap -- indifferent (calm, untroubled withdrawal), searching (restless casting about for a target), reactant (agitation that blames the situation and pushes to escape it), and apathetic (depleted boredom that no longer expects relief) -- and what each demands of the persona moving through it.

The spine is the attention model of boredom: boredom is the experience of wanting, but being unable, to engage in satisfying activity. Attention is the mechanism -- the bored person has attentional resources to spend and cannot fix them on anything the situation offers, so the resources go unspent and the unspent capacity is felt as the discomfort. The four types are not degrees of one feeling but distinct states of arousal and agency: the same unengaged mind resolves calmly, restlessly, angrily, or hopelessly. Boredom is read here as a regulatory signal, not a defect -- it reports that attention is misallocated and presses the persona to spend it elsewhere.

## Restrictions

What the engine refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **The resting appetite for the unknown**: The engine models the failure to engage a present target, not the standing hunger to seek novelty and information. The disposition that reaches toward the unfamiliar belongs to the **curiosity** engine.
- **The allocation of the attentional spotlight**: Boredom is the felt state of attention left unspent; the mechanics of where attention points, how it is captured, and how it is held belong to the **attention** engine.
- **The absorbed state boredom is the absence of**: Engagement, immersion, the loss of self-consciousness in an activity matched to skill -- the state on the far side of boredom -- belongs to the **play-mode** engine.
- **What moves the persona to act on the signal**: Boredom presses for resolution, but the drive that converts the press into pursuit -- the seeking of a better target or an exit -- belongs to the **motivation** engine.

## Encoding

Source to constraint, per file.

- **[boredom](process_boredom.md)** (the root): Boredom as process: the unengaged mind whose mobilized attention finds no target. Anchored by Eastwood, Frischen, Fenske & Smilek (2012) -- boredom as wanting but being unable to engage; unspent attention as the discomfort.
- **[indifferent](process_indifferent.md)** (type): Calm, low-arousal withdrawal; unengaged but untroubled. Anchored by Goetz et al. (2014) -- the indifferent type, low arousal and relatively positive valence; the checked-out floor of boredom.
- **[searching](process_searching.md)** (type): Restless casting about for a target; the agentic search for engagement. Anchored by Goetz et al. (2014) -- the searching type, higher arousal and the active pursuit of alternatives; Elpidorou (2014) -- boredom as a signal that motivates the search for engagement.
- **[reactant](process_reactant.md)** (type): Agitation that blames the situation and pushes to escape it. Anchored by Goetz et al. (2014) -- the reactant type, high arousal and negative valence, oriented toward leaving the aversive situation.
- **[apathetic](process_apathetic.md)** (type): Depleted, helpless boredom that no longer expects relief. Anchored by Goetz et al. (2014) -- the apathetic type, low arousal and strongly negative valence; Fahlman, Mercer-Lynn et al. (2013) -- disengagement and low-arousal aversion as a measured dimension of state boredom.
