# Instructions: Boredom

How a Playwright wires the boredom engine into a play. You wire by linking the
play's own content to the engine's files; the engine files stay untouched, they
are canon. The Roadie plumbs the engine's law into Instructions Knowledge on
deploy. Authoring guidance, not runtime content, and it does not go on tour.

## Human

- The human defines the unengaging situation: what the persona is held in, what they want to engage and cannot, whether escape is available, and how much the wish to be occupied still presses. The human also sets the persona's arousal -- calm, restless, agitated, or depleted.

## Agent

- From the play's own content, a persona links the boredom type the moment runs (indifferent, searching, reactant, or apathetic) under its Projection. Let the type shift as arousal rises, as the search succeeds or fails, or as escape opens or closes. You link to the engine, you never edit it.

## Collaboration

- The curiosity engine holds the persona's standing appetite for the unknown, which shapes how readily boredom resolves into a search. The attention engine holds where the spotlight points and what captures it. The play-mode engine holds the absorbed state that ends boredom when a target is finally matched to skill. The motivation engine holds the drive that converts the boredom signal into a pursuit or an exit. The emotion and mood engines hold the felt charge -- the irritation of reactance, the flatness of apathy.

## Knowledge

- Boredom is the aversive state of an unengaged mind: the persona wants to be engaged and cannot be, and the mobilized attention finds no target the situation will supply. The state resolves four ways -- indifferent (calm, untroubled withdrawal), searching (restless casting about for a target), reactant (agitation that blames the situation and pushes to escape it), and apathetic (depleted, no longer expecting relief). The types are not a sequence but four settlements of the same gap, sorted by arousal and by whether escape still seems possible.

## System

- Do let the type shift with arousal and agency; a searching persona whose looking keeps failing may collapse into apathy, and a calm indifference may sharpen into reactance when the situation comes to feel like a trap.
- Do treat boredom as a signal, not a defect; it reports that attention is unspent and points the persona toward spending it elsewhere.
- Do not collapse the four types into one flat dullness; the calm of the indifferent persona and the agitation of the reactant persona are opposite states of the same unengaged mind.
- Do not edit the engine's files; wire only from the play's side.
