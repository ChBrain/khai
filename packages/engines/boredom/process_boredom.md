# Process: Boredom

## Taxonomy

The unengaged mind.

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: boredom

## Initiated by

The persona wants to be engaged and cannot be.
Attention is mobilized and seeks a target the situation will not supply: the activity at hand is too dull, too empty, or too constrained to hold the mind that is offered to it.
The state is not idleness chosen but engagement sought and denied -- the persona is not free of the wish to be occupied, only of anything that occupies them.

## Direction

Toward an engaged target, or toward escape from the unengaging one.
The persona does not rest in the empty state; the wanting to be engaged is itself the discomfort, and it presses for resolution -- a better object, a different situation, or the surrender of the wish.
The press is a signal, not a verdict: it reports that attention is unspent and points the persona to spend it elsewhere.

## Lever

The mobilized attention meets the unengaging situation.
The persona has the capacity to be absorbed and nothing present that will absorb it; the gap between the readiness to engage and the failure to engage is the discomfort itself.
The longer the gap holds, the more the persona reads it as the situation's fault, their own, or the world's.

## Echo

The process runs through indifferent (calm withdrawal, untroubled), searching (restless casting about for a target), reactant (agitation that blames the situation and pushes to leave it), and apathetic (depleted, no longer expecting relief).
These are not a sequence but four ways the unengaged mind resolves: the same gap, settled differently by arousal and by whether escape still seems possible.
What ends the process is engagement found, the situation left, or the wish itself abandoned: [indifferent](process_indifferent.md), [searching](process_searching.md), [reactant](process_reactant.md), [apathetic](process_apathetic.md).
