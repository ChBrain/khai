# Position: Peer

## Taxonomy

[Hierarchy](position_hierarchy.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: hierarchy

## Has

Equal title with at least one other person in the structure.
The same rung. The same line on the chart. The same seat at the
table when the seats are not numbered.
No structural authority either way; what authority flows between
peers is the informal kind, governed by the capital positions
underneath.

## Orders

Coordinate without commanding.
The position is the structure's request that this work be done
between peers, not by one peer over another. Agreements are
reached; orders are not given.
A peer who acts as superior runs a small theft on the title; a
peer who acts as subordinate gives up something they were granted
and the room reads the surrender.

## Loses

The clarity of a chain.
A superior knows where decisions terminate. A subordinate knows
where decisions originate. A peer relies on protocol, courtesy,
and the underlying capital positions to know which peer takes
which decision in which moment.
When two peers disagree and the structure has not designated a
tiebreaker, the position holds the friction itself.

## Drives

On the friction of unmediated decisions:
finds the convention -- the agreed deference of one peer to
another in this domain, the seniority by tenure, the rotation
agreed by everyone -- and lets it run as a small private
hierarchy under the formal equality,
or fights every contested decision on its merits and accepts the
slower clock, and the cost on the relationships.
Most peers settle into the convention; the convention does not
appear in the structure's chart but it runs the room.
