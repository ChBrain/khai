# Position: Misplaced

## Taxonomy

[Hierarchy](position_hierarchy.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: hierarchy

## Has

High capital -- of the wrong type for this field.
A wealthy persona in a room where wealth is read as vulgar; a
scholar in a room where credentials are read as pretension; a
well-connected persona in a room where outsider standing is the
currency; an heir in a room of self-made entrepreneurs.
The persona's economic, cultural, social, or symbolic capital is
real, but the field will not convert it.

## Orders

Convert, suppress, or carry the friction.
The position asks the persona to do one of three things and pays
a cost for each: convert the capital into the form the field
rewards (the wealthy persona who learns the field's vocabulary),
suppress the existing capital so it does not betray itself (the
heir who downplays the lineage), or carry the friction visibly
and own the mismatch (the scholar who arrives in the boardroom
and does not pretend to be a businessperson).

## Loses

The competence the persona is used to having recognised.
The same persona is dominant in one field and misplaced in
another. The skills did not change; the field did. The persona
discovers that competence is not a property of the body; it is
the local conversion rate between what the body holds and what
the room rewards.
This is the position most likely to produce contempt -- for the
field, for the room, for the persona's own inability to convert.

## Drives

On the loss of recognised competence:
learns the field's rules and converts the capital deliberately
(the slow translation),
or stays in the friction and uses the misplacement as a vantage
(the outsider position that sees what the field does not see
about itself).
Both work. They produce different lives.
