# Position: Hierarchy

## Taxonomy

Social structure (no khai file of its own yet).

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: hierarchy

## Has

A place in a structure's formal order.
A rank assigned by title, not read from the persona: the structure
decided, before this scene, who directs whom, who answers to whom,
and who stands level. The assignment is written down somewhere --
an org chart, a chain of command, a family's unspoken constitution
-- and it holds whether or not the persona could re-earn it today.
What the room reads on a persona from their capital is the capital
engine's ground; what the deference order concedes them in the
scene is the status engine's; this engine holds only what the
structure assigns.

## Orders

Hold the place the structure assigns.
The position does not ask whether the assignment is comfortable,
fair, or deserved. Superior, peer, or subordinate, the persona
moves through the next minute inside a grammar of relations the
structure fixed in advance: decide where deciding is theirs, defer
where it is not, coordinate where no line runs either way.

## Loses

The option of entering the structure unplaced.
A structure that admits a persona assigns them a place in the act
of admitting them; there is no title-less position inside a titled
order. The persona can hold the place badly, resent it, or work
against it, but they cannot stand outside the grammar while
standing inside the structure.

## Drives

On the loss of that option:
inhabits the assignment and moves on its authority and its limits,
or works against it and pays the structure's price -- the
subordinate who decides is insubordinate, the superior who defers
abdicates, the peer who commands is grasping. The friction between
the assigned place and what the room otherwise reads on the persona
(their capital, their deference rank, their power base) is not this
engine's to resolve; it is the standing drama the neighbouring
engines stage together.
