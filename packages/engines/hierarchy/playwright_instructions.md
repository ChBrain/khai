# Instructions: Hierarchy

How a Playwright wires the hierarchy engine into a play. You wire by linking the
play's own content to the engine's files; the engine files stay untouched, they
are canon. The Roadie plumbs the engine's law into Instructions Knowledge on
deploy. Authoring guidance, not runtime content, and it does not go on tour.

## Human

- The human sets the structures: the org, the chain of command, the family
  constitution -- whatever formal orders the play's personas stand inside.

## Agent

- From the play's own content, place each persona who stands inside a
  structure: link the title position the structure assigns (superior, peer,
  subordinate) under Projection. The assignment is per structure, so the same
  persona is superior in one order and subordinate in the next; cast it per
  structure, and let promotion or demotion be the play's arc, not a recast
  mid-scene. A persona outside every structure carries no title position,
  which is not a fault. You link to the engine, you never edit it.

## Collaboration

- The rest of standing is the neighbouring engines': what the room reads
  from the persona's capital is capital's, the scene's deference order is
  status's, the basis on which the title is obeyed is power's, the structure
  that issues the titles is org's, and the felt sting or lift of being
  placed is emotion's. The drama this engine feeds is the disagreement --
  the titled boss nobody defers to, the subordinate the room orients to --
  and staging that disagreement is the team's review.

## Knowledge

- Hierarchy is the assigned place: a rank the structure fixed before the
  scene -- who directs whom, who answers to whom, who stands level -- held
  whether or not the persona could re-earn it today. It is structure-
  relative and slow: the assignment does not move because a scene went well,
  and that inertia is its dramatic value. Read the title on the persona per
  structure, and read everything else standing is made of -- the room's
  read, the deference order, the power base -- from the engines that own it.

## System

- Do anchor the law under Instructions Knowledge; do place every persona
  who stands inside a structure; do stage the friction between the assigned
  place and what the room otherwise reads.
- Do not use a title position for the room's read of capital or for the
  deference order; those are capital's and status's. Do not author the
  structure itself; that is org's and the play's. Do not edit the engine's
  files; wire only from the play's side.
