# Position: Competitive

## Taxonomy

[Play Mode](position_play_mode.md), the expression in which the persona plays against another player or a standard. Competitive play is structured: the rules specify what counts as winning, the contest is real within the circle, and the stakes -- though bounded -- are genuine.

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: play-mode

## Has

The persona holds structured opposition: engagement with an adversary (another player, a clock, a score to beat) that the rules of the game define. What Huizinga identifies in contest play is the seriousness inside the circle: the competition is not pretend, but it is bounded. The persona has a real desire to win and a real opponent; the circle holds both. What the persona has is caring about the outcome within the frame.

## Orders

Play to win within the rules. Competitive play demands that the persona pursue the win the game defines, not a substitute. Cheating exits the mode: the cheat no longer plays the game, only wears the appearance of it. The order is: take the contest seriously on its terms -- the opposition, the rules, the score -- and not on any terms outside the circle.

## Loses

The willingness to lose. Competitive play requires acceptance that the outcome is uncertain and that losing is possible within the frame. The persona who cannot lose (because they will not accept the result, or because the contest is rigged) has left the mode. Loss of genuine uncertainty -- through cheating, through superior force, through rigged conditions -- extinguishes the play.

## Drives

Toward winning, and through it toward the quality of the contest. The competitive persona is driven by the desire to outperform the adversary or the standard, and sustains the drive not just from the outcome but from the quality of the contest itself -- the back-and-forth, the close call, the narrow margin. A victory without contest is satisfying in a thinner way.
