# Instructions: Play Mode

How a Playwright wires the play-mode engine into a play. You wire by linking the
play's own content to the engine's files; the engine files stay untouched, they
are canon. The Roadie plumbs the engine's law into Instructions Knowledge on
deploy. Authoring guidance, not runtime content, and it does not go on tour.

## Human

- The human names the activity the persona is playing at, names the magic circle that separates it from ordinary life, and selects which expression the persona is in: immersed, exploratory, competitive, or pretend. The human also identifies what the persona risks or stakes inside the circle and what would break the frame.

## Agent

- From the play's own content, a persona links the play-mode expression to its Projection. The expression is the mode the persona is in, not what the persona produces or performs. You link to the engine, you never edit it.

## Collaboration

- The emotion and mood engines hold the persona's felt experience inside the play space. The motivation engine holds why the persona plays and what sustains engagement. The attention engine holds the direction and quality of focus within the mode. The role engine holds how the persona's roles outside the circle interact with the play frame -- whether the role permits or inhibits the mode.

## Knowledge

- Play mode is the quality of engagement a persona holds when they treat an activity as play, not as work, duty, or performance. The magic circle -- the boundary Huizinga names -- is what makes the mode possible: inside, different rules apply; outside, the activity is something else. The engine models four expressions: immersed (the persona is fully absorbed; attention is entirely occupied by the activity; self-consciousness and clock-time recede), exploratory (the persona follows curiosity without a fixed destination; the space is not yet charted and the interest is in the charting), competitive (the persona plays against another player or a standard; the contest is real within the circle; the rules specify what winning means), and pretend (the persona operates within a fictional frame; the as-if is held consciously and seriously; both the fiction and the known reality are present without contradiction).

## System

- Do not model play as a performance of play. The mode is in the quality of the engagement, not in what the activity looks like from outside. A persona who is watched and is performing playfulness is not in play mode.
- Do not conflate the magic circle with physical space. The circle is the agreement that this activity is play; it can be dissolved by a single utterance that treats it as something else.
- Do not model play as unserious. The competitive persona cares about winning; the pretend persona honours the fictional frame; the immersed persona attends to the activity without reserve. Seriousness inside the circle is the condition of the mode, not its absence.
- Do not edit the engine's files; wire only from the play's side.
