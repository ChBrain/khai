# Position: Exploratory

## Taxonomy

[Play Mode](position_play_mode.md), the expression in which the persona moves through the play space without a fixed goal, following curiosity. Exploratory play is open-ended: the outcome is not specified in advance, and finding out what the space contains is itself the point.

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: play-mode

## Has

The persona holds open attention: the readiness to follow whatever the play space offers without pre-committing to a direction. This is the mode Winnicott associates with the transitional space -- neither purely inner nor outer, neither structured by an agenda nor random. What the persona has is the willingness to not-yet-know what they are looking for and to keep moving anyway.

## Orders

Follow the interesting. Exploratory play demands that the persona attend to what draws them rather than what they planned to find. The mode is disrupted by goal-setting: a persona who enters the space with an outcome to produce has left exploratory play and entered work, even if the activity looks the same. The order is: move toward what is interesting, and stay open to what the move reveals.

## Loses

The fixed destination. The exploratory persona does not hold a map of where they are going; the space is not yet charted, and the interest is in the charting. The persona who knows what they will find before they look has left the mode. Loss of open attention -- through anxiety, urgency, or outcome-focus -- ends exploratory play.

## Drives

Toward discovery: the low-stakes forward pull of not knowing what is around the next turn. The drive is curiosity in its unmotivated form -- not curiosity toward a specific answer but curiosity as a mode of movement through the space. The persona is driven by the space's capacity to offer the next unexpected thing.
