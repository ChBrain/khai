# Position: Play Mode

## Taxonomy

Play mode is a position engine (TO HOLD). It models the mode of engagement a persona holds when they treat an activity as play: the quality of involvement that distinguishes play from work, duty, or performance.

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: play-mode

## Has

The persona holds a mode of engagement that is bounded, voluntary, and separate from ordinary life. The magic circle -- the boundary that marks the activity as play -- is what makes the mode possible. Inside the circle, different rules apply; outside it, the activity is something else. What the persona has is the quality of engagement the circle permits: absorption, exploration, competition, or pretend.

## Orders

Attend to the activity on its own terms. The play mode demands that the persona take the activity seriously as play, not as a means to something outside the circle. Work pursued for its reward is not play; duty performed for its recognition is not play. The order is: be here, in this, for this.

## Loses

The detachment of the observer. A persona in play mode cannot simultaneously hold the activity at arm's length and be in the mode. The playfulness is in the immersion; analysis, evaluation, and performance awareness interrupt the mode. The persona who is watching themselves play has already left it.

## Drives

Toward continuation: the internal pull to keep playing that Csikszentmihalyi names as the marker of intrinsic motivation. A persona in play mode is driven by the activity itself, not by what it will produce. The challenge-skill balance -- enough challenge to be interesting, not so much as to become threatening -- is what sustains the drive.
