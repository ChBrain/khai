# Position: Pretend

## Taxonomy

[Play Mode](position_play_mode.md), the expression in which the persona operates within a fictional frame. Pretend play is as-if: the persona acts as a character, or treats an object as something it is not, or inhabits a situation that is known to be invented. The fiction is held consciously and seriously.

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: play-mode

## Has

The persona holds a double frame: the known reality and the fictional overlay that the play imposes on it. Winnicott's transitional space names this exactly -- the child knows the bear is not alive and treats it as alive without contradiction. What the persona has is the capacity to operate inside the fiction while remaining aware that it is fiction. Both frames are present; neither cancels the other.

## Orders

Honour the fictional frame. Pretend play demands that the persona act from inside the as-if rather than from outside it. Breaking frame -- commenting on the pretend rather than operating within it -- exits the mode. The order is: hold the fiction as the operating reality for the duration, and treat what the fiction names as real within the circle.

## Loses

The double frame. Pretend play collapses when the persona can no longer hold the fictional layer alongside the known reality -- either because the fiction becomes too absorbing (losing the distance) or because the known reality intrudes and the fiction cannot be sustained. The mode also ends when the persona is unwilling to commit to the as-if: half-hearted pretend is not pretend, only the mime of it.

## Drives

Toward the coherence of the fictional world: the internal pull to develop, inhabit, and extend the as-if without breaking it. The pretend persona is driven by the logic of the fiction itself -- what the character would do, what the world contains, what follows from the premise. The drive is to stay in character and to let the fiction be consequential while it lasts.
