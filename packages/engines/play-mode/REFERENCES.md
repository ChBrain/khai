# Play Mode: Reference

## Line of Work

Play mode as **position**: the mode of engagement a persona holds when they treat an activity as play. The domain models the anchor (what play mode is; what it has, orders, loses, and drives) and four expressions -- immersed (full absorption; the flow channel), exploratory (open attention; discovery without a destination), competitive (structured opposition within a circle), and pretend (fictional frame; the as-if that is held seriously) -- and what each mode enables and forecloses.

The spine is Huizinga's concept of play as a free, separate, uncertain, unproductive, rule-governed, make-believe activity bounded by a magic circle that marks it off from ordinary life. Winnicott extends this to the transitional space -- the domain between inner and outer reality where play occurs and where the capacity to be alone, to create, and to be absorbed originates. Csikszentmihalyi supplies the experiential account of immersed play through the flow construct: the challenge-skill balance, the absorption that drops self-consciousness, the altered experience of time, and the intrinsic motivation that makes the activity self-sustaining.

## Origin

Play theory, developmental psychology, and positive psychology.

## Restrictions

What the engine refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **The persona's felt experience inside the play space**: Whether the persona is enjoying themselves, what they feel during the activity, belongs to the **emotion** and **mood** engines. Play mode models the quality of engagement, not the affective content of the experience.
- **Why the persona plays**: The motivation to engage in the first place -- what draws the persona to play over other activities -- belongs to the **motivation** engine. Play mode models the mode once the persona is in it, not the decision to enter.
- **What the persona attends to within the mode**: The specific direction and quality of focus belongs to the **attention** engine. Play mode establishes the frame of engagement; attention operates within it.
- **The social and cultural conditions that produce or prohibit play**: Whether a persona's role, context, or situation permits them to play belongs to the **role** and **power** engines. Play mode models the mode itself, not its conditions of possibility.

## Encoding

Source to constraint, per file.

- **[play-mode](position_play_mode.md)** (the anchor): The mode of engagement as a position: what it has, orders, loses, and drives. Anchored by Huizinga (1938) -- the magic circle and the separateness of play; Winnicott (1971) -- play as serious; Csikszentmihalyi (1990) -- intrinsic motivation and continuation.
- **[immersed](position_immersed.md)** (expression): Full absorption; self-consciousness and clock-time recede; the activity fills the field of attention without remainder. Anchored by Csikszentmihalyi (1990) -- the flow state; challenge-skill balance; the forward pull toward the next challenge.
- **[exploratory](position_exploratory.md)** (expression): Open attention; outcome unspecified in advance; the persona follows curiosity without a fixed destination. Anchored by Winnicott (1971) -- the transitional space as the domain of open, purposeless play.
- **[competitive](position_competitive.md)** (expression): Structured opposition; rules specify what winning means; the contest is real within the circle. Anchored by Huizinga (1938) -- contest play as one of the primary forms; Caillois (1958) -- agon as a category of play.
- **[pretend](position_pretend.md)** (expression): As-if mode; fictional frame held consciously and seriously; both the fiction and known reality are present without contradiction. Anchored by Winnicott (1971) -- the double frame of transitional phenomena; Caillois (1958) -- mimicry as a category of play.
