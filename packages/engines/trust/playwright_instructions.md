# Instructions: Trust

How a Playwright wires the trust engine into a play. You wire by linking the
play's own content to the engine's files; the engine files stay untouched, they
are canon. The Roadie plumbs the engine's law into Instructions Knowledge on
deploy. Authoring guidance, not runtime content, and it does not go on tour.

## Human

- The human sets the stake the persona has exposed and the other who could harm or protect it.

## Agent

- From the play's own content, a persona links the trust move it runs toward another (extend, test, withhold, betrayed, or repair) under its Projection, and lets the read update as the other acts across the run. You link to the engine, you never edit it.

## Collaboration

- The felt sting of being let down is the emotion engine's; the alarm-driven reach for a secure base is attachment's (trust is the calibrated bet, not the alarm); who the other is and the live tie between them are the scene's. Whether the bet rings true is the team's review.

## Knowledge

- Trust is a bet on another: a stake sits where another could harm or protect it and the persona cannot control them, so it hands the stake over or holds it back. The read that sets the bet has three faces, the other's ability, their benevolence, and their integrity, and the bet runs as a move (extend, test, withhold, betrayed, repair). As the other acts again, the read updates and the bet is placed anew: it lives over the relationship, not in a single beat.

## System

- Do tie the move to a specific other; do let the read update as that other acts, over the run, so the bet moves with the relationship.
- Do not fix it as a static label; the bet is re-placed on the evidence of conduct. Do not model the other or the live tie here; that is the scene's. Do not edit the engine's files; wire only from the play's side.
