# Trust: Reference

## Line of Work

Trust as **process**: the persona's bet on another's reliability, placed by accepting vulnerability to someone whose conduct cannot be controlled, then tested and updated by what that other does. The domain does not model the survival-driven bond to a secure base, the disposition to trust, or the feeling of being let down; it models the live, calibrated, cognitive-relational bet -- the move by which a persona hands a stake to another, or holds it back, and revises the read on the evidence of conduct.

The spine is the trust-as-vulnerability tradition: to trust is the willingness to be vulnerable to another's actions on the expectation that they will not exploit it. The read that sets the bet has three faces -- the other's ability to deliver, their benevolence toward the persona, and their integrity under pressure -- and the bet runs as a process: extended ahead of proof, probed in miniature, withheld behind a guard, broken by default, and re-earned through asymmetric, effortful repair. The engine is dyadic: it models the persona's stance toward another, not the other or the live relationship, which belong to the scene.

## Origin

The organizational and social-psychological trust literature, applied directly as process constraints.

| Source                               | Key Work                                                                | Scope                                                                                                      |
| :----------------------------------- | :---------------------------------------------------------------------- | :--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Mayer, Davis & Schoorman**         | _"An integrative model of organizational trust"_ (1995)                 | The read of the other across three faces: ability, benevolence, and integrity as the bases of trust.       |
| **Rousseau, Sitkin, Burt & Camerer** | _"Not so different after all: a cross-discipline view of trust"_ (1998) | Trust as the willingness to accept vulnerability based on positive expectations of another's intentions.   |
| **Morton Deutsch**                   | _"Trust and suspicion"_ (1958)                                          | The game-theoretic root: trust as a choice to expose oneself under uncertainty about another's response.   |
| **Lewicki & Bunker**                 | _"Developing and maintaining trust in work relationships"_ (1996)       | The development and repair of trust over time: how a broken bet is re-extended, asymmetrically and slowly. |

## Restrictions

What the engine refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **The secure-base bond**: The engine models the calibrated, cognitive-relational bet, not the survival-driven reach for a secure base under threat. The alarm-driven bond is delegated to the **attachment** engine -- attachment is the alarm-driven reach; trust is the calibrated bet on another's reliability.
- **Who is trusted**: The engine models only the persona's stance, not the specific other or the live relationship. WHO is trusted -- the particular other and the tie between them -- is delegated to the scene's personas and links; the engine is dyadic and stops at the persona's bet.
- **The feeling of betrayal**: The engine models the recalibration of the bet, not the anger and grief of being let down. The felt charge of betrayal is delegated to the **emotion** engine.
- **Structural force**: The engine models the voluntary bet under uncertainty, not the structural power that can force or guarantee compliance. Power that removes the need to trust is delegated to the **hierarchy** engine.
- **The trusting disposition**: The engine models the live bet on a particular other, not the persona's general baseline readiness to trust. That standing disposition is delegated to the **temperament** engine -- temperament is the baseline; trust is the live bet.

## Encoding

Source to constraint, per file.

- **[trust](process_trust.md)** (the root): The bet on another -- a stake sits where another could harm or protect it with no way to control them, and the persona hands it over or holds it back, the read updated by conduct. Anchored by Rousseau et al. (willingness to be vulnerable) and Mayer, Davis & Schoorman (ability, benevolence, integrity).
- **[extend](process_extend.md)** (expression): Place the bet ahead of proof -- make oneself vulnerable before the evidence is in, the leap that lets a relationship start. Anchored by Deutsch (the choice to expose oneself under uncertainty).
- **[test](process_test.md)** (expression): Give a small, recoverable stake first to read how the other handles it before risking more -- calibrated probing. Anchored by Lewicki & Bunker (trust built incrementally on tested conduct).
- **[withhold](process_withhold.md)** (expression): Refuse the bet -- keep the stake back and stay defended, paying for safety with the closeness foregone. Anchored by Deutsch (suspicion as the withheld counterpart of trust).
- **[betrayed](process_betrayed.md)** (expression): The bet was placed and the other defaulted -- the violation, the shock, the recalibration across everything they touched. Anchored by Mayer, Davis & Schoorman (the read revised when conduct violates it).
- **[repair](process_repair.md)** (expression): After a break, slow re-extension on evidence -- earning back, asymmetric and effortful, rarely all the way to the old line. Anchored by Lewicki & Bunker (the development and repair of trust over time).
