# Position: Role

## Taxonomy

Role is a position engine (TO HOLD). It models the socially sanctioned expectations that govern what a persona may do and be: the script the scene attaches to the position the persona occupies and the sanctions the scene applies when that script is violated, exceeded, or unclear.

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: role

## Has

The persona occupies a role: a socially defined position (parent, expert, manager, newcomer, caregiver, authority figure) to which the scene attaches expectations about behaviour, presentation, and priorities. Those expectations define what the persona is permitted to do, what they are required to do, what they are forbidden to do, and what penalties follow from violations. The role is held even when not explicitly named; the scene treats the persona in terms of it.

## Orders

The position demands performance of the expected script. The persona must act in ways the scene recognises as appropriate to the role: not just in formal moments but in how they enter, speak, decline, and respond to challenge. The script is not fully explicit; part of what the scene demands is that the persona know what is expected without being told.

## Loses

The position forecloses behaviour that violates the script without sanction. Every role has forbidden acts and forbidden registers; the persona who crosses them pays a cost the scene imposes. The position also forecloses behaviours associated with conflicting roles: a persona cannot easily hold two roles whose scripts are mutually exclusive.

## Drives

When the script is violated by others -- when the persona is asked to act outside their role, when the role's expectations conflict with what the situation requires, or when the persona's own behaviour strays from the script -- the persona either restores the script (corrects the violation, returns to the expected register) or negotiates a revision to the expectations (claims a different role, redefines what the current role permits).
