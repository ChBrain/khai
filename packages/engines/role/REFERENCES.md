# Role: Reference

## Line of Work

Role as **position**: the socially sanctioned expectations that govern what a persona may do and be. The domain models four expressions -- congruent (persona matches the script; sanctions are low), incongruent (persona violates the script; sanctions are active), overloaded (demands exceed capacity or conflict with each other), and ambiguous (expectations are unclear or contested) -- and what each demands of the persona who holds it.

The spine is Merton's role theory: the role as a set of expectations attached to a position, and the role set as the cluster of relationships from which those expectations flow. Eagly's role congruity theory extends this by specifying the conditions under which incongruence produces prescriptive penalties: when descriptive expectations (what the role incumbent typically does) diverge from what the incumbent actually does, the scene applies correction, and when prescriptive expectations (what the incumbent should do) are violated, the penalties are heavier.

## Restrictions

What the engine refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Status as deference rank**: The engine models the behavioural script attached to a position, not the persona's rank in the scene's deference order. Expectation-states and deference belong to the **status** engine.
- **Power as resource capacity**: The engine models the normative script, not the resource base from which the persona can move others. Power bases belong to the **power** engine.
- **The identity significance of the role**: The engine models the expectations attached to the role, not the degree to which the persona defines themselves through it. Role-identity and its salience belong to the **identity** engine.
- **How the role shifts across the play**: The engine models the role the persona holds at entry. How the script evolves or is renegotiated across the run belongs to the play's arc.

## Encoding

Source to constraint, per file.

- **[role](position_role.md)** (the anchor): Role as position: the socially sanctioned expectations that govern what a persona may do and be. Anchored by Merton (1968) -- role as expectations attached to a position; the role set; sanctions for violation.
- **[congruent](position_congruent.md)** (expression): The persona matches the script; sanctions are low; the persona acts from a settled relationship with their position. Anchored by Eagly & Karau (2002) -- congruence as the baseline against which violations are measured.
- **[incongruent](position_incongruent.md)** (expression): The persona violates the script; sanctions are active; the mismatch is noticed and responded to. Anchored by Eagly & Karau (2002) -- role congruity theory; prescriptive and descriptive penalties for incongruence.
- **[overloaded](position_overloaded.md)** (expression): Demands exceed capacity or conflict with each other; the role is held under pressure. Anchored by Kahn et al. (1964) -- role conflict and role strain; Merton (1968) -- competing demands from different parties in the role set.
- **[ambiguous](position_ambiguous.md)** (expression): Expectations are unclear or contested; the persona performs without a reliable script. Anchored by Kahn et al. (1964) -- role ambiguity as a distinct condition from role conflict.
