# Position: Overloaded Role

## Taxonomy

Overloaded role is an expression of the role engine. The persona holds expectations from the role that exceed their capacity or that conflict with each other; the position is held but under pressure that the script was not designed to absorb.

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: role

## Has

The persona occupies a role -- or more than one -- whose expectations collectively exceed what can be met without cost. The overload may come from volume (too many demands within a single role), from conflict (two roles whose scripts pull in opposite directions), or from the gap between what the script requires and the resources available to meet it. The persona is still trying to hold the role; they are not yet failing it, but the margin is narrow.

## Orders

The position demands constant triage: the persona must decide, often without the time or information to decide well, which demands to meet, which to defer, and which to let fail silently. The triage itself must often be performed invisibly; the scene that imposed the expectations may not recognise the overload as a legitimate condition.

## Loses

The position forecloses full performance of any single demand. A persona under role overload cannot give the script its due at every point; something is always compromised. It also forecloses the ease of refusal: the very fact of holding the role, or the roles, imposes obligations that cannot simply be declined without cost to standing.

## Drives

When the overload produces visible failure -- a demand unmet, a script violated not by choice but by incapacity -- the persona either makes the overload explicit (claims that the demands are irreconcilable), absorbs the failure and compensates later, or negotiates a reduction in the scope of what the role requires.
