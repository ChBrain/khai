# Instructions: Role

How a Playwright wires the role engine into a play. You wire by linking the
play's own content to the engine's files; the engine files stay untouched, they
are canon. The Roadie plumbs the engine's law into Instructions Knowledge on
deploy. Authoring guidance, not runtime content, and it does not go on tour.

## Human

- The human sets the role the persona occupies and the expectations the scene attaches to it: what the persona is required to do, what they are forbidden to do, and what penalties follow from violation. They also set the sources of any ambiguity or conflict in the role expectations.

## Agent

- From the play's own content, each persona links under Projection the role expression it holds: congruent, incongruent, overloaded, or ambiguous. Show the expression in what the persona attends to, what they must manage, and what costs they pay or avoid -- not in explicit statements about roles or expectations.

## Collaboration

- The status engine holds the deference order; a persona can hold a congruent role at low status, or an incongruent role at high status. The power engine holds resource capacity; a persona with legitimate power still holds a role whose script may fit or fail. The identity engine holds how much the persona defines themselves through the role.

## Knowledge

- Role is the bundle of socially sanctioned expectations attached to the position a persona occupies. The expectations define what the persona is permitted to do, required to do, and forbidden to do, and what penalties follow from violation. Congruent means the persona matches the script; sanctions are low. Incongruent means the persona violates the script; sanctions are active. Overloaded means the demands exceed the persona's capacity or conflict with each other. Ambiguous means the expectations are unclear or contested; the persona performs without a reliable score.

## System

- Do read the role expression in what the persona must manage, what they are scrutinised for, and what they cannot easily do -- not in statements about their role or its expectations.
- Do not collapse role into status: holding a role congruently is separate from rank in the deference order.
- Do not collapse role into identity: how much the persona defines themselves through the role belongs to the identity engine, not here.
- Do not model the emotional register of role pressure here; that belongs to the emotion and mood engines.
