# Position: Congruent Role

## Taxonomy

Congruent role is an expression of the role engine. The persona matches the scene's expectations for their role; the script fits, sanctions are low, and the persona acts from a settled relationship with the position they occupy.

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: role

## Has

The persona occupies a role whose expectations they meet: their behaviour, presentation, and priorities align with what the scene treats as appropriate. The fit is not effortless -- the persona must still perform the role -- but the performance is not contested; what they do and what the scene expects are in correspondence.

## Orders

The position demands continued performance of a script that already works. The persona is expected to act within the expectations, to model them for others who are watching, and to uphold the role's norms when they are tested. Congruence must be maintained; it is not a permanent state but a continued correspondence.

## Loses

The position forecloses explicit challenge to the role's expectations. A persona who is congruent with their role and then rejects the script is read as changing position, not as merely expressing individuality. The congruence also forecloses the licence that incongruence sometimes generates: the permission to violate expectations can only be taken by those already outside them.

## Drives

When an interaction pushes the persona toward violating the script -- through an unexpected demand, a provocative challenge, or a situation the script does not fully cover -- the persona first finds a way to respond within the expected register before considering whether the situation warrants deviation.
