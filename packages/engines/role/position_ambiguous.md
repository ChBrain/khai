# Position: Ambiguous Role

## Taxonomy

Ambiguous role is an expression of the role engine. The persona holds a role whose expectations are unclear, contested, or inconsistently signalled; the script has not been specified, and the persona cannot know what will count as correct performance.

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: role

## Has

The persona occupies a role -- new, transitional, poorly defined, or contested by those around them -- whose expectations have not been settled. Different parties in the scene may hold different expectations for the role; the formal script and the informal one may diverge; the persona may have received contradictory signals about what is required. The persona is performing without a reliable score.

## Orders

The position demands active interpretation. The persona must infer what the scene expects -- from context, from the responses of others, from the formal definition of the role and its informal elaboration -- and act on those inferences while remaining ready to revise them. The persona cannot wait for clarity that may not come; they must perform the role while its definition remains open.

## Loses

The position forecloses confident enactment. An ambiguous persona acts under the knowledge that their performance may be wrong in ways they cannot anticipate. It also forecloses the immunity from sanction that congruence provides: without a settled script, the persona cannot know in advance which acts are protected and which are exposed.

## Drives

When the ambiguity produces a visible violation -- when the persona's interpretation of the script turns out to be wrong in ways the scene makes known -- the persona seeks clarification of the expectations (asks directly, looks for precedents, tests adjacent cases) or recalibrates the performance based on the feedback the violation produced.
