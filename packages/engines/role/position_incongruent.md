# Position: Incongruent Role

## Taxonomy

Incongruent role is an expression of the role engine. The persona violates the scene's expectations for their role; the script does not fit, and the persona pays -- or extracts -- the cost of that mismatch.

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: role

## Has

The persona occupies a role whose expectations they do not meet: their behaviour, presentation, or priorities deviate from what the scene treats as appropriate for the position they hold. The mismatch may be deliberate (the persona rejects the script) or structural (the persona's attributes do not match what the role is expected to look like). In either case, the scene notices the violation and responds.

## Orders

The position demands that the persona manage the violation. The scene will correct, dismiss, or sanction the incongruent persona; the persona must either absorb that response, justify the deviation in terms the scene can accept, or work to shift what the scene expects from the role. The violation cannot simply be ignored; the scene will make it visible.

## Loses

The position forecloses the ease of congruence. An incongruent persona must spend more effort to be credited, to have contributions accepted, and to avoid the costs the scene attaches to violations. It also forecloses simply inhabiting the role without scrutiny; the persona is watched in ways the congruent persona is not.

## Drives

When the scene corrects the violation -- through sanction, dismissal, or explicit challenge -- the persona either justifies the deviation on grounds the scene might accept (redefines what the role permits, invokes a higher norm), absorbs the correction and continues, or escalates the violation to force a renegotiation of the script itself.
