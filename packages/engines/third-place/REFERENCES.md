# Third Place: Reference

## Line of Work

Third Place as **place**: the informal public gathering place that hosts community life on neutral ground. The domain models the venue's anchor (what a third place is; what it shows, holds, offers, and withholds) and three expressions -- neutral-ground (no host, no guest, no obligation to be there), leveler (outside status and rank stop mattering), home-away (the warmth and rootedness of home, without being home) -- and what each facet makes available or withholds.

The spine is Oldenburg's sociology of informal public life. Oldenburg names the recurring, low-cost, highly accessible gathering places -- cafés, pubs, barbershops, corner stores -- that anchor community sociability outside the first place (home) and the second place (work). He identifies the defining characteristics: neutral ground where attendance is voluntary and no one is host or guest; a leveling function that admits people regardless of status outside; conversation as the main activity, playful in tone and low in profile; a regular clientele that sets the tone and gives newcomers a mood to enter; and the accumulated sense of being a home away from home. Oldenburg argues these places are not incidental to community life but load-bearing for it, and that their decline in car-dependent, zoned environments erodes the informal sociability that formal institutions cannot replace.

## Restrictions

What the engine refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **The first place and the second place themselves**: Home and work are not modeled here; this engine models only the third place that stands apart from both. What a persona owes to either belongs to the play's own content.
- **The rite that first admitted a regular**: Any formal or informal ceremony of initiation into a group of regulars belongs to the **ritual** engine. This engine models the venue and its standing offer, not the crossing into belonging.
- **Outside status itself**: What rank, title, or wealth a persona carries in from elsewhere belongs to the **status** and **hierarchy** engines. This engine models only what happens to that status once it enters the leveler.
- **Bonds between specific regulars**: The dyadic trust or attachment formed between particular people who meet here belongs to the **trust** and **attachment** engines. This engine models the place, not the relationships it hosts.
- **The persona's felt sense of warmth or belonging**: The subjective experience of comfort, ease, or restoration belongs to the **emotion** and **mood** engines. This engine models what the place offers, not what is felt on receiving it.

Source to constraint, per file.

- **[third-place](place_third_place.md)** (the anchor): The informal public gathering place as a place: what it shows, holds, offers, and withholds. Anchored by Oldenburg (1989) -- the third place construct as a whole, distinct from first and second place.
- **[neutral-ground](place_neutral_ground.md)** (expression): No host, no guest, no obligation to be there; freedom from the accounting that governs home and work. Anchored by Oldenburg (1989) -- neutral ground as the first defining characteristic.
- **[leveler](place_leveler.md)** (expression): Outside status and rank stop mattering; inclusiveness is the operating condition. Anchored by Oldenburg (1989) -- the leveler function; Oldenburg (2001) -- case studies of levelling in practice.
- **[home-away](place_home_away.md)** (expression): The warmth and rootedness of home, accumulated through repeated presence, without the burdens of an actual household. Anchored by Oldenburg (1989) -- the home-away-from-home character of the established third place.
