# Instructions: Third Place

How a Playwright wires the third-place engine into a play. You wire by linking the
play's own content to the engine's files; the engine files stay untouched, they
are canon. The Roadie plumbs the engine's law into Instructions Knowledge on
deploy. Authoring guidance, not runtime content, and it does not go on tour.

## Human

- The human defines the venue: which expression (neutral-ground, leveler, or home-away) is load-bearing for the scene, who counts as a regular, and what conversation is already underway when a persona arrives.

## Agent

- From the play's own content, a persona links the third-place expression to its Projection. The expression is the facet of the venue the scene is drawing on, not what the persona feels about being there. You link to the engine, you never edit it.

## Collaboration

- The ritual engine holds the formal rite, if any, that first admitted a persona among the regulars. The status and hierarchy engines hold the outside rank that the leveler flattens. The trust and attachment engines hold the bonds formed between specific regulars. The emotion and mood engines hold the persona's felt experience of warmth or belonging. The play's own content holds the first place (home) and second place (work) that the third place stands apart from.

## Knowledge

- Third Place is the informal public gathering place that hosts community life on neutral ground: the café, the pub, the barbershop, the corner store. It is a place: what it shows is a plain, unpretentious presence and a mood already playful before anyone arrives; what it holds is the regulars and the fixed props built for their return; what it offers is conversation as the main activity, free and unscheduled; what it withholds is standing among the regulars, released only to a persona who keeps returning. The engine models three expressions: neutral-ground (no host, no guest, no obligation to be there -- freedom from the accounting of home and work), leveler (outside status and rank stop mattering; inclusiveness is the operating condition), and home-away (the warmth and rootedness of home, accumulated through repeated presence, without the burdens of an actual household).

## System

- Do not model the first place or the second place themselves here; this engine models only the third place that stands apart from both.
- Do not model the rite that first admitted a regular; that belongs to the ritual engine. This engine models the standing offer of the venue, not the crossing into belonging.
- Do not model outside status itself, only what happens to it once it enters the leveler; the status and hierarchy engines hold the outside rank.
- Do not model what the persona feels here; that belongs to the emotion and mood engines.
- Do not edit the engine's files; wire only from the play's side.
