# Contagion: Reference

## Line of Work

Contagion as **process**: the tendency to automatically and continuously mimic and synchronize facial expressions, vocalizations, postures, and movements with another person's, and, through the afferent feedback of doing so, to converge emotionally -- to come to feel what one is now wearing. The domain does not model conforming to a group's judged standard (that is the conformity engine's), the crowd as the environment the spread occurs within (the crowd engine's), the discrete emotion being spread (the emotion engine's), or empathic understanding of another's state (the empathy engine's). It models the primitive, largely unconscious channel itself: one persona's affect moving into another's body before either judges or agrees to it.

The spine is Hatfield, Cacioppo & Rapson's theory of emotional contagion: a two-step mechanism in which motor mimicry comes first -- automatic, continuous, largely outside awareness -- and subjective convergence follows it, carried by the afferent feedback of one's own mimicked expression. Contagion is distinguished explicitly from cognitive or normative processes: it is not empathic perspective-taking, and it is not aligning with a standard the group holds up for judgment.

## Origin

The psychology and social psychology of emotional contagion, applied directly as one anchor process and its two constituent processes.

## Restrictions

What the engine refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Conformity**: The engine models an automatic, largely unconscious spread of affect, not the deliberate or semi-deliberate matching of a group's judged standard. Bringing one's display in line with what the group approves of is delegated to the **conformity engine**.
- **The crowd**: The engine models the person-to-person mechanism of the spread, not the aggregate environment in which it can cascade. The crowd as a place -- density, anonymity, the loss of individual restraint -- is delegated to the **crowd engine**.
- **The discrete emotion**: The engine models the channel by which a feeling moves from one persona to another, not which feeling it is once it has arrived. Naming the felt state -- joy, sadness, anger, fear, disgust -- is delegated to the **emotion engine**.
- **Empathy**: The engine models a primitive, bottom-up mimicry-and-feedback loop, not the cognitive, perspective-taking understanding of another's state. Deliberately imagining or reasoning about what another feels is delegated to the **empathy engine**. A persona may run both -- catch the affect and also understand it -- but the understanding is delegated.

## Encoding

Source to constraint, per file.

- **[contagion](process_contagion.md)** (the root): The channel itself -- proximity to another's displayed affect, pulling the persona's own state toward it without judgment or choice. Anchored by Hatfield, Cacioppo & Rapson (the two-step mechanism, stated whole).
- **[mimicry](process_mimicry.md)** (constituent): The automatic, continuous imitation of expression, vocalization, and posture, driven by the perception-behavior link rather than deliberate intent. Anchored by Hatfield, Cacioppo & Rapson (1994, motor mimicry as the mechanism's first step).
- **[convergence](process_convergence.md)** (constituent): The afferent feedback from the mimicked display producing or intensifying the matching subjective feeling, so the persona comes to feel what it copied. Anchored by Hatfield, Cacioppo & Rapson (1994, afferent feedback and emotional convergence as the mechanism's second step).
