# Memory: Reference

## Line of Work

Memory as **process**: how the past re-enters the present. The domain does not store the content of what happened -- that is the scene's pieces and personas; it models the act of remembering: a cue meets a trace, the persona rebuilds what was, and refiles it changed. The core claim is that memory is reconstruction, not playback -- so the past a persona carries forward is the last rebuild, not the original, and the rebuilding distorts on every pass.

The engine is original khai framing built on established memory science, not lifted from the Cultures engine catalogue. It reads the kinds of memory through the question of how the past shows up in a scene: re-lived, known, enacted, re-fired, or held forward.

## Origin

The psychology of memory, applied directly as process constraints.

| Source                | Key Work                                                             | Scope                                                                                                          |
| :-------------------- | :------------------------------------------------------------------- | :------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Frederic Bartlett** | _Remembering_ (1932)                                                 | Reconstructive memory: recall is an active rebuild shaped by present schemas, not the replay of a recording.   |
| **Endel Tulving**     | _"Episodic and Semantic Memory"_ (1972)                              | The episodic/semantic distinction: the re-lived event versus the fact stripped of its occasion.                |
| **Larry Squire**      | _Memory and Brain_ (1987)                                            | Declarative vs non-declarative memory: the procedural skill the body runs without words.                       |
| **Karim Nader**       | _"Fear memories require protein synthesis... upon retrieval"_ (2000) | Reconsolidation: a recalled memory is re-stored, and changed in the re-storing -- recall rewrites the record.  |
| **Elizabeth Loftus**  | _"Reconstruction of automobile destruction"_ (1974)                  | The misinformation effect: present cues edit the remembered past; confident memories can be confidently wrong. |

## Restrictions

What the engine refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **The present feeling**: The emotional-memory kind supplies the re-fired _past_ charge -- the body taking an old posture before the mind names a reason. What the persona actually feels _now_, as a present appraisal, is **delegated to the emotion engine**; this engine names the firing of the trace, not the emotion it becomes.
- **The autonomic surge**: Where a cue fires a full survival response rather than a remembered charge, that routing is the stress engine's. Memory re-fires the trace; stress mobilises the body.
- **Forgetting as a kind**: Loss and distortion are not a sixth member -- they are the root's Echo, inherited by every kind, because every reconstruction decays and tints. The engine models forgetting as what happens to all remembering, not as a separate operation.
- **The content remembered**: What the memory is _of_ -- the specific scene, fact, skill, or person -- is owned by the scene's pieces and personas, not the engine. Memory ships the act of recall; the scene supplies the material.
- **The neural mechanism**: The engine models the felt operation of remembering, not the biology of consolidation or the brain regions involved. The mechanism is background to the source, not content of the engine.

## Encoding

Source to constraint, per file. One process root (the reconstruction) and five kinds of remembering.

- **[memory](process_memory.md)** (the root): A cue meets a trace; the persona rebuilds what was and refiles it changed. Anchored by Bartlett (reconstruction) and Nader (reconsolidation).
- **[episodic](process_episodic.md)** (expression): The lived scene re-entered, with its when and where; the persona is there again. Anchored by Tulving (episodic memory).
- **[semantic](process_semantic.md)** (expression): The fact stripped of the scene that taught it; known without being relived. Anchored by Tulving (semantic memory).
- **[procedural](process_procedural.md)** (expression): The body running the grooved skill without words; strengthened by use, jammed by attention. Anchored by Squire (non-declarative memory).
- **[emotional](process_emotional.md)** (expression): An old charge re-firing ahead of any scene or fact; the body braces before the mind explains. Anchored by Squire and Loftus (the affective trace).
- **[prospective](process_prospective.md)** (expression): An intention held for later and fired by its cue; missed when the cue is weak. Anchored by the prospective-memory literature (Einstein & McDaniel).
