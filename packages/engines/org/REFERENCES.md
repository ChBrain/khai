# Organisational Culture: Reference

## Line of Work

Organisational culture as **process**: how an organisation operates -- the stance it takes and the processes that stance makes it reach for. The domain does not model a specific company, its people, or its history; it models the universal scaffold of organisational culture as a position on two axes, and the four operating processes the position runs. The stance colours every persona who acts inside the organisation, without belonging to any one of them.

The spine is the Competing Values Framework: two axes -- flexibility versus stability, internal versus external focus -- cut the space into four quadrants, each a coherent culture type with its own dominant process. A real organisation is a mix, but one quadrant usually leads, and the room runs on its defaults.

## Origin

Organisational psychology, applied directly as process constraints. The engine-level scaffold is Quinn & Cameron's Competing Values Framework.

| Source                | Key Work                                                                | Scope                                                                                                           |
| :-------------------- | :---------------------------------------------------------------------- | :-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Quinn & Rohrbaugh** | _"A spatial model of effectiveness criteria"_ (1983)                    | The two axes (flexibility/stability, internal/external) and the quadrant model of organisational effectiveness. |
| **Cameron & Quinn**   | _Diagnosing and Changing Organizational Culture_ (1999 / 2011, 3rd ed.) | The four culture types -- clan, adhocracy, market, hierarchy -- as the canonical Competing Values Framework.    |

## Restrictions

What the engine refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **The specific organisation**: The engine models the universal stance and its processes, not a named company, its staff, or its history. A particular organisation is the scene's, materialised in its own files, not carried in the engine.
- **Rank inside the organisation**: Where a persona stands relative to others within the structure -- superior, peer, subordinate -- is the hierarchy engine's. Org models the culture the organisation runs on; hierarchy models the persona's place within it.
- **The individual's drive**: Why a persona works inside the organisation, and how the effort is sustained, is the motivation engine's. Org sets the field; motivation runs inside it.
- **Change as arc**: A culture shifting quadrant over time belongs to the play's design; the engine reads the organisation's current stance, not its trajectory.

## Encoding

Source to constraint. One process anchor (operating) with four paired processes and four stance positions -- the Competing Values quadrants.

- **[operate org](process_operate_org.md)** (the anchor): How an organisation operates -- a stance on the two axes that sets which process it reaches for. Anchored by Quinn & Rohrbaugh (the spatial model).
- **Clan / Collaborate** -- [clan](position_clan_org.md) (internal + flexible) runs [collaborate](process_collaborate_org.md): the family stance, cohesion and mentoring.
- **Adhocracy / Create** -- [adhocracy](position_adhocracy_org.md) (external + flexible) runs [create](process_create_org.md): the innovate stance, risk and the new.
- **Market / Compete** -- [market](position_market_org.md) (external + stable) runs [compete](process_compete_org.md): the results stance, targets and winning.
- **Hierarchy / Control** -- [hierarchy](position_hierarchy_org.md) (internal + stable) runs [control](process_control_org.md): the structure stance, procedure and reliability.
