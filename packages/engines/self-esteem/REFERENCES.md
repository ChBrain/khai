# Self-Esteem: Reference

## Line of Work

Self-esteem as **position**: the persona's resting, global attitude toward
the self, positive or negative, carried into the moment rather than
assembled fresh from it. The domain does not model a task-specific belief
about one skill, nor the momentary flare of pride or shame after a single
result; it models the standing overall verdict on the self that colours how
every other verdict is read.

## Origin

The self-esteem literature, applied directly as position constraints.

| Source                                       | Key Work                                                                       | Scope                                                                                                                                                         |
| :------------------------------------------- | :----------------------------------------------------------------------------- | :------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **Morris Rosenberg**                         | _Society and the Adolescent Self-Image_ (1965)                                 | Global self-esteem as a single favorable-or-unfavorable attitude toward the self as an object; the ten-item scale as the canonical operationalization.        |
| **Romin W. Tafarodi & William B. Swann Jr.** | _"Self-liking and self-competence as dimensions of global self-esteem"_ (1995) | The two-factor view: self-liking (moral-social worth) and self-competence (perceived efficacy) as separable components that compose into the one global read. |
| **Jennifer Crocker & Connie T. Wolfe**       | _"Contingencies of self-worth"_ (2001)                                         | Esteem staked domain by domain on external validation; worth rises and falls with the outcome in the domain it is staked on.                                  |

## Restrictions

What the engine refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **The task-specific capability belief**: "Can I do this particular thing"
  is not "am I, on the whole, worth something." A persona's read on its own
  competence at a task **delegates to the self-efficacy engine**. Self-esteem
  is the standing global verdict; self-efficacy is the local one.
- **The self-story**: The narrative content of who the persona is -- the
  story told about the self, its history and its roles -- **delegates to the
  identity engine**. Self-esteem is the attitude taken toward that self, not
  the self it is an attitude about.
- **The momentary feeling**: The sharp, event-bound flare of pride after a
  win or shame after a loss **delegates to the pride and shame engines**.
  Self-esteem is the standing baseline those flares land on, not the flare
  itself.
- **A persisting low mood**: A generalized affective flatness or heaviness
  that colours everything, independent of any verdict on the self,
  **delegates to the mood engine**. Self-esteem is specifically an attitude
  toward the self; mood is a background tone that need not be about the self
  at all.
- **Clinical self-concept pathology**: The engine models the normal range
  only. Diagnosable conditions bound up with self-regard are **out of
  scope**.

## Encoding

Source to constraint, per file.

- **[self-esteem](position_self_esteem.md)** (the anchor): the resting
  global attitude toward the self, carried into the moment rather than
  assembled from it. Anchored by Rosenberg (global self-esteem as a single
  attitude).
- **[high](position_high.md)** (expression): a stable, positive overall
  regard, secure enough that no single verdict moves it -- the self met
  without defense. Anchored by Rosenberg (high self-esteem as stable
  positive regard) and Tafarodi & Swann (liking and competence both settled
  rather than propped on one alone).
- **[low](position_low.md)** (expression): a negative or unstable overall
  regard, the self met with doubt before the evidence is in. Anchored by
  Rosenberg (low self-esteem as a predominantly unfavorable attitude toward
  the self) and Tafarodi & Swann (a deficit in liking, competence, or both).
- **[contingent](position_contingent.md)** (expression): regard staked on a
  named domain, a running total rather than a settled sum. Anchored by
  Crocker & Wolfe (contingencies of self-worth).
