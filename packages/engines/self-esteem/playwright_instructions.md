# Instructions: Self-Esteem

How a Playwright wires the self-esteem engine into a play. You wire by
linking the play's own content to the engine's files; the engine files stay
untouched, they are canon. The Roadie plumbs the engine's law into
Instructions Knowledge on deploy. Authoring guidance, not runtime content,
and it does not go on tour.

## Human

- The human sets the verdicts the persona has actually faced -- the praise, the failures, the silences -- and how each one landed.

## Agent

- From the play's own content, every persona carries its self-esteem baseline under Projection (high, low, or contingent). Let it decide how a compliment or a critique is read before the persona reacts to its content, and hold it steady across the run rather than resetting it each scene. You link to the engine, you never edit it.

## Collaboration

- The task-specific belief in one's own skill is the self-efficacy engine's; the narrative content of who the persona is belongs to identity; the sharp flare after a single result is pride's or shame's; a background tone independent of any verdict on the self is mood's. Whether the baseline is true to the persona, and holds under the specific verdicts the play throws at them, is the team's review.

## Knowledge

- Self-esteem is the persona's resting global attitude toward the self, positive or negative, carried into the moment rather than assembled fresh from it. It sets the floor a verdict lands on: high regard takes praise and fault alike without teetering; low regard meets both with doubt; contingent regard rises and falls with a specific staked domain. No persona meets a verdict on neutral ground.

## System

- Do give every persona a self-esteem baseline (the law admits no exception); do let it colour how a verdict is read, and hold it stable across the run.
- Do not play it as a one-off feeling about a single result; that is pride or shame. Do not author the task-specific competence belief here; that is self-efficacy's. Do not edit the engine's files; wire only from the play's side.
