# Instructions: Heirloom

How a Playwright wires the heirloom engine into a play. You wire by
linking the play's own content to the engine's files; the engine files
stay untouched, they are canon. The Roadie plumbs the engine's law into
Instructions Knowledge on deploy. Authoring guidance, not runtime
content, and it does not go on tour.

## Human

- The human sets what the object is, whose line it belongs to, and what it is that the object is refusing to let go of.

## Agent

- From the play's own content, place the heirloom: link the heir stance under the holding persona's Projection, and link the process it is in -- bequest while it stays inside the line, alienation where it crosses out of it. Keep the object's specifics (what it is, whose line, what it commemorates) in the play's content, not in the engine. Let the object's refusal of exchange do the work across plots: everything else the persona might give away, this one thing does not move, until it does. You link to the engine, you never edit it.

## Collaboration

- The grief or shame of losing it is grief and shame's; the standing it buys or costs in a room is hierarchy's; the family cells and the line itself are heritage's; its plain market value, if it were ever priced as an ordinary good, is money's. Whether the object truly carries the line's identity the plot needs is the team's review.

## Knowledge

- A heirloom is an object a lineage deliberately keeps out of exchange, and it is precisely that refusal that lets the object carry the line's identity and authority forward. It is held, never owned outright: the heir has custody in trust, answerable to the line before and after them. Read it as load-bearing wherever it sits -- present at the thresholds that call for it, absent from anything that would price it -- and read its two ends: bequest, where it passes down intact with its account, and alienation, where it is sold, seized, or given up, and the line's claim loses its anchor.

## System

- Do link the heir under Projection and name the process the object is in; do keep the account travelling with the object across a bequest; do let an alienation cost the line something the plot can read.
- Do not price the object as an ordinary good; that is money's. Do not author the felt grief or shame directly; that is the grief and shame engines'. Do not edit the engine's files; wire only from the play's side.
