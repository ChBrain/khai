# Position: Heir

## Taxonomy

[Heirloom](piece_heirloom.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: heirloom

## Has

Custody, not ownership. The heir holds the object in trust for the
line, answerable to those who held it before and those who will hold
it after. Holding it legitimately grants the heir a share of the
line's authority for as long as the custody lasts -- borrowed
standing, not a possession to spend down.

## Orders

Keep it, not spend it. The position is to guard the object against
the ordinary pressures of the market and of need, and to decide --
rarely alone -- when and to whom it eventually passes. What the heir
decides here is what the line will treat as given for a generation.

## Loses

The freedom to treat it as ordinary property. The heir cannot sell
it under hardship without indicting themselves before the whole
line, and cannot simply prefer a different heir without the line
weighing in. Under strain, the pull to convert the object into cash
it could plainly become is the sharpest test the position faces.

## Drives

On that loss:
holds tighter and makes the guarding visible, proving worthiness of
the custody in front of the line watching, or alienates the object
quietly, under duress or in secret, and carries the weight of having
broken the claim whether or not the line ever learns of it. Either
way the position does not end cleanly; it is renewed by holding on,
or haunted by having let go.
