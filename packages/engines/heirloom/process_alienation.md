# Process: Alienation

## Taxonomy

[Heirloom](piece_heirloom.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: heirloom

## Initiated by

Under duress or by choice. The [heir](position_heir.md) sells to a
market, the object is lost to seizure, theft, or disaster, or the
heir willingly divests to end a claim they no longer want to carry.
The threshold is crossed the moment a price attaches to the object:
custody becomes a transaction, whatever the reason behind it.

## Direction

Out of the line's control -- into a market, a collection, another
lineage entirely, or simply gone. Once it leaves, the object is read
as a good or an artifact rather than a kept thing; whoever receives
it next inherits the object without the claim it used to carry.

## Lever

The pressure that breaks a bond meant to be unbreakable: financial
need, a line with no heir able or willing to hold it, a rupture
inside the line itself. The same forces that would move an ordinary
possession along finally reach the one the line had set apart from
them.

## Echo

The line's account survives without an anchor. The object's absence
becomes part of the family story -- "we used to have..." -- a wound
recited alongside, or in place of, the object that used to be
recited over. Whoever let it go carries what the loss costs among
those who still hold the rest of the line's identity, whether they
are blamed for it or only remembered as the one who was there when
it happened.
