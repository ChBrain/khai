# Process: Turn

## Taxonomy

[Reversal](process_reversal.md), the pivot phase in which the direction inverts. The turn is the moment of _peripeteia_: the event or revelation that makes the prior direction untenable and the inverted direction unavoidable.

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: reversal

## Initiated by

The surfacing of the concealed tension as an event the persona cannot absorb into the prior direction. The turn is initiated when what was hidden becomes visible, when what was underestimated resolves, or when the persona's own action produces the opposite of its intended effect. The tension that was building in the approach phase releases here.

## Direction

From the prior trajectory to its inversion. The turn does not merely interrupt the prior direction; it makes return to it impossible. The persona who has passed through the turn cannot treat the situation as if the inversion has not occurred. This is the diagnostic of the turn: the prior direction is foreclosed.

## Lever

The specific event or revelation: the disclosure, the discovery, the consequence, the defection, the fact that was hidden and is now known. The lever is narrow -- the turn pivots on this, not on a general deterioration or drift. A reversal has a specific moment of inversion, even if that moment has a long preparation.

## Echo

The break in the persona's expectation. The turn leaves the persona without the prior direction as an available frame. What immediately follows the turn is the gap: the prior direction is gone and the new direction is not yet oriented. The echo of the turn is the interval between the inversion and the persona's new settlement.
