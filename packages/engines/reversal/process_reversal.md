# Process: Reversal

## Taxonomy

A process (TO IDLE). How a situation's direction inverts: the trajectory the persona held as settled, the pivot that turns it, and the new direction that results. Root of the reversal process tree.

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: reversal

## Initiated by

The divergence between the direction the situation was travelling and what it can sustain. A reversal does not come from nowhere; it is prepared by a tension between the established trajectory and the forces -- concealed, ignored, or underestimated -- that will invert it. What initiates the process is the point at which that tension resolves.

## Direction

From the established trajectory through the pivot to the inverted one. The reversal moves the situation from one direction to its opposite: fortune to misfortune, ascent to descent, power-over to power-under, trust to betrayal. The direction of the process is the arc of inversion, not merely surprise or change.

## Lever

The event or revelation that pivots the direction. Aristotle calls this _peripeteia_: the moment the hero's action produces the opposite of its intended effect, or the moment concealed information becomes visible. The lever is the fulcrum; without it, the tension does not resolve into inversion.

## Echo

What the persona carries out of the reversal. The echo is not the new direction itself but the persona's altered orientation to how situations work: a horizon of expectation has been revised, a certainty has been undone. The reversal is finished when the persona has stopped expecting the situation to return to the prior direction.
