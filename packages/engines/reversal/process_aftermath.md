# Process: Aftermath

## Taxonomy

[Reversal](process_reversal.md), the phase in which the persona orients to the inverted direction. The aftermath is the post-reversal condition: the new trajectory has replaced the prior one and the persona is moving within it.

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: reversal

## Initiated by

The persona's first deliberate action taken from the new direction rather than from the prior one. The aftermath begins when the persona stops reaching for the prior direction and starts acting from within the inverted one. This may come quickly or after a period of disorientation; what initiates the aftermath is the settling into the new trajectory.

## Direction

Along the inverted trajectory. The aftermath phase is not recovery to the prior direction; it is orientation within the new one. What the persona carries forward is shaped by the inversion: the resources, relationships, and expectations they hold now are the ones the reversal has left.

## Lever

The persona's revised model of the situation. The aftermath is governed by what the persona has learned from the reversal about how the situation works: what forces were underestimated, what was concealed, what the direction they trusted could not sustain. The lever is the revised understanding applied to navigating the new trajectory.

## Echo

The altered horizon of expectation. The aftermath leaves the persona with a different relationship to the stability of situations: a prior certainty has been undone, and the persona now moves in the knowledge that directions can invert. This is the echo that persists beyond the reversal itself.
