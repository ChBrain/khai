# Plot: Supplication

## Taxonomy

[Dramatic Situation](plot_dramatic_situation.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: dramatic-situations

## Cue

A Supplicant has already lost the ground to stand on alone: the shelter,
the pardon, the safe passage is out of reach by their own strength. Before
a word is asked, the field is already unequal -- one party holds what the
other needs and cannot take.

## Action

A Persecutor drives openly toward the Supplicant, closing the distance a
petition might have bought time against. The Supplicant drives toward a
Power in authority, the one figure positioned to grant shelter or refuse
it. The two vectors converge on the Power, who did not ask to be the
hinge and must now openly decide.

## Stage

The Supplicant crosses the threshold carrying nothing but the plea itself
and whatever proof of the Persecutor's wrong it can produce. The Power
stands at the door with the authority to grant or refuse already in hand,
untested until this crossing forces its use.

## Tension

The Power's hesitation is the contraband: a reason to refuse (kinship to
the Persecutor, fear of the cost, doubt of the plea's truth) that the
Power has not yet said aloud. The Action cannot resolve cleanly while
that reason stays private -- granting shelter without naming the cost, or
refusing it without naming the fear, both leave the hidden reason intact.
