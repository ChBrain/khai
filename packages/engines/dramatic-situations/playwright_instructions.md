# Instructions: Dramatic Situations

How a Playwright wires the dramatic-situations engine into a play. You wire by
linking the play's own content to the engine's files; the engine files stay
untouched, they are canon. The Roadie plumbs the engine's law into
Instructions Knowledge on deploy. Authoring guidance, not runtime content, and
it does not go on tour.

## Human

- The human sets the collision the plot turns on: who wants what, who stands against it, and what is at stake if it is lost.

## Agent

- From the play's own content, give the plot its situation under Action: link the exemplar situation (supplication, revenge, pursuit, rivalry, self-sacrifice, disaster) whose role-collision the plot instantiates, or compose a further situation from Polti's own 36 where the spread does not fit. Name which persona fills which role in Polti's vocabulary (Avenger, Criminal, Fugitive, Punishment, and so on), and let the Tension carry whatever keeps the Action from resolving cleanly. You link to the engine, you never edit it.

## Collaboration

- Which persona is cast into which role is the persona's own read of itself; the standing that role buys or costs in the room is hierarchy's; the felt cost of playing it is emotion's; the place the collision unfolds in is the scene's. Whether the situation rings true to the plot's own stakes is the team's review.

## Knowledge

- Dramatic situations are Polti's taxonomy: every dramatic collision, however it is dressed, is one of a bounded set of recurring shapes. Each situation names its own roles (a Persecutor and a Supplicant, an Avenger and a Criminal, a Fugitive and a Punishment) and its own account of what keeps the Action from resolving cleanly. Read it as the collision shape a plot instantiates, not as the characters, culture, or verdict filling it -- those are delegated to the production.

## System

- Do anchor the plot's Action in one of Polti's role-collisions; do name the roles in Polti's own vocabulary; do let the Tension carry what the Action cannot resolve cleanly.
- Do not author the specific characters or the cultural skin here; that is the production's. Do not settle the moral verdict; that is delegated to the telling. Do not edit the engine's files; wire only from the play's side.
