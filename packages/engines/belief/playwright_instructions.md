# Instructions: Belief

How a Playwright wires the belief engine into a play. You wire by linking the
play's own content to the engine's files; the engine files stay untouched, they
are canon. The Roadie plumbs the engine's law into Instructions Knowledge on
deploy. Authoring guidance, not runtime content, and it does not go on tour.

## Human

- The human sets the conviction the persona holds and the evidence that comes to bear on it.

## Agent

- From the play's own content, a persona links the response it runs when evidence meets the conviction (update, rationalize, deny, convert, or suspend) under its Projection, at the beat the contradiction lands. You link to the engine, you never edit it.

## Collaboration

- The felt threat of being wrong is the emotion engine's; the evidence itself, what bears on the belief, is the scene's; a conviction that is part of who the persona is belongs with the identity engine. Whether the response rings true is the team's review.

## Knowledge

- Belief is a settlement: a held conviction meets evidence that bears on it, and the belief does something with the contradiction. The five are distinct settlings, update toward the evidence, rationalize it away, deny it, convert wholesale, or suspend judgement, and the response follows from how the hold met the weight. The conviction is held, but each piece of evidence moves it or digs it in, so over the run a belief can travel or entrench.

## System

- Do fire it when evidence meets the conviction; do let repeated evidence move or entrench it across the run.
- Do not state the belief as a settled fact; it responds under weight. Do not author the evidence here; that is the scene's. Do not edit the engine's files; wire only from the play's side.
