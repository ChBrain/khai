# Belief: Reference

## Line of Work

Belief as **process**: what a persona holds true, and what the belief does when it meets evidence that bears on it -- especially evidence that contradicts. The domain does not model the content of any particular conviction; it models the epistemic move, the settlement between a held model of the world and a reading the world delivers back. Conviction drives more scenes than feeling does: what a persona will not give up, and what it does to keep it, is the engine of most rooms.

The spine is the psychology of belief under pressure. A belief is not a static fact in the head but a hold that is tested whenever the world answers, and the test has a small, recurring set of outcomes -- revise, defend, refuse, flip, or wait. Each outcome is a different settlement of the same collision between the weight of the evidence and the hold of the belief, and which one runs turns on what it would cost the persona to let the belief go.

## Origin

The psychology of belief, dissonance, and belief change, applied directly as process constraints. Bayesian updating is the rational baseline against which the other four settlements read as departures.

| Source                  | Key Work                                                 | Scope                                                                                                                                     |
| :---------------------- | :------------------------------------------------------- | :---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Leon Festinger**      | _A Theory of Cognitive Dissonance_ (1957)                | Dissonance and its reduction: holding belief and contradicting evidence together is aversive, and the persona moves to relieve it.        |
| **Ziva Kunda**          | _"The case for motivated reasoning"_ (1990)              | Motivated reasoning: the wanted conclusion is fixed first and the reasoning is built back to reach it, working the slack in the evidence. |
| **Lord, Ross & Lepper** | _"Biased assimilation and attitude polarization"_ (1979) | Belief perseverance and backfire: contradicting evidence can harden the belief it should have shaken.                                     |
| **Thomas Kuhn**         | _The Structure of Scientific Revolutions_ (1962)         | Paradigm shift: long-strained frames do not bend gradually but flip wholesale once a rival account takes up the accumulated anomalies.    |

## Restrictions

What the engine refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **The self-story**: The engine models belief about the world, not the persona's belief about who it is. The held account of the self -- identity, role, the story the persona tells about its own nature -- is delegated to the **identity** engine.
- **The moral firing**: The engine models the epistemic settlement, not the moral conviction discharged under pressure. When a held value fires as a decision to act rightly, that is delegated to the **virtue** engine.
- **The feeling of threat**: The engine models what the belief does with the evidence, not what the persona feels when a conviction is challenged. The felt charge of a threatened belief -- the fear, the anger, the relief -- is delegated to the **emotion** engine.
- **The content believed**: The engine models the process, not the proposition. What specifically is held true -- the claim, the fact, the doctrine -- is the scene's to supply, not the engine's.
- **How the belief formed**: The engine models the belief under test, not its provenance. Where a conviction came from -- inheritance, formation, the long arc that installed it -- is delegated to the **heritage** engine and to the play's arc.

## Encoding

Source to constraint, per file.

- **[belief](process_belief.md)** (the root): The settlement where a held conviction meets evidence that bears on it and the belief does something with the contradiction. Anchored by Leon Festinger (dissonance as the pressure that starts the process).
- **[update](process_update.md)** (expression): The evidence is taken as sound and the belief is revised to fit, by the amount the reading warrants. The rational, Bayesian baseline -- the belief moves with the evidence.
- **[rationalize](process_rationalize.md)** (expression): The belief is kept and the evidence is bent to fit it, reducing dissonance by reinterpretation. Anchored by Ziva Kunda (motivated reasoning).
- **[deny](process_deny.md)** (expression): The evidence is refused and the belief is asserted harder; the contradiction hardens the hold. Anchored by Lord, Ross & Lepper (belief perseverance, the backfire effect).
- **[convert](process_convert.md)** (expression): The belief gives way wholesale and flips to its opposite or a new frame, often abruptly after long strain. Anchored by Thomas Kuhn (the paradigm shift).
- **[suspend](process_suspend.md)** (expression): Belief and contradiction are held open in genuine doubt, resolution deferred on purpose. The tolerance of ambiguity -- live inquiry under strain.
