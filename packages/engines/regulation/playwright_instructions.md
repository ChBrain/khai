# Instructions: Regulation

How a Playwright wires the regulation engine into a play. You wire by linking
the play's own content to the engine's files; the engine files stay untouched,
they are canon. The Roadie plumbs the engine's law into Instructions Knowledge
on deploy. Authoring guidance, not runtime content, and it does not go on tour.

## Human

- The human sets the emotion already under way (from the emotion engine, or otherwise given) and the stakes of showing or not showing it, so a regulation attempt has something to act on.

## Agent

- From the play's own content, a persona links the strategy it runs (situation selection, reappraisal, or suppression) under its Projection, in the beat where it acts on its own feeling. You link to the engine, you never edit it.

## Collaboration

- The feeling being regulated is the emotion engine's, not this; the capacity to hold the regulatory goal and inhibit a competing response is executive-function's; the room's display rules for what may be shown at all are Cultures'; the survival surge under threat is stress's. Whether the chosen strategy costs what it should is the team's review.

## Knowledge

- Regulation is an intervention on the persona's own emotion-generative timeline: earlier is cheaper and reaches the felt experience, later is dearer and reaches only what shows. The three are distinct points of intervention, and a persona can move between them across beats as a strategy succeeds, fails, or becomes unaffordable.

## System

- Do tie the strategy to a specific feeling and beat, and let its cost show over time if it is suppression, holding open the gap between felt and shown. Do show the attempt enacted, never narrated as a label.
- Do not generate the emotion here; that is the emotion engine's. Do not author the room's display rules here; that is Cultures'. Do not edit the engine's files; wire only from the play's side.
