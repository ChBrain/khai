# Heritage: Reference

## Line of Work

Heritage as **position**: the formation a persona arrives carrying -- the habitus absorbed before any choice was possible, and not put down once it is held. The domain does not model the persona's current standing (that is hierarchy's read of the room now); it models where the standing came from -- the family cells the formation was absorbed in, the capital those cells transmitted, and the friction that fires when an old formation meets a field it was not made for.

The spine is Bourdieu's habitus: the durable dispositions a body takes on from its conditions of formation, carried into every later field as the things the persona reaches for without deciding to. Heritage names what that formation is, where it came from, what it carries, and what happens when it is displaced.

## Origin

Structural sociology, applied directly as position and process constraints: Bourdieu's habitus and capital theory.

| Source                  | Key Work                                                | Scope                                                                                                            |
| :---------------------- | :------------------------------------------------------ | :--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Pierre Bourdieu**     | _Outline of a Theory of Practice_ (1977)                | Habitus: the durable, transposable dispositions formed by conditions of existence and carried into later fields. |
| **Pierre Bourdieu**     | _Distinction_ (1984) / _"The Forms of Capital"_ (1986)  | The four forms of capital -- cultural, economic, social, symbolic -- and their transmission across generations.  |
| **Bourdieu & Passeron** | _Reproduction in Education, Society and Culture_ (1977) | Cultural transmission and reproduction: how the family cell passes its formation to the next generation.         |

## Restrictions

What the engine refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Current standing**: The engine models formation, not the rank the room reads now. Where the persona stands in the field they are currently in is the hierarchy engine's; heritage is the history behind that read, not the read itself.
- **The specific family**: The engine ships the nuclear-cell base -- mother, father, child, sibling -- as the irreducible primitive. A named family, and extended configurations (grandparent, only-child, blended, adopted), are compositions of the base materialised outside the engine for personas that need that depth, not carried here.
- **Live relationships**: The engine models the formation a relation transmitted, not the ongoing relationship. How a persona relates to their mother in the scene is the scene's; heritage names what that cell formed in them.
- **The cultural tint**: Which capital a particular culture rewards, and how transmission runs locally, is the consuming culture's. The engine ships the universal forms; the culture supplies the conversion rate.

- **Capital pieces** -- [cultural](piece_cultural_capital.md), [economic](piece_economic_capital.md), [social](piece_social_capital.md), [symbolic](piece_symbolic_capital.md): what the cell transmitted to the persona over the formation arc. Anchored by Bourdieu (_The Forms of Capital_).
