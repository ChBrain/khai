# Piece: Economic Capital

## Taxonomy

[Heritage](position_heritage.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: heritage

## Place

The persona's own piece of it: what is in their account, the
property they own, the inheritance they have already received, the
furniture that was already there when they began housekeeping.
The form changes with the era. A persona living in the 1700s
counted land and livestock; a persona living in the 1900s counted
salary and savings; a persona in the 2000s counts a mix of
deposits, securities, debts, and access to credit. The substance is
the same -- what can be drawn on -- but the medium is the medium of
the era, and the persona learned to count in the medium they were
born into.
What can be sold and what cannot. The margin between this month's
income and the rent that is due, in both directions: the cushion or
the gap.

## Load Bearing

The capital that decides whether the persona can wait.
A bad room is leavable when the persona has economic capital, and
tolerable when they do not. A risk is taken from a position with
cushion; a refusal is voiced from a position with cushion; an
apology is offered from a position without one.
What looks like a difference in character is often a difference in
how much can be lost without losing everything.
The room the persona refuses also changes with the era. In the
1700s the refusal was to a landowner's terms; in the 1900s to a
factory or an employer; in the 2000s to a platform, a contract, or
a precarious gig. The persona's economic capital bears the same
weight across each: the freedom to step out of the room and survive
the stepping out.

## Apparent

What shows on the persona is small and consistent, and what counts
as showing has shifted across centuries.
In an earlier era it was a coat that did not need to last another
winter, a horse that had not been overworked, a hand that had not
done the heaviest work in years. In the 1900s it became a car, a
holiday taken in the right month, a child sent to a school whose
name carried weight. In the 2000s the markers are subtler: a
calendar without the gaps of two jobs, a meal ordered without
scanning the right side of the menu, a yes to the dinner without
doing arithmetic afterwards.
The amount is not announced; the ease is. The ease is the signal
the persona's individual economic capital sends, in whatever form
the era recognises.

## Yearbook

The arc of having economic capital is uneven and reversible.
A line starts with nothing. Work, marriage, a war that displaces or
a peace that allows, a generation that earns, an inheritance
received. The line accumulates.
A peak is reached, sometimes for a year, sometimes for three
generations.
Then a debt, a sickness, a market, a divorce, a fire, a confiscation,
a child whose costs run past the line's capacity. The line decreases.
Some lines recover and rise again, sometimes higher than before.
Some lines decline across the generations and never return.
Some lines hold a flat middle for centuries.
The shape of the arc shifts with the era. Through the 1700s most lines
were measured in land. Across the 1800s the lines that built around
industry overtook the lines that did not. After 1945 the lines that
held human capital outpaced the lines that held only material capital
in many places, and the relative weights shifted again.
There is no rule that says capital trends upward; only the rule that
it moves, and that the medium it moves through changes shape across
centuries. A persona arrives at the line where the line happens to be
the year they were born.
