# Process: Transmission

## Taxonomy

[Heritage](position_heritage.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: heritage

## Initiated by

Capital moves between positions in the cell.
A handover, a name pinned on a child, a key passed across a table,
a phone number written on the back of a contact card, a debt
forgiven, a refusal to teach.
The process opens whenever capital changes hands between
[mother](position_mother.md), [father](position_father.md),
[child](position_child.md), or [sibling](position_sibling.md) -- in
any direction.

## Direction

From a holder to a recipient, with a vector the room can read.
Down the generations is common; across is also common; up the
generations is rarer but happens when the child carries something
the parents did not have.
What moves can be [economic](piece_economic_capital.md) (provision,
inheritance), [cultural](piece_cultural_capital.md) (book, accent,
tools of attention), [social](piece_social_capital.md) (introduction,
obligation), or [symbolic](piece_symbolic_capital.md) (name, blessing,
the weight of a family's word). Often more than one at once.

## Lever

Friction: what does not arrive in the shape it was sent.
Some capital converts on the way (the parents' money becomes the
child's education becomes the persona's vocabulary becomes the
ease in a meeting room).
Some capital refuses to convert and stays in its original form (a
network introduction that does not produce employment, a name that
does not open the door it was supposed to).
Some capital is refused on receipt (the child who declines the
business the father built).
The lever is the conversion rate the cell does not control.

## Echo

What arrived is now held; what did not arrive is now absent.
The recipient is a holder in the next round.
A persona who received cultural capital fluently transmits it
fluently; a persona whose transmission was refused becomes the
holder of an inheritance they did not want and now carry quietly.
The next generation reads the cell with the residue of what moved
through it.
