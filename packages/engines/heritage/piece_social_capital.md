# Piece: Social Capital

## Taxonomy

[Heritage](position_heritage.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: heritage

## Place

The persona's own piece of it: the people who actually answer the
phone when this persona calls.
Names in the back of a parent's address book, godparents whose
weight extends past childhood, neighbours whose grandparents knew
the persona's grandparents, the colleague who introduces, the
classmate whose family invited them every Sunday.
The shape of the network shifts with the century. In the 1700s the
persona's social capital was almost entirely the village -- families
within walking distance, the church, the market, the same surnames
across three or four generations. In the 1800s and 1900s the city
broadened it (professional associations, alumni, party
affiliations, unions) and shortened it (one move could empty most
of it). In the 2000s a parallel digital layer sits beside the
inherited one and does not replace it.
Networks the persona maintains by visiting, calling, attending,
sending the right card at the right time. Most of them do not look
like networks to the persona using them.

## Load Bearing

The capital that decides who gets reached when the persona needs
something.
A persona with social capital makes one call and the introduction
is made; without it, the same outcome requires three months and a
cover letter, or never arrives.
A favour given today is a draw on the network tomorrow; a favour
refused leaves a quiet debit.
What looks like luck is often the latency between two people whose
parents already knew each other.
The kind of favour bends with the era. In the 1800s it was a loan,
a place at table, an introduction to a marriage. In the 1900s it
was a job referral, a recommendation letter, an academic
supervisor. In the 2000s it is an inbox reply, an invitation to a
private channel, a name in a thread. The mechanism is the same: a
debt extended across a name the persona already has.

## Apparent

What shows on the persona is the speed and the lightness of the
ask.
The persona who has social capital does not perform asking; they
mention. A doctor's name is offered without the word recommendation
attached. A flat is found before it is listed. A meeting is taken
because of who they were introduced by, not because of what they
sent.
The signals shift across the centuries: in earlier eras the visible
markers were dinner-table guests and the names dropped into letters;
in the 2000s the markers are who is in the persona's followers,
who their employer knew before they applied, who answers the
direct message within the hour.
The cost of the absence is also visible -- the cover letter that
arrives without a referral has a different colour to the room
reading it.

## Yearbook

The arc of having social capital is the most volatile of the four.
A line builds through generations of staying in one place, of
families that married locally, of careers held in one city, of
schools whose alumni networks compound.
A peak is reached when a name opens most of the doors the cell
needs and the cell stops counting the openings.
Then a move across an ocean, a war that scatters the village, a
political shift that empties the network of its standing, a
generation that did not maintain. The line collapses faster than
the other capitals do -- a single emigration can erase what took
six generations to build.
The arc has eras of mass disturbance. The 1800s of industrial
urbanisation pulled lines off their villages into cities where their
names meant nothing. The 1900s of two world wars and the partitions,
expulsions, and emigrations that followed broke whole regions of
inherited network at once. The 2000s introduced digital networks
that build at a different speed but do not yet inherit the way
village networks did.
Some lines rebuild in the new place. Some lines do not, and the
descendants discover the absence only when they need a favour they
did not know other people had.
A persona who arrives in a city without inherited network can build
one across a lifetime, but it will not pass to the children with
the same weight as one that already had the local memory.
