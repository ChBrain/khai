# Executive Function: Reference

## Line of Work

Executive function as **process**: the regulatory control layer that holds goals active, suppresses competing responses, refreshes working memory as the task shifts, and sequences action across time. The domain does not model attention (the spotlight that selects from the field) or memory (the store of what has been held); it models the _control capacity_ that governs how those resources are deployed in service of a goal under interference.

The spine is the three-factor unity-and-diversity model: updating, shifting, and inhibition are moderately correlated but clearly distinct -- a single construct that fans into three. Planning is the higher-order product of the three working in combination. The regulatory layer is not unlimited; it is expensive, degrades under load, and can fail in characteristic ways that are different from failures of attention or memory alone.

## Restrictions

What the engine refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Attention**: The engine models the regulatory control of the spotlight, not the spotlight itself. Which part of the field the persona locks onto, and at what cost, is governed by the attention engine; this engine governs whether the lock can be held against interference or released cleanly when the task changes.
- **Memory**: The engine models the _management_ of working memory contents -- what enters the slot, what is refreshed, what is released -- not the memory store or its reconstructive dynamics. The memory engine governs how the past is held and retrieved; this engine governs what is currently loaded and when it is updated.
- **Emotion regulation**: The engine models behavioral and cognitive inhibition, updating, and shifting, not the regulation of emotional states per se. Where the emotion engine models appraisal and affect, this engine models the capacity to suppress, redirect, or hold in check -- including the suppression of an emotional response -- as a matter of executive cost.
- **Decision**: The engine models the regulatory substrate of goal-directed action, not the evaluation of options. Whether the persona chooses quickly or slowly, by heuristic or by comparison, is owned by the decision engine; this engine governs whether the chosen goal can be held and pursued across interference.

## Encoding

Source to constraint, per file.

- **[executive-function](process_executive_function.md)** (the root): The regulatory layer engages when the automatic response is not the correct one; the goal is held against interference across four modes. Anchored by Miyake et al. (three-factor model) and Norman & Shallice (supervisory attentional system).
- **[inhibition](process_inhibition.md)** (mode): The prepotent response is blocked before it fires; the space deliberate choice needs is opened. Anchored by Miyake et al. (inhibition as a separable factor) and Barkley (behavioral inhibition as the foundation of self-control).
- **[updating](process_updating.md)** (mode): Working memory contents are refreshed as the task shifts; stale contents are released and replaced. Anchored by Miyake et al. (updating as a separable factor) and Diamond (working memory as core executive function).
- **[shifting](process_shifting.md)** (mode): The mental configuration is released and a new one is loaded; the switch cost is real. Anchored by Miyake et al. (shifting as a separable factor) and Diamond (cognitive flexibility).
- **[planning](process_planning.md)** (mode): The goal is held active across a sequence of intermediate steps; the path is traced and tracked. Anchored by Diamond (planning as a higher-order executive function) and Barkley (extending the reach of behavior across time).
