# Process: Inhibition

## Taxonomy

[Executive Function](process_executive_function.md), the mode this is one form of.

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: executive-function

## Initiated by

A prepotent response is available -- the quick answer, the impulse, the automatic move -- and it is not the right one.
The goal requires the automatic to be blocked before it fires, or the ongoing response to be interrupted before it completes.

## Direction

Against the pull of the first move.
The persona brakes -- not because the impulse is wrong in kind but because it arrives before it is wanted or warranted, or because the goal demands a different response entirely.

## Lever

The weight of the prepotent response meets the cost of holding it back.
Strong habits, strong emotions, and strong reinforcement histories make the brake expensive; the cost scales with the force of what must be blocked.
A mild habit held back costs little; a well-reinforced impulse held back under emotional load costs a great deal.

## Echo

The space that inhibition opens is the space deliberate choice needs.
Without it, the chain of planned action cannot begin; the next step in the sequence starts from impulse rather than from the goal.
Where inhibition holds, the persona acts from intention.
Where it fails, the persona acts before they are ready -- the wrong word said, the wrong move taken, the response sent before the pause that would have changed it.
