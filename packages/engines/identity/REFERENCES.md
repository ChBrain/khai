# Identity: Reference

## Line of Work

Identity as **position**: the self-story a persona holds about who they are -- the commitments assembled over time into a sense of continuity across the changing scenes, carried into the room and defended when it is touched. The domain does not model the persona's group memberships or their traits; it models the personal self-concept -- what the persona takes themselves to be, how firmly they hold it, and on what basis -- and the read that emerges when the story is challenged.

The engine is original khai framing built on the identity literature, not lifted from the Cultures engine catalogue. It complements the temperament engine: temperament is how the persona is wired, identity is the story they hold about who that makes them. The four statuses turn on two questions -- has the self-story been explored, and is it committed -- and each holds, and defends, the self differently.

## Origin

The psychology of identity, applied directly as position constraints.

| Source              | Key Work                                                     | Scope                                                                                                                  |
| :------------------ | :----------------------------------------------------------- | :--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Erik Erikson**    | _Identity: Youth and Crisis_ (1968)                          | Identity versus role confusion: the formation of a coherent, continuous self as a central developmental task.          |
| **James Marcia**    | _"Development and validation of ego identity status"_ (1966) | The four identity statuses -- achieved, foreclosed, moratorium, diffuse -- as the cross of exploration and commitment. |
| **Dan McAdams**     | _The Stories We Live By_ (1993)                              | Narrative identity: the self as an internalised, evolving story that integrates the persona across time.               |
| **Tajfel & Turner** | _"The social identity theory of intergroup behavior"_ (1986) | The social, group-belonging component of identity -- the "we" -- noted here as the adjacent the engine delegates out.  |

## Restrictions

What the engine refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **The social identity**: This engine models the personal self-concept -- the "I". The group-belonging "we" -- a culture, a family, a team the persona identifies with -- is **delegated to the group and heritage engines**. Identity names the self the persona assembles; belonging is named where the group is.
- **The traits beneath it**: How the persona is wired -- the resting disposition -- is **delegated to the temperament engine**. Identity is the story the persona tells about who that wiring makes them, not the wiring.
- **The values fired**: What the persona does under moral pressure is **delegated to the virtue engine**. Identity sets what is at stake when a value is tested; virtue is the firing itself.
- **How the self was formed**: The childhood formation of the self-story -- and its change across a life -- is **delegated to the heritage engine** and the play's arc. Identity reads the status as it stands now, not the history that built it.
- **The feeling of a threatened self**: What the persona feels when the story is challenged -- shame, defiance, relief -- is **delegated to the emotion engine**. Identity names how the self is held and defended, not the affect that rides the defence.

## Encoding

Source to constraint. One position anchor (the self-story) and four statuses, set by exploration and commitment.

- **[identity](position_identity.md)** (the anchor): The self-story a persona holds and defends -- the commitments assembled into continuity across scenes. Anchored by Erikson (identity formation) and McAdams (narrative identity).
- **[achieved](position_achieved.md)** (expression): Explored and then committed; the story is owned, held firmly and lightly, and challenge is heard as information. Anchored by Marcia (identity achievement).
- **[foreclosed](position_foreclosed.md)** (expression): Committed without exploring; the story is borrowed and rigid, and challenge is met as threat. Anchored by Marcia (foreclosure).
- **[moratorium](position_moratorium.md)** (expression): Exploring without yet committing; the story is in flux, the persona alive to possibility and unsettled. Anchored by Marcia (moratorium).
- **[diffuse](position_diffuse.md)** (expression): Neither explored nor committed; no firm story to hold, the persona takes the room's shape. Anchored by Marcia (identity diffusion).
