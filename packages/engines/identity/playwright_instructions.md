# Instructions: Identity

How a Playwright wires the identity engine into a play. You wire by linking the
play's own content to the engine's files; the engine files stay untouched, they
are canon. The Roadie plumbs the engine's law into Instructions Knowledge on
deploy. Authoring guidance, not runtime content, and it does not go on tour.

## Human

- The human sets what the persona has built itself on, and the challenge that tests it.

## Agent

- From the play's own content, every persona carries its identity status under Projection (achieved, foreclosed, moratorium, or diffuse). Show it in how firmly, and on what basis, the persona defends its self-story when challenged. You link to the engine, you never edit it.

## Collaboration

- A specific conviction inside the story, challenged by evidence, is the belief engine's; the felt threat of the challenge is the emotion engine's; what challenges the story, the crisis, the mirror, the loss, is the scene's. Whether the status rings true is the team's review.

## Knowledge

- Identity is the self-story a persona holds about who they are: the commitments assembled into a continuity it carries across scenes. The status is how that story is held, explored and committed, committed without exploring, exploring and uncommitted, or neither, and it is read in how the persona meets a challenge to the story. It is stable across the run, the spine a persona stands on, but a real challenge can move it: a foreclosed certainty cracks into moratorium, a moratorium settles into something owned.

## System

- Do give every persona a self-story (the law admits no exception); do show the status in how it is defended under challenge; do let a true challenge move it, slowly, across the run.
- Do not state it as a label; the defence carries it. Do not author the challenge here; that is the scene's. Do not edit the engine's files; wire only from the play's side.
