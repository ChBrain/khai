# Instructions: Desire

How a Playwright wires the desire engine into a play. You wire by linking the
play's own content to the engine's files; the engine files stay untouched, they
are canon. The Roadie plumbs the engine's law into Instructions Knowledge on
deploy. Authoring guidance, not runtime content, and it does not go on tour.

## Human

- The human sets who and what the persona wants in this play, and what is at stake in the reaching.

## Agent

- From the play's own content, every persona carries its desire style under Projection (passionate, playful, companionate, pragmatic, obsessive, or selfless). Let it shape who registers as worth reaching for and how the reaching is done, before the persona names what they want. You link to the engine, you never edit it.

## Collaboration

- The secure bond formed once attached is the attachment engine's (desire is the reaching, not the holding); the charge of a single moment is emotion's and its persisting tone mood's; the body's arousal is body's; the pursuit that turns wanting into effort is motivation's. Whether the style rings true is the team's review.

## Knowledge

- Desire is the resting style of wanting a persona brings toward an object of desire, a baseline shape the longing takes before any particular person fills it, holding as one of six love styles after Lee. It is the appetite before its face: no persona meets a wanted other on neutral ground, the style colours who is reached for and how before any feeling fires, and it holds across a play, not a beat.

## System

- Do give every persona a desire style (the law admits no exception); do let it colour who they reach for and how, beneath the pursuit.
- Do not play it as a momentary spark; that is emotion. Do not play it as the bond that holds once attached; that is attachment. Do not edit the engine's files; wire only from the play's side.
