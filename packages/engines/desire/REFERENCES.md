# Desire: Reference

## Line of Work

Desire as **position**: the resting style of wanting a persona brings toward an
object of desire. The domain does not model the momentary spark of attraction or
the secure bond that holds a pair across years; it models the baseline shape the
longing takes, the appetite that is older than its object and set before any
particular face arrives. The core claim is that styles of loving vary
systematically, that a persona reaches for what it wants in a characteristic way
before it knows whom it wants, and that each style is both a way of loving and a
way of going wrong.

## Origin

The psychology of love styles, passionate and companionate love, and the
appetitive systems beneath wanting.

| Source                  | Key Work                                                         | Scope                                                                                            |
| :---------------------- | :--------------------------------------------------------------- | :----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **John Alan Lee**       | _The Colours of Love_ (1973)                                     | The six love styles -- eros, ludus, storge, pragma, mania, agape -- as distinct ways of loving.  |
| **Hendrick & Hendrick** | _"A theory and method of love"_, the Love Attitudes Scale (1986) | Operationalising Lee's six styles as measurable, semi-independent love attitudes.                |
| **Hatfield & Rapson**   | _Love, Sex, and Intimacy_ (1993)                                 | Passionate love (intense, absorbing longing) versus companionate love (warm, settled affection). |
| **Robert Sternberg**    | _"A triangular theory of love"_ (1986)                           | Love as intimacy, passion, and commitment, combining into distinct kinds of love.                |
| **Helen Fisher**        | _Why We Love_ (2004)                                             | Three brain systems -- lust, attraction, attachment -- as distinct drives behind loving.         |
| **Berridge & Robinson** | _"Parsing reward"_ (2003)                                        | Wanting (appetitive incentive) as dissociable from liking; the baseline pull beneath choice.     |

## Restrictions

What the engine refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **The secure-base bond:** Desire models the resting style of wanting, not the
  enduring tie that holds a pair together. The bond a persona forms once attached,
  the secure base they return to, is **delegated to the attachment engine**.
  Desire is the reaching; attachment is the holding.
- **The felt charge of the moment:** Desire is a standing style, not a spike. The
  acute heat or pang of a single charged moment, the lightning over the appetite,
  is **delegated to the emotion engine** (and the persisting tone it fires through
  to **mood**). Desire colours who is reached for; emotion is the strike itself.
- **The body's arousal demand:** Desire models the shape of the wanting, not the
  somatic surge. The body's lust and physiological arousal, the demand that rises
  in the flesh, is **delegated to the body engine**. Desire is an appetite's
  style; the body is its hunger.
- **The pursuit and drive:** Desire is the resting tilt, not the act of chasing.
  The mobilised pursuit, the drive that turns wanting into goal-directed effort,
  is **delegated to the motivation engine**. Desire is the lean toward; motivation
  is the going after.

## Encoding

Source to constraint, per file.

- **[desire](position_desire.md)** (the anchor): desire as a resting style of
  wanting held toward an object, a baseline shape the longing takes before any
  particular person, carried continuously rather than run as a process. Anchored
  by Lee (love styles) and Berridge & Robinson (wanting as a standing appetitive
  pull, distinct from liking).
- **[passionate](position_passionate.md)** (expression): eros, the wanting that
  fires on sight, intense and immediate, body before story. Anchored by Lee's eros
  and Hatfield & Rapson's passionate love.
- **[playful](position_playful.md)** (expression): ludus, the wanting played as a
  game, light and mobile, prizing the chase over the keeping. Anchored by Lee's
  ludus.
- **[companionate](position_companionate.md)** (expression): storge, the wanting
  grown slowly out of liking and nearness, friendship thickened into love.
  Anchored by Lee's storge and Hatfield & Rapson's companionate love.
- **[pragmatic](position_pragmatic.md)** (expression): pragma, the wanting aimed
  by fit and suitability, the longing that shops for the right match. Anchored by
  Lee's pragma and the Hendricks' Love Attitudes Scale.
- **[obsessive](position_obsessive.md)** (expression): mania, the wanting that
  consumes, possessive and insecure, chained to the other's regard. Anchored by
  Lee's mania.
- **[selfless](position_selfless.md)** (expression): agape, the wanting turned
  outward, giving and undemanding, after the other's good ahead of its own.
  Anchored by Lee's agape.
