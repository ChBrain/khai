# Conformity: Reference

## Line of Work

Conformity as **process**: the pull of a present group's standard on the persona's response, and how that pull resolves. The domain models four readings -- compliance (a public go-along over private disagreement), internalization (a private acceptance, the group taken as evidence), obedience (yielding to an authority's command rather than a peer norm), and independence (the pull resisted, the deviant position held) -- and what each demands of the persona caught in the discrepancy.

The spine is the social-influence literature. Sherif showed how a group converges on a norm where the situation is ambiguous, and how that norm is carried away by the individual; Asch showed that a unanimous majority can pull a person against the plain evidence of the senses, and that a single ally dissolves most of the pull. Deutsch and Gerard split the pull in two -- the normative (the wish not to stand apart) and the informational (the group as evidence about reality) -- which is the axis between compliance and internalization. Milgram moved the source from peer to authority: obedience is conformity to a command, held by a legitimate office, against the persona's own conscience. Moscovici turned the arrow around: a consistent minority can move the majority, which is the ground of independence as more than mere refusal.

## Restrictions

What the engine refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **How an idea spreads through a system over time**: The engine models the pull on one persona at the moment of a present group. The diffusion of a practice or belief across a population -- adopters, thresholds, tipping points -- belongs to the **adoption** engine.
- **One party deliberately changing another's mind**: The pull here is the mere presence of others' positions, not a campaign aimed at the persona. Argument, framing, and the deliberate work of moving a belief belong to the **persuasion** engine.
- **The group's developmental stage**: The engine takes the group as a present standard, not as a thing with a history. How a group forms, storms, and matures -- and how its stage changes the pull -- belongs to the **group** engine.
- **The standing role-expectation as a script**: Where the pressure is a stable expectation attached to a position rather than the live answer of a present group, that script belongs to the **role** engine.
