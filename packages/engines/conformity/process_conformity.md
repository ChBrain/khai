# Process: Conformity

## Taxonomy

Social influence.

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: conformity

## Initiated by

The persona holds a response -- a judgement, a choice, a belief -- and discovers that a group around it holds another.
The group's position is present and visible: the others have already answered, and their answer is not the persona's.
The pull begins the moment the discrepancy is felt; where the persona is alone, or where the group agrees with it, the process does not fire.

## Direction

Toward the group's standard.
The persona's response bends toward the position the others hold -- in what it shows, or in what it actually believes, or in what an authority commands.
The bend is not automatic; it is the resolution of a pressure, and the persona may yield, may take the standard in as true, or may hold its ground against it.

## Lever

The persona's own answer presses against the group's.
The cost of standing apart -- the look, the silence, the exclusion -- weighs against the cost of being wrong alone; the group is at once a verdict to be feared and evidence to be trusted.
The press is strongest where the group is unanimous, where the persona is unsure, and where the answer must be given in the open.

## Echo

The persona has moved, or refused to.
What looks the same from outside -- the answer given to the room -- may be a public go-along over private doubt, a genuine acceptance, or an obedience to command; or the room may hold an answer the persona would not give.
The read resolves into one of [compliance](process_compliance.md), [internalization](process_internalization.md), [obedience](process_obedience.md), or [independence](process_independence.md), depending on whether the pull is normative or informational, whether it issues from a peer or an authority, and whether the persona bends or holds.
When the group reassembles or the standard shifts, the process returns here to be read again.
