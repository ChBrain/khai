# Process: Obedience

## Taxonomy

[Conformity](process_conformity.md), the pull this reading is one resolution of.

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: conformity

## Initiated by

An authority issues a command, and the persona is positioned to carry it out.
This is not a peer's example to be matched but an instruction from someone whose right to instruct the persona accepts -- a rank, an office, a coat that marks the giver as the one in charge.
The pull begins where the command meets the persona's own sense of what it should do, and the two do not agree.

## Direction

Toward the authority's command, and away from the persona's own judgement of the act.
The persona does what it is told, even as the act runs against what it would choose; the responsibility for the act is handed up to the one who commanded it.
The bend is toward compliance with the instruction, not toward belief that the instruction is right.

## Lever

The authority's legitimacy presses against the persona's conscience.
The persona enters a frame in which it is the instrument and the authority is the agent; the question shifts from "is this right" to "am I permitted to refuse", and refusal must be enacted against the weight of the office.
The press is strongest where the authority is present and close, where its legitimacy is unquestioned, and where the steps escalate by degrees too small to mark a line.

## Echo

The persona has done what it was commanded, against its own reading of the act.
The strain does not vanish -- the persona feels the act as wrong even while performing it -- but the agentic frame holds the responsibility elsewhere, with the one who gave the order.
The bind breaks where the authority's legitimacy cracks, where a peer refuses first, or where the persona reclaims the act as its own and answers for it; until then the command carries the persona past where its conscience would have stopped.
