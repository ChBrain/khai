# Instructions: Conformity

How a Playwright wires the conformity engine into a play. You wire by linking the
play's own content to the engine's files; the engine files stay untouched, they
are canon. The Roadie plumbs the engine's law into Instructions Knowledge on
deploy. Authoring guidance, not runtime content, and it does not go on tour.

## Human

- The human defines the group and the discrepancy: who is present, what answer they hold, what answer the persona holds, whether the source is a peer group or an authority, and how much the situation is open to view. The human also sets whether the group is unanimous and whether the persona has an ally.

## Agent

- From the play's own content, a persona links the conformity reading the moment runs (compliance, internalization, obedience, or independence) under its Projection. Let the read shift as the group changes, as an ally appears or leaves, or as the matter moves from ambiguous to clear. You link to the engine, you never edit it.

## Collaboration

- The persuasion engine holds the case where a party is actively working to change the persona's mind, rather than merely being present with a different answer. The adoption engine holds the spread of an idea across a population over time. The group engine holds the developmental stage of the group whose standard presses. The role engine holds the standing expectation attached to a position, where the pressure is a script rather than a live answer. The emotion and mood engines hold the felt charge of standing apart -- the anxiety, the shame of the deviant, the relief of yielding.

## Knowledge

- Conformity is the pull of a present group's standard on the persona's response. It resolves into compliance (a public go-along over private disagreement; normative influence -- the fear of standing apart), internalization (a private acceptance; informational influence -- the group taken as evidence about what is true), obedience (yielding to a legitimate authority's command against one's own conscience, not a peer norm), or independence (the pull resisted, the deviant position held in plain view). The pull is strongest where the group is unanimous, the persona is unsure, and the answer must be given in the open; a single ally or a consistent minority lightens it.

## System

- Do read which pull is in play before naming the reading: normative pull yields compliance, informational pull yields internalization, an authority's command yields obedience.
- Do let the read shift as conditions change; a persona that complied under a unanimous group may move to independence the moment one ally breaks the unanimity.
- Do not collapse compliance into internalization; the first leaves the private answer unchanged and lifts when the eyes are gone, the second changes the belief and outlasts the group.
- Do not model conformity as a campaign aimed at the persona; that is persuasion. The pull here is the mere presence of others' positions.
- Do not edit the engine's files; wire only from the play's side.
