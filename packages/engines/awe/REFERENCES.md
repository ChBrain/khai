# Awe: Reference

## Line of Work

Awe as **process**: the response through which a persona accommodates a vastness that exceeds its current frame. The domain models the two core features of awe -- perceived vastness, and the need for accommodation when an encounter cannot be assimilated into existing mental structures -- and the four colours the accommodation may take by what the vastness is read as: beauty (perceptual abundance), threat (the sublime), moral (elevation), and sacred (the numinous).

The spine is the appraisal account of awe: awe fires only where the vast overruns the frame, and the frame -- not the object -- must give. The widening goes with a small self: the personal recedes, a larger order comes forward, and the persona is opened outward. The flavour of any given awe is set by the second-order reading of the vast thing, which is what the four members partition. The older aesthetics of the sublime and the phenomenology of the holy stand behind the threat and sacred colours, supplying the menace and the transcendence the modern appraisal model later folded into its flavouring dimensions. Within the sublime the two classical accounts divide: Burke supplies the menace, the power that overwhelms, while Kant supplies its completion, the defeat of the senses before the magnitude turning into the mind's elevation above it -- the one movement in awe that ends in a self enlarged rather than small.

## Restrictions

What the engine refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **The felt affective baseline**: The engine models the accommodation structure -- the frame overrun and widening -- not the raw affective register beneath it. The persona's baseline feeling-tone belongs to the **emotion** engine.
- **The collective manufacture of awe**: Awe staged and shared through ceremony -- the collective effervescence a group generates together -- is not the persona's solitary accommodation. The ritual that manufactures shared awe belongs to the **ritual** engine.
- **Whether life matters**: Awe often resets the persona's sense of significance, but the standing question of whether a life matters, and how, belongs to the **meaning** engine. The awe engine models the encounter, not the verdict on mattering it may provoke.
- **How the awe changes across the play**: The engine models the persona's current colour at entry. Movement across colours, and the after-effects of an encounter, belong to the play's arc.

Source to constraint, per file.

- **[awe](process_awe.md)** (the root): Awe as process: vastness plus need for accommodation, the small self in the widening. Anchored by Keltner & Haidt (2003) -- the two core appraisals; Piff et al. (2015) -- the small self and the outward opening.
- **[beauty](process_beauty.md)** (colour): Perceptual abundance overruns the frame; awe without menace. Anchored by Shiota, Keltner & Mossman (2007) -- the aesthetic elicitors and self-diminishing absorption; Keltner & Haidt (2003) -- beauty as a flavouring dimension.
- **[threat](process_threat.md)** (colour): The sublime; vastness that menaces while it overwhelms, and the one resolution in awe that can end in a self enlarged. Anchored by Burke (1757) -- the sublime as power that compels; Kant (1790) -- the defeat of imagination turning into the elevation of reason; Keltner & Haidt (2003) -- threat as a flavouring dimension.
- **[moral](process_moral.md)** (colour): Elevation; virtue witnessed in another exceeds the frame for what people can be. Anchored by Keltner & Haidt (2003) -- virtue and exceptional ability as flavouring dimensions of awe.
- **[sacred](process_sacred.md)** (colour): The numinous; vastness read as transcendent, supernatural, or cosmic. Anchored by Otto (1917) -- the numinous and the mysterium tremendum et fascinans; Keltner & Haidt (2003) -- the supernatural as a flavouring dimension.
