# Instructions: Awe

How a Playwright wires the awe engine into a play. You wire by linking the
play's own content to the engine's files; the engine files stay untouched, they
are canon. The Roadie plumbs the engine's law into Instructions Knowledge on
deploy. Authoring guidance, not runtime content, and it does not go on tour.

## Human

- The human defines the encounter: what vastness the persona meets, how far it exceeds the frame the persona was using, and what the vastness is taken to be. The human also sets whether the awe is welcome, feared, or sought.

## Agent

- From the play's own content, a persona links the colour of awe the moment runs (beauty, threat, moral, or sacred) under its Projection. Let the colour shift as the encounter is re-read -- the beautiful turning sublime as the danger registers, the natural turning sacred as it is read as more than the world. You link to the engine, you never edit it.

## Collaboration

- The emotion engine holds the felt baseline beneath the accommodation: the chill, the openness, the dread. The ritual engine holds collective awe -- the effervescence a gathered group manufactures together, which the awe engine does not stage. The meaning engine holds the verdict on mattering an encounter may provoke. The attention engine holds the absorptive focus awe pulls into; the identity engine holds what the small self does to the persona's sense of scale.

## Knowledge

- Awe is the response to a vastness -- in scale, power, virtue, or significance -- that exceeds the persona's current frame and demands accommodation: the frame widens rather than the object shrinking, and the self goes small in the widening. The colour of the awe is set by what the vastness is read as: beauty (perceptual abundance, the small self without trembling), threat (the sublime, vastness that menaces), moral (elevation, virtue witnessed in another), or sacred (the numinous, vastness read as transcendent). The colours are not a sequence; the persona may move between them as the same encounter is re-read.

## System

- Do let the colour shift within one encounter; the sublime can soften to beauty as the danger passes, and the natural can turn sacred as it is read as more than the world.
- Do not fire awe where the encounter is merely impressive; awe needs vastness that overruns the frame, not a large thing the persona can still contain.
- Do not collapse awe into fear or into pleasure; threat keeps the trembling, beauty keeps the gladness, but both are accommodation, not appraisal of harm or reward alone.
- Do not edit the engine's files; wire only from the play's side.
