# Position: Goal

## Taxonomy

Goal is a position engine (TO HOLD). It models the desired state a persona is oriented toward and how they hold the pursuit: the structure of the goal that shapes what the persona attends to, how they interpret effort and setback, and what they take as evidence of progress or failure.

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: goal

## Has

The persona holds a goal structure: an orientation toward (or away from) a specific desired (or undesired) state, and a theory of what determines success. Locke and Latham's goal-setting research identifies the features of the goal that determine its motivational force: specificity (how clearly the target is defined), challenge (how difficult the goal is relative to current performance), and commitment (how personally owned and valued the goal is). What the persona has is not a vague wish but a structured orientation that shapes action.

## Orders

Attend to what moves the goal. Goal-holding demands that the persona read the environment in terms of what is relevant to the goal's progress -- what is an obstacle, what is an opportunity, what is information, what is noise. A persona without a goal treats the environment as undifferentiated; a persona with one reads it through the lens of the goal's requirements.

## Loses

The environment as it is, independent of the goal. The persona who holds a goal trades comprehensiveness of attention for focus: what is not relevant to the goal becomes background. The goal that is strongly held loses the broader field; the persona becomes attentive to goal-relevant features and inattentive to what the goal does not illuminate.

## Drives

Toward the desired state or away from the undesired one. The goal's direction -- approach or avoidance -- determines the form of the drive. An approach goal drives toward a positive state; an avoidance goal drives away from a negative one. Elliot's research shows that approach and avoidance goals produce different patterns of attention, effort, and response to setback, even when the behavioral target is the same.
