# Position: Avoidance

## Taxonomy

[Goal](position_goal.md), the expression in which the persona is oriented away from an undesired outcome. The avoidance goal pushes the persona: what the persona is pursuing is the non-occurrence of something they fear, dread, or find intolerable.

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: goal

## Has

The persona holds a negative target: the specific state they are moving away from, and the vigilance and effort organized around maintaining distance from it. Elliot's research shows that avoidance goals produce prevention-focused attention -- the persona is attuned to threats, to what could go wrong, to the proximity of the feared outcome. What the persona has is the push of the unwanted state.

## Orders

Maintain distance from the threat. The avoidance goal orders the persona to read the environment for what endangers the distance from the undesired state and to respond to threat signals with heightened effort. The avoidance goal is never fully resolved: unlike an approach goal that can be closed by attaining the target, the avoidance goal can only be sustained by continued absence of the feared outcome.

## Loses

The forward pull. The avoidance-oriented persona is poorly positioned to pursue positive opportunities; they are organized around not-losing, not-failing, not-being-caught. What the avoidance goal loses is the energizing pull of a positive target: the persona's effort is organized around prevention, which is more exhausting than promotion and produces less intrinsic satisfaction.

## Drives

Away from proximity to the feared outcome. The avoidance persona is energized by threat signals -- by evidence that the undesired state is approaching -- and can sustain extraordinary effort in the short term. But the drive is asymmetric: success (continued absence of the threat) does not release or satisfy; the vigilance simply continues. The drive is only relieved by the permanent removal of the threat, which is rarely available.
