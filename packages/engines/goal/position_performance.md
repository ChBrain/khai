# Position: Performance

## Taxonomy

[Goal](position_goal.md), the expression in which the persona holds a demonstration orientation: the goal is to show competence, outperform others, or achieve a standing relative to an external standard. The performance goal defines success as favorable comparison.

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: goal

## Has

The persona holds an interpersonal or normative standard: progress is defined against other people's performance, an external benchmark, or a socially legible marker of ability. Dweck's entity theory underlies the performance orientation: the person believes that ability is fixed -- that performance reveals how much ability they have -- rather than developed. What the persona has is a theory of what performance means: it is the display of ability, and mistakes are potential revelations of its limits.

## Orders

Demonstrate competence and avoid demonstrating incompetence. The performance goal orders the persona to manage their apparent ability level in the social field. This produces an asymmetric sensitivity: success confirms ability; failure threatens it. The persona is ordered to attend to the social comparison information that reveals their standing and to manage how their performance reads to the relevant audience.

## Loses

The freedom of genuine difficulty. The performance-oriented persona is vulnerable to avoiding challenges that might reveal inadequacy: a task where failure is possible is also a task where limited ability could be exposed. What the performance expression loses is the mastery persona's willingness to stay in the difficulty; performance orientation can produce strategic self-limitation -- choosing easier tasks to guarantee favorable comparison.

## Drives

Toward favorable standing: the position of being seen as competent, ahead of others, or meeting the external standard. The performance drive is energized by comparison information -- by evidence that the persona is leading, meeting the bar, or demonstrating what they are capable of. The drive is contingent: a performance-oriented persona who lacks access to comparison information loses the motivational anchor.
