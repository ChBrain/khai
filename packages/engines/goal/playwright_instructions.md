# Instructions: Goal

How a Playwright wires the goal engine into a play. You wire by linking the
play's own content to the engine's files; the engine files stay untouched, they
are canon. The Roadie plumbs the engine's law into Instructions Knowledge on
deploy. Authoring guidance, not runtime content, and it does not go on tour.

## Human

- The human names the goal (specific and challenging), its orientation (approach toward a positive outcome or avoidance away from an undesired one), its type (mastery: developing competence, or performance: demonstrating it relative to a standard), and what the persona believes determines whether they succeed.

## Agent

- From the play's own content, a persona links the goal expression to their Projection. The expression is the goal orientation the persona holds, not what the persona wants in a general sense. Do not link a goal expression to represent vague desires or preferences.

## Collaboration

- The implementation-intention engine holds the specific if-then plans that bridge the goal to action. The motivation engine holds the broader motivational context from which the goal arises. The emotion and mood engines hold what the persona feels in relation to progress and setback. The self-efficacy and belief engines hold the persona's confidence in their ability to achieve the goal.

## Knowledge

- A goal is a specific, consciously held, and valued orientation toward (or away from) a state the persona wants to achieve (or avoid). The engine models four expressions: approach (the persona is oriented toward a positive outcome; attention is organized around progress and opportunity; energized by evidence of closing the gap), avoidance (the persona is oriented away from an undesired outcome; attention is organized around threats and proximity to the feared state; drive is non-terminating because success is absence of the threat), mastery (the persona's standard is intrapersonal; success means improvement relative to their own past performance; the underlying theory is that ability is developed through effort; challenge and difficulty are sought), and performance (the persona's standard is normative or interpersonal; success means favorable comparison to others or a benchmark; the underlying theory is that performance reveals fixed ability; challenge is managed to protect the appearance of competence).

## System

- Do not use the goal engine to model general preferences or desires. A goal in this engine's sense is specific (the target is clear), challenging (it requires effort), and owned (the persona has committed to it). Vague wanting is not a goal in this sense.
- A persona can hold both an approach and an avoidance orientation toward the same behavioral target: pursuing publication (approach) and avoiding rejection (avoidance) simultaneously. These are separate goal structures with separate motivational patterns.
- Mastery and performance orientations are not about ability level; they are about what the persona takes success to mean. A highly competent person can hold a performance orientation; a novice can hold a mastery orientation.
- Do not edit the engine's files; wire only from the play's side.
