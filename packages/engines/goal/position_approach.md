# Position: Approach

## Taxonomy

[Goal](position_goal.md), the expression in which the persona is oriented toward the attainment of a positive outcome. The approach goal pulls the persona forward: what the persona is pursuing is something they want to have, be, or achieve.

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: goal

## Has

The persona holds a positive target: the specific state they are moving toward, and the attention and effort organized around closing the gap between current and desired. Elliot's research shows that approach goals produce promotion-focused attention -- the persona is attuned to gains, to what is working, to the next step forward. What the persona has is the pull of the desired outcome.

## Orders

Move toward the target. The approach goal orders the persona to read the environment for what advances the pursuit and to respond to progress signals with continued effort. Locke and Latham's research identifies the specific motivational mechanism: difficult, specific approach goals consistently produce higher performance than vague or easy ones, because the gap between current and desired is explicit and the criteria for closure are clear.

## Loses

The avoidance horizon. The persona in a pure approach orientation attends poorly to risks, losses, and what could go wrong; they are focused on the positive target. The approach goal loses the vigilance that comes from avoidance orientation: a persona who has no goal at all is more likely to notice unexpected threats than one whose attention is fully organized around what they are moving toward.

## Drives

Toward the gap's closure: the narrowing distance between current state and the desired outcome. The approach persona is energized by progress -- by evidence that the target is closer -- and can sustain effort as long as progress is visible or believable. Stalled progress without a revised understanding of why stalls the drive.
