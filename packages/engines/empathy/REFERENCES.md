# Empathy: Reference

## Line of Work

Empathy as **position**: the baseline capacity a persona holds for taking in the inner state of another -- how open the channel is, and what form the opening takes. The domain does not model the persona's own emotion (that is the emotion engine's); it models the other-directed capacity that determines how much of another's interior the persona receives, and what it does with what it receives.

The spine is the multi-component model of empathy: cognitive perspective-taking, affective resonance, compassionate action, and empathic distress are distinct capacities that can combine in any configuration. A persona may read others clearly without feeling them; may feel them without acting; may be overwhelmed by what it feels and withdraw.

## Origin

Social neuroscience and developmental psychology of empathy, applied directly as position constraints.

| Source | Key Work | Scope |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Jean Decety &amp; Philip Jackson** | *"The Functional Architecture of Human Empathy"* (2004) | Multi-component model: shared affect, self/other awareness, mental flexibility, and self-regulation as distinct empathy dimensions. |
| **Daniel Batson** | *Altruism in Humans* (2011) | Empathic concern as distinct from personal distress: feeling *for* the other vs. feeling *overwhelmed by* the other. |
| **Simon Baron-Cohen** | *Zero Degrees of Empathy* (2011) | Cognitive and affective empathy as separable: zero cognitive empathy vs. zero affective empathy are different failure modes. |
| **Tania Singer &amp; Claus Lamm** | *"The Social Neuroscience of Empathy"* (2009) | Neural basis of empathic resonance and the regulation that keeps it from tipping into personal distress. |

## Restrictions

What the engine refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Own emotion**: The engine models the capacity to receive another's state, not the persona's own appraisal-driven feeling. The emotion engine governs the persona's own discrete emotion; this engine governs the channel to the other's.
- **Trust and attachment**: The engine models the capacity for other-directed resonance, not the bet the persona places on the other (trust engine) or the strategy it uses to seek closeness under threat (attachment engine). Empathy is about reception, not the strategy of relating.
- **Social skill**: The engine models the capacity as a position -- a stable baseline -- not the social skills the persona deploys in interaction. How the persona performs, expresses, or uses its empathic capacity in a specific scene is owned by the persona's Action and Tell sections.
- **Compassion fatigue as trait**: The engine models empathic distress as a running state -- what happens when the channel takes in more than it can hold -- not as a chronic professional condition. Long-term compassion fatigue is a biographical fact owned by the persona's heritage and arc.

## Encoding

Source to constraint, per file.

- **[empathy](position_empathy.md)** (the anchor): The baseline capacity to take in another's inner state -- the channel and its width -- before any particular other has appeared. Anchored by Decety &amp; Jackson (the multi-component model).
- **[cognitive](position_cognitive.md)** (expression): Perspective-taking without resonance -- the other's state modelled clearly and accurately from outside. Anchored by Baron-Cohen (cognitive empathy as separable from affective).
- **[affective](position_affective.md)** (expression): Resonance -- the other's state taken in and felt, not just read. Anchored by Singer &amp; Lamm (empathic resonance and its regulation).
- **[compassionate](position_compassionate.md)** (expression): The reception becomes a reach -- the persona is moved toward action on the other's behalf. Anchored by Batson (empathic concern as distinct from personal distress; the prosocial action tendency).
- **[distressed](position_distressed.md)** (expression): The channel takes in more than it can hold; the other's state floods the self and the persona withdraws. Anchored by Batson (personal distress as the failure mode of affective empathy when self/other regulation fails).
