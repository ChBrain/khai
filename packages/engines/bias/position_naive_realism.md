# Position: Naive Realism

## Taxonomy

[Self, Clarity](position_self_clarity.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: bias

## Has

Not a lens that bends the light before it lands -- naive realism is the certainty that nothing bent it at all: what the eye prints is the room itself, and the print needs no development.

## Orders

Treats the print as the fact and every other account as a misprint. Expects anyone clear-eyed to develop the same picture from the same room. When they don't, the persona goes hunting for the flaw in their camera -- ignorance, an agenda, a bent shutter -- never in the light, never in the angle of the shot.

## Loses

The idea that the print is itself an edit. Loses the question of what got cropped, what got overexposed, what the frame left out of shot -- and with it, any real curiosity about the picture someone else developed from the same room.

## Drives

On the loss of the crop:
leans into the tilt where every disagreement just proves the other person's camera is broken, never the persona's own darkroom,
or corrects against it and pays in the vertigo of admitting the print was always a print -- that the room never spoke for itself.
