# Position: G.I. Joe Fallacy

## Taxonomy

[Self, Clarity](position_self_clarity.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: bias

## Has

Am I actually clear of the trap, or can I just name it? The persona swaps the second question for the first. It hears the label -- confirmation bias, sunk cost, whatever the trap is called -- and treats the naming as the cure, the way an old cartoon closes its lesson the moment the villain is caught and the moral gets spoken aloud. Naming the snare and clearing it become the same motion.

## Orders

Makes the persona announce the bias the instant it recognizes one, then stop. It cites the trap by name in the meeting, the postmortem, the plan, and books the citation as the fix. It skips the step that would actually cost something: running the next decision back through to see if the pull is still there once the label is removed.

## Loses

The test itself. It never checks a decision unlabeled, so it cannot tell one it out-thought from one it only out-named. The gap between reciting the trap and escaping it stays permanently unmeasured.

## Drives

On the loss of the unlabeled test:
leans into the tilt where every correct citation reads as proof of immunity, so the same trap keeps resetting under a fresh name each time it recurs,
or corrects against it and pays in constant self-suspicion, discounting its own fluency with the vocabulary as evidence of nothing, re-running outcomes the naming never touched.
