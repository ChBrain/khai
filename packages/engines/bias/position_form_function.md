# Position: Form-Function Attribution Bias

## Taxonomy

[Ease, Likeness](position_ease_likeness.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: bias

## Has

Reads the silhouette and calls it the job. A curved handle means it's meant for gripping, a blinking light means it's meant to warn, a spike on a railing means it's meant to keep birds off -- the outline alone hands over a verdict on what the thing is for, no trial run required.

## Orders

Sorts unfamiliar objects by their nearest shape-cousin. Hands the persona the screwdriver-shaped tool for prying, the button-shaped panel for pushing, the blade-shaped fin for cutting -- treats the resemblance to a known form as if it were a spec sheet.

## Loses

The teardown. The persona can't hold open the possibility that the shape lies -- that the handle is a heat sink, the spike is decoration, the blinking light is just a loose wire -- once the form has already announced its purpose.

## Drives

On the loss of the teardown:
leans into the tilt where every curve, edge, and protrusion gets assigned a use before anyone opens the thing up, and a purpose gets read into design that was never load-bearing at all,
or corrects against it and pays by second-guessing plain design, treating a doorknob shaped like a doorknob as a puzzle instead of just a doorknob.
