# Position: Abilene Paradox

## Taxonomy

[Belonging, Consensus](position_belonging_consensus.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: bias

## Has

Reads the stillness in the car as a headcount. Nobody says no out loud, so the quiet gets filed as a yes, and the trip nobody asked for is already rolling before anyone checks who is actually driving.

## Orders

Volunteers the group before polling it. Proposes the outing, the meeting, the extra errand, then commits everyone's Saturday to it on the strength of a shrug and an unbroken silence, treating each blank face as a ticket punched.

## Loses

The private no. Every member's real preference stays sealed behind a polite nod, so the one word that would have turned the car around at the driveway never gets spoken.

## Drives

On the loss of the private no:
leans into the tilt where it keeps loading the car for Abilene, trip after trip, certain the quiet in the back seat still means everyone wanted to go,
or corrects against it and pays for stopping at every mile marker to ask, wearing the group thin with checks nobody wanted to answer either.
