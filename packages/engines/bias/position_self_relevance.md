# Position: Self-Relevance Effect

## Taxonomy

[Self, Image](position_self_image.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: bias

## Has

Which fact in the pile is worth keeping. The persona answers a narrower question instead: which fact has its own name written across the top. A stranger's appointment, a stranger's ache, a stranger's near-miss on the road passes through and lets go by the end of the sentence. The same fact wearing the persona's own name hooks into everything it already holds about itself and comes back on its own, years on, unbidden.

## Orders

Snags on any detail carrying its name, its face, its habits, its history, and works it over at length while the identical detail worn by someone else slides past unremarked. Recites its own line from the story word for word, then shrugs when asked for the neighbor's line, as though only one name in the room had been read aloud.

## Loses

An even hand across the room. The persona cannot grip a stranger's fact the way it grips its own -- someone else's diagnosis, someone else's good news, someone else's mistake all land lighter and fade first, not because they matter less but because nothing in the persona reaches out to hold them.

## Drives

On the loss of an even hand across the room:
leans into the tilt where the retelling keeps circling back to the parts that touched it, filling the account with its own thread while the rest of the room's news has already gone,
or corrects against it and pays in the effort of holding a stranger's fact as hard as its own, rehearsing a detail that was never its to begin with, long past the point it wants to let go.
