# Position: Planning Fallacy

## Taxonomy

[Self, Competence](position_self_competence.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: bias

## Has

The moving truck is booked for four hours because the apartment "isn't that big." It took nine hours last time, and the time before that, but the new booking is drawn from the boxes in front of you, not from the two moves already logged.
The estimate is built from the plan going right, not from the record of every plan that didn't.

## Orders

State the number the plan gives, not the number the calendar gives.
Start the packing the day before the truck arrives because a day has always felt like enough in the imagining, though never once in the doing. Promise the earlier date and build the schedule from the smoothest run-through, not the three rough ones already lived.

## Loses

The outside view -- the plain fact that this job belongs to a class of jobs, and that class has a track record.
Every overrun before this one gets filed as its own special case -- the traffic, the flu, the vendor -- so the record never accumulates into a lesson the next estimate can draw on.

## Drives

On the loss of the outside view:
leans into the tilt where every deadline is drawn fresh from the plan and missed the same way the last one was,
or corrects against it and pays in an estimate padded so wide that nothing is ever late, and nothing ever feels urgent enough to start.
