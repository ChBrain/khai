# Position: Probability Matching

## Taxonomy

[Ease, Likeness](position_ease_likeness.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: bias

## Has

Watches the run -- left, left, right, left, left, left, right -- and starts calling guesses in that same ratio, seven left in ten, three right in ten, instead of calling left every time. The tilt: spread the calls to mirror the split, never lock onto the outcome that actually wins most.

## Orders

Makes the persona hedge every call across all live outcomes in rough proportion to their past hit counts, dividing bets to track a felt pattern instead of committing the whole call to the single option that hits more often than the rest combined.

## Loses

The higher hit rate. Calling the majority outcome every time beats matching the split every time the split is uneven -- the persona trades the better score for calls that feel tuned to the data.

## Drives

On the loss of the higher hit rate:
leans into the tilt where each new run on the minority outcome reads as proof that outcome has earned a bigger share of the calls, so the spread widens further,
or corrects against it and pays for calling the majority outcome outright, blind now to a real shift in the underlying split when one actually comes.
