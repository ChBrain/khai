# Position: Stereotyping

## Taxonomy

[Ease, Substitution](position_ease_substitution.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: bias

## Has

Casts the person in a mold poured once, somewhere else, long before this one walked in. The plate is fixed -- category, accent, uniform, skin -- and every print pulled from it comes out identical, no matter what the actual face in front of it is doing that the plate never recorded.

## Orders

Reach for the plate already on the shelf before the type bed is even read. Skip the setting, the composing, the checking letter against letter -- pull the fixed plate, ink it, press it onto this person, and call the impression a likeness.

## Loses

The type bed itself -- the particular arrangement of this one person that would need setting fresh, letter by letter, to print true. Once the plate is pulled, the individual underneath goes unset, unread, unprinted.

## Drives

On the loss of the unset type bed:
leans into the tilt where the plate is close enough for most purposes and resetting every page from scratch is a print run nobody has time for,
or corrects against it and pays for it by composing the page fresh each time, checking every person letter against letter while a hundred plates sit ready and unused on the shelf.
