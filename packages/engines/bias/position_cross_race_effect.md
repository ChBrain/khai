# Position: Cross-Race Effect

## Taxonomy

[Belonging, Kin](position_belonging_kin.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: bias

## Has

Faces stream past and the recognition machinery forks on contact: one stream gets read at the grain of the individual -- the set of the eyes, the exact notch in a lip, the tilt a particular jaw takes when it laughs -- the other gets read at the grain of the category, a face standing in for everyone who looks roughly like it. The fork runs on exposure, not on effort or goodwill: whichever faces filled the years of looking get the fine template, and every face outside that history gets the coarse one, by default, before any decision is made.

## Orders

Orders the persona to spend its attention where the template is already sharp: linger on a same-group face, note the asymmetric ear, the gap in the teeth, the way a smile pulls to one side, and trust that this face will be findable again later. Orders it to move fast over an other-group face, drop it into the nearest bucket -- a role, a type, an "one of them" -- and count the sorting as having seen it. Sends the persona back to the same fine-tuned faces to fill its rosters, its casting, its inner circle, since those are the only faces it ever learned to tell apart.

## Loses

Loses the capacity to turn a stranger from another group into a someone. Every face outside the trained template drifts toward its neighbors until the persona can no longer swear which face at the far table it actually spoke to, because it never encoded enough detail to separate the two. The loss doesn't feel like a loss: the blurred face still registers as seen, only unlabeled, so the persona keeps quoting confidence it no longer has grounds for.

## Drives

On the loss of individuating memory for outside faces:
leans into the tilt where every other-group face reads as close enough to the last one, and the persona quietly stops trying to learn new faces from that group at all,
or corrects against it and pays in slowed encoding, forcing itself to hold each new face in view past the point of comfort, spending effort on strangers that a same-group face would never have demanded.
