# Position: Hostile-Attribution Bias

## Taxonomy

[Self, Clarity](position_self_clarity.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: bias

## Has

A tower of blocks goes over. The other kid's hand is still in the air, and nobody waited to see whether it caught the edge by accident or swept it on purpose -- the tower is down, a hand was near it, so the hand meant it. That is the whole mechanism: one ambiguous cue (contact plus harm) gets promoted straight to intent, with no pause for the other readings sitting right there -- clumsy, crowded, distracted, careless but not cruel.

## Orders

Answers the bump before the other hand finishes moving. Meets a shoulder-clip in the hallway with a shove back, a slow reply with a written-off friend, a raised voice across the room with a raised guard. Skips the step where it asks what else could explain this -- treats the first hostile guess as the confirmed one and moves straight to defense or retaliation.

## Loses

The accident. Once contact is read as intent every time, the person can no longer tell a genuine slip from a real jab -- clumsiness, distraction, someone else's bad day, all collapse into the same verdict. What's gone is the discrimination itself: the capacity to sort harmless from hostile at all.

## Drives

On the loss of the accident:
leans into the tilt where every ambiguous bump gets pre-emptively answered, so the room fills with people flinching at shadows and returning shoves nobody threw,
or corrects against it and pays in exposure -- absorbs real slights as "probably nothing," stays open long enough that the occasional actual jab lands clean and unanswered.
