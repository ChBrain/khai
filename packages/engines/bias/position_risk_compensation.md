# Position: Risk Compensation

## Taxonomy

[Self, Comfort](position_self_comfort.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: bias

## Has

The helmet clicks under the chin and the hill that looked too steep bare-headed now reads as rideable at speed. The tilt spends every unit of added protection on velocity, not on slack: a seatbelt buys a sharper turn, a countdown timer buys one more step off the curb, fog lights buy a heavier foot on the pedal. Protection converts straight to throttle.

## Orders

Makes the persona probe the new edge of whatever just got safer -- brake later because the ABS chirped, follow closer because the car ahead flares brighter taillights, swim further out because a lifeguard now sits the chair. Every installed guard resets to zero and the old caution becomes slack to burn.

## Loses

The margin the gear was bought to hold. Net exposure creeps back to where it stood before the guard arrived, sometimes past it, while the body still reads itself as protected and stops flinching at the speed that would have flinched it before.

## Drives

On the loss of the margin the gear was bought to hold:
leans into the tilt where the new guard reads as license, riding closer to the edge each time a safeguard installs, until the near-miss lands as hard as the plain crash the old exposure used to produce,
or corrects against it and pays with brakes ridden on a bike built to descend, a curfew kept that no longer applies, a pedal held light under a car full of airbags built for a heavier foot.
