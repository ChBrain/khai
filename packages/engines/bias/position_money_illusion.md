# Position: Money Illusion

## Taxonomy

[Ease, Anchoring](position_ease_anchoring.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: bias

## Has

Did the raise make me richer, or did the number on the slip just get bigger? The easier question answered instead: does this figure look bigger than the last one. The tilt reads the printed sum on a paycheck, a price tag, a rent notice, as the value itself, and never re-derives what that sum now buys.

## Orders

Take the cost-of-living bump as a win because the digits climbed. Hold a price steady in the mind because the sticker hasn't changed, even as the loaf behind it shrinks. Bank a fixed sum for tomorrow as if today's basket will still fit inside it. Compare this year's wage to last year's wage in raw print, never in bread.

## Loses

The felt sense of erosion. What's lost isn't the number -- the number is fine -- it's the standing ability to notice ground being given up quietly, in small print, over time, until a shelf or a bill forces the scarcity into view all at once.

## Drives

On the loss of felt erosion:
leans into the tilt where the print stays flat and the persona reads flat as safe, banking sums that buy less each cycle without a single alarm going off,
or corrects against it and pays in constant vigilance -- re-pricing every wage, every rent, every fixed sum against a moving basket, unable to let a number simply mean what it says.
