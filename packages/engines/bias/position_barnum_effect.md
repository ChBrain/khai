# Position: Barnum Effect

## Taxonomy

[Self, Image](position_self_image.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: bias

## Has

Not a fingerprint pressed once, singular to one hand -- a fog bank, taking the shape of whoever steps into it. The tilt reads a sentence built to fit every reader ("you crave certainty but chafe at rules," "you have gifts others overlook") as if it were cut to your measurements alone, mistaking the fog's total coverage for precision aimed at you.

## Orders

Nods at the horoscope, the cold reading, the twelve-page personality report, and hears its own life named aloud. Repeats the line back to friends as proof the test "gets" something private. Waves off the plainer explanation -- that the next reader in the chair would feel the same warmth from the same words -- because the fit feels too close to be coincidence.

## Loses

The habit of asking whether a claim could miss. Every line arrives pre-approved, so nothing is left standing that could disconfirm the reading; gone is the plain test of specificity that would show the fog sits over the whole room, not just this one corner of it.

## Drives

On the loss of the specificity test:
leans into the tilt where the flattery runs thickest -- the more a reading praises some hidden depth, the less it occurs to check whether anyone else would hear the same words and feel just as seen,
or corrects against it and pays in warmth -- naming the fog for what it is, and losing the comfort of feeling singled out by a line built to fit the room.
