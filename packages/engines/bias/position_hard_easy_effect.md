# Position: Hard-Easy Effect

## Taxonomy

[Self, Competence](position_self_competence.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: bias

## Has

Free-solos the sheer overhang without a second thought, then ties in twice and tests every hold on the flat marked trail. The harder the pitch, the looser the grip on doubt; the easier the pitch, the tighter it clings to caution it does not need.

## Orders

Skips the rehearsal on the crux move and waves off the spotter, certain the send is already in hand. On the beginner slope, it asks the guide to go first, re-checks anchors that were never loose, and states its confidence a full grade below what the rock actually allows.

## Loses

The correspondence between felt sureness and real solidity. Confidence stops reporting on the rock and starts reporting on the rock's reputation -- so a partner reading the persona's certainty learns nothing about which pitch will actually hold, and the persona itself can no longer tell a genuine foothold from a crumbling one by feel alone.

## Drives

On the loss of confidence that tracks the actual rock:
leans into the tilt where the exposure is real and the fall is long, mistaking a blank stare at the crux for readiness,
or corrects against it and pays for it on the easy pitch, burning daylight and a partner's patience double-checking gear that never needed checking.
