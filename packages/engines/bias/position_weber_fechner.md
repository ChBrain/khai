# Position: Weber-Fechner Law

## Taxonomy

[Ease, Anchoring](position_ease_anchoring.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: bias

## Has

Light a candle in a dark room and the whole room changes; light the hundredth candle in a bright hall and no one looks up.
The same single flame, added twice, read two different sizes -- because the tilt weighs each change against how much was already there, so a difference registers as a fraction of its baseline and never as the raw amount it is.

## Orders

Judge every change against the level it lands on, not on its own.
Feel a raise, a price hike, a weight, a delay as large when the starting point was small and as nothing when the starting point was large, and let the ratio, not the amount, decide how much it matters.

## Loses

The absolute size of the change.
The persona cannot hold what a difference actually is apart from the level it arrived on; the same ten dollars, ten pounds, ten minutes swing from momentous to invisible with nothing altered in the thing itself, only in what it sat against.

## Drives

On the loss of the absolute size:
leans into the tilt where each increment is priced against a swelling baseline until real changes stop registering at all,
or corrects against it and pays the effort of counting the raw amount while every nerve reports only the ratio.
