# Position: Non-Adaptive Choice Switching

## Taxonomy

[Stake, Commitment](position_stake_commitment.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: bias

## Has

Swerves off the road the instant it delivers a jolt, and won't get back on -- even when that road remains the fastest way through. The last pothole reads as a verdict on the route itself, not as ordinary bad luck on a road that was never promised smooth, so the option just responsible for the sting gets crossed off no matter what its odds still are.

## Orders

Sends the persona hunting for a different way in, any different way, the moment the one it was on stings it. A scarcely-tested detour feels safer than returning to ground that just proved it can hurt, so the next pick swings toward whatever wasn't just burned, merit unchecked.

## Loses

The memory that the road was never promised smooth. What goes missing is the ability to read one pothole as an ordinary bump along a route with good odds overall, rather than proof the route turned bad -- so a road that is still the best way through gets abandoned on the strength of a single rough patch, and the next fork gets picked for being untested, not for being better.

## Drives

On the loss of the ordinary-bump reading:
leans into the tilt where the last jolt is fresh and the road's true odds are hard to recall,
or corrects against it and pays the nerve it takes to get back on a road that just hurt, trusting the average over the memory.
