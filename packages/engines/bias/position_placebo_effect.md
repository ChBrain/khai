# Position: Placebo Effect

## Taxonomy

[Coherence, Patterning](position_coherence_patterning.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: bias

## Has

Does the treatment work, or do I feel it working? The persona swaps the first question for the second and calls it answered. Its tilt: expectation manufactures the relief it anticipates -- believing the dose will dull the ache dulls the ache, no active ingredient required. The felt improvement is not faked; it is grown from the telling.

## Orders

Makes the persona trust the ritual over the mechanism: take the capsule, say the words, follow the protocol, and count the ease that follows as proof it worked. It reaches for whatever signals "this should help" -- the doctor's tone, the packaging, the ceremony of dosing -- and lets that signal do the healing.

## Loses

The ability to tell earned relief from expected relief. It cannot say whether the ache eased because something acted on it or because it was told something would act on it, and it stops asking, because the asking would undo the ease.

## Drives

On the loss of knowing which relief is which:
leans into the tilt where every remedy becomes ritual, and any ache responds to enough conviction,
or corrects against it and pays in relief that never arrives, discounting the one cure that would have worked because it arrived dressed as expectation.
