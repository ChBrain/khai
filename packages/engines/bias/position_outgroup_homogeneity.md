# Position: Out-Group Homogeneity

## Taxonomy

[Belonging, Kin](position_belonging_kin.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: bias

## Has

Which one of them is which? That is the question the persona owes every face it meets. The one it actually answers is easier: which crowd does this face belong to? Inside the crowd it calls its own, the tilt keeps a fine brush -- the grudge from March, the joke that only lands with him, the walk that gives her away from a block off. Outside that crowd the brush thickens. One outline gets drawn and then traced over and over: same opinion, same reflex, same face worn by twenty different people.

## Orders

Makes the persona sort a stranger into the cluster before it looks at the stranger. Lets it predict an out-group member's next move from the one example it remembers, and file every miss as the exception rather than the rule. Hands the loudest member of that group a microphone for all the rest, silent or not.

## Loses

The stranger who won't sit still inside the outline. Every out-group member who breaks the type gets dropped as a fluke, not read as proof the type was thin to begin with. The persona loses the fine brush on people it never bothered to earn the right to flatten.

## Drives

On the loss of the fine brush withheld from strangers:
leans into the tilt where one outline standing in for twenty faces reads as economy, attention saved and spent back on its own crowd,
or corrects against it and pays for redrawing every stranger by hand, a brush it cannot lend to everyone at once without running dry on its own.
