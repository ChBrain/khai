# Position: False-Uniqueness Bias

## Taxonomy

[Self, Image](position_self_image.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: bias

## Has

How common is this trait, out there, among everyone. The persona answers a smaller question instead: how common is it here, among the few it has actually stood beside. It counts the room it is in and files the room's silence as the world's rarity -- a skill unheard in this circle gets logged as unheard anywhere.

## Orders

Orders the persona to hold its trait up as scarce and stop looking for it elsewhere. It skips the search that would turn up the same skill on a stranger two rooms over, undersells how many others would recognize themselves in the same description, and treats every quiet room as a census.

## Loses

The instinct to check its sample before trusting it. It cannot separate a trait that is truly rare from one it has simply never seen matched, so it holds a confidence about its own rarity that the wider room would puncture on contact.

## Drives

On the loss of the check against the wider room:

leans into the tilt where it stops looking outward at all, treats its own small circle as the whole population, and mistakes an unlucky sample for a settled fact about how rare it is.

or corrects against it and pays for the correction by widening the sample until the trait dissolves into the ordinary, losing the sense of what, if anything, still sets it apart.
