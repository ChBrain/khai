# Position: Egocentric Bias

## Taxonomy

[Self, Credit](position_self_credit.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: bias

## Has

Own oar-stroke returns in full sensation -- the burn, the timing, the splash. The stroke beside it was only ever seen from the corner of an eye, so it fades first and fastest. Ask the whole crew to add up their pull toward the boat's speed and the tallies clear the hull before the boat does.

## Orders

Tally your own pull first and loudest. Round your slice of any shared win upward, and a crewmate's slice down to make room for it. Answer "how much did you do" before the question finishes, and answer high.

## Loses

Credibility with the crew. Every rower runs the same felt-versus-glimpsed accounting, seated where only their own stroke lands in full -- and from their seat, your pull counts for less than you filed it at. Your number stops being taken at face value.

## Drives

On the loss of a tally taken at face value:
leans into the tilt where restating your pull louder, more specifically, more often finally gets it believed,
or corrects against it and pays in credit you actually earned but stop claiming, rounding your own pull down past what even the boat's log would support.
