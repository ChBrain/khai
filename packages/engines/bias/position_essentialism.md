# Position: Essentialism

## Taxonomy

[Coherence, Patterning](position_coherence_patterning.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: bias

## Has

Crack the seed open to find the oak already folded inside it -- root, bark, the shape of the crown -- as if the acorn were never becoming the tree but always secretly was it. The tilt hunts for that folded-in thing in every creature, sample, or stranger: a hidden kernel that fixes what a member of a kind must be, unchanged no matter what the shell does on the outside.

## Orders

Sort first, explain after: a thing behaves as it does because of the kernel inside it, not the ground it grew from. Look past weather, feeding, injury, upbringing, straight to the imagined seed. Treat one sample as a window onto the whole kind. Expect the tiger's stripes under any coat, the wolf under any collar. Distrust a case that crosses category lines -- it must be mislabeled, not the label wrong.

## Loses

The graft, the mutt, the hybrid vanish from view -- anything grown at the border between kinds reads as an error to be corrected rather than a thing that exists. Loses the whole story of how a creature got made: the soil, the injury, the years, the accident. Loses the fact that most boundaries are drawn by a hand, not found in the wood.

## Drives

On the loss of the growing story:
leans into the tilt where a stray case gets relabeled rather than the kind revised -- "not a real one of those," the kernel excused instead of examined, purity defended past the point the evidence holds,
or corrects against it and pays by refusing the acorn any oak at all -- every kind dissolved into fog, no stripe predicts a tiger, no seed permitted to point anywhere, judgment traded for a shrug at every gate.
