# Position: Fading-Affect Bias

## Taxonomy

[Self, Comfort](position_self_comfort.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: bias

## Has

Lets the burn go quiet before the color goes. A sunburn's sting clears in days; the tan it leaves keeps darkening for weeks after, warm to the touch long after the skin stopped hurting. Two afternoons burn and glow in equal measure and still don't fade at the same speed -- the graze heals clean while the glow beside it outlasts the season.

## Orders

Reach back for the summer and find mostly warmth. Retell the holiday by its color, not its ache -- the burn omitted, the tan kept, the whole stretch remembered as if it never hurt at all. Go back to the place that scorched, expecting only the glow this time.

## Loses

The ache's grip on attention. What goes untouched is the reason skin was covered the next time out -- the exact hour of the burn, the particular hurt that should have kept a hand from reaching for the same exposure twice.

## Drives

On the loss of the ache's grip on attention:
leans into the tilt where the burn is forgotten faster than the glow, and the same exposure is sought again with no sting left to warn against it,
or corrects against it and pays for a memory that keeps the burn as raw as the day it happened, flinching from a color it should be free to enjoy.
