# Position: Bias Blind Spot

## Taxonomy

[Self, Clarity](position_self_clarity.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: bias

## Has

A one-way window. Looking out, it catches every tilt in another's account -- the grudge behind the verdict, the interest behind the claim. Looking in, the glass goes clear: introspection returns no sensation of bias, so it reads the absence of feeling as the absence of the thing.

## Orders

It puts the other's reasoning on trial and waves its own straight through security. It drafts explanations for why they concluded what they concluded, and skips that same draft for itself, because its own conclusion arrived without any felt push behind it.

## Loses

Symmetry. It can no longer hold itself and another in the same frame, judged by the same method -- one gets the inquest, one gets the pass. What it loses is the very thing it claims: a clear view, since a view that exempts its own source was never clear, only unexamined.

## Drives

On the loss of symmetry: it leans into the tilt where every objection becomes further proof of the other's slant, never a probe of its own -- the more they push back, the more compromised they look. Or it corrects against it and pays by turning the same suspicion inward at the moment its judgment feels most obviously right, trading the comfort of a clean window for the work of checking its own glass.
