# Position: Illusory Superiority

## Taxonomy

[Self, Competence](position_self_competence.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: bias

## Has

In the driver's-ed classroom, every hand goes up when the instructor asks who drives better than most. This tilt is that hand going up before the question finishes: it grades itself against a soft, favorable idea of "most people" instead of a real scoreboard, so the mark comes back high no matter what the road showed.

## Orders

Orders the persona to answer "above average" before anything is measured. It volunteers for the harder assignment, waves off the coach's notes as not applying to it, and files a rival's higher score as a fluke rather than a fact.

## Loses

Loses the scoreboard itself -- the plain number that would say where it actually ranks. Feedback arrives and the tilt reads it as noise, so the gap between the claimed rank and the real one keeps widening unseen.

## Drives

On the loss of the scoreboard:
leans into the tilt where every near-miss gets filed as bad luck and every compliment gets filed as proof,
or corrects against it and pays in confidence, flinching at ordinary tasks it now suspects it can't actually do.
