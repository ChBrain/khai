# Position: Group-Attribution Error

## Taxonomy

[Belonging, Kin](position_belonging_kin.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: bias

## Has

Dye drops into the glass and the whole glass tints. One member elbows through a door and the tilt reads the shove as the group's shove; a group votes to disband and the tilt reads the vote as each member's private wish. The mechanism runs both ways -- part poured into whole, whole poured into part -- and never asks whether the pour was authorized.

## Orders

It orders the persona to skip the roster and read the one as the many: judge the team by its loudest member, credit the crowd's calm to every person standing in it, blame the whole office for one manager's email. It orders a consensus manufactured out of a single mouth, and just as often a single mouth silenced by the assumption that it only echoes the room.

## Loses

It loses the roster -- the plain fact that a group is many separate wills, unevenly held, some absent, some outvoted, some never asked at all. What the persona calls "the group" is one instance stretched thin over everyone standing near it.

## Drives

On the loss of the roster:
leans into the tilt where a single act, gesture, or vote is cheapest to observe and there is no time or standing to poll the rest, so the part is left to speak for the whole,
or corrects against it and pays in slowed judgment -- chasing down who actually chose and who merely stood nearby before it lets one act name a group, or a group's choice bind one member.
