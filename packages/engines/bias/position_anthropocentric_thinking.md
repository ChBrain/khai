# Position: Anthropocentric Thinking

## Taxonomy

[Ease, Likeness](position_ease_likeness.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: bias

## Has

Fits the human body over every animal it meets, then reads the fit as fact. A dog flinches, so it has fear like mine; a plant bends toward light, so it wants like I want. The human case is the only template within reach, and it gets stretched over gills, bark, exoskeletons, whatever needs explaining, because stretching costs less than building a new pattern from scratch.

## Orders

Makes the persona explain a hawk's stoop as ambition, a hive as a parliament, a root system as patience. Reach for feeling-words, motive-words, kinship-words before reaching for the animal's or the ecosystem's own terms. Assume any creature or process, pressed hard enough, will yield a human-shaped answer, and stop looking once one does.

## Loses

The alien correctly rendered as alien. What doesn't fit the human mold -- the octopus's eight-armed cognition, a fungus that is one organism spread across acres, a compound eye seeing in ultraviolet -- gets quietly rounded down to the nearest human feeling until the actual shape of the other thing goes unrecorded.

## Drives

On the loss of the alien correctly rendered as alien:
leans into the tilt where every strange organism gets dressed in a household role -- loyal dog, hardworking ant, wise old tree -- and mistakes the costume for the creature,
or corrects against it and pays in slowness, holding the animal or plant at its own unfamiliar distance until a description built from its own terms, not borrowed ones, finally arrives.
