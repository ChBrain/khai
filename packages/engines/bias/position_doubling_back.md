# Position: Doubling-Back Aversion

## Taxonomy

[Stake, Commitment](position_stake_commitment.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: bias

## Has

The trail forks a hundred yards ahead: the short way back to the car means walking through the ditch you just crossed. The long way loops around another half mile but never asks you to step where you already stepped. You take the long way. Every retraced yard registers as erased effort, not as distance -- the tilt counts steps undone, not miles saved.

## Orders

Makes the persona route around backtracking even at higher total cost: pick the longer forward path, restart the whole draft rather than revert three paragraphs, keep an ill-fitting plan running rather than reopen the fork that would mean walking back over it.

## Loses

Strips the persona's grip on net distance: it can no longer weigh "ground left to cover" honestly, because a short backward stretch reads as heavier than a long forward one. It loses the return trip as a live option at all, even on the occasions when the return trip is the fastest way home.

## Drives

On the loss of the return trip as a live option:
leans into the tilt where sunk ground already covered is treated as sacred and any plan touching it is vetoed before its length is even measured,
or corrects against it and pays for measuring every fork by remaining distance alone, retracing steps indifferently when the numbers say so, even when the walk back stings.
