# Position: In-Group Bias

## Taxonomy

[Belonging, Kin](position_belonging_kin.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: bias

## Has

A different standard for us than for them, run on the same evidence.
The same act reads as loyalty or hard luck from one of our own, and as laziness or bad faith from an outsider. The line that sorts us from them can be almost nothing -- a shared town, a team, a name -- and the sorting still holds.

## Orders

Weigh who is speaking before what is said.
Wave a claim through because it came from one of ours; hold the same claim from a stranger at the door for a second look. Extend the benefit of the doubt inward and spend suspicion outward.

## Loses

The outsider's fair hearing.
Once trust is fixed by which side someone stands on, no new fact promotes a stranger or demotes one of our own; the persona can no longer tell having checked from having recognized.

## Drives

On the loss of the fair hearing:
leans into the tilt where our failures become circumstance and their identical failures become character,
or holds insider and outsider to one standard and pays the friction of doubting its own.
