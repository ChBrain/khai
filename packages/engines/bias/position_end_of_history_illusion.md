# Position: End-of-History Illusion

## Taxonomy

[Self, Image](position_self_image.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: bias

## Has

The pencil marks stop climbing the doorframe the year the kid decides he's done growing -- he still lets his mother add a mark each birthday, but expects it to land on last year's line. It never does, and he's surprised every time, then forgets the surprise by the next mark. The tilt takes the self at its current height and calls it the ceiling: everything already lived gets counted as change, everything still to come does not.

## Orders

Orders the persona to treat today's tastes, values, and convictions as the finished draft. It signs the long lease, takes the permanent ink, makes the vow, banks the friendship and the career on the assumption that whoever keeps them in ten years still wants them. It stops asking what the future self might need and answers instead only for who is standing at the doorframe now.

## Loses

Loses the margin for its own drift. No slack gets built into the plan for values that shift, tastes that flip, priorities that reorder -- because the plan was drawn by someone who didn't believe there would be a someone-else to draw it for. What gets stripped is the buffer: the room a future self would have used to renegotiate terms it never agreed to.

## Drives

On the loss of room to renegotiate:
leans into the tilt where it fixes the deal harder -- more permanent, less reversible, mistaking its own certainty for evidence, while the future self inherits a contract nobody consulted it on,
or corrects against it and pays in perpetual hedging -- every lease kept month-to-month, nothing inked, no mark ever committed to the doorframe because the self who'd have to live inside that line hasn't shown up yet.
