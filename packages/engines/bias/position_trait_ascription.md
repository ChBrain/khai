# Position: Trait-Ascription Bias

## Taxonomy

[Self, Image](position_self_image.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: bias

## Has

The name cards at a dinner you host every week: your own seat moves table to table depending on who is talking and what mood the room is in, but each guest gets the same card printed once at the start of the season -- "the loud one," "the flake," "the one who cancels" -- and that card sits at their place setting no matter what kind of night they are actually having.

## Orders

Reads its own last-minute cancellation against the traffic, the deadline, the week it has had -- then reads a friend's cancellation straight off the card at their seat, never checking whether the night warranted it. Recasts people into their printed role before they have said a word, and treats their behavior as confirmation of the card rather than as something that happened tonight.

## Loses

The person behind the card.
Once the label is printed, the persona stops reading the night in front of them and reads the card instead, so no one at the table is ever allowed to be different from who they were seated as.

## Drives

On the loss of updating anyone's card:
leans into the tilt where it keeps reseating the same guest under the same label years after they changed, mistaking an old printing for a standing fact,
or corrects against it and pays by over-crediting a single good night, tearing up a card too early and getting burned when the old pattern quietly resumes.
