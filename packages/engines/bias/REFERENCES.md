# Bias: Reference

## Line of Work

Bias as **position**: the resting tilt a persona brings to judgment before any evidence lands. It does not model the settling of a belief against contradicting evidence, the selection among options, or the act of yielding to a group (those are dynamic processes); it models the standing weight the scale already carries. The cut is deliberate: the comprehensive catalogue (Benson and Manoogian's Cognitive Bias Codex, some 180 biases) is organized by cognitive stage (what we filter, how we fill meaning, when we act fast, what we store), and that axis is already khai's map (attention, belief, decision, memory). To stay distinct, bias divides not by stage but by **motive**, what the lean serves. The core claim: judgment is never taken from a level scale, the tilt is systematic, and it sorts by what it protects: the self, the standing model, effort, the group, or the stake. A full classification of the standard corpus bears the cut out: 201 named biases sort into these five with no sixth motive left over, and the method and the catalogue are set out under Encoding.

## Origin

The heuristics-and-biases tradition in cognitive and social psychology, and the motivational accounts of why judgment tilts.

| Source                     | Key Work                                                             | Scope                                                                                     |
| :------------------------- | :------------------------------------------------------------------- | :---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |

| **Kahneman & Tversky**     | _"Prospect Theory: An Analysis of Decision under Risk"_ (1979)       | Loss aversion and the reference point: the stake family.                                  |
| **Festinger**              | _A Theory of Cognitive Dissonance_ (1957)                            | The drive to keep the standing model consistent: the coherence family.                    |
| **Nickerson**              | _"Confirmation Bias: A Ubiquitous Phenomenon in Many Guises"_ (1998) | Confirmation as a general tilt in seeking and weighing evidence (coherence).              |

## Restrictions

What the engine refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **The settling of a belief against disconfirming evidence:** Bias models the resting weight the scale carries (coherence); the update, denial, or rationalization that resolves a contradiction is **delegated to the belief engine**.
- **The selection among options:** Bias is the thumb on the scale the choice reads from; the act of selecting is **delegated to the decision engine**.
- **The act of yielding to the group:** Bias models the standing weight already given to the in-group (belonging); the dynamic conforming of one's reading to the group is **delegated to the conformity engine**.
- **The moral verdict:** Bias colors the reading a verdict inherits; the verdict of right and wrong is **delegated to the moral-judgment engine**.
- **The processing stage:** What is filtered, stored, or recalled is **delegated to the attention and memory engines**. Bias cuts across the stages by motive, never along them. The corpus below sets 44 such stage-only effects aside for exactly this reason: serial-position, spacing, peak-end, testing, source-confusion, and their kin are memory and attention mechanics with no motive to protect, so they are not bias-engine content.

## Encoding

Source to constraint. One position anchor (the resting tilt) and five motive families, each governing a catalogue of named biases.

- **[bias](position_bias.md)** (the anchor): The scale that does not rest level, read before the evidence lands.
- **Self (the ego):** [self](position_self.md) -- the tilt toward one's own competence, decency, and control.
- **Coherence (the standing model):** [coherence](position_coherence.md) -- the tilt toward what the persona already holds true.
- **Ease (the cheapest reading):** [ease](position_ease.md) -- the tilt toward the near, the fluent, and the first.
- **Belonging (the group):** [belonging](position_belonging.md) -- the tilt toward one's own people and their authority.
- **Stake (what is already held):** [stake](position_stake.md) -- the tilt toward not losing what is already the persona's.

### The classified catalogue
