# Bias: Reference

## Line of Work

Bias as **position**: the resting tilt a persona brings to judgment before any evidence lands. It does not model the settling of a belief against contradicting evidence, the selection among options, or the act of yielding to a group (those are dynamic processes); it models the standing weight the scale already carries. The cut is deliberate: the comprehensive catalogue (Benson and Manoogian's Cognitive Bias Codex, some 180 biases) is organized by cognitive stage (what we filter, how we fill meaning, when we act fast, what we store), and that axis is already khai's map (attention, belief, decision, memory). To stay distinct, bias divides not by stage but by **motive**, what the lean serves. The core claim: judgment is never taken from a level scale, the tilt is systematic, and it sorts by what it protects: the self, the standing model, effort, the group, or the stake.

## Origin

The heuristics-and-biases tradition in cognitive and social psychology, and the motivational accounts of why judgment tilts.

| Source                     | Key Work                                                             | Scope                                                                                     |
| :------------------------- | :------------------------------------------------------------------- | :---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Tversky & Kahneman**     | _"Judgment under Uncertainty: Heuristics and Biases"_ (1974)         | Availability, anchoring, and representativeness: the cheap-reading family (ease).         |
| **Kahneman & Tversky**     | _"Prospect Theory: An Analysis of Decision under Risk"_ (1979)       | Loss aversion and the reference point: the stake family.                                  |
| **Festinger**              | _A Theory of Cognitive Dissonance_ (1957)                            | The drive to keep the standing model consistent: the coherence family.                    |
| **Nickerson**              | _"Confirmation Bias: A Ubiquitous Phenomenon in Many Guises"_ (1998) | Confirmation as a general tilt in seeking and weighing evidence (coherence).              |
| **Kunda**                  | _"The Case for Motivated Reasoning"_ (1990)                          | Reasoning steered by what the self wants to conclude: the self family.                    |
| **Tajfel & Turner**        | _"An Integrative Theory of Intergroup Conflict"_ (1979)              | Social identity and in-group favoritism: the belonging family.                            |
| **Jost & Banaji**          | _"The Role of Stereotyping in System-Justification"_ (1994)          | The tilt to read the existing arrangement as just (belonging).                            |
| **Benson & Manoogian III** | _Cognitive Bias Codex / "Cognitive bias cheat sheet"_ (2016)         | The comprehensive catalogue the five families map, organized by stage rather than motive. |

## Restrictions

What the engine refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **The settling of a belief against disconfirming evidence:** Bias models the resting weight the scale carries (coherence); the update, denial, or rationalization that resolves a contradiction is **delegated to the belief engine**.
- **The selection among options:** Bias is the thumb on the scale the choice reads from; the act of selecting is **delegated to the decision engine**.
- **The act of yielding to the group:** Bias models the standing weight already given to the in-group (belonging); the dynamic conforming of one's reading to the group is **delegated to the conformity engine**.
- **The moral verdict:** Bias colors the reading a verdict inherits; the verdict of right and wrong is **delegated to the moral-judgment engine**.
- **The processing stage:** What is filtered, stored, or recalled is **delegated to the attention and memory engines**. Bias cuts across the stages by motive, never along them.

## Encoding

Source to constraint. One position anchor (the resting tilt) and five motive families, each governing a catalogue of named biases.

- **[bias](position_bias.md)** (the anchor): The scale that does not rest level, read before the evidence lands.
- **Self (the ego):** [self](position_self.md) -- self-serving attribution, overconfidence, optimism bias, illusory superiority, egocentric bias, the illusion of control, the bias blind spot, actor-observer asymmetry, false consensus, Dunning-Kruger.
- **Coherence (the standing model):** [coherence](position_coherence.md) -- confirmation bias, congruence bias, belief bias, conservatism, the backfire effect, the Semmelweis reflex, choice-supportive memory, selective perception, subjective validation.
- **Ease (the cheapest reading):** [ease](position_ease.md) -- the availability heuristic, anchoring, representativeness, base-rate neglect, mere exposure, the illusory-truth effect, framing, the conjunction fallacy, the affect heuristic.
- **Belonging (the group):** [belonging](position_belonging.md) -- in-group favoritism, out-group homogeneity, the bandwagon effect, authority bias, the halo effect, system justification, the cross-race effect, the ultimate attribution error, social-desirability bias.
- **Stake (what is already held):** [stake](position_stake.md) -- loss aversion, the sunk-cost fallacy, the endowment effect, status-quo bias, the IKEA effect, zero-risk bias, negativity bias, escalation of commitment, the default effect, the disposition effect.
