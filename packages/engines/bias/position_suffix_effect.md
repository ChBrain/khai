# Position: Suffix Effect

## Taxonomy

[Ease, Availability](position_ease_availability.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: bias

## Has

Reads back a phone number and the final digit is gone -- not unheard, but scrubbed by the throwaway "got it?" that landed right behind it.
Whatever sound follows the last item of a heard list takes that item's place in the fading trace, so the tail of anything spoken aloud arrives already erased, and only what the erased tail displaced stays available to recall.

## Orders

Trust the heard list to its end, then lose the end.
Carry the middle of what was said and act on it, and let the final item -- the one a cough, a chair scrape, a closing word came in behind -- drop out of the account as though it was never given.

## Loses

The last thing said.
The persona keeps the body of a spoken list and forfeits its tail, so the closing digit, the final name, the last instruction goes missing exactly when it was most recent and should have been surest.

## Drives

On the loss of the last thing said:
leans into the tilt where it builds on a heard list whose ending quietly fell off, certain it has the whole,
or corrects against it and pays the friction of asking for the final item again, distrusting an ear that was working fine until the sound behind it.
