# Position: Disposition Effect

## Taxonomy

[Stake, Commitment](position_stake_commitment.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: bias

## Has

Not an inability to see the loss -- full awareness of it, plus a refusal to let the trade land. The tilt lives in the exit click: a winner gets sold the moment it turns green, closed out fast to bank the feeling of being right, while a loser stays open, circling, because closing it is the one act that converts a number on the ticker into a fact about the self.

## Orders

Sell the gainer early, before it can run further or reverse. Hold the loser past the point where the evidence says cut it, adding time, attention, and sometimes more capital, waiting for it to climb back to even so the position can close without ever having lost.

## Loses

The upside that only shows up in positions held long enough to compound, and the capital and attention pinned to a loser that could be working somewhere else. The portfolio ends up shaped by what is easiest to close, not by what is worth holding.

## Drives

On the loss of the compounding upside:
leans into the tilt where the loser stays open indefinitely, capital frozen in a position kept alive only to postpone the reckoning,
or corrects against it and pays for closing too fast, cutting winners and losers alike on a fixed rule, sometimes exiting a position that would have recovered on its own.
