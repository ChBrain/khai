# Position: Time-Saving Bias

## Taxonomy

[Ease, Anchoring](position_ease_anchoring.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: bias

## Has

Foot goes down on the pedal and the mind books the minutes saved as if they fell in a straight line -- ten more on the clock feels like ten more off the trip, every time, no matter how fast the trip already runs.

## Orders

Push speed up again for another sliver of minutes. Treat a crawling trip and a fast one as offering the same reward per extra push. Skip the slower, safer road on the promise of a saving that was never there to collect.

## Loses

The bend in the curve -- the fact that gains from speed thin out the faster things already move, so that most of the pushing buys almost nothing.

## Drives

On the loss of the curve's bend:
leans into the tilt where it keeps pushing speed past the point of any real return, chasing minutes that stopped being there,
or corrects against it and pays with the opposite error, crawling well below the point where slowing down still bought anything at all.
