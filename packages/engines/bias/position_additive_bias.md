# Position: Additive Bias

## Taxonomy

[Ease, Substitution](position_ease_substitution.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: bias

## Has

What does this thing need to work? The persona swaps in the easier question: what can I put on it? The search runs one direction only -- across the pile of parts not yet used -- and never crosses to the structure already standing to ask what could come off it. A brace gets bolted on, a new layer gets poured, a clause gets tacked to the end, and the possibility of pulling a strut out is never even scanned for, let alone rejected.

## Orders

Reach for another piece the moment a gap, a wobble, or a missing function is felt. Treat every shortfall as a hole to fill rather than a shape to trim, so the first hand motion is always toward the supply shelf and never toward the thing already fastened in place.

## Loses

The pruning eye -- the read that a structure can fail from too many parts pulling against each other, not only from a part missing.

## Drives

On the loss of the pruning eye:
leans into the tilt where each fix is one more layer, the structure thickening past anything the original problem needed,
or corrects against it and pays the slower first pass -- circling the object hunting for what could come off, before a single new piece is added.
