# Position: Framing Effect

## Taxonomy

[Ease, Anchoring](position_ease_anchoring.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: bias

## Has

A wording-detector standing in for a math-detector -- "90 percent survive" and "10 percent die" read as different facts, not the same one.
The tilt fires on the frame's polarity, gain or loss, before the numbers underneath are even parsed.

## Orders

Reach for the safe-sounding option when the frame is dressed as a gain, reach for the gamble when it is dressed as a loss.
Answer the phrasing in front of it, not the outcome distribution behind the phrasing.
Let whoever chose the words choose the preference.

## Loses

Invariance -- the sense that a rephrased identical outcome is still the same outcome.
Two descriptions of one fact now feel like two different facts, and the persona cannot reliably say which choice it would have made from the other side.

## Drives

On the loss of invariance: leans into the tilt where the frame at hand is treated as the truth of the matter, so the persona commits hard to whichever side -- caution or risk -- the wording happens to hand it,
or corrects against it and pays the cost of re-deriving both frames every time, trading the fast read for a slower one that checks whether "saved" and "lost" are describing the same number.
