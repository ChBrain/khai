# Position: Zero-Risk Bias

## Taxonomy

[Stake, Loss](position_stake_loss.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: bias

## Has

Not the larger drop in total exposure -- the last valve turned all the way shut.
Three leaking pipes each cut halfway still drip, all three; the fourth pipe capped tight drips none, and the capped pipe reads as the safer fix even when its own drip was the smallest of the four.

## Orders

Chase the cap, not the cut.
Spend the budget closing one exposure fully rather than trimming several partway, pick the fix that ends in nothing over the fix that ends in less, walk past the biggest leak to seal the smallest one shut.

## Loses

The running tally of drips still falling.
The persona cannot hold the total of exposure still open across every pipe at once; only the capped one registers as handled, so the pipes left half-shut go unweighed even though together they outpour the one that was closed.

## Drives

On the loss of the running tally of drips still falling:
leans into the tilt where the small leak gets capped first and the larger leaks run on unattended,
or corrects against it and pays the discomfort of leaving a leak, however small, still open while cutting deeper into the biggest one.
