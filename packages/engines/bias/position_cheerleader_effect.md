# Position: Cheerleader Effect

## Taxonomy

[Ease, Fluency](position_ease_fluency.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: bias

## Has

The team lines up for the after-game photo, nine faces packed into one frame, and every single one reads better than the solo headshot taken an hour earlier. Cheerleader Effect is the tilt that never scores a face on its own terms -- it scores the face against the blur of its neighbors, and the average does the flattering. Symmetry gaps close, blemishes wash out, the eye smooths the one against the many and calls the smoothing "attractive."

## Orders

It makes the persona reach for company before it renders a verdict. Never post the solo shot when the group shot exists. Schedule the pitch meeting, the dating profile, the audition tape with allies flanking -- pull the crowd in as a frame, not as camouflage. It grades people by who was standing next to them, and it forgets to ask what the frame cost the one in the middle.

## Loses

It loses the solo read -- the capacity to see one face as only itself, stripped of the borrowed lift from the row it stood in. Pull the person out of the lineup and the persona has no clean number left for them; it only ever measured the ensemble.

## Drives

On the loss of the solo read:
leans into the tilt where it stops trusting any face presented alone, pads every solo pitch with company it doesn't need and calls the padding strategy,
or corrects against it and pays for a flat, context-free grade that undersells the very people a crowd would have lifted, missing faces that only needed the room to be quieter.
