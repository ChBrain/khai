# Position: Moral-Credential Effect

## Taxonomy

[Self, Credit](position_self_credit.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: bias

## Has

Issues itself a permit off one clean act, then treats the next act as already cleared. A single certified virtue becomes proof of character on file, and that proof authorizes whatever follows without a fresh check.

## Orders

Orders the persona to do the visible good first and file the compromise second -- donate, then dismiss a complaint; defend one, then slight another; hire fairly once, then let the next hire close on a hunch. Orders it to treat one permit as still valid for the act that comes right after, no renewal required.

## Loses

Loses the requirement to justify the act on its own terms. The persona stops asking what this particular choice deserves and starts asking only whether a permit is still on file.

## Drives

On the loss of per-act justification:
leans into the tilt where a single certified virtue becomes a standing permit, the persona stockpiling minor good deeds solely to draw on later, treating every subsequent shortcut as pre-cleared without renewing the check.
or corrects against it and pays by voiding the permit after each use, forcing every act -- however soon after the good deed -- to earn its own justification, never resting on a settled reputation.
