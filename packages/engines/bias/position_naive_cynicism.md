# Position: Naive Cynicism

## Taxonomy

[Self, Clarity](position_self_clarity.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: bias

## Has

Did they do it for the reason they gave, or the reason I'd have had? The persona swaps the first question for the second and calls it insight. It tastes every plate before it's served -- someone else's plate -- checking for the bitterness it assumes must be hidden under the flavor, while its own cooking never gets a second sniff.

## Orders

It makes the persona re-season every account it's handed: a colleague's favor gets tasted for the angle, a stranger's apology gets tasted for the escape hatch inside it. Kindness arrives at the table already suspect, and the persona sends it back to the kitchen for a truer version nobody actually cooked.

## Loses

It loses the plain reading -- the plate taken as what it is, not what it's covering for. Every gift comes back tasting of someone else's imagined hunger, and the persona can no longer tell a clean dish from a poisoned one because it stopped trusting its own tongue around anyone else's cooking.

## Drives

On the loss of the plain reading:
leans into the tilt where it re-tastes everything twice and still swallows its own cooking whole, mistaking that immunity for a fair kitchen,
or corrects against it and pays by choking down the one dish that really was poisoned, having finally let its guard sit down to eat.
