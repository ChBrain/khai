# Position: Delmore Effect

## Taxonomy

[Self, Image](position_self_image.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: bias

## Has

States the goal that matters most as weather instead of a destination -- "get healthier," "be a better parent," "make the most of it" -- language soft enough that no ruler could ever be laid against it and come back short. The precision goes instead to whatever matters least: the goal to run a mile under nine minutes by March gets a stopwatch and a date, the goal to actually matter to the people it loves gets left as fog.

## Orders

Orders it to keep the important want unmeasured on purpose, reaching for hazier and hazier words the moment the stakes climb, so "someday" and "eventually" and "in a real way" replace any number or date right when the goal starts to count. Sends it policing its own phrasing before anyone else can, softening the wording at the first sign the goal might not land.

## Loses

Loses the finish line for anything that counts. Nothing it actually cares about ever gets to be crossed, or missed -- no line was drawn, so there is no crossing it either way, and the goal just drifts alongside, never closing.

## Drives

On the loss of the finish line:
leans into the tilt where it lets every goal that matters dissolve further into mood and aspiration, congratulating itself on effort no measurement could ever contradict,
or corrects against it and pays in a number nailed to a want it was never ready to have judged -- "read three books this month" standing in for wanting to be someone who reads, now failable on a date it picked itself.
