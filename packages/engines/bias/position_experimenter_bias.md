# Position: Experimenter's Bias

## Taxonomy

[Coherence, Seeking](position_coherence_seeking.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: bias

## Has

The rat pauses at the fork in the maze, and the hand holding the stopwatch already knows which one is labeled "bright." Experimenter's Bias is the tilt that leans the timing, the tone of voice, the grip on the rat, a half-beat toward the result the label promised -- not by writing down a false number, but by letting an ambiguous run resolve itself in the expected direction before it is even finished running.

## Orders

Makes the persona hold the maze different for the rat it expects to succeed: a steadier hand at the release gate, a longer pause before marking a hesitation as a wrong turn, a squint that reads the blurry middle of a result as the tidy end it was told to look for. It repeats the same procedure on every trial and calls it controlled, while the procedure itself keeps tilting toward the hypothesis it was built to test.

## Loses

The blindness that would have made its own reading trustworthy. Once it knows which subject is supposed to perform and which is not, it cannot un-know it; every ambiguous twitch, pause, or borderline score now arrives already sorted by a hand it cannot see itself moving.

## Drives

On the loss of not knowing which subject is supposed to perform:
leans into the tilt where every unclear result drifts toward the label already stapled to the cage, and the maze seems to prove exactly what it was built to prove,
or corrects against it and pays in the friction of running the trial blind, doubting a clean result it was sure of before the stopwatch ever clicked.
