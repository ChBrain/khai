# Position: Impact Bias

## Taxonomy

[Self, Comfort](position_self_comfort.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: bias

## Has

How long will the win or the wreck still be felt a year out? The persona answers a nearer question instead: how loud does it sound right now, imagined in full color, with nothing else let into the frame. The promotion fills the whole picture; so does the diagnosis. Left out of the imagining is the rest of the week that keeps happening regardless -- the commute, the grocery list, the ordinary Tuesday that will sit right beside the extraordinary one and quietly cut it down to size.

## Orders

Chase the good news as if the glow will not fade, and dodge the bad news as if the ache never will. Take the harder path toward the promotion, certain the relief will keep outshining the effort for years. Turn down the surgery, or the move, or the breakup, certain the regret would sit forever at full weight. Every choice answers to the peak of the imagined feeling, never to how soon that peak actually levels off.

## Loses

The true half-life of the feeling. Not the fade itself -- that happens regardless -- but the sense of how soon it arrives: how fast the joy flattens into routine, how fast the grief loosens its grip enough to let the persona work again. Without that clock, every decision is made for a self still standing in the wreckage, or still floating on the win, long after both have quietly leveled off.

## Drives

On the loss of the true half-life of the feeling:
leans into the tilt where the big decision keeps chasing the next peak -- trading the ordinary good already in hand for a future glow expected to last, and bracing against a hurt that will in fact ease within a season as though it never will,
or corrects against it and pays in a flattened choice -- turning down a real chance at joy because the imagined payoff has been shrunk down to a month's worth of feeling, so a life optimized for the eventual plateau never gets to stand at the peak at all.
