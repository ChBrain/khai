# Position: Mere-Exposure Effect

## Taxonomy

[Ease, Fluency](position_ease_fluency.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: bias

## Has

Do I like this, or have I simply stopped bracing against it? The tilt answers the second question and files it under the first: a face, a jingle, a logo, a phrase crossed enough times sheds its friction, and the persona reads that thinning resistance as warmth toward the thing itself. Nothing about the thing changed. The count did.

## Orders

Orders the persona back to the same face, tune, name, slogan -- not to learn anything further about it, but because the next pass is what banks the glow. Pick the name seen thirty times over the one seen once, sight unseen, and mistake the returning for a verdict.

## Loses

Loses the seam between recognizing and endorsing. The persona can no longer feel where "I have met this before" ends and "this is good" begins, so a jingle looped past it forty times sits in memory with the same shine as a claim it actually tested and found true.

## Drives

On the loss of the seam between recognizing and endorsing:
leans into the tilt where whoever repeats longest and loudest wins the persona's fondness cheapest, no case ever argued,
or corrects against it and pays a standing suspicion of comfort, doubting every warm familiar thing as maybe just overplayed.
