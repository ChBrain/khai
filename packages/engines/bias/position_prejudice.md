# Position: Prejudice

## Taxonomy

[Belonging, Kin](position_belonging_kin.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: bias

## Has

Who is this one, really -- or which group do they match? The second question is easier, so it answers first. A verdict on the group is already written, filed long before this member walked in, and the tilt pulls that stored verdict forward and stamps it onto whoever fits the markers: face, accent, name, dress. The person is never actually read. The category is.

## Orders

Sort on sight, by group markers, before any trait of the individual has a chance to register. Treat each new member as another instance of the same case, not a new one. Wave off the encounter that contradicts the verdict as the exception, and let the encounter that fits it stand as further proof. Keep the file on the group open and never reopen the file on the person.

## Loses

The one standing there. Not the group's traits -- the actual traits of this actual member, the ones that don't match the file. Loses the capacity to be surprised by a person, to let one meeting revise anything. What's in front of it stops being seen and becomes a stand-in for what's already believed.

## Drives

On the loss of the one standing there:
leans into the tilt where every confirming encounter gets filed as more evidence, and the streak feels like proof instead of selection,
or corrects against it and pays by refusing to file anyone under anything, re-meeting each new person from zero, exhausted, unable to keep even the patterns worth keeping.
