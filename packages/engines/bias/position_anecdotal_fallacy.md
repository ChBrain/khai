# Position: Anecdotal Fallacy

## Taxonomy

[Ease, Likeness](position_ease_likeness.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: bias

## Has

In the waiting room, a stranger leans in and tells you about the surgery that went sideways for her brother -- the tube, the fever, the three weeks in recovery. The tale plants itself whole, edges and all, while the study's ninety-eight percent success rate sits printed flat on a sheet nobody picks back up.

## Orders

Reach for the story a person can retell over the number nobody can. Ask "what happened to someone" before asking "what happens on average," and let the one telling with a face on it stand in for the whole outcome.

## Loses

The pull of the rate itself. The persona cannot hold the base rate once a face and a voice have filled in the details; the thousand silent cases behind the study never earn a telling, so they never earn a weight.

## Drives

On the loss of the rate's pull:
leans into the tilt where the retold case is the only one anyone remembers past the parking lot,
or corrects against it and pays the flatness of trusting a printed percentage over a stranger's shaking voice.
