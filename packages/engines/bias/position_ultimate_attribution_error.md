# Position: Ultimate Attribution Error

## Taxonomy

[Belonging, Kin](position_belonging_kin.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: bias

## Has

Your kid shoves another kid at the party and it's "he's overtired, it's past his bedtime." The neighbor's kid shoves yours and it's "that family never taught him manners." Same shove, two different verdicts, and the verdict was fixed before the shove happened -- it rode in on whose kid it was.

## Orders

Sorts every act through the group filter first, the act itself second. Good deed, our side: character, it's who they are. Good deed, their side: fluke, luck, an exception that proves nothing. Bad deed, our side: circumstance, he was tired, she was pushed into it. Bad deed, their side: nature, it's what they're like. The sorting runs before the details of the act ever get read.

## Loses

The capacity to let a single act speak for itself. A friend's cruelty can't register as cruelty -- it gets explained away before it lands. A stranger's decency can't register as decency -- it gets waved off as a one-time thing. The persona stops learning anything new about anyone, because the group tag already wrote the ending.

## Drives

On the loss of reading the act on its own terms:
leans into the tilt where every fresh incident gets sorted on arrival, in-group slip-ups excused as bad luck and out-group slip-ups filed as more proof, out-group kindness dismissed as the exception that isn't real, each round hardening the line between who gets grace and who doesn't,
or corrects against it and pays the friction of defending an outsider's good act to an audience that wants it explained away, or naming an insider's fault out loud and wearing the charge of disloyalty that follows.
