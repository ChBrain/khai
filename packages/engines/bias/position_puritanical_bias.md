# Position: Puritanical Bias

## Taxonomy

[Self, Image](position_self_image.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: bias

## Has

Reads the backslide as a stain on the character, not a symptom of the room the body was standing in. Sees the second helping, the missed rent, the relapse into the bottle, and reaches past hunger, debt, and withdrawal for a single verdict: soft will, a soul gone slack. The mechanism swaps the circumstantial account for the moral one, every time a body fails to govern itself.

## Orders

Orders the persona to name the failure a sin before it asks what caused it. Preach discipline at the smoker, the sleeper who missed the alarm, the friend who poured one more; hand out shame and promises to do better in place of a question about the room, the stress, the wiring underneath. Treat every stumble as proof of a soul gone soft, never as proof the wind was against it.

## Loses

The circumstantial read. Cannot see scarcity, stress, or the addicted brain's own machinery as anything but an excuse dressed up -- every hard circumstance gets absorbed into a verdict on character, so the persona loses the one account that would explain the failure without shaming whoever failed.

## Drives

On the loss of the circumstantial read:
leans into the tilt where every stumble gets read as a stain on the soul, and the one who fell is scolded for a fall that gravity and the room conspired to cause,
or corrects against it and pays in the discomfort of forgiving what looks, on its face, like plain weakness -- granting the relapse its circumstance, and losing the clean satisfaction of a verdict.
