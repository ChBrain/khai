# Position: Denomination Effect

## Taxonomy

[Stake, Ownership](position_stake_ownership.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: bias

## Has

Treats a single folded note as too whole to break and the same sum in loose change as already halfway spent -- identical total, opposite readiness, sorted by shape alone.

## Orders

Guards the intact note and reaches past it, breaking small money first: the toll, the tip, the vending machine all come out of the loose pile, the bill stays folded in the pocket until the pile runs dry and there is nothing smaller left to spend.

## Loses

Loses the arithmetic that the pile and the note are the same money -- what signals "stop" is the shape of what remains, not the sum of it, so spending runs past what the actual total allows.

## Drives

On the loss of a single running total:
leans into the tilt where the note goes untouchable, hoarded long past the point it was needed, while the loose pile bleeds out unwatched underneath it,
or corrects against it and pays the cost of breaking the note early, turning a sum that was hard to spend into change that no longer resists spending at all.
