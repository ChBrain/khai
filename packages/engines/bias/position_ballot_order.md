# Position: Ballot-Order Effect

## Taxonomy

[Ease, Anchoring](position_ease_anchoring.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: bias

## Has

Not judgment earned by the option itself -- judgment earned by where the printer put it. The tilt lives in sequence: first name on the ballot, first item on the menu, first resume in the stack pulls extra weight simply for standing closest to the top.

## Orders

Scans down a list and settles before reaching the end. Grants the top slot credit it never argued for. Treats order of appearance as if it were order of merit, and stops comparing once a leader is picked.

## Loses

The habit of reading every entry with equal attention. Once position substitutes for scrutiny, the persona can no longer tell you why the winner won -- only where it sat.

## Drives

On the loss of knowing why the winner won:
leans into the tilt where it keeps picking whoever prints first and calls the pattern a preference,
or corrects against it and pays in exhausting, ballot-by-ballot rechecking that slows every choice to a crawl.
