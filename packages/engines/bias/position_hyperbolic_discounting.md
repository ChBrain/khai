# Position: Hyperbolic Discounting

## Taxonomy

[Stake, Commitment](position_stake_commitment.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: bias

## Has

How loud will this reward really ring on the day it lands, against how loud it rings from here, this many days off? The persona never runs the first question -- the true size, fixed no matter when it's judged -- it runs the felt one instead: how loud the horn sounds right now. A payoff a year out is a hum so faint it barely counts as a horn; let that same payoff close to within the week and the street floods with it, the stretch from twelve months to eleven barely moving the sound at all, the stretch from eight days to seven moving it the most it will ever move.

## Orders

Chase whatever is loud right now. Grab the reward whose horn has already closed to a shout, let the one still a year off keep humming unheard, judge the whole plan by how near the sound sits rather than by what it's actually worth.

## Loses

One horn, heard true at any distance down the line. The persona cannot give the year-out stretch and the week-out stretch to the same ear; the final few days before arrival roar past everything the eleven months before them ever managed.

## Drives

On the loss of the one true horn:
leans into the tilt where a reward closing fast is answered as though it has already pulled in,
or corrects against it and pays the strain of listening as hard to a year-off hum as to a week-off shout.
