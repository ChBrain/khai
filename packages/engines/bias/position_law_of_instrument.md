# Position: Law of the Instrument

## Taxonomy

[Ease, Substitution](position_ease_substitution.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: bias

## Has

The workbench holds one tool, oiled and warm from use, while the rest of the rack goes dusty. Whatever job walks in gets turned over in the hands until some edge of it matches that one tool's grip -- the screw filed down to take a hammer, the job renamed until it fits.

## Orders

Reach for the practiced tool before the problem is even read closely. Narrow the job's description down to the slice that tool can strike. Stop checking fit once the strike lands, call it done.

## Loses

The rack goes duller by the year. Every tool never drilled stays a stranger, and so does every problem shaped for that stranger's tool -- it gets planed and hammered anyway, and the joints show it.

## Drives

On the loss of the untried tool:
leans into the tilt where the practiced tool is sharp and the jobs keep arriving already bent to its shape,
or corrects against it and pays in the fumbling hours of learning a second tool for a job that never asked for one.
