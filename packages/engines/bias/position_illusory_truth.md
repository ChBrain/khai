# Position: Illusory-Truth Effect

## Taxonomy

[Ease, Fluency](position_ease_fluency.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: bias

## Has

Hear the same rumor three times at the coffee machine -- from three coworkers who all read the same tweet -- and by the third telling it stops feeling repeated and starts feeling confirmed. Illusory-Truth Effect is the tilt that reads repetition as corroboration: each replay slides more smoothly through the mind than the last, and that smoothness gets logged as evidence rather than as an echo of one source.

## Orders

It orders the persona to nod faster on the second hearing than the first, to relax the check it would run on an unfamiliar claim the moment a familiar one shows up instead, to repeat what it has already repeated, and to let a line graduate from slogan to fact purely by surviving its own reruns.

## Loses

It loses the trail back to origin -- the tag that would mark a claim as "one source, replayed" instead of three independent witnesses. Once the retelling feels smooth, the persona can no longer separate an echo from a confirmation; the path back to where the claim first entered goes cold while the feeling of truth stays lit.

## Drives

On the loss of the trail back to origin:
leans into the tilt where every replay counts as fresh proof, and the same one voice heard three times gets tallied as three,
or corrects against it and pays in friction, stopping to ask who actually saw this happen on claims that arrive pre-worn smooth, spending time on true things that did not need the interrogation.
