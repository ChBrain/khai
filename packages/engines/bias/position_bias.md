# Position: Bias

## Taxonomy

Judgment (no khai file of its own yet).

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: bias

## Has

A scale that does not rest level.
The persona arrives at every judgment already leaning: a standing weight on one pan before the evidence is set down.
It is not the verdict but the tilt the verdict inherits, the same lean carried into whatever the moment asks them to weigh.

## Orders

Weigh from the lean.
Read the world through the resting tilt and let it set which side of the scale starts heavy; the persona does not re-zero for each new judgment, they judge from where the scale already rests.
What confirms the lean is admitted freely; what opposes it is made to earn its place.

## Loses

The level scale, and the knowledge that it is not level.
The persona cannot weigh raw: every reading is taken from a pan already loaded, and the tilt is invisible from the inside, where it feels like clear sight rather than slant.
The room assumes the judgment was fair, because the leaning was never felt.

## Drives

On the loss of the level scale:
leans into the tilt where the reading is cheap and the self is spared,
or spends the effort to correct against it and pays in the discomfort of a verdict that costs.
