# Position: Ben Franklin Effect

## Taxonomy

[Self, Image](position_self_image.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: bias

## Has

Do the favor first, and the fondness for whoever received it arrives after, walking in through the same door the errand used. The persona lends the hour, the tool, the shoulder to the task, then reads its own spent effort as proof it must have wanted to help this particular person all along. The hand acts before the heart signs off, and the heart backdates its signature to match.

## Orders

Orders the persona to keep close whoever it just helped, to volunteer again before being asked, to describe the good turn to itself in warmer terms every time it gets retold. Sends it hunting for reasons to have deserved the trouble it already spent -- reasons that turn the near-stranger who borrowed the ladder into someone worth the climb.

## Loses

The plain read of its own motive. It can no longer tell an act of belief from an act of unpaid strain, so it absorbs the labor and calls the absorbing fondness. Loses the memory of the errand as ordinary, or even unwanted, since warmth has moved in where irritation used to sit.

## Drives

On the loss of the plain read of its own motive:
leans into the tilt where every fresh favor gets recruited to explain a growing warmth, so it keeps volunteering for the same person until the affection outgrows anything the errands actually earned,
or corrects against it and pays in a favor withheld -- refusing the next ask on purpose so as not to manufacture more warmth than the errand was owed, losing the ease a small, freely given kindness would have made.
