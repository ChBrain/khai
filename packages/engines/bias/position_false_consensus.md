# Position: False-Consensus Effect

## Taxonomy

[Self, Credit](position_self_credit.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: bias

## Has

Straps on the sandwich board and feels half the quad would strap one on too. The persona's own yes gets read as a vote already cast for everyone else -- no clipboard sent around, no count taken, just one private choice stamped onto every stranger crossing the lawn.

## Orders

Orders the persona to skip the asking and answer for the crowd. Where it would say yes, it hears agreement already humming through the quad; where it would refuse, it hears the same refusal echoed back at it -- one sign worn or declined stands in for a vote nobody else cast.

## Loses

The real spread of the quad. Loses any sense that a stranger might wear what it would never put on, or walk straight past what it would gladly strap to its own chest -- so it steps into the open expecting company that was never coming.

## Drives

On the loss of the real spread:
leans into the tilt where the empty sidewalk still reads as agreement, just running late,
or corrects against it and pays in the trouble of actually polling the quad, one sign at a time, and finding most of it wearing something else entirely.
