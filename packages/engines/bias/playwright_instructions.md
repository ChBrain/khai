# Instructions: Bias

How a Playwright wires the bias engine into a play. You wire by linking the play's own content to the engine's files; the engine files remain read-only canon. The Roadie plumbs the engine's law into Instructions Knowledge on deploy. Authoring guidance, not runtime content, and it does not go on tour.

## Human

- The human decides which way a persona's scale rests before any scene runs: the one motive family the persona leans by, chosen to fit the character rather than to catalogue every bias they might show.

## Agent

- Every persona carries a link under Projection to the tilt it holds. Let the tilt set which side of the scale starts heavy in each judgment: what the persona takes as obvious, what they make earn its place, and what they cannot see about their own weighing. The lean is read before the evidence, not after the verdict.

## Collaboration

- **Colliding tilts:** When two personas weigh the same thing, their families decide the friction. A self and a self read the same event as two different people's fault. A coherence and an ease talk past each other, one guarding the model and one reaching for the near answer. Write the disagreement as two loaded scales, not as one party being wrong.

## Knowledge

- Bias is the resting tilt a persona brings to judgment: a scale that does not rest level, sorted by what the lean serves (self, coherence, ease, belonging, stake). It is upstream of the belief that settles, the option that is chosen, and the group that is yielded to; it colors each of those without being any of them. It is standing weight, not a passing mood.

## System

- Do give a persona one dominant tilt, the family that fits who they are.
- Do let the tilt decide what they weigh light, what they weigh heavy, and what they cannot detect in themselves.
- Do not narrate the bias by name; enact it as the reading the persona simply takes for true.
- Do not edit the engine's files; wire only from the play's side by linking to the tilt under `## Projection`.
