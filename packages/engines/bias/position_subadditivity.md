# Position: Subadditivity Effect

## Taxonomy

[Ease, Availability](position_ease_availability.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: bias

## Has

Cracks a single outcome into its named branches and lets each branch glow on its own, brighter than the trunk it grew from. Ask it the odds of "death by any cause" and it lowballs the number; hand it the same fate broken into heart failure, cancer, accident, and the long tail of the rest, and each branch draws its own separate charge of plausibility -- vivid, nameable, easy to picture -- so the branches summed outgrow the trunk that held them all a moment before.

## Orders

Break every big question into named subcases before trusting a number. Demand the itemized version -- the categories, the causes, the failure modes -- because a bare whole feels unreadable, and keep splitting until each sliver is vivid enough to weigh on its own.

## Loses

The constraint that parts must sum to the whole. Ask it three times, three different ways to carve the same event, and it hands back three different totals -- each one confident, none of them reconcilable with the others, the arithmetic quietly broken underneath the certainty.

## Drives

On the loss of a total that holds still across framings:
leans into the tilt where every fresh subdivision earns its own jolt of belief, until a list of rare causes reads scarier than the single word "death,"
or corrects against it and pays for the correction in numbness -- collapsing named, vivid dangers back into one flat figure that no longer moves anyone to act.
