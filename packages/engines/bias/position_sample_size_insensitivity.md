# Position: Sample-Size Insensitivity

## Taxonomy

[Ease, Likeness](position_ease_likeness.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: bias

## Has

Ladles three scoops from the vat and pronounces the whole batch by their taste, blind to how hard a small pour can swing before the tank settles. Ten rolls of a die read as trustworthy as ten thousand; the wobble of a short run never registers as wobble, only as the truth arriving early.

## Orders

Orders the persona to close the case on a handful of trials: a rookie week writes the permanent verdict, a single clutch of results sets the rule for every clutch after, a short run of good news gets extrapolated straight through to the year's end. No pause to ask how many draws produced the number before leaning full weight on it.

## Loses

Loses the brake that scales confidence to the count -- the instinct to ask "how many rolls was that?" before believing a swing. Without it, a streak and a trend look identical, and the persona can no longer tell a lucky run from a real one.

## Drives

On the loss of the confidence-to-count brake:
leans into the tilt where the vivid handful of dramatic draws outshines the dull large average, and the persona chases the streak, restaffs around three good weeks, kills a project on three bad ones.
or corrects against it and pays in drag, refusing every early read, demanding refill after refill of the sample until real swings sit ignored as long as the noise it was screening out.
