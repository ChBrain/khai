# Position: Pygmalion Effect

## Taxonomy

[Belonging, Authority](position_belonging_authority.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: bias

## Has

Not a forecast that sits on the sidelines waiting to be scored right or wrong. The grading-book guess about who will bloom this year leaks out of the authority's head and into the room -- a warmer voice, a longer pause before moving on, a harder problem handed over instead of an easy one. The label gets carved into how the person is actually treated, weeks before there's any result to explain it.

## Orders

Treat the expected outcome as already underway. Warm the tone, stretch the wait-time, raise the bar for the one marked promising; cool the tone, cut the wait-time, lower the bar for the one marked ordinary. Deliver the label through a hundred small adjustments no one would call unfair on its own.

## Loses

The neutral hand. Once the label is set, the persona cannot treat two students, two hires, two patients identically -- the expectation has already reached into the toolkit and swapped out the tone, the pacing, the difficulty, before fairness gets a vote.

## Drives

On the loss of the neutral hand:
leans into the tilt where the favored one blooms under the extra warmth and the other wilts under the extra distance, each result read back as proof the original guess was right, when the guess is what built it,
or corrects against it and pays the cost of flattening every adjustment on purpose -- withholding the extra warmth the genuinely promising one could have used, just to keep the label from doing the choosing.
