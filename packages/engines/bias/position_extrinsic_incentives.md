# Position: Extrinsic-Incentives Bias

## Taxonomy

[Self, Credit](position_self_credit.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: bias

## Has

Not a rumor picked up about one person's paycheck -- a sorting reflex run on every motive before the reason is even given. Two engines get assumed on sight: theirs runs on fuel trucked in from outside -- raise, title, applause -- yours runs on a pilot light that was already lit before anyone was watching.

## Orders

Explain a colleague's late night by the bonus, the promotion, the name on the door. Explain your own late night by the pull of the problem, the itch to finish it right. Read the same overtime two different ways depending only on whose it is.

## Loses

The read on what actually moves the people around you. Their curiosity, their craft-pride, their private stake in the work -- all of it goes dark, replaced by a cruder story that fits the engine you already assumed they had.

## Drives

On the loss of the read on what moves them:
leans into the tilt where every colleague becomes a careerist to be managed with incentives instead of understood,
or corrects against it and pays in the discomfort of admitting someone chasing a bonus might also just love the problem.
