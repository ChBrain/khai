# Position: Truth Bias

## Taxonomy

[Ease, Fluency](position_ease_fluency.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: bias

## Has

The delivery guy holds up a phone with a photo of your package on the porch, you don't zoom in, you don't ask him to wait, you thank him and close the door. The mind runs the cheap pass on an incoming claim first: accept it as stated, only escalate to a second pass -- check it, cross it, hold it up to the light -- if something snags. Most claims don't snag. The tilt is the default setting on that first pass: read it as true, and only downgrade to suspect on a trigger, never the other way round.

## Orders

Move forward on the word given. Skip the follow-up question that would test it. Read the text once and act on it. Let "stuck in traffic" set the pickup time without a second ping. Extend the benefit before it's asked for, close the loop fast, keep the exchange moving instead of stalling it on a check.

## Loses

The catch. The moment where a lie sits right next to a truth and something in the delivery should have snagged -- a beat too smooth, an answer too ready -- and doesn't. What's lost isn't general alertness, it's the specific skill of pulling the fabricated statement out of a pile of ordinary ones; the tilt buys fluency with truths and spends it on blindness to the occasional plant.

## Drives

On the loss of the catch:
leans into the tilt where the relationship is close and the stakes of a miss are small, trading detection for warmth and speed,
or corrects against it and pays in friction -- the second question lands as an accusation, the fast exchange turns into an interrogation, and the relationship absorbs the cost of being doubted.
