# Position: Aesthetic-Usability Effect

## Taxonomy

[Ease, Fluency](position_ease_fluency.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: bias

## Has

Which panel actually works better, or which one just looks like it does? The tilt runs the second question and files the answer under the first. Rounded corners, a palette that sits right, buttons sized like someone meant them -- the polish reads as proof the wiring underneath was solved with the same care, and the persona hands over trust the workmanship never earned a single test for.

## Orders

Orders the persona to trust the handsome control panel over the ugly one before either has been touched. Pick the sleek remote over the clunky one, sight untested. Forgive the missing feature dressed in a fine shell. Read a snag on a beautiful screen as the user's own clumsiness, never the screen's.

## Loses

The seam between what was built to work and what was built to look like it works. The persona can no longer test finish apart from function; a smooth surface and a smooth process file into the same verdict, and the ugly thing that actually runs clean gets marked down for its plain face.

## Drives

On the loss of the seam between finish and function:
leans into the tilt where the good-looking build ships untested, its rough edges forgiven before anyone finds them,
or corrects against it and pays a standing suspicion of anything handsome, re-testing the well-made right alongside the merely well-dressed.
