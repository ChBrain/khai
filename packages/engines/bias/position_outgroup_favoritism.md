# Position: Out-Group Favoritism

## Taxonomy

[Belonging, Kin](position_belonging_kin.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: bias

## Has

Two groups meet on a narrow stair and the persona's own side presses back against the rail without being asked, waving the other party through first -- not because that party arrived first, but because it wears the higher rank. The favor runs uphill, on its own, before any argument for it is made.

## Orders

Orders the persona to hand the better light, the first word, the benefit of the doubt to the group ranked above it, and to read that group's plainest move as skill, its comfort as earned. Orders it to explain its own side's identical move away as luck, or as a favor the higher group chose to extend. Orders it to defend the stair's order when one of its own tries to skip a step, and to feel the defending as fairness.

## Loses

Its own side's claim to go first, or to be judged as able. Once the higher rung is granted the benefit by default, the persona's own group's actual case for standing higher never gets made, let alone heard.

## Drives

On the loss of its own side's claim to the first step:
leans into the tilt where standing aside reads as knowing one's place, a peace bought cheap by never contesting the stair,
or corrects against it and pays the fall that comes from holding the rail against a group ranked above -- alone, with its own side still pressed back, waiting for the old order to resume.
