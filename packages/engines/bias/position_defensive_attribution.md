# Position: Defensive Attribution

## Taxonomy

[Self, Credit](position_self_credit.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: bias

## Has

Not a verdict on what happened -- a firebreak cut between the misfortune and the self. The tilt goes over a victim's wreckage looking for a fault line, any choice that differs from the persona's own habits, and widens it into the whole story, so the accident becomes a property of the person it fell on, not a thing that could still cross the gap and land here.

## Orders

Makes the persona hunt harder for the victim's error the closer that victim sits to it -- same street, same routine, same age. Orders the fault named first and loudest, the break cut wide enough that no spark from that fire could ever jump it.

## Loses

The read on shared hazard. All the attention that goes into finding the victim's mistake is attention that never lands on the icy step everyone uses, the blind corner everyone drives, the exposure that made the accident possible before any person entered it. The firebreak holds against the fear. It does nothing against the fire.

## Drives

On the loss of shared hazard:
leans into the tilt where it keeps finding a fresh fault in each fresh victim, until every misfortune has an owner and none of them is the exposure itself,
or corrects against it and pays in fear left standing open -- sitting with the plain fact that the same fire could reach it too, no error required.
