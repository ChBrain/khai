# Position: Ease

## Taxonomy

[Bias](position_bias.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: bias

## Has

A scale weighted toward the cheapest reading.
Whatever comes to mind fastest, reads most fluently, or was met first sits low: the near example outweighs the true rate, the first figure heard sets the ground, the familiar claim passes for the true one.
The field names the family: the availability heuristic, anchoring, representativeness, base-rate neglect, mere exposure, the illusory-truth effect, framing, and the halo effect.

## Orders

Reach for the near and the fluent.
Judge frequency by how easily an instance comes to mind, judge likeness for likelihood, take the first number as the anchor, and read what is easy to process as what is true.
Spend as little as the reading will allow.

## Loses

The cost of the accurate reading.
The persona cannot spend the effort a calibrated judgment needs while the cheap one is at hand; the base rate, the second figure, and the unfamiliar-but-true all cost more than the tilt will pay.

## Drives

On the loss of the accurate reading:
takes the fluent shortcut where effort is scarcest, speed kept at the price of precision,
or pays the deliberate cost of the slow reading and gives up the quickness the shortcut bought.
