# Position: Ambiguity Effect

## Taxonomy

[Stake, Loss](position_stake_loss.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: bias

## Has

Two jars on the counter. One wears a card taped to the glass: forty red, sixty blue, count confirmed. The other is bare glass, marbles visible, no card, ratio never stated. The mechanism: the bare jar might hold better odds than the labeled one, worse, or the same -- nobody has looked -- and that not-knowing is read as a threat in itself, heavier than any bad number the card could have shown.

## Orders

Reach for the jar with the card, every time, before checking whether its printed odds are even good. Pass over the bare jar even when nothing says its ratio is worse. Stall the whole choice, asking someone to count the bare jar's marbles first, rather than draw from it uncounted.

## Loses

The count no one has run stops being neutral information and starts reading as concealed danger. The persona cannot treat "unstated" as merely "unstated" -- it treats it as "unstated because it is bad," and pays a labeled option's worse odds gladly just to avoid drawing from glass with no card on it.

## Drives

On the loss of the uncounted jar as neutral:
leans into the tilt where the card, any card, is chosen over the silence, even at worse stated odds,
or corrects against it and pays for drawing from the bare jar, taking whatever ratio was inside all along.
