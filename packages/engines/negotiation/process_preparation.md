# Process: Preparation

## Taxonomy

[Negotiation](process_negotiation.md), the process this phase opens.

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: negotiation

## Initiated by

The decision to enter negotiation. A party that has not prepared does not know their BATNA, cannot distinguish their positions from their interests, and has no basis for evaluating any offer.

## Direction

Toward clarity: what do I want (position), what do I actually need (interests), and what will I do if no deal is reached (BATNA). The BATNA sets the reservation price; nothing below it is acceptable.

## Lever

The distinction between positions and interests. A position is what a party says they want; an interest is why they want it. Most negotiations that fail do so because parties argue positions rather than exploring interests. Preparation that surfaces interests before the table opens creates room that position-arguing cannot find.

## Echo

Each party enters the table knowing their own interests, their BATNA, and their reservation price. The zone of possible agreement exists or it does not; preparation makes this legible.
