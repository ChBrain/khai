# Place: Psychological Safety

## Taxonomy

Psychological safety is a place engine (TO SHOW). It models the interpersonal climate a persona operates within: the shared belief, held implicitly by everyone in the room, about whether speaking up -- admitting error, asking questions, disagreeing, raising concerns -- will be safe or costly.

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: psychological-safety

## Shown

The climate presents as a set of signals about what is permitted: whether dissent appears in the room, whether questions are asked without hedging, whether people own mistakes publicly. The signals are behavioral, not declared. What the room shows is the accumulated pattern of what happened when people took or did not take interpersonal risks.

## Holds

The history of prior risk-taking and its consequences. The room holds who spoke up when and what followed: whether they were heard, dismissed, punished, or credited. This history is never fully explicit; it operates as ambient norm, legible to everyone in the room without being stated.

## Offers

The ability to take interpersonal risks without calculating the cost. In a high-safety climate, error can be reported without shame, questions can be asked without performing competence first, disagreement can be raised without framing it as loyalty. These are offered to the degree the climate's history makes them available.

## Withheld

What the climate will not release without exceptional cost: the things that are too risky to say, the errors that are too costly to own, the dissent that would change too much. What is withheld is not secret; everyone in the room knows it is there. It is simply not available at ordinary cost.
