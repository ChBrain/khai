# Place: Performative Psychological Safety

## Taxonomy

Performative psychological safety is an expression of the psychological safety engine. The interpersonal climate officially encourages speaking up while structurally punishing it: safety is claimed but not conferred.

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: psychological-safety

## Shown

The declared norm of openness: the official language of psychological safety, the rituals of invitation -- "no bad ideas," "speak freely," "we want to hear from everyone." What is shown is a room that looks like high safety to anyone who reads the policy rather than the behavior.

## Holds

The enforcement that contradicts the official norm: what happened to the person who actually spoke up, who pointed out the error, who disagreed with the strategy. This history is held by everyone in the room and is the real operating manual. The policy is the surface; the enforcement history is the structure.

## Offers

The performance of safety: the ability to voice agreement, to raise concerns that the leadership is already planning to address, to ask questions whose answers are already known to be acceptable. Speaking up that reinforces the existing direction is genuinely available and genuinely welcome. What is offered is the appearance of candor, not the substance.

## Withheld

Everything high safety would offer: genuine disagreement, error reports that implicate the strategy, questions that challenge the direction, minority views that diverge from the consensus. What is withheld is the contradiction between the official norm and the enforcement history; the room holds this knowledge and does not say it aloud.
