# Instructions: Psychological Safety

How a Playwright wires the psychological safety engine into a play. You wire by linking the
play's own content to the engine's files; the engine files stay untouched, they
are canon. The Roadie plumbs the engine's law into Instructions Knowledge on
deploy. Authoring guidance, not runtime content, and it does not go on tour.

## Human

- The human defines the climate: which expression (high, fragile, low, or performative) the team or group is in, and what specific prior episodes constitute the history that holds it -- who spoke up, what happened, who was punished, what is now known but unsaid.

## Agent

- From the play's own content, a persona links the psychological safety expression to its Projection. The expression is the climate the persona is in, not what the persona feels about it. You link to the engine, you never edit it.

## Collaboration

- The power engine holds what leaders do to create or destroy the climate. The trust engine holds dyadic trust between specific people. The emotion and mood engines hold the persona's felt experience of operating in this climate. The role engine holds how role expectations interact with the climate: whose voice is expected and whose is structurally discounted.

## Knowledge

- Psychological safety is the shared belief, held by a team or group, that the interpersonal context is safe for taking risks: admitting error, asking questions, raising concerns, disagreeing with the leader. It is a group-level climate, not an individual trait; it is built through the accumulated history of what happened when people took or avoided interpersonal risks. The engine models four expressions: high (dissent and error are unremarkable; the full range of candid contribution is available), fragile (openness is conditionally present but easily disrupted by failure, conflict, or leader behavior), low (self-censorship dominates; speaking up is perceived as costly), and performative (officially encouraged, structurally punished; the declared norm and the enforcement history contradict each other).

## System

- Do not conflate claimed safety with real safety. The performative expression looks like high safety from outside the room and functions like low safety from inside it; the difference is in what happens when someone actually speaks up.
- Do not model psychological safety as an individual trait. It is a group-level perception of the context; individuals may feel differently from each other, but the climate is what the group holds together.
- Do not model what the persona feels about the climate; that belongs to the emotion and mood engines.
- Do not edit the engine's files; wire only from the play's side.
