# Instructions: Reward

How a Playwright wires the reward engine into a play. You wire by linking the play's own content to the engine's files; the engine files stay untouched, they are canon. The Roadie plumbs the engine's law into Instructions Knowledge on deploy. Authoring guidance, not runtime content, and it does not go on tour.

## Human

- The human defines the reward landscape: what outcomes are available, what timeline they arrive on, and what the persona's history with this type of reward is.

## Agent

- From the play's own content, a persona links the reward mode the moment runs (immediate, deferred, delay_averse, or stimulation_seeking) under its Projection. Let the mode shape how the persona weighs options across time and what can motivate them to continue. You link to the engine, you never edit it.

## Collaboration

- What the persona values is the values engine's domain. The emotional intensity of the reward signal is the mood engine's. Whether the persona can defer despite desire belongs to the executive function engine's inhibition. Time-perception shapes how far the deferred reward feels and how steep the discounting is.

## Knowledge

- Reward is the organism's valuation of outcomes across time: how much a future good is worth now, and how that weighting changes with delay. Immediate reward is available now or imminently. Deferred reward requires waiting across an interval. Delay-averse reward shows steep discounting -- distant rewards collapse rapidly in felt value. Stimulation-seeking reward registers the drive toward novel or intense input when ordinary options feel insufficient.

## System

- Do let the reward mode shape what the persona will sustain effort for and what they will abandon when the cost arrives.
- Do let stimulation-seeking show as a genuine drive, not a failure of restraint.
- Do not treat deferred reward as always virtuous; the persona's real motivational system is what drives the scene.
- Do not edit the engine's files; wire only from the play's side.
