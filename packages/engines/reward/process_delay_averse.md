# Process: Delay-Averse

## Taxonomy

[Reward](process_reward.md), the mode this is one form of.

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: reward

## Initiated by

A wait is required -- the outcome is available later, and the interval itself is the obstacle.
The persona is not simply indifferent to waiting; the waiting is aversive, an active source of discomfort that generates its own push.

## Direction

Away from the interval.
The persona collapses the wait wherever possible -- takes a smaller now-reward to escape the gap, seeks any activity that shortens the felt duration, or abandons the sequence when the wait cannot be avoided.
The move is not toward a preferred outcome but away from the condition of waiting.

## Lever

The aversiveness of the interval meets the cost of the available alternative.
The delay does not merely reduce the value of the future reward; it produces an active push away from the wait itself.
The persona is not calculating expected value; they are escaping discomfort, and the escape is the reward.

## Echo

The persona acts to end the wait, not to maximise the outcome.
The chosen option is not necessarily the preferred one; it is the one that removes the gap.
From outside, the choice looks impulsive -- a smaller reward taken when a larger one was available with patience.
From inside, the calculation included the cost of the interval, which is real and felt, and which the outside observer did not see.
