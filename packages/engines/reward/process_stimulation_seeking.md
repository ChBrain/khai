# Process: Stimulation-Seeking

## Taxonomy

[Reward](process_reward.md), the mode this is one form of.

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: reward

## Initiated by

Arousal is too low and the environment is not supplying enough signal to run well.
The persona is understimulated -- bored, flat, or cognitively idle -- and the deficit produces an urge to move, disrupt, or create.
The urge is not toward a specific outcome but toward the activation itself.

## Direction

Toward whatever raises the signal.
The target is not the result of the activity but the stimulation the activity provides; the persona is seeking the input, not the output.
The direction is away from the flat state, not toward any particular destination.

## Lever

The arousal deficit meets the availability of stimulation.
The organism regulates arousal through behavior -- where the environment is flat, the persona introduces novelty, conflict, or challenge to raise the level.
The drive is homeostatic, not pathological: the organism is correcting a deficit.
But it takes precedence over external agendas and produces behavior that may look purposeless or disruptive to observers who do not see the underlying regulation.

## Echo

Arousal rises and the urge resolves -- or the persona cycles to the next source.
Where the environment supplies adequate input, the drive is quiet.
Where it does not, the persona finds their own inputs: some productive, some disruptive, some both.
The behavior reads as thrill-seeking or sensation-seeking from outside; the underlying process is the repair of a deficit the organism will not tolerate for long.
