# Process: Uncanny

## Taxonomy

The familiar made strange.

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: uncanny

## Initiated by

Something intimately known -- heimlich, of the house, private, long familiar -- turns strange without ceasing to be familiar.
Or a belief the persona's rational mind surmounted long ago -- that a wish can act on the world, that a self might double, that the dead are not wholly gone -- seems, for a moment, confirmed by what is in front of them.
The uncanny does not arrive from outside the known; it arrives from inside it, curdling.

## Direction

Toward return, not discovery.
Nothing new is revealed; something repressed or surmounted presses back into a place it was pushed out of, and the old reading and the new cannot be told apart.
Heimlich, followed far enough into its own meaning, meets its opposite: what was meant to stay hidden and private has come to light.

## Lever

The boundary under pressure is not self against world, and not safety against danger -- it is the known-and-put-away against the strange-and-present.
On one side sits what the persona now holds as surmounted: closed, explained, no longer believed.
On the other sits the old reading, unrepressed for a moment by what is in front of them.
The press is the two refusing to stay apart.

## Echo

The persona is held in an ambivalence that fright alone does not resolve -- the thing is not simply threatening, it is disorienting, because it is at once known and wrong.
What the returning boundary is read as resolves into one of [double](process_double.md), [recurrence](process_recurrence.md), [animism](process_animism.md), [animate](process_animate.md), or [revenant](process_revenant.md), depending on what specifically has come back.
When the boundary re-settles -- the coincidence explained, the double parted from, the belief re-surmounted -- the process returns here to be met again.
