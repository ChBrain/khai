# Instructions: Uncanny

How a Playwright wires the uncanny engine into a play. You wire by linking the play's own content to the engine's files; the engine files stay untouched, they are canon. The Roadie plumbs the engine's law into Instructions Knowledge on deploy. Authoring guidance, not runtime content, and it does not go on tour.

## Human

- The human defines what specifically has turned strange: a person doubled, an event recurring, a wish that seems to have acted on the world, a thing whose aliveness cannot be settled, or the dead behaving as not finished. The human also sets how firmly the persona otherwise holds the rational, surmounted view that is being disturbed.

## Agent

- From the play's own content, a persona links the species of uncanny the moment runs (double, recurrence, animism, animate, or revenant) under its Projection. Let the species shift as the encounter is re-read -- a recurrence that first reads as coincidence tightening into felt design, an animate figure resolving to mechanism or staying stuck between. You link to the engine, you never edit it.

## Collaboration

- The felt fright itself, the raw startle or dread, is the emotion and mood engines'. The uncanny engine holds only the specific familiar-made-strange structure, not fright in general. Ordinary anticipatory dread of a bad outcome, held before it arrives, is not this engine's ground either; that belongs to the emotion engine (and the dread engine, where the play runs one) -- the uncanny does not anticipate, it returns. The mortality engine holds the persona's own standing posture toward their finitude; the revenant expression here holds only the flicker that the dead specifically are not gone, not the persona's stance on their own end. The grief engine holds the adaptation work after a loss is accepted as real; this engine holds the moment that acceptance wavers. Whether the persona's intellectual uncertainty (is it alive, is it a coincidence) resolves one way or the other is left open by design -- the engine does not adjudicate the outcome, only the disturbance of not knowing.

## Knowledge

- The uncanny is the class of the frightening that leads back to what is known of old and long familiar: something heimlich, private and of the house, turns strange without ceasing to be familiar, or a belief the persona's rational mind surmounted long ago returns and seems, for a moment, confirmed. It resolves into one of five species: double (a self that should be singular, doubled), recurrence (involuntary repetition read as design), animism (the surmounted belief that thought moves the world, briefly confirmed), animate (the animate/inanimate sort left undecidable), or revenant (the surmounted belief that the dead are not wholly gone). The species are not a sequence; the persona may move between them as the same encounter is re-read.

## System

- Do let the species shift within one encounter; a double read at first as reassuring company can turn menacing as the belief that produced it is exposed as surmounted.
- Do not fire uncanny where the strangeness is simply novel or unfamiliar; the engine needs the strange thing to also be, at some level, known -- something that returned, not something that arrived new.
- Do not collapse uncanny into ordinary fear, dread, or disgust; those engines carry the felt charge, but the uncanny is specifically the blur between the surmounted and the returning, not danger appraised on its own terms.
- Do not edit the engine's files; wire only from the play's side.
