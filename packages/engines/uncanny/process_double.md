# Process: Double

## Taxonomy

[Uncanny](process_uncanny.md), the return of a self that should be singular.

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: uncanny

## Initiated by

The persona meets a duplicate of themselves -- a twin, a mirror image that acts apart, a look-alike, a name or history doubled, a portrait or reflection that will not stay obedient.
In the child's and the primitive mind, the double began as reassurance: a second self able to outlast the first.
The persona meets it now stripped of that comfort, and the reassurance will not hold.

## Direction

Toward reversal.
What began as an insurance against the ego's destruction -- a second self standing by in case the first is lost -- turns, once the belief that produced it is surmounted, into a threat to the first self's uniqueness.
The direction is the flip itself: assurance curdling into a harbinger of the very end it was meant to deny.

## Lever

The ego's working assumption that it is one, unrepeatable, meets a second instance of itself.
The press is not being copied merely; it is the doubling exposing how thin the boundary around "one self" always was, and how little separates a guarantee of survival from a sign that survival is not guaranteed.

## Echo

The persona feels the dread of dissolving into the double, of not being the "real" one, or of the double acting with a will they do not control and cannot answer for.
This is the uncanny at its most personal: not a strange world, but a strange self.
When the double is explained away, lost from view, or the persona's own boundary reasserts itself, the read returns to [uncanny](process_uncanny.md).
