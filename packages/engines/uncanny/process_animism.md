# Process: Animism

## Taxonomy

[Uncanny](process_uncanny.md), the surmounted belief in the power of thought, briefly confirmed.

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: uncanny

## Initiated by

A belief the persona's rational mind surmounted long ago -- that a wish or a thought can act directly on the world, that naming a fear can cause it -- seems, for a moment, confirmed: a wished harm arrives, a coincidence lines up exactly with what was only thought.
Nothing has actually changed about how the world works; what has changed is the persona's certainty that it doesn't work that way.

## Direction

Toward confirmation of the surmounted.
The old animistic mode of thought -- that thinking has force, that the mind reaches the world without the world's cooperation -- is not remembered as a childhood belief but relived, believed again for as long as the coincidence holds.

## Lever

The overvaluation of one's own mental processes, ordinarily kept in check by the adult, rational frame, meets an event that appears to prove the overvaluation true.
The press is the frame's authority slipping for exactly as long as the coincidence stands unexplained.

## Echo

The persona feels a private, illicit sense of power, or a private, illicit guilt -- as though thinking made it so, for good or harm.
This is the uncanny at its most inward: not a strange object but a strange authority suddenly credited to the self.
Once the rational frame reasserts and the coincidence is filed back as coincidence, the read returns to [uncanny](process_uncanny.md).
