# Place: Restorative Environment

## Taxonomy

Restorative Environment is a place engine (TO SHOW). It models a setting whose pressure runs opposite to depletion: rather than taxing the directed attention a demanding place requires, it refills it. Where an ordinary effortful place makes the persona keep holding distraction off, a restorative environment lets that holding rest by drawing attention softly, without demand.

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: restorative-environment

## Shown

Soft fascination: pattern, movement, or texture that pulls the eye without asking for it -- clouds, foliage, water, a shifting light. Nothing here competes for the effortful, voluntary attention that depleted directed attention can no longer supply; what arrives instead asks for nothing and holds anyway.

## Holds

Extent: a world coherent and large enough to be absorbed into -- connected enough that what is visible implies more of the same beyond it, so the persona is inside a whole rather than facing a display. The environment holds its own scope; it does not depend on the persona to supply the connections.

## Offers

Being away: psychological distance from the routine that drained the attention in the first place -- not necessarily physical distance, but freedom from the tasks, demands, and voluntary effort the ordinary place required. What the place offers is relief from having to hold anything off.

## Withheld

Compatibility: the fit between what the environment supports and what the persona actually wants to do here. This is not handed out freely -- it depends on the persona's own purpose meeting the place's affordances, and where the two do not align, no amount of fascination or extent restores anything. Full restoration is withheld until that fit is real.
