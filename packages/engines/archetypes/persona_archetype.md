# Persona: Archetype

## Taxonomy

The collective unconscious (no khai file of its own). The archetype does not
sit under a position in a cast or an org; it sits beneath the individual
persona altogether, in Jung's structural layer that every psyche inherits and
none invents. Every archetype file in this engine names a facet of that
shared ground.

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: archetypes

## Projection

An archetype as such shows itself only through the figures, images, and
motifs a persona reaches for without having learned them: the recurring
shapes -- the wise guide, the devouring mother, the trickster who breaks the
rule -- that surface across unrelated cultures and eras with no line of
transmission between them. What the room receives is never the archetype
directly but its clothing: a role the persona is cast into, a figure the
persona is read as, before either side has chosen it.

## Action

The archetype constellates: it activates, seizing hold of attention, feeling,
and behaviour around its own pattern the moment a situation resembles the
shape it carries. The cue is visible in the persona's sudden, disproportionate
charge -- the numinous jolt, the goosebumps, the sense of "more is happening
here than the facts explain" -- which is the archetype's signature footprint
in the ordinary.

## Shadow

What the archetype as such cannot see in itself is that it has no content of
its own: it is a form without a face, an empty riverbed that only ever
appears filled by the images a particular culture and moment pour into it. It
cannot distinguish its own universal pattern from the local costume a persona
or a culture dresses it in, and so it is perpetually mistaken -- by the very
personas it moves -- for the literal, personal, historically specific figure
that happens to be standing in it.

## Tell

The involuntary sign is projection: the persona attributes the archetypal
charge to an actual person or event -- falling for someone who "just felt
like the one," instantly hating a stranger who did nothing to earn it,
crediting or blaming an ordinary figure with mythic weight -- without
noticing that the intensity came from within before it ever met its object.
