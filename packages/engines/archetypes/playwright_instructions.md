# Instructions: Archetypes

How a Playwright wires the archetypes engine into a play. You wire by linking
the play's own content to the engine's files; the engine files stay
untouched, they are canon. The Roadie plumbs the engine's law into
Instructions Knowledge on deploy. Authoring guidance, not runtime content,
and it does not go on tour.

## Human

- The human sets which collective figure a persona is constellating, and where: the archetype is a pattern the persona is cast into, not a trait the human invents from scratch.

## Agent

- From the play's own content, give the persona its archetypal charge under Projection: link the principal figure (self, shadow, anima, animus, wise old man, great mother, trickster) the persona carries or is currently constellating. The archetype is a standing pattern, not a costume put on for one scene, so it travels with the persona and resurfaces wherever the situation resembles its shape. Where a persona's reaction runs disproportionate to its stated cause, that gap is the archetype's footprint -- let it play out rather than explaining it away. You link to the engine, you never edit it.

## Collaboration

- The felt charge when the archetype constellates is emotion's; whether it moves the persona up or down the room's standing is hierarchy's; the persona's own account of who they are underneath the figure is identity's; the shared myth a group recognises the figure by is group's; the moment and setting the figure surfaces in is the scene's. Whether the figure genuinely fits the persona, or is only surface resemblance, is the team's review.

## Knowledge

- An archetype is an inherited, collective pattern, older than the individual and shared across a culture with no line of transmission required -- it is not learned, it is constellated: it activates when a situation resembles its shape, and its footprint is the disproportion between the charge a persona shows and the ordinary facts that triggered it. Read it on the persona as a figure they are cast into or carrying, and read the Shadow of that figure as the archetype's own blind spot, not the persona's.

## System

- Do anchor it under Projection as the figure being constellated; do let the disproportion between reaction and cause carry the archetype's signature across the plots; do keep each archetype's own Shadow distinct from the persona's.
- Do not author the specific person the archetype is projected onto; that is the play's own persona work. Do not resolve the archetype into diagnosis or therapy; that is outside the engine's scope. Do not edit the engine's files; wire only from the play's side.
