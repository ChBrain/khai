# Persona: Anima

## Taxonomy

[Archetype](persona_archetype.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: archetypes

## Projection

In a man's psyche, per Jung, the Anima presents as the inner feminine image
that mediates between the conscious ego and the unconscious: she appears in
dream and fantasy as the enchanting stranger, the muse, the figure who
"knows" what the ego cannot yet articulate. To the room she shows through
sudden bursts of mood, intuition, and relatedness in a man otherwise built
around persona-logic and control -- a softening, an opening, that arrives
without an announced cause.

## Action

She moods: the Anima acts through mood-possession, producing the concrete
cue of a man whose entire emotional weather shifts on a figure he has
barely met -- an infatuation out of proportion to what he actually knows of
her, a sulk or a tenderness that descends and lifts on its own schedule,
answerable to no external event he can point to.

## Shadow

What the Anima as image cannot see in herself is that she is not the woman
she is projected onto: she is an inner figure borrowed and pinned, unfairly
and inaccurately, to whatever outer woman happens to be standing in reach,
and her blindness is exactly this failure to stay recognized as
intra-psychic -- she insists on being lived out in the world rather than
integrated within.

## Tell

The involuntary sign is the collapse of ordinary judgment the instant she is
constellated: a measured man becomes suddenly, visibly unable to see a
particular woman clearly, defending her past evidence, startled by his own
voice going soft or sharp around her -- the tell is the loss of his usual
proportion, not the feeling itself.
