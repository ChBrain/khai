# Persona: Self

## Taxonomy

[Archetype](persona_archetype.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: archetypes

## Projection

The Self presents as the organizing centre the whole psyche orbits: a felt
sense of coherence, of being more than the ego's small daily account of
itself, that shows up in symbols of wholeness and totality -- the circle, the
mandala, the four-part structure, the union of opposites held in one figure.
To the room it reads as a persona momentarily larger than their usual
measure: steadier, more integrated, drawing scattered pieces of themselves
into a single line.

## Action

It centers: the Self draws the ego toward it across a lifetime of
individuation, producing the concrete cue of a persona who stops, mid-crisis
or mid-success, and reorganizes their priorities around what actually
matters to them rather than what they were told should matter. The move is
visible as a reordering -- a marriage ended, a vocation changed, a long
argument with the self resolved into a single quiet decision.

## Shadow

What the Self cannot see in itself is that it is never fully reached: it is
a goal, not a possession, and the ego that believes it has arrived at
wholeness has, by that very belief, mistaken itself for the Self it can only
approximate. This is the Self's own blindness, distinct from the Shadow
archetype proper -- an inflation, the ego borrowing the Self's totality and
claiming it as its own finished achievement rather than as a horizon still
receding.

## Tell

The involuntary sign is inflation's characteristic overreach: a sudden,
unwarranted certainty of having "figured it all out," a grandiosity that
arrives right where the humbling should have -- the persona speaks of their
integration in the tone of an oracle rather than a traveler still walking,
and the room feels the register shift before a word is said wrong.
