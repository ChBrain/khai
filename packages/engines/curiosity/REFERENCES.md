# Curiosity: Reference

## Line of Work

Curiosity as **position**: the resting inclination a persona holds toward the unknown -- the posture at the edge of what is already understood, before any particular gap or target has appeared. The domain does not model attention (that is the attention engine's); attention allocates a spotlight reactively; curiosity is the disposition toward uncertainty itself, the shape of the approach before any target is selected.

The spine is the multi-dimensional model of trait curiosity: epistemic, perceptual, social, and exploratory curiosity are distinct expressions of the same underlying inclination, each with its own object and its own form of engagement.

## Origin

Curiosity research in positive psychology and motivation science, applied directly as position constraints.

| Source | Key Work | Scope |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Todd Kashdan, Paul Rose &amp; Frank Fincham** | *"Curiosity and Exploration: Facilitating Positive Subjective Experiences and Personal Growth Opportunities"* (2004) | Curiosity as a multidimensional trait: joyous exploration, deprivation sensitivity, stress tolerance, social curiosity, thrill-seeking. |
| **George Loewenstein** | *"The Psychology of Curiosity: A Review and Reinterpretation"* (1994) | Information gap theory: curiosity is the felt tension between what is known and what could be known; the gap is the source of the pull. |
| **Daniel Berlyne** | *Conflict, Arousal, and Curiosity* (1960) | Perceptual curiosity and the novelty-complexity dimension: the organism is pulled toward stimuli that are neither too familiar nor too complex. |
| **Mario Livio** | *Why?: What Makes Us Curious* (2017) | Distinction between epistemic curiosity (knowledge-gap driven) and perceptual curiosity (novelty-driven); each has a different signature. |

## Restrictions

What the engine refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Attention**: The engine models the resting inclination toward novelty and the unknown, not the dynamic allocation of the spotlight. How the persona distributes attention in a live scene is **delegated to the attention engine**. A highly curious persona still allocates attention moment to moment; curiosity sets the direction of the inclination, not the moment-by-moment target.
- **Motivation**: The engine models the epistemic stance -- the posture toward not-knowing -- not the drive source behind action generally. Why a persona acts, and from what source, is owned by the motivation engine; curiosity sets the orientation, not the energy behind it.
- **Belief revision**: The engine models the inclination to explore, not what the persona does when it encounters disconfirming evidence. How the persona responds when a held conviction meets a challenge is **delegated to the belief engine**.
- **Knowledge content**: The engine models the disposition toward the unknown, not what the persona already knows or seeks to know in a specific domain. Domain expertise is biographical, owned by the persona's heritage and the play's knowledge design.

## Encoding

Source to constraint, per file.

- **[curiosity](position_curiosity.md)** (the anchor): The resting inclination toward the unknown -- the posture at the edge of knowing, before any gap or target has appeared. Anchored by Kashdan (curiosity as trait disposition) and Loewenstein (the information gap as the structural source of the pull).
- **[epistemic](position_epistemic.md)** (expression): The pull toward knowledge gaps -- the itch of not-knowing that does not settle until the gap is closed. Anchored by Loewenstein (information gap theory) and Livio (epistemic curiosity).
- **[perceptual](position_perceptual.md)** (expression): The pull toward novelty in the sensory field -- restlessness in the familiar, appetite for new stimulus. Anchored by Berlyne (perceptual curiosity, novelty-complexity) and Livio (perceptual curiosity).
- **[social](position_social.md)** (expression): The pull toward the interior lives of others -- people as the inexhaustible interesting unknown. Anchored by Kashdan (social curiosity as a distinct curiosity dimension).
- **[exploratory](position_exploratory.md)** (expression): The pull toward the uncharted for its own sake -- path over destination, discovery as the reward. Anchored by Kashdan (joyous exploration and thrill-seeking) and Berlyne (diversive curiosity).
