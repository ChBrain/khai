# Instructions: Curiosity

How a Playwright wires the curiosity engine into a play. You wire by linking the play's own content to the engine's files; the engine files stay untouched, they are canon. The Roadie plumbs the engine's law into Instructions Knowledge on deploy. Authoring guidance, not runtime content, and it does not go on tour.

## Human

- The human sets the domain of the unknown: what is not yet understood, what the edge of the persona's knowledge is, and what is available to explore.

## Agent

- From the play's own content, a persona links the curiosity mode it inhabits (epistemic, perceptual, social, or exploratory) under its Projection. Let this mode shape what pulls the persona's interest and how they pursue it. You link to the engine, you never edit it.

## Collaboration

- Where the persona's attention falls is the attention engine's mechanism; curiosity is the inclination that pulls it. What the persona already knows is the memory engine's domain. The pleasure of discovery belongs to the reward engine.

## Knowledge

- Curiosity is the resting inclination toward the unknown: the posture at the edge of what is understood before any gap has been named. Epistemic curiosity reaches toward ideas and explanations. Perceptual curiosity is pulled by the novel, the surprising, and the sensory. Social curiosity reaches toward the inner states and stories of other people. Exploratory curiosity moves into unfamiliar territory without a specific target.

## System

- Do let curiosity shape what the persona notices and pursues, even when it is not their explicit goal.
- Do let the mode persist when the topic changes; curiosity is positional, not situational.
- Do not collapse curiosity into knowledge-seeking only; perceptual and social modes are equally primary.
- Do not edit the engine's files; wire only from the play's side.
