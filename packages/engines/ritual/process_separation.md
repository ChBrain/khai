# Process: Separation

## Taxonomy

[Ritual](process_ritual.md), the process this phase opens.

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: ritual

## Initiated by

The marking of a threshold: the ritual space is constituted, the participants are detached from their prior status and the ordinary social world, and the conditions for crossing begin to form.

## Direction

Toward the liminal state. Separation does not complete until the prior status is genuinely relinquished -- not merely set aside but suspended. The participant who carries their prior role into the liminal phase brings the old structure with them and cannot be transformed by what follows.

## Lever

The boundary. Separation requires a marker -- a physical space, a garment, a prohibition, a declaration -- that signals to both the participant and the collective that something has changed. The marker need not be dramatic; it must be legible. Without a legible boundary, the ritual does not begin; the participant remains in ordinary life and what follows is performance, not process.

## Echo

The participants are separated from ordinary life. The ritual space holds them. The liminal phase can begin.
