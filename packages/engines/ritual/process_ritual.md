# Process: Ritual

## Taxonomy

A process. How collective action crosses a threshold and marks or transforms participants. Root of the ritual process tree.

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: ritual

## Initiated by

The recognition that a threshold must be crossed collectively, and that the crossing requires form: actions performed in sequence, symbols shared, attention focused together. Ritual is not merely habit or custom; it is action that constitutes or transforms social reality rather than operating within it.

## Direction

Toward transformation or marking: participants emerge from the ritual in a different status, bound by a new solidarity, or carrying a charge of emotional energy that ordinary interaction does not produce.

## Lever

Collective effervescence: the intensification of shared feeling that arises when participants focus their attention on a common object and coordinate their action and mood together. Effervescence is not guaranteed; it emerges when the conditions are right -- bodily co-presence, shared focus, barriers that separate the ritual from ordinary life, and mutual awareness among participants that something is happening.

## Echo

Participants have crossed the threshold. Their status is transformed or confirmed; the collective is renewed. The emotional energy produced disperses into ordinary life and decays unless renewed by further ritual interaction.
