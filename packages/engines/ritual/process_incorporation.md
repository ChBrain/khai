# Process: Incorporation

## Taxonomy

[Ritual](process_ritual.md), the process this phase closes.

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: ritual

## Initiated by

The end of the liminal phase. The participants cross back into the social world, but they cross as someone who has undergone the ritual. The new status must be received and recognized by the collective; without that reception, the transformation is incomplete.

## Direction

Toward the re-entry of a changed participant into an ordinary social world that now knows them differently. The transformation is not merely internal; it is publicly marked and publicly held. The community confirms what the ritual declared.

## Lever

Public recognition. A status change that is not received by the community does not hold: the individual's sense of transformation cannot sustain itself without the mirroring of others. The ritual's final act is the declaration and the reception -- the new name, the new role, the new membership -- and both must occur for the incorporation to be complete.

## Echo

The participants are re-incorporated into social life in their new status. The ritual is over. What they carry is the transformed identity, the solidarity produced in liminality, and the emotional energy of the effervescence -- each decaying at its own rate, each renewable by further ritual interaction.
