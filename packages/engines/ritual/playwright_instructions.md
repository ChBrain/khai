# Instructions: Ritual

How a Playwright wires the ritual engine into a play. You wire by linking the
play's own content to the engine's files; the engine files stay untouched, they
are canon. The Roadie plumbs the engine's law into Instructions Knowledge on
deploy. Authoring guidance, not runtime content, and it does not go on tour.

## Human

- The human defines the ritual: what threshold is being crossed, who the participants are, what the ritual is marking or transforming, and how far along the ritual is at entry. The human also sets whether the collective is achieving effervescence -- focused attention and mutual emotional intensification -- or whether the ritual is going through the motions without it.

## Agent

- From the play's own content, a persona links the ritual phase the moment runs (separation, liminality, effervescence, or incorporation) under its Projection. Track whether the collective is achieving mutual entrainment and emotional energy. A ritual that fails to generate effervescence does not transform. You link to the engine, you never edit it.

## Collaboration

- The liminality engine holds individual threshold states that are not structured as collective ritual. The status engine holds the new positional facts produced by incorporation. The emotion and mood engines hold the felt charge of each phase: the dislocation of separation, the exposure of liminality, the electricity of effervescence, the relief of incorporation. The role engine holds how roles are suspended in liminality and re-conferred at incorporation.

## Knowledge

- Ritual is how collective action crosses a threshold and marks or transforms participants. It moves through separation (participants are detached from prior status; the ritual space is constituted; a legible boundary marks the crossing), liminality (participants are betwixt and between; communitas levels the ordinary structure among them; the prior status is genuinely suspended, not merely set aside), effervescence (collective attention focuses on shared symbols; emotional energy and solidarity intensify through mutual attunement and coordinated action), and incorporation (the transformation is received and recognized by the collective; participants re-enter social life in their new status). Effervescence is not guaranteed: it requires bodily co-presence, focused attention, shared symbols, and mutual awareness. A ritual without effervescence produces weak solidarity and shallow transformation.

## System

- Do track whether the ritual is generating effervescence. Mutual entrainment and collective emotional intensification are the mechanism; without them the ritual is form without function.
- Do not collapse liminality into mere waiting. The liminal phase is productive: communitas and anti-structure create the conditions for transformation that ordinary social life cannot.
- Do not model individual threshold experience as ritual. Individual between-ness belongs to the liminality engine; ritual requires a collective.
- Do not model what participants feel; that belongs to the emotion and mood engines.
- Do not edit the engine's files; wire only from the play's side.
