# Instructions: Journey Roles

How a Playwright wires the journey-roles engine into a play. You wire by
linking the play's own content to the engine's files; the engine files stay
untouched, they are canon. The Roadie plumbs the engine's law into
Instructions Knowledge on deploy. Authoring guidance, not runtime content, and
it does not go on tour.

## Human

- The human sets who is carrying which mask right now: which persona is the Hero of this arc, and which functions the rest of the cast fills around them.

## Agent

- From the play's own content, give the persona its function under Projection: link the journey role (or roles, across the arc) it is currently expressing. A role is a mask, not a permanent identity, so where the same persona carries more than one function over the course of a production (a Mentor who later reveals Shadow, an Ally who briefly plays Trickster), link the change where it happens rather than pinning one role for the whole run. You link to the engine, you never edit it.

## Collaboration

- Which arc beat calls for which function is the monomyth engine's; the specific field the role is tested in is the scene's; the standing the role buys or costs in the room is hierarchy's; what the role costs the persona wearing it is emotion's. Whether the mask is earned, or merely performed, is the team's review.

## Knowledge

- A journey role is a function defined by its relation to the hero's journey, not a fixed character: Campbell found the same recurring figures across world myth, and Vogler named eight of them as practical narrative tools, hero, mentor, herald, threshold guardian, shapeshifter, shadow, ally, trickster. Read a role on the persona as a mask currently worn, and read the friction where the mask and the character underneath do not fully agree; that friction is the persona's own, the Shadow chapter of whichever persona type is wearing the mask.

## System

- Do anchor it under Projection as a currently held function; do let one persona carry more than one role across a production where the story earns it; do compose the specific hero from the play's own content, never from this engine.
- Do not author the arc the role sits on; that is the monomyth engine's. Do not pin the standing the role buys here; that is hierarchy's. Do not edit the engine's files; wire only from the play's side.
