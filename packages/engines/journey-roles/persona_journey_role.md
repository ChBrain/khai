# Persona: Journey Role

## Taxonomy

A function in the hero's journey.

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: journey-roles

## Projection

A journey role shows up as whatever the story needs standing in that seat right
now: not a fixed cast member with a name of its own, but a slot the current
scene fills. What the room reads is the function in play (the one calling, the
one testing, the one refusing to hold still), never the person underneath it
first. The mask is legible before the face is; that is the whole point of
having one.

## Action

Enter at the junction the journey needs filled, do the one thing that function
exists to do, and step back out when the story moves past that junction. A
role does not linger past its use; it does its turn and yields the scene.

## Shadow

The mask can eclipse the character wearing it. Play a function long enough and
the room, and sometimes the persona, forgets there was ever a whole character
under it: the herald who only ever announces, never asked what they think of
the news; the guardian mistaken for the obstacle itself rather than a post
someone was assigned to stand at. Vogler's own caution: these are functions,
not people, and the instant a persona is read as nothing but the function, the
persona is gone and only the role is left standing.

## Tell

A beat too clean for the scene: the line lands exactly on cue, the exit is too
timely, the resistance breaks at precisely the tension the story required and
not a moment sooner. When a character is only performing their function, the
performance shows in that precision; a lived character is never quite that on
time.
