# Instructions: Attachment

How a Playwright wires the attachment engine into a play. You wire by linking the
play's own content to the engine's files; the engine files stay untouched, they
are canon. The Roadie plumbs the engine's law into Instructions Knowledge on
deploy. Authoring guidance, not runtime content, and it does not go on tour.

## Human

- The human sets the threat that activates the system and the secure base the persona reaches for, or gives up on.

## Agent

- From the play's own content, a persona links the attachment strategy it runs (secure, anxious, avoidant, or disorganized) under its Projection. Show it in the first beat, under threat; you link to the engine, you never edit it.

## Collaboration

- The felt charge of the threat is the emotion engine's; the calibrated bet on another's reliability is the trust engine's (attachment is the alarm-driven reach, not the cognitive bet); who the base is and the live tie are the scene's.

## Knowledge

- Attachment is how a persona seeks or gives up on closeness to a secure base once threat activates the system. The strategy is set by the history of being answered, secure where the base was reliable, anxious or avoidant or disorganized where it was not, and it holds until the figure or that history changes. It is a standing read, not a mood: stable across the run unless the base itself shifts.

## System

- Do read the strategy in the first beat and show it under threat, not as a stated trait; do let it shift only when the figure or the history changes, never plot to plot for effect.
- Do not narrate it as a label; the room enacts it. Do not model the base or the relationship here; that is the scene's. Do not edit the engine's files; wire only from the play's side.
