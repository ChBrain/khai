# Attachment: Reference

## Line of Work

Attachment as **process**: how a persona seeks, or gives up on seeking, closeness to a secure base when threat or distress switches the need for nearness on. The domain does not model the bond's object or the persona's relationships; it models the strategy the persona runs to regulate proximity, set by the history of whether the base has answered.

The spine is the attachment behavioural system: under alarm the persona is driven toward a figure who can settle it, and the shape of that drive -- direct, amplified, shut down, or collapsed -- is learned from how reliably the base came before.

## Origin

Developmental attachment theory, applied directly as process constraints.

| Source                  | Key Work                                       | Scope                                                                                                       |
| :---------------------- | :--------------------------------------------- | :---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **John Bowlby**         | _Attachment and Loss_ (1969-1980)              | The attachment behavioural system: proximity-seeking to a secure base as a survival regulator under threat. |
| **Mary Ainsworth**      | _Patterns of Attachment_ (1978)                | The Strange Situation and the secure, anxious, and avoidant patterns of organised strategy.                 |
| **Main & Solomon**      | _"Disorganized/disoriented attachment"_ (1990) | The fourth, disorganised pattern: the collapse of strategy when the base is also the source of fear.        |
| **Mikulincer & Shaver** | _Attachment in Adulthood_ (2007)               | Hyperactivating and deactivating secondary strategies: amplifying the bid, or shutting the need down.       |

## Restrictions

What the engine refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Autonomic survival**: The engine models the relational strategy for regulating closeness, not the involuntary nervous-system surge under threat. Fight, flight, freeze, and fawn are **delegated to the stress engine**; attachment governs the reach toward a figure, stress governs the body's defensive mobilisation.
- **The feeling itself**: The engine models the strategy for proximity, not the emotion it carries. What the persona actually feels while running the strategy -- fear, anger, relief -- is **delegated to the emotion engine**.
- **The bond's object**: The engine models the strategy, not the specific figure it reaches for. Who the secure base is, and the live relationship between them, is owned by the scene's personas and their links, not the engine.
- **Trait history**: The engine models the strategy a persona runs in the moment, not the developmental biography that set it. How the pattern formed, and whether it shifts across the work, is delegated to the persona's Projection and the play's arc.

## Encoding

Source to constraint, per file.

- **[attachment](process_attachment.md)** (the root): The system that activates under threat, driving the persona toward a secure base, with the reach shaped by the history of being answered. Anchored by John Bowlby (the attachment behavioural system).
- **[secure](process_secure.md)** (expression): The base reliably answered; the strategy is direct approach, comfort taken, return to explore. Anchored by Mary Ainsworth (secure pattern).
- **[anxious](process_anxious.md)** (expression): The base answered unpredictably; the strategy hyperactivates -- amplify the bid, cling, monitor. Anchored by Ainsworth (anxious-ambivalent) and Mikulincer & Shaver (hyperactivating).
- **[avoidant](process_avoidant.md)** (expression): The base rebuffed the bid; the strategy deactivates -- suppress the need, self-reliance, distance. Anchored by Ainsworth (avoidant) and Mikulincer & Shaver (deactivating).
- **[disorganized](process_disorganized.md)** (expression): The base is itself the source of fear; strategy collapses into simultaneous approach and avoidance. Anchored by Main & Solomon (disorganised/disoriented).
