# Process: Transgression, Survival

## Taxonomy

[Transgression](process_transgression.md), the crossing this is one kind of.

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: guilt

## Initiated by

The persona came through what others did not -- the accident, the layoff,
the war, the illness, the lottery of any shared catastrophe -- and reads
their own survival as an unearned advantage over the ones who paid. A kind
of [transgression](process_transgression.md) with no act at its center: no
one chose to live at another's expense, and the crossing is constructed
afterward, out of counterfactuals.

## Direction

Backward, into what could have been done.
The vector runs against time: the persona replays the event and authors
themselves into it -- I should have warned them, traded places, been there,
died too. Lifton's survivors called it failed enactment: the self measured
against an act that was never actually available, and found wanting anyway.

## Lever

Authorship the persona builds where none existed. The other crossings need a
free alternative; survival manufactures one in retrospect, and the
manufactured choice presses the standard exactly as a real one would. That
is what makes this crossing resistant to every ordinary repair: no harmed
party remains to accept anything, and the act to be undone never happened.

## Echo

An account that cannot be closed by its own rules -- the debt is held toward
the dead, the gone, the ones who cannot receive. What survivors find, when
the account moves at all, is substitution: living in a way that honors what
was lost, the repair delivered to the world in place of the ones who left
it. Unsubstituted, this crossing grades toward the haunting faster than any
other.
