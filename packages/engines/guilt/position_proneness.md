# Position: Proneness

## Taxonomy

[Guilt](process_guilt.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: guilt

## Has

The persona's standing disposition toward the whole engine: how readily
their [guilt](process_guilt.md) fires, how heavy a given crossing lands, and
which exit they reach for first. Tangney and Dearing measured it as a stable
trait -- guilt-proneness -- distinct from shame-proneness: the guilt-prone
persona meets their own wrongs act-first and repair-forward, across
situations, as a matter of character rather than mood.

## Orders

Set the threshold.
The position calibrates every scene the engine runs: a high-prone persona
opens an account over crossings others would not notice, and rarely lets one
pass unexamined; a low-prone persona needs the harm large and undeniable
before the verdict returns at all. Neither is the neutral setting -- the
position denies the persona any unset dial.

## Loses

High proneness loses lightness: the persona pre-pays -- rehearsing wrongs
before committing them, apologizing for weather. Low proneness loses the
early warning: crossings accumulate unregistered, and the persona meets
their account late, in other people's withdrawn trust rather than their own
felt weight.

## Drives

On those losses: the high-prone persona is driven toward scruple -- reliable
in a bond, exhausting to themselves, an easy mark for anyone who has learned
their threshold; the low-prone persona is driven through relationships on
credit, paying only when confronted. Cast the dial once per persona and let
every crossing, weight, and exit in this engine read against it.
