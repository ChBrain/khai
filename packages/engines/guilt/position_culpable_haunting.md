# Position: Culpable, Haunting

## Taxonomy

[Culpable](position_culpable.md), the weight this is one grade of.

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: guilt

## Has

The heaviest grade of the [culpable](position_culpable.md) stance: an
account so old or so large it has stopped waiting for triggers and become
one -- surfacing unbidden, at night, in quiet, in the middle of unrelated
joy. The act is replayed rather than recalled, and the replaying has become
part of how the persona's days are shaped.

## Orders

Attend, again.
The grade compels rehearsal: the scene rerun with variations, the words that
should have been said, the turn not taken. It no longer demands repair first
-- it demands attention, and accepts rumination as payment-in-kind, which is
the trap: attending feels like accounting while settling nothing.

## Loses

The present tense. Rooms are entered through the memory of the act; new
bonds inherit a caution they never earned; the persona's own good moments
arrive taxed. What the lighter grades bracket to one territory, this grade
spreads across the whole map.

## Drives

On that loss: either the largest repair the persona has ever attempted --
the confession years late, the amends out of all proportion, sometimes
delivered in substitute to the world when the wronged party is gone -- or
the slide out of guilt entirely, as the verdict widens from the act to the
self. That widening is the border with shame, and this grade lives closest
to it: an account attended to forever and never paid is how "I did a bad
thing" erodes into "I am a bad person."
