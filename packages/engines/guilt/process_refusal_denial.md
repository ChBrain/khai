# Process: Refusal, Denial

## Taxonomy

[Refusal](process_refusal.md), the dodge this is one technique of.

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: guilt

## Initiated by

The account's opening facts come under revision. One technique of
[refusal](process_refusal.md), running Sykes and Matza's first two moves:
denial of responsibility -- "I had no choice, I was pushed, anyone would
have" -- and denial of injury -- "no real harm was done, they can afford it,
they never even noticed."

## Direction

At the crossing itself.
The vector runs backward into the transgression and edits it: the free
alternative is written out (so there was no real choice), or the damage is
written down (so there was no real harm). Either edit, if it holds, means
nothing was crossed at all -- the cleanest possible dodge.

## Lever

The genuine softness of the facts. Choices are never perfectly free and harm
is never perfectly measurable, and denial presses on exactly that give: it
stretches a real constraint into compulsion, rounds a real injury down to
nothing. The lever fails where the facts are hard -- a named victim, a
visible wound -- which is why denial prefers crossings of omission and harms
that do not bruise.

## Echo

The persona keeps a version of events that cannot survive contact with the
wronged party's version, so contact is what gets avoided. The bond continues
on a fork: two accounts of one act, reconciled never. Where denial is later
surrendered -- the fact admitted at last -- the original account reopens
whole, with the dodge itself now a second entry in it.
