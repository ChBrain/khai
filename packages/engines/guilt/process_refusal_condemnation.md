# Process: Refusal, Condemnation

## Taxonomy

[Refusal](process_refusal.md), the dodge this is one technique of.

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: guilt

## Initiated by

The account's judges come under attack. One technique of
[refusal](process_refusal.md), running Sykes and Matza's outward-facing
moves: condemning the condemners -- "who are they to talk, everyone does it,
the ones pointing fingers are worse" -- and the appeal to higher loyalties --
"I did it for the family, the team, the cause; the smaller rule had to
yield."

## Direction

At the standard's authority.
The vector runs outward, from the act to the act's would-be judges: their
hypocrisy, their motives, their own records. Every fault found in the court
discounts the verdict, and the persona's own crossing recedes behind the
prosecution of the prosecutors.

## Lever

The judges' real imperfection, and the real pull of rival loyalties. The
technique presses on truths -- condemners often are compromised, and
loyalties genuinely do conflict -- which is what lets it pass as moral
seriousness rather than dodge. The tell is the asymmetry: the standards
applied to the judges are never applied to the act.

## Echo

The persona exits the scene more certain, not less -- refusal by
condemnation is the one dodge that leaves the persona feeling principled.
The cost lands on the bond and the room: judges answered with indictments
stop judging aloud and start deciding quietly, and the persona loses the
early warnings that franker rooms provide. Held long, this technique curates
a world with no court left in it -- and an account that no verdict will now
ever close.
