# Process: Reparation, Self-Punishment

## Taxonomy

[Reparation](process_reparation.md), the paying this is one road of.

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: guilt

## Initiated by

The account is open and the outward road is blocked -- the wronged party is
unreachable, unwilling, unaware, or gone -- or the persona cannot bring
themselves to walk it. The pull to pay remains, and turns inward. Nelissen
and Zeelenberg named it the Dobby effect, after the house-elf who irons his
own hands: guilt seeking a penalty when it cannot deliver a repair.

## Direction

Onto the self.
The vector runs at the persona's own comfort and claims: the denied
pleasure, the punishing workload, the refusal of help or credit or rest, the
pain sought in ways the persona would never explain aloud. Payment is made
-- but to the standard, not to the harmed; nothing travels toward the one
who holds the damage.

## Lever

The felt exchange rate between suffering and debt. Self-punishment presses
because paying something feels truer than paying nothing: the persona's own
cost stands in for the amends they cannot or will not deliver. The lever is
real -- studies find the guilty genuinely choose their own discomfort -- and
it is also counterfeit, because the account's holder receives none of it.

## Echo

The weight lifts a little, and the account stays open. That is the road's
signature: relief without repair, so the toll must be paid again, and the
persona can mistake the paying for virtue. Where the outward road ever
reopens -- the person found, the words possible -- one delivered
[reparation](process_reparation.md) retires more debt than any amount of
this; a persona who keeps choosing the inward road anyway is no longer
paying the account but keeping it.
