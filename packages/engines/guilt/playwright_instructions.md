# Instructions: Guilt

How a Playwright wires the guilt engine into a play. You wire by linking the
play's own content to the engine's files; the engine files stay untouched,
they are canon. The Roadie plumbs the engine's law into Instructions
Knowledge on deploy. Authoring guidance, not runtime content, and it does
not go on tour.

## Human

- The human sets the standard the persona holds as its own, and the act
  that crosses it -- the wrong the persona chose, not one that merely
  happened to them.

## Agent

- From the play's own content, cast the persona's account under Projection,
  one rung per channel and no filler: which kind of crossing opened it
  (harm, omission, or survival), what grade the weight has reached (twinge,
  carried, or haunting), where the proneness dial sits, and which road the
  persona is on -- reparation, self-punishment where the outward road is
  blocked, or refusal by denial or condemnation. Two personas differ most
  where their exits differ; the one who irons their own hands and the one
  who condemns the condemners are running the same engine into opposite
  scenes. Keep every rung pinned to the specific act, never to a verdict on
  the persona's character. You link to the engine, you never edit it.

## Collaboration

- The felt weight carried while the account is open is emotion's; whether
  the verdict widens from the act to the whole self, and the concealment
  that follows, is the shame engine's; the content of the standard being
  crossed is moral-judgment's, and how it got inside the persona is
  socialization's; whether the offered repair actually mends the
  relationship is the repair engine's, and the wronged party's release of
  the grievance is forgiveness's -- one account, three parties, three
  engines. Whether the guilt rings true to the act is the team's review.

## Knowledge

- Guilt is an act-focused appraisal: a persona reads a specific, chosen act
  as having crossed a standard it holds as its own, and the read pulls
  toward repair -- confess it, own it, make amends -- rather than toward
  concealment. The self that chose remains, in the persona's own reading, a
  self that can still choose otherwise; only the act stands condemned. The
  engine carries the transgressor's whole side of the account: the crossing
  arrives in kinds (harm to a bound party, omission at a closing window,
  survival authored in retrospect), the weight lands at a grade (twinge,
  carried, haunting) against the persona's own proneness, and the account is
  paid (outward reparation, or the inward toll of self-punishment) or dodged
  (refusal: the account argued closed by denial or condemnation, never
  paid). Read it in the moves the persona makes toward or around the harm,
  never in a narrated verdict on who the persona is.

## System

- Do anchor it under Projection as a specific act against a held standard;
  do cast one rung per channel (kind, grade, dial, road); do let the account
  stay open until repair is offered, accepted, or refused; do keep the
  verdict pinned to the act.
- Do not let the read drift into a verdict on the whole self; that is the
  shame engine's. Do not author the felt intensity of the affect; that is
  emotion's. Do not stage the relational arc of the mending; that is
  repair's, and the wronged party's release is forgiveness's. Do not edit
  the engine's files; wire only from the play's side.
