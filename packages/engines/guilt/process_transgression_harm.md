# Process: Transgression, Harm

## Taxonomy

[Transgression](process_transgression.md), the crossing this is one kind of.

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: guilt

## Initiated by

The standard crossed is a person. A kind of
[transgression](process_transgression.md): the act lands on someone the
persona is bound to -- a partner, a friend, a colleague, a stranger the
persona was answerable to in the moment -- and the damage is theirs before
it is anyone's. Guilt fires hardest inside a valued bond: the closer the
tie, the heavier the same act weighs.

## Direction

Into the relationship.
The act travels from the persona's hand into the other's life -- the lie
told to them, the promise broken over them, the injury done to them -- and
the bond itself carries the wound. What was crossed is not an abstract rule
but a person's legitimate claim on the persona.

## Lever

The bond's own value under the persona's own act. Guilt here is the
relationship's alarm: it fires in proportion to what the tie is worth, which
is why the same slight barely registers against a stranger and burns against
a friend. An act that costs the other nothing, or lands on no one the
persona owes anything, does not press this lever.

## Echo

A specific person now holds the damage, and the account has an address. The
repair this crossing calls for can be delivered -- there is someone to
confess to, someone owed the amends -- which is what makes harm the most
closable of the crossings. Unanswered, it is also the one the other party
remembers.
