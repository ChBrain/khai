# Process: Reparation

## Taxonomy

[Guilt](process_guilt.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: guilt

## Initiated by

The [culpable](position_culpable.md) persona holds an open account and a
party, or a standard, to whom it is owed. The process does not open on
remorse alone; it needs somewhere the repair can be delivered -- a person to
confess to, a harm to undo, a standard to visibly meet again. Remorse with
nowhere to land does not start this process; it only keeps the account open.

## Direction

Toward the harmed party or the broken standard.
The vector runs outward -- confession, apology, restitution, the visible
remaking of what the act damaged -- rather than inward toward the persona's
own relief. Relief is a byproduct the process may leave behind; it is never
the aim the vector points at. Where the paying runs through a live
relationship, the staging of that arc -- the accounting given and received,
the restitution enacted, the bond re-integrated -- is the repair engine's;
what lives here is the pull that delivers the persona to it. Where the
outward road is blocked or unbearable, the pull turns inward instead:
[self-punishment](process_reparation_self_punishment.md), payment made to
the standard and never to the harmed.

## Lever

What the repair costs against what the account demands. A repair cheaper
than the harm reads as a dodge and leaves the account open; a repair the
persona cannot actually deliver stalls the process before it can close. The
lever is the fit between what is offered and what is owed, not the sincerity
behind the offer alone.

## Echo

Where the offer is accepted, the account closes and the [culpable](position_culpable.md)
position lifts -- the persona returns to standing without having had to
forget what they did. Where it is refused, or where no repair was ever
possible, the account stays open, and [guilt](process_guilt.md) keeps running
un-repaired.
