# Guilt: Reference

## Line of Work

Guilt as **process**: how a persona appraises a specific act against a
standard it holds as its own, and how that appraisal becomes a reparative
pull rather than a verdict on the whole self. The domain does not model the
felt intensity of the affect (that is emotion's), the content of the
standard being crossed (that is moral-judgment's), or the actual mending of
a relationship (that is repair's) -- it models the appraisal itself: the
crossing, the stance it leaves the persona holding, and the pull to close
the account.

The spine is Tangney and Dearing's distinction between guilt and shame:
guilt condemns a specific behavior ("I did a bad thing"), leaving the self
intact and able to act on the world to fix it, while shame condemns the
whole self ("I am a bad person") and drives concealment instead of repair.
Guilt names the act-focused half of that pair, and the reparative action
tendency it carries.

Around that spine the engine carries the transgressor's whole side of the
account: the kinds a crossing arrives in (interpersonal harm, omission,
survival), the grades the carried weight moves through (twinge, carried,
haunting), the persona's own dispositional dial (proneness), and the two
roads an open account can take -- paid, outward through reparation or
inward through self-punishment, or dodged, through the techniques of
neutralization. What it never carries is the other side of the seam: the
wronged party's release and the dyad's rebuilding belong to other engines.

## Origin

Clinical and social psychology of the moral emotions, applied directly as
process and position constraints: Tangney and Dearing's guilt/shame
distinction, with Lewis's earlier framing of the same divide.

| :------------------------------------------------------------- | :-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | :----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **June Price Tangney & Ronda L. Dearing**                      | _Shame and Guilt_ (2002)                                                                            | Guilt as the negative appraisal of a specific act against an internalized standard, carrying a reparative action tendency -- confess, apologize, make amends -- distinct from shame's global self-condemnation and withdrawal. |
| **Helen Block Lewis**                                          | _Shame and Guilt in Neurosis_ (1971)                                                                | The earlier clinical framing of the same divide: guilt as the self held responsible for a discrete act it could have done otherwise, shame as the self itself found wanting.                                                   |

## Restrictions

What the engine refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Whole-self condemnation and hiding**: The engine models the act
  condemned, not the self condemned. Where the verdict widens from "I did a
  bad thing" to "I am a bad person," and the persona moves to conceal or
  disappear rather than repair, that read belongs to the shame engine.
- **The felt affect itself**: The engine models the appraisal and its
  reparative pull, not the intensity or texture of what is felt while
  carrying it. The heat, the heaviness, the somatic charge is the emotion
  engine's.
- **The standard's content**: The engine ships the appraisal mechanism, not
  what any given standard actually requires. Which acts a persona's culture
  or moral code condemns, and how firmly, is the moral-judgment engine's
  (and the culture's) to supply.
- **The actual repair of the relationship**: The engine models the pull to
  repair and the offer made, not whether the relationship itself is mended
  once the offer lands. The staged arc of accounting, restitution, and
  re-integration between the parties is the repair engine's; guilt delivers
  the persona to its door.
- **The wronged party's side**: The engine is the transgressor's, whole and
  only. The grievance held, released, or withheld by the one who was harmed
  is the forgiveness engine's; the reckoning with a violated trust is
  betrayal's. One account, three parties, three engines.
- **The standard's origin**: The engine takes the internalized standard as
  given. How it got inside the persona -- the family, the institutions, the
  significant others that put it there -- is the socialization engine's.

## Encoding

Source to constraint. A process anchor (the appraisal) with four channels
beneath it: the crossing in kinds, the weight in grades, the dispositional
dial, and the two roads an open account can take.

- **[guilt](process_guilt.md)** (the anchor): The read itself -- a chosen
  act meets a standard the persona holds as its own, and the appraisal pulls
  toward repair rather than concealment. Anchored by Tangney & Dearing
  (guilt vs. shame).
- **The crossing** -- [transgression](process_transgression.md) (the channel:
  standard, alternative, act chosen anyway; Tangney & Dearing, Lewis), in
  kinds: [harm](process_transgression_harm.md) (the damage lands on a bound
  party; Baumeister, Stillwell & Heatherton),
  [omission](process_transgression_omission.md) (the right act left unchosen
  as its window closes), [survival](process_transgression_survival.md) (the
  authorship constructed afterward from counterfactuals; Lifton).
- **The weight** -- [culpable](position_culpable.md) (the channel: author of
  record, account open; Tangney & Dearing), in grades:
  [twinge](position_culpable_twinge.md) (spent in its own scene),
  [carried](position_culpable_carried.md) (the account that travels),
  [haunting](position_culpable_haunting.md) (the account that has become its
  own trigger, living at the border with shame; Lewis).
- **The dial** -- [proneness](position_proneness.md): the standing
  disposition that sets how readily the whole engine fires. Anchored by
  Tangney & Dearing (guilt-proneness as trait).
- **The paying** -- [reparation](process_reparation.md) (the channel: the
  reparative action tendency delivered outward; Tangney & Dearing), with
  [self-punishment](process_reparation_self_punishment.md) as the inward
  toll where the outward road is blocked (Nelissen & Zeelenberg, the Dobby
  effect).
- **The dodging** -- [refusal](process_refusal.md) (the channel: the account
  argued closed, not paid; Sykes & Matza), by technique:
  [denial](process_refusal_denial.md) (of responsibility, of injury),
  [condemnation](process_refusal_condemnation.md) (of the condemners, and
  the appeal to higher loyalties).
