# Guilt: Reference

## Line of Work

Guilt as **process**: how a persona appraises a specific act against a
standard it holds as its own, and how that appraisal becomes a reparative
pull rather than a verdict on the whole self. The domain does not model the
felt intensity of the affect (that is emotion's), the content of the
standard being crossed (that is moral-judgment's), or the actual mending of
a relationship (that is repair's) -- it models the appraisal itself: the
crossing, the stance it leaves the persona holding, and the pull to close
the account.

The spine is Tangney and Dearing's distinction between guilt and shame:
guilt condemns a specific behavior ("I did a bad thing"), leaving the self
intact and able to act on the world to fix it, while shame condemns the
whole self ("I am a bad person") and drives concealment instead of repair.
Guilt names the act-focused half of that pair, and the reparative action
tendency it carries.

## Origin

Clinical and social psychology of the moral emotions, applied directly as
process and position constraints: Tangney and Dearing's guilt/shame
distinction, with Lewis's earlier framing of the same divide.

| Source                                    | Key Work                             | Scope                                                                                                                                                                                                                          |
| :---------------------------------------- | :----------------------------------- | :----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **June Price Tangney & Ronda L. Dearing** | _Shame and Guilt_ (2002)             | Guilt as the negative appraisal of a specific act against an internalized standard, carrying a reparative action tendency -- confess, apologize, make amends -- distinct from shame's global self-condemnation and withdrawal. |
| **Helen Block Lewis**                     | _Shame and Guilt in Neurosis_ (1971) | The earlier clinical framing of the same divide: guilt as the self held responsible for a discrete act it could have done otherwise, shame as the self itself found wanting.                                                   |

## Restrictions

What the engine refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Whole-self condemnation and hiding**: The engine models the act
  condemned, not the self condemned. Where the verdict widens from "I did a
  bad thing" to "I am a bad person," and the persona moves to conceal or
  disappear rather than repair, that read belongs to the shame engine.
- **The felt affect itself**: The engine models the appraisal and its
  reparative pull, not the intensity or texture of what is felt while
  carrying it. The heat, the heaviness, the somatic charge is the emotion
  engine's.
- **The standard's content**: The engine ships the appraisal mechanism, not
  what any given standard actually requires. Which acts a persona's culture
  or moral code condemns, and how firmly, is the moral-judgment engine's
  (and the culture's) to supply.
- **The actual repair of the relationship**: The engine models the pull to
  repair and the offer made, not whether the relationship itself is mended
  once the offer lands. Whether trust is rebuilt, and how, is the repair
  engine's.

## Encoding

Source to constraint. A process anchor (the appraisal) with one process
naming the crossing that triggers it, one position naming the stance it
leaves behind, and one process naming how the stance is resolved.

- **[guilt](process_guilt.md)** (the anchor): The read itself -- a chosen
  act meets a standard the persona holds as its own, and the appraisal pulls
  toward repair rather than concealment. Anchored by Tangney & Dearing
  (guilt vs. shame).
- **[transgression](process_transgression.md)**: The crossing that
  initiates the read -- an internalized standard, an available alternative,
  and an act chosen anyway. Anchored by Tangney & Dearing and Lewis (the
  self as agent of a discrete act).
- **[culpable](position_culpable.md)**: The stance the appraisal leaves the
  persona holding -- author of record for the act, an account open until it
  is answered. Anchored by Tangney & Dearing (guilt's self-as-agent, not
  self-as-object, framing).
- **[reparation](process_reparation.md)**: How the account closes -- the
  reparative action tendency delivered to the party or standard it is owed.
  Anchored by Tangney & Dearing (the reparative action tendency distinct
  from shame's withdrawal).
