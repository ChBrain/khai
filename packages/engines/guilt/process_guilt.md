# Process: Guilt

## Taxonomy

Moral self-appraisal (no khai file of its own yet).

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: guilt

## Initiated by

A specific act has crossed a standard the persona holds as their own, and the
persona reads themselves as the act's author. [Transgression](process_transgression.md)
is the crossing itself; guilt fires once the persona reads the crossing as
their own doing, chosen and not merely suffered. The verdict lands on the
act, not on the persona's whole character -- the self that chose remains, in
the persona's own reading, a self that can still choose otherwise.

## Direction

Toward repair.
The vector is reparative, not evasive: the persona moves to undo, confess,
apologize, or make amends, working on the damage rather than away from the
one who witnessed it. Where a self-condemning read turns the body from the
room, guilt turns the body toward the harm it caused.

## Lever

The act meets the standard under the persona's own authorship, and the
authorship is what makes the verdict stick. A wrong the persona did not
choose -- an accident with no negligence, a harm inflicted by another --
does not press the lever; only a chosen act, held against a standard the
persona has made their own, produces the weight. The [culpable](position_culpable.md)
stance is what carries that weight once it presses.

## Echo

The persona now holds an unpaid account: the act stands until it is
addressed, refused, or worn down by time and repetition. Where the account
is paid -- through [reparation](process_reparation.md) -- the weight lifts and
the self that chose is restored to standing without having to forget what it
chose. Where it goes unpaid and unrefused, the read can widen from the act
into the whole self; that widening, and the hiding it drives, belongs to the
persona's other appraisals, not to this one.
