# Debt: Reference

## Line of Work

Debt as **piece**: a quantified obligation one persona owes another, binding the two unequally until it is settled. The domain does not model the felt guilt of owing (that is the guilt and shame engines'), nor the gift that asks nothing back; it models the _owing itself_ -- the transfer that must return, the creditor and debtor stances it sets, and its arc from incurring through servicing to discharge or default.

The spine is Graeber's history of debt: debt is a promise rendered precise by money and enforceable by power -- an obligation between unequals dressed as a temporary, mutual exchange, and moralised so that "one must pay one's debts" makes refusal a wrong. Mauss supplies the deeper root: the transfer that obligates -- the gift that binds the receiver to return. Nietzsche supplies the moral residue: the ancient tie between debt and guilt (_Schuld_ is both).

## Origin

The anthropology, history, and morality of obligation, applied directly as one piece with its poles and its arc.

| Source                  | Key Work                                         | Scope                                                                                                                                |
| :---------------------- | :----------------------------------------------- | :----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **David Graeber**       | _Debt: The First 5,000 Years_ (2011)             | Debt as quantified, enforceable obligation; the creditor--debtor relation and its inequality; the morality that makes debts binding. |
| **Marcel Mauss**        | _The Gift_ (1925)                                | The transfer that obligates: the duty to give, receive, and reciprocate; how a handover binds the receiver to a return.              |
| **Friedrich Nietzsche** | _On the Genealogy of Morality_ (1887), 2nd essay | The creditor--debtor relation as an origin of moral feeling; the tie between debt and guilt (_Schuld_) and the weight of owing.      |

## Restrictions

What the engine refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **The felt weight**: The guilt of owing, the anxiety of the call, the relief of clearing -- those are the guilt, stress, and emotion engines'. Debt names the obligation and its arc; what it feels like to carry is delegated.
- **The pure gift**: A transfer that asks nothing back is the gift engine's. Debt begins only where the return is owed; a gift that quietly obligates is the gift becoming a debt, and the handover belongs to that engine until it does.
- **The instrument**: The contract, the bond, the IOU, the collateral pledged -- those are pieces or documents in the scene. The engine ships the _obligation_; the paper it is written on is supplied by the play.
- **The market**: Interest rates, credit systems, and how a whole economy runs on debt are the scene's and the culture's. The engine models one owing between parties, not the system of owings around them.

## Encoding

Source to constraint. A piece anchor (the obligation) with two positions (its poles) and three processes (its arc).

- **[debt](piece_debt.md)** (the anchor): The quantified obligation itself -- load-bearing because it holds up an unequal tie dressed as a temporary one. Anchored by Graeber (debt as enforceable, moralised obligation) and Mauss (the transfer that must return).
- **[creditor](position_creditor.md)** (position): The pole that holds the claim -- the power to wait, demand, or forgive. Anchored by Graeber (the creditor's leverage) and Nietzsche (the creditor in the origin of obligation).
- **[debtor](position_debtor.md)** (position): The pole that is bound -- the standing of one who owes. Anchored by Graeber (the debtor's subordination) and Nietzsche (debt as guilt).
- **[incurring](process_incurring.md)** (process): The obligation is created and the two are bound. Anchored by Mauss (the handover that obligates).
- **[servicing](process_servicing.md)** (process): The debt carried and kept alive, interest widening it. Anchored by Graeber (the compounding claim).
- **[default](process_default.md)** (process): The debt is not paid -- rupture, seizure, or conversion. Anchored by Graeber (what enforcement does when the debt cannot be met).
