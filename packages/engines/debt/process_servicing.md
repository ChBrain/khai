# Process: Servicing

## Taxonomy

[Debt](piece_debt.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: debt

## Initiated by

The debt stands and time begins to run against it. The threshold is
the first payment due -- the point at which the obligation stops being
a fact and becomes a schedule, a recurring claim on what the debtor
earns, does, or defers.

## Direction

Toward maintenance, and often toward growth. Servicing keeps the debt
alive and the tie intact: each payment holds off default without
ending the obligation, and where interest runs, the carrying can cost
more than the sum first borrowed. The debtor's future is spent keeping
the present debt from breaking.

## Lever

The debtor's means meeting the claim's demand over time. Servicing
holds as long as what can be paid meets what is owed; it strains when
the demand outruns the means, and interest can widen that gap faster
than payment closes it. Remove the demand and the debt is discharged;
remove the means and it tips toward default.

## Echo

A debt serviced but not cleared is a tie kept permanently taut: the
creditor's claim renewed with each payment, the debtor's deference
renewed with it. Where the carrying compounds, the obligation the next
process inherits is larger than the one this process received -- the
debt outpacing the repayment.
