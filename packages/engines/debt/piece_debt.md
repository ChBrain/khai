# Piece: Debt

## Taxonomy

Obligation / the morality of debt (no khai file of its own yet).

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: debt

## Place

Between two persons, pulled tight. A debt does not sit in a ledger so
much as in the tie between the one who owes and the one who is owed;
the ledger only records it. It travels with both -- present in what
the debtor cannot quite refuse and what the creditor may at any time
call in -- and it stays until it is settled or broken.

## Load Bearing

Remove it and the relation loses the asymmetry it was standing on:
the deference the debt bought, the patience the creditor extended,
the claim that let one person ask of another what they otherwise
could not. A debt holds up an unequal tie and dresses it as a
temporary one. What it bears is invisible until it is called, and
then the whole arrangement moves at once.

## Apparent

On the surface, a number and a promise -- clean, mutual, agreed. What
the sum hides is the power it transfers: for as long as it stands,
the creditor holds a claim on the debtor's future, and the debtor
owes not just the amount but the posture of one who owes. The figure
looks neutral; the relation it sets is not.

## Yearbook

Every debt carries the history of the transfer that made it and the
morality wrapped around it. It began as something handed over that
must come back; it accrued weight with time and interest; it drew on
the oldest of obligations -- that one must repay -- to make refusal
feel like a wrong and not merely a default. The longer it runs, the
more the debtor's conduct bends to service it, and the more its
clearing, or its breaking, costs them both.
