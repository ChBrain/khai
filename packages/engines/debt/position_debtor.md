# Position: Debtor

## Taxonomy

[Debt](piece_debt.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: debt

## Has

An obligation that arrived with what they received, and the standing
of one who owes. The debtor holds the use of what was lent and the
weight of having to return it; the room reads them as bound -- to a
schedule, to a creditor, to the posture repayment asks.

## Orders

Repay.
The position does not ask whether the debtor can easily meet it; owing
makes not-repaying a wrong and not merely a shortfall, so the
obligation runs underneath their choices, bending what they can spend,
refuse, or walk away from while the debt stands.

## Loses

The free hand. The debtor cannot fully refuse the creditor, cannot
meet them as an unencumbered equal, and cannot treat their own future
as entirely their own -- part of it is already claimed. Owing spends
the option of standing on level ground.

## Drives

On that loss:
the debtor services the obligation -- paying, deferring, appeasing, or
evading -- and is drawn toward release from it. They are shaped by the
carrying: deferent where they might have been plain, calculating the
cost of every refusal against the claim they have not yet cleared.
