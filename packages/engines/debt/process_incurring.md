# Process: Incurring

## Taxonomy

[Debt](piece_debt.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: debt

## Initiated by

Something is handed across that must come back -- money, a favour, a
life saved, a gift too large to simply accept. The threshold is the
transfer that carries a return with it: the moment the receiving binds
the receiver. A pure gift asks nothing back; a debt is the transfer
that does.

## Direction

Toward binding. Incurring converts a one-time transfer into a standing
tie: it sets a [creditor](position_creditor.md) and a
[debtor](position_debtor.md) where a moment ago there were two
persons, and hangs an obligation between them that did not exist
before the handover.

## Lever

The value of what was received meeting the weight of what is now owed.
The larger or the more needed the transfer, the tighter the bind and
the harder the refusal. Remove the return and it was a gift; remove
the transfer and there is nothing to owe. The obligation is the
distance between having taken and having repaid.

## Echo

The two are now unequal in a way they were not, and the world begins
to arrange around the claim -- schedules, expectations, deference. The
next process inherits a live obligation: one that must be serviced, or
broken. What was freely done is now owed.
