# Instructions: Debt

How a Playwright wires the debt engine into a play. You wire by linking the
play's own content to the engine's files; the engine files stay untouched, they
are canon. The Roadie plumbs the engine's law into Instructions Knowledge on
deploy. Authoring guidance, not runtime content, and it does not go on tour.

## Human

- The human sets what is owed, who holds the claim, and who is bound by it.

## Agent

- From the play's own content, set the debt: link the creditor stance under the owed persona's Projection and the debtor stance under the owing persona's, and link the process the debt is in -- incurring where it is created, servicing where it is carried, default where it breaks. Keep the sum and the instrument in the play's content, not in the engine. Let the asymmetry run underneath the plots: the debtor's deference, the creditor's claim, until the debt is cleared or breaks. You link to the engine, you never edit it.

## Collaboration

- The guilt of owing is guilt's; the dread of the call and the relief of clearing are stress' and emotion's; a pure gift that asks nothing back is gift's; the standing the debt buys or costs is hierarchy's; the contract it is written on is a piece or document in the scene. Whether the debt truly binds the personas the way the plot needs is the team's review.

## Knowledge

- A debt is a quantified obligation one persona owes another: a transfer that must return, binding creditor and debtor unequally until it is settled. It is moralised -- "one must pay one's debts" -- so refusal reads as a wrong, not merely a shortfall. Read it as a claim on the debtor's future and a leverage in the creditor's hand, carried through servicing and resolved by discharge or by default. The creditor can wait, demand, or forgive; the debtor cannot fully refuse.

## System

- Do link both poles under Projection and name the process the debt is in; do let the unequal tie run under the plots; do let default rupture or convert the relation rather than quietly ending it.
- Do not author the felt guilt; that is guilt's. Do not model a gift that asks nothing back here; that is gift's. Do not edit the engine's files; wire only from the play's side.
