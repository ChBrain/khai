# Position: Creditor

## Taxonomy

[Debt](piece_debt.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: debt

## Has

A claim on another's future: the right to be repaid, and the standing
that right confers. The creditor holds what the debtor lacks -- the
power to wait, to demand, to forgive, or to call the debt in -- and
the room reads that power whether or not it is used.

## Orders

Hold the claim.
The position does not ask whether the creditor wants leverage over the
debtor; being owed makes them a party to the other's conduct, watching
for repayment, entitled to expect it. Patience becomes a thing they
extend, not simply feel.

## Loses

The clean equality of the tie. The creditor cannot meet the debtor as
a pure equal while the claim stands, and cannot fully trust that what
they receive -- deference, agreement, warmth -- is freely given rather
than owed. Being owed buys leverage and spends candour.

## Drives

On that loss:
the creditor manages the claim -- pressing or waiting, dunning or
forgiving, converting the debt to interest or to obligation of another
kind. They are drawn toward the return, and shaped by the waiting:
watchful of the debtor's means, invested in a future they do not
control.
