# Process: Default

## Taxonomy

[Debt](piece_debt.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: debt

## Initiated by

The debtor cannot, or will not, pay what is due. The threshold is the
missed obligation that is not made good -- the point at which servicing
fails and the debt can no longer be carried quietly. What was a
schedule becomes a breach.

## Direction

Toward rupture or conversion. Default forces the debt out of its
holding pattern: the creditor calls it in, seizes what was pledged,
writes it off, or converts it into something else -- a new obligation,
a broken tie, a claim on the debtor's standing rather than their
purse. The taut relation snaps or is re-tied on harsher terms.

## Lever

The creditor's claim meeting the debtor's inability to honour it. The
force is the claim now overdue; the resistance is the debtor's empty
hands. What was a moral certainty -- that debts are paid -- meets the
brute fact that this one cannot be, and the relation resolves the
contradiction violently or by writing the sum away.

## Echo

Default does not return the two to where they began. The debtor carries
the mark of the one who did not pay; the creditor carries the loss and
the memory of extending it. Whether by seizure, forgiveness, or feud,
the world rearranges around a debt that will not be settled on its
original terms -- and the moral weight of the owing outlives the sum.
