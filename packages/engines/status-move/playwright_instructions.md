# Instructions: Status Move

How a Playwright wires the status-move engine into a play. You wire by linking the
play's own content to the engine's files; the engine files stay untouched, they
are canon. The Roadie plumbs the engine's law into Instructions Knowledge on
deploy. Authoring guidance, not runtime content, and it does not go on tour.

## Human

- The human names the other the persona is transacting with, the current relative status between them, the specific move being made (ascending, descending, lateral, or deflecting), its form (what the persona is doing or saying), and what the move signals. The human also specifies whether descending is strategic or habitual.

## Agent

- From the play's own content, a persona links the status move they are making to their Projection. The expression is the move's direction, not a general status posture. Status moves are moment-specific and addressed; do not link a status-move expression for a persona's general orientation across a scene.

## Collaboration

- The status engine holds the persona's ongoing rank and how it changes over time. The emotion and mood engines hold what the persona feels while making or receiving a status move. The power engine holds the institutional authority that may back a status move. The role engine holds the expectations that constrain which moves are available to a persona in a given context.

## Knowledge

- A status move is a discrete tactical act in the moment-by-moment negotiation of relative position within an interaction. It is addressed to a specific other and made through specific behaviors: posture, gaze, language register, qualification, assertion, hesitation, or redirection. The engine models four expressions: ascending (the persona presses upward through specific high-status behavior; the move is completed by the other's deference), descending (the persona yields space or self-diminishes; this may be strategic, to put the other at ease or to set up a future move, or habitual, compelled by anxiety), lateral (the persona signals peer equivalence through matched register and reciprocal acknowledgment; completed by the other's acceptance of parity), and deflecting (the persona redirects incoming status pressure through reframe, attribution, or subject change; completed when the redirection holds).

## System

- Status moves are moment-specific and addressed. Do not use this engine to model a persona's general status posture across a scene; use the status engine for that. A status move is a single transactional act directed at a specific other.
- A descending move is not the same as being low-status. Strategic descending is a high-status move available to a persona who can afford to yield. The descent is the means; what it is aimed at is a specific outcome in this interaction.
- A deflecting move is not avoidance. Deflection is a specific transactional act -- a reframe, an attribution, a change of subject -- that keeps the interaction moving without the persona absorbing the status cost of the incoming move. Avoidance is a failure to engage; deflection is engagement by redirection.
- Do not edit the engine's files; wire only from the play's side.
