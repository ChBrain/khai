# Position: Status Move

## Taxonomy

Status move is a position engine (TO HOLD). It models the discrete tactical move a persona makes in the moment-by-moment negotiation of status within an interaction. A status move is not the persona's rank; it is what the persona is doing right now to raise, lower, maintain, or deflect status in relation to a specific other.

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: status-move

## Has

The persona holds a tactical orientation: a specific move addressed to a specific other, made through specific means. What Johnstone identifies in improvisational play is that status is not fixed but transacted continuously through small behaviors -- posture, eye contact, hesitation, self-interruption, qualification, command. The persona in a status move holds the move's direction (where they are pushing the relative status) and its means (how they are making the push).

## Orders

Make the move to the specific other. A status move is not a general stance; it is addressed. The persona who holds an ascending move is making it to this other, in this moment, through this behavior. The order is: keep the move specific, keep it addressed, and do not let the persona float in an undirected orientation.

## Loses

The other. A status move that is not addressed to a specific other has left the mode. The persona who is performing status for a general audience, or performing it for themselves, is not in a status move; they are in a status display. What the mode loses when the address dissolves is the transactional quality -- the move-and-response structure -- that makes it a status move rather than a posture.

## Drives

Toward the response. The status move is incomplete until the other responds: takes the higher status offered, resists the ascending move, absorbs the deflection, or ignores the lateral signal. The drive is not toward a fixed outcome but toward the resolution of the transactional moment -- the question of whether the move lands.
