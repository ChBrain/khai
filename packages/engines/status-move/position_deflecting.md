# Position: Deflecting

## Taxonomy

[Status Move](position_status_move.md), the expression in which the persona redirects an incoming status move without accepting or directly contesting it. The deflecting move neither absorbs the ascending or descending pressure from the other nor meets it head-on; it redirects the transactional moment.

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: status-move

## Has

The persona holds a sidestep: the move meets the other's status pressure by redirecting it -- changing the subject, reframing the question, attributing the relevant competence elsewhere, or shifting the interactional frame. Goffman's analysis of face-work supplies the mechanism: the deflection is a protective move that keeps the interaction moving without the persona having to absorb the status consequence the other's move would impose.

## Orders

Redirect specifically, not evasively. A deflection that reads as avoidance exposes the persona to the inference that they cannot handle the incoming move; a deflection that reads as a graceful redirection maintains the interaction's momentum. The order is: redirect the transactional moment through a move that is plausible in context -- an attribution, a reframe, a change of subject -- without the redirection becoming the focus.

## Loses

The clean redirection. A deflecting move that the other pursues (following the persona into the reframe, pressing despite the attribution, refusing the change of subject) has not been completed; the pressure has not been redirected but compounded. The deflecting expression loses its protective function when the other's persistence makes the deflection visible as deflection.

## Drives

Toward the continuation of the interaction without status cost. The deflecting move is driven by the desire to move past the transactional pressure without absorbing its consequence -- neither accepting a lower position nor expending the energy of an ascending counter-move. The drive resolves when the redirection holds and the interaction continues on the new footing.
