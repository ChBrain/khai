# Position: Lateral

## Taxonomy

[Status Move](position_status_move.md), the expression in which the persona makes a move that maintains relative status rather than raising or lowering it. The lateral move signals peer equivalence: neither pressing upward nor yielding, but transacting at the same level.

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: status-move

## Has

The persona holds an assertion of equivalence: the move says, in effect, that this interaction is between equals, or that the relative position achieved is the position that will hold. Lateral moves appear as collegial acknowledgment, shared reference, matched register, or the calibration of directness to exactly the level the other is using. What the persona has is the orientation to neither rise nor yield.

## Orders

Match without merging. The lateral move must maintain its distinctness; a persona who matches the other so completely that their own position disappears has not made a lateral move but a loss of ground. The order is: signal equivalence through the specific form available -- matching register, acknowledging parity, offering reciprocal disclosure -- while maintaining the persona's own presence in the interaction.

## Loses

The other's willingness to transact as a peer. A lateral move that the other responds to with ascending behavior has not been accepted as lateral; the other is redefining the relative position. The lateral expression depends on the other's cooperation; without it, the persona must decide whether to accept the other's redefinition, contest it, or descend.

## Drives

Toward mutual recognition of parity. The lateral move is driven by the desire to establish and hold a shared level: neither hierarchical deference nor hierarchical assertion, but the transactional comfort of two equivalents moving together. The drive resolves when the other moves laterally in return -- matching the register, accepting the parity, or confirming the peer frame.
