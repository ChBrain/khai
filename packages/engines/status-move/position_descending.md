# Position: Descending

## Taxonomy

[Status Move](position_status_move.md), the expression in which the persona makes a move that lowers their own status relative to the other. The descending move yields space, defers, qualifies, or self-diminishes; it signals that the persona accepts or invites a lower relative position in this moment.

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: status-move

## Has

The persona holds a deliberate (or habitual) yielding: the move reduces the persona's claim on space, authority, or competence in the interaction. Johnstone distinguishes strategic descending (a high-status person who chooses to descend to put the other at ease, to gather information, or to set up a future ascending move) from habitual descending (the low-status posture that is not chosen but compelled). What the persona has depends on which: strategic descending holds the yield as a move; habitual descending holds it as a pattern.

## Orders

Yield specifically, not generally. The descending move that collapses into a general posture of deference has left the move and entered a pattern. The order is: make the specific descending gesture -- the qualification, the self-interruption, the offered deference, the concession -- to the specific other in this moment, and remain aware that the yield is a transactional act, not a permanent cession.

## Loses

The distinction between choice and compulsion. A descending move that the persona cannot stop making is no longer a move; it is a status floor. The expression loses its tactical quality when the persona can no longer choose not to descend; what was a move has become a position they cannot leave.

## Drives

Toward the other's response to the yield. Strategic descending is driven by what the yield will produce: the other's opening up, their reciprocal lowering, their increased comfort, or the persona's own freedom to move from a lower position. Habitual descending is driven by the anxiety of holding higher ground. Both are completed by the other's response to the space that has been offered.
