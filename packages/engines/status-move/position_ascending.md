# Position: Ascending

## Taxonomy

[Status Move](position_status_move.md), the expression in which the persona makes a move that raises their status relative to the other. The ascending move claims space, authority, or competence in the interaction; it signals that the persona holds higher status than the current arrangement acknowledges.

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: status-move

## Has

The persona holds a claim on higher ground: the move asserts -- through behavior, language, posture, or knowledge -- that the persona's position in this interaction is above the other's or above what has been acknowledged so far. Johnstone identifies the specific signals: the steady gaze, the unqualified assertion, the territorial expansion, the absence of apology. What the persona has is the directed press upward.

## Orders

Assert without over-reaching. The ascending move must be calibrated to what the interaction can sustain; a move that claims too much status too quickly provokes resistance or ridicule rather than deference. The order is: press upward through the specific behavior available in this context, and read the other's response as the test of whether the claim has been accepted.

## Loses

The other's willingness to give ground. An ascending move that the other does not accept has not ascended; it has been contested or refused. What the ascending expression loses when the other holds their position is the move's completion: the claim has been made but not confirmed. The persona must then decide whether to press, retreat, or deflect.

## Drives

Toward the other's deference. The ascending move is driven by the desire to establish a higher relative position in this interaction -- not in general but in this specific transactional moment. The drive resolves when the other responds: yields space, accepts the assertion, or defers to the persona's framing.
