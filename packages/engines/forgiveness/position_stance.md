# Position: Stance

## Taxonomy

[Forgiveness](process_forgiveness.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: forgiveness

## Has

The motivational posture the wronged party holds toward the offender before
any form of release is chosen -- the channel every reading of
[forgiveness](process_forgiveness.md) moves through, whether the persona
ever names it. McCullough's model treats forgiveness itself as a change in
this posture: not a verdict, not a single act, but a shift in what the
persona is motivated to do to and around the one who wronged them. The
posture arrives at one of three settings: [avoidant](position_stance_avoidant.md)
(distance as the whole strategy), [vengeful](position_stance_vengeful.md)
(the motivation to see the offender pay), or
[benevolent](position_stance_benevolent.md) (goodwill held despite the
wrong). The first two are the transgression-related motivations the
original TRIM measured; the third was added later, as a distinct prosocial
motivation rather than merely their absence.

## Orders

Set the posture before the form.
The position runs underneath [decisional](process_decisional.md),
[emotional](process_emotional.md), [withheld](process_withheld.md), and
[self](process_self.md) alike: whichever form the release takes, or fails
to take, it is legible as a movement of this stance -- avoidance and
revenge receding, benevolence returning, or neither. A form without a
stance to move is not a reading of forgiveness at all.

## Loses

The presumption that release and its absence are opposites with nothing
between them. The stance denies that binary: a persona can lower revenge
without raising benevolence, or hold benevolence toward an offender they
still route around. What is lost is the single dial the folk model
imagines; what replaces it is three settings that move on their own
schedules.

## Drives

On that plurality: the persona's read is cast to whichever setting the
scene earns, never assumed from the others. Where avoidance and vengefulness
recede and benevolence has not yet arrived, the persona holds a stance still
in motion; where benevolence is reached, the read is carried forward into
whichever form -- [decisional](process_decisional.md) or
[emotional](process_emotional.md) -- gives it conduct or feeling. Where none
of the three moves, the stance holds steady and the persona's read stays
[withheld](process_withheld.md).
