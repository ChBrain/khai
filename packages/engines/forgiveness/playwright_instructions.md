# Instructions: Forgiveness

How a Playwright wires the forgiveness engine into a play. You wire by linking the
play's own content to the engine's files; the engine files stay untouched, they
are canon. The Roadie plumbs the engine's law into Instructions Knowledge on
deploy. Authoring guidance, not runtime content, and it does not go on tour.

## Human

- The human sets the wrong that was done and to whom, and whether the offender is present, repentant, or gone.

## Agent

- From the play's own content, a persona who holds a grievance links its current reading under Projection (decisional, emotional, withheld, or self). Cast one rung per channel and no filler: which form the release takes, which stance it moves through (avoidant, vengeful, or benevolent), and where the persona's forgivingness dial sits. Let the reading govern how they meet the one who wronged them -- collecting the debt or setting it down -- and let it move only as the play earns it. A vengeful-stance persona and an avoidant-stance persona holding the same withheld form play opposite scenes: one is rehearsing what the offender owes, the other is arranging never to meet them, and both are, by the letter of the engine, withholding. You link to the engine, you never edit it.

## Collaboration

- The reckoning of the violation before release is live is the betrayal engine's; the rebuilding of the relationship after release is the repair engine's (forgiveness asks nothing of the offender; reconciliation requires them, and a benevolent stance is not itself reconciliation). The felt charge that is held or released is emotion and mood's; whether the act was a wrong, and how grave, is shame and virtue's. Whether the release rings true is the team's review.

## Knowledge

- Forgiveness is the wronged party's unilateral release of a debt they are owed: the lowering of avoidance and revenge motivation toward the offender, decided or felt, and not waiting on the offender's apology or deserving. It is intrapersonal and one-sided -- it changes the persona's stance toward the wrong, not the fact of the wrong -- and it is distinct from reconciliation, which rebuilds the relationship and requires both parties. Underneath every reading runs the same stance channel, in three settings -- avoidant (distance as the whole strategy), vengeful (the motivation to see the offender pay), and benevolent (goodwill held despite the wrong, never condoning and never reconciliation) -- and how readily the whole engine moves at all, across offenses and offenders, is the persona's own forgivingness.

## System

- Do give a persona a forgiveness reading only against a wrong they actually carry; do let the reading govern the meeting and turn only as the play earns it; do cast one rung per channel (form, stance, dial).
- Do not play release as reconciliation; forgiveness is unilateral and the relationship may stay broken, benevolent stance included. Do not stage the discovery of the wrong here; that is betrayal's. Do not edit the engine's files; wire only from the play's side.
