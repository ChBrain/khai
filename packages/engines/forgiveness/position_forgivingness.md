# Position: Forgivingness

## Taxonomy

[Forgiveness](process_forgiveness.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: forgiveness

## Has

The persona's standing disposition toward the whole engine: how readily
this persona's forgiveness moves at all, across offenses and offenders,
independent of any one wrong. Roberts named the concept -- forgivingness as
a virtue-like trait of character, a settled readiness to release rather
than a one-off achievement -- and Berry, Worthington, O'Connor, Parrott &
Wade supplied the dispositional measure, tying it to low vengeful
rumination and distinguishing it from any single act of forgiving. The
forgiveness counterpart of guilt's proneness dial (see
position_proneness.md in the guilt engine): where proneness sets how
readily guilt fires, forgivingness sets how readily its release does.

## Orders

Set the readiness.
The position calibrates how far the [stance](position_stance.md) moves for
a given wrong: a high-forgiving persona lets avoidance and revenge fall
and benevolence return quickly, across many offenders and many kinds of
harm; a low-forgiving persona holds the stance fixed long after the room
would excuse letting it move, and needs a large, sustained reason before
any form of release begins. Neither is the neutral setting -- the position
denies the persona an unset dial here as much as proneness denies one on
the other side of the account.

## Loses

High forgivingness loses discrimination: the persona releases readily
enough that some grievances go before they have been fully reckoned with,
and can read, to others, as someone who lets things go too easily to be
trusted with what actually happened. Low forgivingness loses ease across
the whole run of a life: minor wrongs accumulate as held stances, ordinary
offenders are met at the same footing as grave ones, and vengeful
rumination has more material to work with the longer any one grievance
stays open.

## Drives

On those losses: the high-forgiving persona is driven toward a wide,
low-friction practice of release -- generous, occasionally too fast, an
easy mark for an offender who has learned how little the dial resists;
the low-forgiving persona is driven toward a narrow, effortful one -- each
release earned late and specifically, the vengeful rumination that fills
the gap doing its own further damage in the meantime. Cast the dial once
per persona and let every reading this engine runs -- which stance moves,
how far, and how fast -- read against it.
