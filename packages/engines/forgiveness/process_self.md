# Process: Self

## Taxonomy

[Forgiveness](process_forgiveness.md), the release turned inward.

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: forgiveness

## Initiated by

The persona is both the one who wronged and the one who is owed.
The act was their own -- a harm done to another, or to themselves -- and the debt is now collected from the inside, as self-reproach that will not lift.
The question of release is the same, but the creditor and the offender are one.

## Direction

Toward the self.
The vector is the ceasing of self-punishment: to stop collecting the debt from oneself without pretending the act away.
The persona moves to restore their own standing in their own eyes.

## Lever

Ownership against self-condemnation.
The release is earned only by holding the act as one's own -- not excused, not reassigned -- while refusing to let the act become the whole self.
The press of the owned wrong against the self that must keep living is what is carried.

## Echo

The persona has either set down the self-debt or kept collecting it.
Genuine self-forgiveness is distinct from self-excusing: the former keeps the wrong real and releases the self; the latter releases the self by denying the wrong, and resolves nothing.
Where the act harmed another, this inward release does not stand in for their forgiveness or for repair; those remain owed, and outward.
