# Instructions: Locus of Control

How a Playwright wires the locus-of-control engine into a play. You wire by
linking the play's own content to the engine's files; the engine files stay
untouched, they are canon. The Roadie plumbs the engine's law into
Instructions Knowledge on deploy. Authoring guidance, not runtime content, and
it does not go on tour.

## Human

- The human sets the standing expectancy a persona reads every outcome
  through: locus of control is what the persona generally expects about where
  control lies, not its read of any one event.

## Agent

- From the play's own content, every persona carries its locus-of-control
  expectancy under Projection, one expression (the engine ships internal and
  external; more slot in as siblings). Where a persona conceals a different
  expectancy, put that under Shadow. You link to the engine, you never edit
  it.

## Collaboration

- The read of any one outcome is the decision engine's; the belief in the
  capability for a specific task is self-efficacy's; the felt collapse after
  repeated external attribution is the emotion/stress engines'; goal-pursuit
  agency is hope's. Whether the expectancy rings true across a persona's
  choices is the team's review.

## Knowledge

- Locus of control is a position: a generalized expectancy about where
  control over reinforcement lies, carried into every outcome before any one
  is read, not a judgment the persona forms fresh each time. The law is
  system-wide, every persona carries an expectancy and none can drop it. The
  carried expectancy sits under Projection; an expectancy concealed, different
  from the one shown, sits under Shadow, and the gap between the two is where
  the engine does its work.

## System

- Do give every persona an expectancy (the law admits no exception); do read
  it in the first beat and hold it under every process that follows; do use
  Shadow only when a persona hides an expectancy different from the one it
  carries.
- Do not treat it as a one-off attribution about a single outcome; that is the
  decision engine's. Do not author the specific task-capability belief here;
  that is self-efficacy's. Do not edit the engine's files; wire only from the
  play's side.
