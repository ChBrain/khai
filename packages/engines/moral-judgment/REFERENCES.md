# Moral Judgment: Reference

## Line of Work

Moral judgment as **position**: the moral lens a persona reads another's conduct
through. The domain does not model the persona's own character firing under
pressure, nor the felt charge of disgust or anger that rides the verdict; it
models the standing set of moral foundations, one of which lights first when the
persona weighs right and wrong in someone else, laying down the verdict before
the reasons are gathered for it.

## Restrictions

What the engine refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **The persona's own conduct**: This engine judges others, not the self. When
  the persona's own character fires under pressure -- the virtue or vice it acts
  from when tested -- that **delegates to the virtue engine**. Moral judgment is
  the lens turned outward; virtue is the standard turned in.
- **The felt charge** (the heat of the verdict): The verdict lands with a charge
  -- the disgust, the anger -- but the engine models which foundation fired, not
  the feeling that rode it. The acute felt charge **delegates to the emotion
  engine**. Moral judgment names the wrong; emotion is the heat of naming it.
- **The social standard of exposure**: When the question is how a breach reads to
  the watching group -- the standing lost, the face -- that **delegates to the
  shame engine**. Moral judgment weighs the act; shame weighs how its exposure
  lands in the eyes of others.
- **The cultural content of the sacred**: The engine models that a sanctity
  foundation fires, never what a given culture holds sacred or defiled. The
  specific content of purity, taboo, and the holy **delegates to the cultures
  layer**. Moral judgment is the foundation; the culture fills it.
- **The reasoned ethical argument**: The engine models the fast verdict, not the
  slow case built after it. Deliberate moral reasoning, the worked argument for a
  position, is **out of scope**; the lens fires first, and the reasons it gathers
  belong to the scene, not to a foundation file.

## Encoding

Source to constraint, per file.

- **[moral_judgment](position_moral_judgment.md)** (the anchor): moral judgment
  as a lens held continuously, a standing set of foundations one of which fires
  first to land a verdict before the reasons, rather than run as a process.
  Anchored by Haidt (the social-intuitionist verdict-first model) and Haidt &
  Joseph (the modular foundations).
- **[care](position_care.md)** (expression): the care/harm foundation, the eye
  for who is hurt and who shields them, the wince at suffering. Anchored by
  Haidt's care foundation and the MFQ's harm subscale.
- **[fairness](position_fairness.md)** (expression): the fairness/cheating
  foundation, the eye for the cheat and the unpaid debt, the flare at a broken
  bargain. Anchored by Haidt's fairness foundation and Graham, Haidt & Nosek.
- **[loyalty](position_loyalty.md)** (expression): the loyalty/betrayal
  foundation, the eye for who stood by the group and who sold it out, the recoil
  at the traitor. Anchored by Haidt's ingroup foundation and Shweder's community
  ethic.
- **[authority](position_authority.md)** (expression): the authority/subversion
  foundation, the eye for rank and the duty owed it, the affront at the upstart.
  Anchored by Haidt's authority foundation and Shweder's community ethic.
- **[sanctity](position_sanctity.md)** (expression): the sanctity/degradation
  foundation, the eye for the pure and the defiled, the disgust at the unclean
  act. Anchored by Haidt's purity foundation and Shweder's divinity ethic.
- **[liberty](position_liberty.md)** (expression): the liberty/oppression
  foundation, the eye for the boot on the neck, the resistance to domination.
  Anchored by Iyer et al. (the libertarian sixth foundation).
