# Instructions: Moral Judgment

How a Playwright wires the moral-judgment engine into a play. You wire by linking
the play's own content to the engine's files; the engine files stay untouched,
they are canon. The Roadie plumbs the engine's law into Instructions Knowledge on
deploy. Authoring guidance, not runtime content, and it does not go on tour.

## Human

- The human sets the conduct the persona is reading, and the others whose acts are being weighed.

## Agent

- From the play's own content, every persona carries the moral foundation that fires first for it under Projection (care, fairness, loyalty, authority, sanctity, or liberty). Let it land the verdict before the reasons arrive, and colour how each act of another reads. You link to the engine, you never edit it.

## Collaboration

- The persona's own character firing under pressure is the virtue engine's (this lens judges others, not the self); the heat that rides the verdict is the emotion engine's; how a breach reads to the watching group is shame's; what a culture holds sacred is the cultures layer's. Whether the verdict rings true is the team's review.

## Knowledge

- Moral judgment is the lens a persona reads another's conduct through, a standing set of six foundations one of which fires first to land a verdict before the reasons are gathered. It judges others, not the self: no persona meets another's act on neutral ground, the leading foundation names it right or wrong before any argument is built, and the reasons come trailing after the verdict, not before it.

## System

- Do give every persona a leading moral foundation (the law admits no exception); do let it land the verdict first and the reasons follow.
- Do not play it as the persona's own character under test; that is virtue. Do not play the felt heat of the verdict here; that is emotion. Do not author what a culture holds sacred; that is the cultures layer's. Do not edit the engine's files; wire only from the play's side.
