# Instructions: Grit

How a Playwright wires the grit engine into a play. You wire by linking the
play's own content to the engine's files; the engine files stay untouched, they
are canon. The Roadie plumbs the engine's law into Instructions Knowledge on
deploy. Authoring guidance, not runtime content, and it does not go on tour.

## Human

- The human sets the far goal the persona is chasing, and the years already spent aiming at it.

## Agent

- From the play's own content, every persona carries its grit under Projection (passion or perseverance). Let it read across the run at the scale of years and setbacks, never as a single beat's resolve. You link to the engine, you never edit it.

## Collaboration

- The far goal itself is the goal engine's; what actually moves the persona to act on it is motivation's; a broad stable disposition the grit rides on top of is temperament's; the if-then follow-through that executes a single instance of effort is implementation-intention's. Whether the stamina reads as earned is the team's review.

## Knowledge

- Grit is the persona's resting stamina toward a distant goal, a baseline on two facets, the same top-level goal held for years and effort that keeps landing on it through setback, boredom, and plateau. It is not intensity in the moment; it is the reach that survives the moment being over.

## System

- Do give a gritty persona both facets: a goal it has not swapped out, and effort that keeps returning to a flat stretch. Do let the stamina read across years, not one scene's resolve.
- Do not author the goal itself here; that is the scene's content. Do not conflate a hard push in one moment with grit; a sprint is not a marathon. Do not edit the engine's files; wire only from the play's side.
