# Instructions: Gift

How a Playwright wires the gift engine into a play. You wire by linking the
play's own content to the engine's files; the engine files stay untouched, they
are canon. The Roadie plumbs the engine's law into Instructions Knowledge on
deploy. Authoring guidance, not runtime content, and it does not go on tour.

## Human

- The human defines the gift: what is being given, who gives it, who receives it, and what history of exchange exists between the parties. The human also sets what phase the exchange is in at entry and what the cost or stakes of the gift are for each party.

## Agent

- From the play's own content, a persona links the gift phase the moment runs (giving, receiving, or reciprocating) under its Projection. Track whether the exchange is sustaining or straining the relationship. You link to the engine, you never edit it.

## Collaboration

- The status engine holds how gift-giving shifts the deference order -- who has given more, who is indebted. The capital engine holds the social credit that gift exchange accumulates and spends. The emotion and mood engines hold the discomfort, warmth, or resentment that each phase carries. The negotiation engine holds what happens when the terms of exchange are made explicit; at that point the gift has become a transaction.

## Knowledge

- Gift is how a gift creates obligation, binds the parties, and requires a return. It moves through giving (the giver parts with something of value; the transfer must appear free or it is not a gift; the obligation the giving creates cannot be named), receiving (acceptance is not passive; the receiver takes on the obligation to reciprocate; refusal refuses the relationship), and reciprocating (the counter-gift completes the cycle and renews it; the interval between receiving and returning is what makes exchange a relationship rather than a transaction; too quick a return settles the account, too slow a return makes the debt permanent). The gift must appear free while creating real obligation; both parties maintain the appearance of disinterest because naming the obligation dissolves the relationship the gift was offered to sustain.

## System

- Do not model the gift as a free or neutral exchange. Every gift creates an obligation; the appearance of freedom is structural, not descriptive.
- Do not name the obligation in the gift exchange itself. A giver who names the expected return has made a transaction; a receiver who names the debt they are accepting has refused the relationship.
- Do not model market exchange as gift exchange. Commodity exchange at agreed equivalence is not a gift; this engine models gift exchange only.
- Do not model what either party feels; that belongs to the emotion and mood engines.
- Do not edit the engine's files; wire only from the play's side.
