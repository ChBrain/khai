# Process: Giving

## Taxonomy

[Gift](process_gift.md), the process this phase opens.

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: gift

## Initiated by

The decision to transfer something to another without naming the return. The giver parts with something of value -- a material object, time, effort, access, honor -- and transfers it to the receiver. The transfer must appear voluntary. The giver who names the expected return converts the gift into a transaction.

## Direction

Toward the receiver's acceptance. The giver cannot complete the act alone; a gift that is not received is not yet a gift. The giver offers with the expectation that the receiver will take and that taking will create the obligation the giver cannot name.

## Lever

Misrecognition: the structural necessity that the gift appear free. Both parties know the gift is not free -- it creates obligation -- but both parties must behave as though it is, or the relationship the gift creates cannot function. The giver must not appear to be making a claim; the receiver must not appear to be incurring a debt. The gift's social power depends on the appearance of disinterest.

## Echo

The transfer is complete. The gift is now with the receiver. The obligation to receive and to reciprocate is now on the receiver's side. The giver has made a claim that cannot be named.
