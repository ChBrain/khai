# Process: Gift

## Taxonomy

A process. How a gift creates obligation, binds the parties, and requires a return. Root of the gift process tree.

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: gift

## Initiated by

A transfer of something between two parties that is not a commodity exchange: not payment for services, not barter at an agreed equivalence. The gift appears free; the appearance of freedom is structural, not accidental. The gift must look voluntary or it is not a gift -- and the obligation it creates must not be named or the relationship it builds cannot function.

## Direction

Toward a counter-gift: a return that completes the cycle and, in completing it, begins it again. The gift cycle does not end; a gift acknowledged and returned becomes the basis of the next gift. The parties are bound by the exchange into an ongoing relationship.

## Lever

The triple obligation: to give, to receive, and to reciprocate. Each is binding. To refuse to give when one should give is to withhold from the relationship. To refuse to receive is to refuse the relationship itself. To fail to reciprocate is to acknowledge a debt without repaying it, which is a permanent claim on the receiver. The cycle is maintained by all three obligations operating together.

## Echo

The parties are bound. The gift has created a relationship -- or renewed it, or deepened it, or changed its terms. The counter-gift, when it arrives, will begin the next iteration of the same process on new terms.
