# Process: Receiving

## Taxonomy

[Gift](process_gift.md), the process this phase advances.

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: gift

## Initiated by

The receiver's encounter with the gift. Receiving is not passive; it is the acceptance of the gift and, with it, the obligation to reciprocate. A receiver who takes without the intention to return has accepted a debt they cannot close. A receiver who refuses has declined the relationship the gift was offered to create or sustain.

## Direction

Toward acknowledgement: the gesture, the expression, the word that signals the gift has been received with appropriate weight. The acknowledgement must match the gift in register -- too little signals disregard; too much makes the obligation explicit in a way that embarrasses the giver's disinterest. Getting the acknowledgement right is not mere courtesy; it is the move that makes the gift cycle sustainable.

## Lever

The freedom to refuse and the cost of refusing. Receiving is in principle voluntary: one can decline a gift. But refusal refuses the relationship. In contexts where the relationship cannot be refused -- between hierarchical parties, between parties who depend on each other -- refusal is not available at ordinary cost. The obligation to receive is the lever that makes the giver's claim real even when no claim is named.

## Echo

The gift is received and acknowledged. The receiver now holds the obligation to reciprocate. The terms of the return -- what is adequate, when it must arrive, in what form -- are set by context and prior history, not by any explicit agreement.
