# Process: Reciprocating

## Taxonomy

[Gift](process_gift.md), the process this phase completes and renews.

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: gift

## Initiated by

The accumulation of obligation past the threshold of return. The receiver cannot reciprocate immediately -- to return a gift at once signals that you are settling a debt, not sustaining a relationship. But the return must arrive within a time and in a form that the context makes appropriate. Too long without a return and the debt becomes a permanent claim; too quick a return and it becomes a transaction.

## Direction

Toward the counter-gift: a return that is neither identical to the gift (which would signal equivalence and close the account) nor wildly disproportionate (which would reverse the obligation). The counter-gift must acknowledge the original gift without naming the obligation it discharges. In completing the cycle, it begins the next one.

## Lever

The interval. The time between receiving and reciprocating is what makes the exchange a relationship rather than a transaction. An immediate return settles the account; a delayed return sustains the bond. The interval is not arbitrary; it is governed by what each party knows about the other, about their resources, and about what the relationship means. Getting the interval wrong -- too short or too long -- changes what the exchange is.

## Echo

The counter-gift has been given. The obligation on the receiver's side is discharged; the obligation on the original giver's side is now renewed. The cycle has completed one turn and is ready to begin again. The relationship is sustained -- or, if the reciprocation was inadequate, damaged.
