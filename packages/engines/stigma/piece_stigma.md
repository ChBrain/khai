# Piece: Stigma

## Taxonomy

Spoiled identity / the sociology of deviance (no khai file of its own yet).

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: stigma

## Place

Not on the body alone -- in the gap between the identity a persona
claims and the identity the room is willing to grant. A stigma sits
on an attribute (a mark, a history, a trait) but it only exists as
stigma inside an interaction, read by someone against a norm they
hold. It travels with the bearer into every room; some rooms already
know it, others do not yet.

## Load Bearing

Remove it and the bearer is read as a whole and usual person again,
not a tainted, discounted one. A stigma is load-bearing precisely
because the room has already recalculated everyone's standing around
it -- the trust extended, the roles offered, the benefit of the doubt
given or withheld, all reset lower once the mark is read. What it
holds down is invisible until the mark is named.

## Apparent

Split by kind. A discredited mark is apparent outright: visible on
sight, and the interaction is shaped by it before a word is spoken.
A discreditable mark is apparent only as an absence -- nothing shows
until it is disclosed or discovered, and the bearer's ordinary
composure is itself the performance covering the gap. Either way,
what is seen is never only the attribute; it is the attribute already
read through a stereotype.

## Yearbook

Every stigma carries a moral career: the attribute existed before it
was read as spoiling, became a mark the day a room first discounted
the bearer for it, and accumulated weight with every interaction that
followed the same script. The bearer learns their own history in two
versions -- the one lived, and the one the mark rewrites it into. A
stigma's age is measured in the adjustments the bearer has already
made in anticipation of the next room's read.
