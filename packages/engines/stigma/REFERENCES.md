# Stigma: Reference

## Line of Work

Stigma as **piece**: a deeply discrediting attribute carried by a persona,
reducing them, in others' eyes, from a whole and usual person to a tainted,
discounted one. The domain does not model every difference or every unwanted
trait; it models the attribute a room reads through a stereotype and
discounts standing for -- the gap between the identity a persona claims
(actual social identity) and the identity a room is willing to grant once the
mark is read (virtual social identity).

The spine is Goffman's account of spoiled identity: stigma is not a property
of the attribute itself but of the relation between the attribute and a
stereotype the room already holds, so the same trait stigmatises in one
setting and passes unremarked in another. Goffman's discredited/discreditable
split -- whether the mark already shows or can still be managed out of view
-- gives the engine its two processes: the discounting that fires once a mark
is read, and the standing work (passing, covering) of keeping a discreditable
mark from being read at all. Link & Phelan supply the structural condition
under which an attribute becomes stigma in the first place: labeling and
stereotyping co-occurring with separation, status loss, and discrimination,
inside a situation where power lets the labeler's read stick.

## Origin

The sociology of deviance and social identity, applied directly as piece,
position, and process constraints.

| Source                               | Key Work                                                      | Scope                                                                                                                                                             |
| :----------------------------------- | :------------------------------------------------------------ | :---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Erving Goffman**                   | _Stigma: Notes on the Management of Spoiled Identity_ (1963)  | The attribute as spoiled identity; discredited vs. discreditable; virtual vs. actual social identity; passing, covering, and the moral career of the stigmatized. |
| **Bruce G. Link &amp; Jo C. Phelan** | _"Conceptualizing Stigma"_ (Annual Review of Sociology, 2001) | Stigma as the co-occurrence of labeling, stereotyping, separation, status loss, and discrimination, contingent on the exercise of power.                          |

## Restrictions

What the engine refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **The felt shame**: The engine models the discrediting attribute and its
  management, not the emotion the bearer feels while carrying or managing it.
  The dread of exposure, the sting of a discrediting encounter, and the
  self-condemnation that can follow are **delegated to the shame engine**. A
  persona may run stigma and shame at once -- the piece and the process
  compose, they do not replace each other.
- **The standing cost**: The engine names that standing is discounted, not
  the room's rank structure the discount is measured against. Where the
  bearer lands in a specific hierarchy once discredited is the hierarchy
  engine's.
- **The self-story**: The engine models the moral career of the mark, not
  the longer-arc identity the bearer builds around or against it (owning it,
  reclaiming it, building a life story that answers it). That territory is
  the identity engine's and the play's arc.
- **The stereotype's content**: The engine ships the mechanism -- a mark
  read through a stereotype discounts standing -- not which attributes a
  given culture or era treats as marks, nor the content of the stereotype
  applied. That conversion rate is the consuming culture's and the
  moral-judgment engine's.
- **The fabricated cover story**: Where passing extends to actively planting
  a false account rather than withholding a true one, the fabrication is the
  deception engine's; stigma's passing controls information about a real
  attribute, it does not invent one.

## Encoding

Source to constraint. A piece anchor (the discrediting attribute) with one
position (who carries it) and two processes (what happens once it is read,
and how it is kept from being read).

- **[stigma](piece_stigma.md)** (the anchor): The attribute itself --
  load-bearing because the room recalculates standing around it the moment
  it is read. Anchored by Goffman (spoiled identity; virtual vs. actual
  social identity).
- **[bearer](position_bearer.md)** (position): The stance of holding the
  split identity and the standing task of managing what shows. Anchored by
  Goffman (the stigmatized individual's management problem).
- **[discrediting](process_discrediting.md)** (process): The room reading
  the mark through a stereotype and discounting standing accordingly.
  Anchored by Goffman (the discredited) and Link &amp; Phelan (labeling,
  stereotyping, status loss under power).
- **[passing](process_passing.md)** (process): The standing work of keeping
  a discreditable mark from being read -- concealing, or covering what is
  already known. Anchored by Goffman (passing and covering as information
  control).
