# Instructions: Superstition

How a Playwright wires the superstition engine into a play. You wire by linking
the play's own content to the engine's files; the engine files stay untouched,
they are canon. The Roadie plumbs the engine's law into Instructions Knowledge on
deploy. Authoring guidance, not runtime content, and it does not go on tour.

## Human

- The human decides which personas are superstitious and which links they read; a persona may hold none, and most outside the scene's frame will.

## Agent

- From the play's own content, a superstitious persona links the magical link it reads (contagion, similarity, control, omen, or luck) under its Projection. You link to the engine, you never edit it; a persona that holds no superstition links nothing.

## Collaboration

- What the link does when evidence presses on it -- revise, defend, refuse -- is the belief engine's; where the link was installed, inherited or absorbed, is the heritage engine's; the dread or comfort that rides the omen or the charm is the emotion engine's; the uncertainty that makes it flare is the scene's. Whether the read rings true is the team's review.

## Knowledge

- Superstition is a read that a link holds between events that share no mechanism -- by contact, resemblance, control, sign, or luck. Its mark is that disconfirming evidence does not move it: a failed outcome reads as the wrong rite or the dormant charge, never as no link at all. The function is perceived control under uncertainty, not better outcomes; the rite does not change the world, it changes how in hand the world feels.

## System

- Do fire it under uncertainty and stakes; do let the persona explain away the miss in the link's own terms rather than drop the link.
- Do not state the link as a true cause; it is a read, not a mechanism. Do not claim it improves the outcome; it returns felt control, not results. Do not author the trigger here; that is the scene's. Do not edit the engine's files; wire only from the play's side.
