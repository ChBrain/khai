# Superstition: Reference

## Line of Work

Superstition as **position**: the magical-causal link a persona reads between events that share no mechanism. The domain does not model the content of any one belief; it models the _stance toward causation_ -- the read that an act, object, sign, or contact bears on an outcome it cannot reach by ordinary cause. It is the counterpart to ordinary causal cognition: where one tracks mechanism, the other tracks contact, resemblance, agency, and luck.

The spine is the cognitive psychology of magical thinking. A superstition is not held the way an empirical belief is held: its mark is that disconfirming evidence does not move it, because the link is non-falsifiable from inside -- a failed outcome reads as the wrong rite or the dormant charge, never as no link at all. The function is the management of uncertainty: the read returns a felt sense of control where there is none. **This is perceived control, not performance** -- the claim that enacting a superstition improves the outcome (the "knock on wood" effect) has not survived replication, and the engine makes no such claim.

## Origin

The cognitive and behavioural psychology of magical thinking, applied as position constraints. Each leaf encodes one well-attested mechanism.

## Restrictions

What the engine refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **The settlement under evidence**: Superstition models the magical link and how it holds in its own terms; what a held conviction does as evidence presses on it -- revise, defend, refuse -- is **delegated to the belief engine**. The two compose: the link is superstition's, the move under pressure is belief's.
- **How the link was installed**: Where a superstition came from -- inherited, absorbed in childhood, passed down -- is **delegated to the heritage engine** and the play's arc. The engine reads the link as the persona holds it now, not the history that laid it down.
- **The felt charge**: What the persona _feels_ when the omen lands or the charm is lost -- the dread, the relief, the comfort -- is **delegated to the emotion engine**. Superstition supplies the read; the feeling that rides it is emotion's.
- **What makes it flare**: The uncertainty and stakes that make the read fire harder are the scene's to supply; the engine names the stance, not the pressure that triggers it.
- **The content of any one superstition**: The specific charm, sign, or taboo is the scene's to supply, not the engine's -- the engine models the kind of link, never the proposition.
- **The clinical extreme**: The engine models normal-range magical cognition, not obsessive-compulsive ritual or delusion. The positions are ordinary variants, not diagnoses.

## Encoding

Source to constraint, per file.

- **[superstition](position_superstition.md)** (the anchor): The read that a link holds between things that share no mechanism, and that resists disconfirmation because it cannot be disproved from within. The root stance the five links specialise.
- **[contagion](position_contagion.md)** (expression): Contact leaves a trace that persists; conduct routes by what has touched what. Anchored by Rozin & Nemeroff (the law of contagion).
- **[similarity](position_similarity.md)** (expression): Resemblance is connection; the image is the thing, like makes like. Anchored by Rozin & Nemeroff (the law of similarity).
- **[control](position_control.md)** (expression): One's own act is read to sway a chance outcome; the rite tilts the odds. Anchored by Ellen Langer (the illusion of control), with adventitious conditioning (Skinner) as the route in.
- **[omen](position_omen.md)** (expression): A sign carries intent or forecast; coincidence is message. Anchored by Barrett (HADD) and Shermer (patternicity/agenticity).
- **[luck](position_luck.md)** (expression): Fortune is a substance that adheres and is spent; luck is courted and jinxed. Anchored by Vyse (luck as resource under uncertainty).
