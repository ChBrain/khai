# Instructions: Mood

How a Playwright wires the mood engine into a play. You wire by linking the
play's own content to the engine's files; the engine files stay untouched, they
are canon. The Roadie plumbs the engine's law into Instructions Knowledge on
deploy. Authoring guidance, not runtime content, and it does not go on tour.

## Human

- The human sets the stretch the persona is living through, and what has been colouring it.

## Agent

- From the play's own content, every persona carries its mood quadrant under Projection (buoyant, serene, irritable, or flat). Let it tint how each moment lands and drift slowly across the run, never snap beat to beat. You link to the engine, you never edit it.

## Collaboration

- The sharp feeling of a single moment is the emotion engine's (mood is the weather under that lightning); the inborn baseline a mood swings around is temperament's; what has shifted the weather is the scene's. Whether the tone rings true is the team's review.

## Knowledge

- Mood is the persisting affective background a persona carries across a stretch, a baseline on two axes, pleasant to unpleasant and activated to quiet, holding as one of four quadrants. It is the weather, not the lightning: no persona meets a moment on neutral, the mood colours how the moment lands before any feeling fires, and it moves on the timescale of a stretch, not a beat.

## System

- Do give every persona a mood (the law admits no exception); do let it colour the moment and drift slowly across the run.
- Do not play it as a momentary feeling; that is emotion. Do not author what shifted it here; that is the scene's. Do not edit the engine's files; wire only from the play's side.
