# Process: Desecration

## Taxonomy

[Totem](piece_totem.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: totem

## Initiated by

The prohibition around the totem is crossed -- it is handled by the
wrong hands, mocked, burned, dropped, or put to profane use. The
threshold is the violation of the line that kept it apart: the sacred
touched as though it were ordinary.

## Direction

Toward rupture and reaction. Desecration does not merely damage an
object; it strikes at the group the object stands for, and the group
answers -- with outrage, sanction, purification, or violence. The act
converts a thing into an injury and forces the collective to defend
the sacred or watch it fall.

## Lever

The force of the taboo meeting the act that breaks it. What makes
desecration powerful is exactly what made the totem sacred: the group's
investment in keeping it apart. The greater the reverence, the greater
the offence -- and the more the violator is treated not as clumsy but
as an enemy. Remove the investment and it is only a broken object.

## Echo

The line has been shown to be crossable, and the group must either
re-draw it harder -- punishing the violator, re-consecrating the object,
tightening the prohibition -- or absorb the fact that its sacred can be
profaned. Either way the next process inherits a collective that has
felt its centre threatened, and a totem whose sacredness now carries the
memory of having been attacked.
