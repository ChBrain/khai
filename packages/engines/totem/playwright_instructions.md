# Instructions: Totem

How a Playwright wires the totem engine into a play. You wire by linking the
play's own content to the engine's files; the engine files stay untouched, they
are canon. The Roadie plumbs the engine's law into Instructions Knowledge on
deploy. Authoring guidance, not runtime content, and it does not go on tour.

## Human

- The human sets the object, the group it stands for, and who holds it sacred.

## Agent

- From the play's own content, set the totem: link the adherent stance under the revering persona's Projection, and link the process the object is in -- consecration where the gathering charges it, desecration where it is profaned. Keep the object and the group in the play's content, not in the engine. Let the disproportion do the work: a thing of little substance that the group will defend past reason, because their unity is lodged in it. You link to the engine, you never edit it.

## Collaboration

- The awe before the sacred is awe's; the reverence, and the grief or rage at its violation, are emotion's; the rite that charges it is ritual's; the "we" it stands for is group's; belief about the sacred is belief's. Whether the object truly holds the group's force the way the plot needs is the team's review.

## Knowledge

- A totem is an object set apart as sacred that stands for a group: its force is the collective's own, lodged in a thing by the assembled group and kept apart by prohibition. It is charged by gathering (collective effervescence) and fades without renewal; its violation (desecration) strikes the group, not just the object, and forces a reaction. Read it in the gap between what the object is materially and what it commands -- and read an attack on it as an attack on the "we".

## System

- Do link the adherent under Projection and name the process the totem is in; do let the object's force exceed its substance; do let desecration land on the group, not just the thing.
- Do not author the felt awe or grief; those are awe's and emotion's. Do not stage the charging rite here; that is ritual's. Do not edit the engine's files; wire only from the play's side.
