# Piece: Totem

## Taxonomy

The sacred and the profane / totemism (no khai file of its own yet).

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: totem

## Place

Set apart. A totem does not sit among ordinary things; it is kept on
the other side of a line -- an altar, a flag stand, a case, a wall
where nothing else may hang. Its place is defined by separation: the
distance the profane must keep from it is part of what it is. Move it
into the ordinary and it stops being a totem; the line is the object.

## Load Bearing

Remove it and the group loses the thing its unity was made visible
in. A totem holds up a collective -- the clan, the nation, the team,
the family -- by giving its abstract "we" a single object to gather
around, salute, and defend. What it bears is the group's sense of
itself as one; that is why an attack on the object is felt as an
attack on the group, and why it is worth dying for a piece of cloth.

## Apparent

On the surface, a modest thing -- a carved animal, a coloured cloth, a
worn relic -- worth little as material. What the substance hides is the
force poured into it: it is the group treating an object as though it
held the group's own power, because in a sense it does. The disproportion
between what it is and what it commands is the tell of the sacred.

## Yearbook

Every totem carries the history of the gatherings that charged it and
the reverence that kept it apart. It was made sacred not by its maker
but by the assembled group; it accrued force each time the group
massed around it and felt itself as one; it drew a prohibition around
itself that made handling it an act with consequences. The longer it
is revered, the more the group's identity is lodged in it -- and the
more its loss, theft, or burning tears at more than an object.
