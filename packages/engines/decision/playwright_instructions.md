# Instructions: Decision

How a Playwright wires the decision engine into a play. You wire by linking the play's own content to the engine's files; the engine files stay untouched, they are canon. The Roadie plumbs the engine's law into Instructions Knowledge on deploy. Authoring guidance, not runtime content, and it does not go on tour.

## Human

- The human defines the decision: what options exist, how much information is available, what the stakes are, and what the deadline is.

## Agent

- From the play's own content, a persona links the decision mode the moment runs (intuitive, deliberate, heuristic, satisficing, or paralysed) under its Projection. Let the mode reflect how the persona actually resolves under these conditions, not how they wish they resolved. You link to the engine, you never edit it.

## Collaboration

- What information the persona has available is the attention and memory engines'. The emotion that weights the options is the mood or stress engines'. The persona's tolerance for risk belongs to the values or temperament engines'. Paralysis may involve the executive function engine when the governing mechanism has stalled.

## Knowledge

- Decision is resolution under uncertainty: the persona must select one path when the outcome is unknown and resources for analysis are finite. Intuitive decisions draw on pattern recognition outside awareness. Deliberate decisions work through evidence and probability. Heuristic decisions apply a rule of thumb that sacrifices precision for speed. Satisficing accepts the first option that clears a threshold. Paralysis is the failure to resolve: the loop continues without selecting.

## System

- Do let the decision mode reflect the conditions, not a stable trait; a deliberate persona may satisfice under time pressure.
- Do let paralysis be a real outcome; a decision not made is itself a decision.
- Do not resolve uncertainty artificially; the engine is about how the persona operates under it.
- Do not edit the engine's files; wire only from the play's side.
