# Place: Backstage

## Taxonomy

Backstage is a place engine (TO SHOW). It models Goffman's back region: the space barred to the audience where a performance is prepared, rehearsed, corrected, and dropped -- where the team that gives it can step out of character between the moments they are seen.

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: backstage

## Shown

The performer with the front set down: the fatigue, the rehearsal of a line that will be delivered as spontaneous out front, the sorting of props into what is ready and what is not. What backstage shows is exactly what the front region cannot afford to -- the work the performance takes, visible now because no one who is meant to believe in it is watching.

## Holds

The equipment of the performance not currently in use: the costume half on, the script marked up, the leftover materials of an earlier show. Backstage holds the team itself as a standing unit -- the people bound to put on the same front together and therefore trusted with what it costs.

## Offers

The suspension of the performance without loss: a joke about the audience just addressed in earnest, a mistake owned instead of covered, the accent or posture dropped. What backstage offers is the relief of being ratified without having to perform for the people who ratify you -- available only to those already inside the region.

## Withheld

Admission itself. Backstage does not open to whoever wants in; it opens only to those accepted as team members, bound by the discretion the region demands of anyone who sees the performance unmade. What it withholds from the excluded is exactly what it offers the included -- and a breach that lets an outsider or the audience through does not just cost a secret, it can collapse the performance itself.
