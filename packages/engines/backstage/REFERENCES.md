# Backstage: Reference

## Line of Work

Backstage as **place**: the region hidden from the audience where a performance is prepared and dropped. The domain models the back region's anchor (what backstage is; what it shows, holds, offers, and withholds) and two contrasting expressions -- frontstage (the front region, where the performance is given, before an audience) and outside (the residual region: neither front nor back for this performance) -- completing Goffman's tripartite scheme of regions.

The spine is Goffman's dramaturgical sociology of everyday interaction, specifically the region theory developed in Chapter 3 ("Regions and Region Behavior") of _The Presentation of Self in Everyday Life_. A performance, for Goffman, requires a front region where it is given to an audience under expressive standards, and a back region where the performer can suppress the facts the front cannot show: the rehearsal, the discarded take, the private read on the audience, the self set down between performances. Access to the back region is restricted to the performer's team, bound by the discretion the region demands; a breach that lets the audience through does not just leak a secret, it risks the performance itself. The outside is the residual third region: neither front nor back for the performance in question, where a persona is neither audience nor team.

## Restrictions

What the engine refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **The performance itself**: What a persona projects, does, hides, and lets slip while in character -- the mask, the front, the idealized self -- belongs to the persona's own Projection, Action, Shadow, and Tell. This engine models the region a performance occurs within and is prepared inside, not the act of performing.
- **The team's internal politics**: Who leads the team, who defers to whom while preparing the front, belongs to the **hierarchy** and **power** engines. Backstage models the region the team shares, not the standing of its members within it.
- **What the performer feels dropping the front**: The relief, the exposure, the fatigue of the back region belongs to the **emotion** and **mood** engines. This engine models the space, not the felt charge of being in it.
- **The secret the back region shelters**: A specific fact suppressed from the audience, if it needs its own file, belongs to the **secret** engine. Backstage models the region that shelters suppressed facts in general, not any one of them.
