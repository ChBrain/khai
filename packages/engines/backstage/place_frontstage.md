# Place: Frontstage

## Taxonomy

Frontstage is an expression of the backstage engine. It is Goffman's front region: the space where the performance is given, before an audience, held to the expressive standards the performance claims -- the region backstage exists to prepare and protect.

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: backstage

## Shown

The performance itself, in progress: the idealized front, the manner and setting fitted to what the audience is meant to take away. What frontstage shows is only what the performance has decided to give; the audience sees the finished delivery, not the work behind it.

## Holds

The setting proper to the performance: the fixed decor, the furniture of the scene, the standards of decorum the team is expected to sustain for as long as the audience is present. Frontstage holds the audience too, as a standing presence the performance is staged for and shaped by.

## Offers

The claim to the idealized identity the performance presents: to be taken at face value, to have the front believed rather than inspected. Frontstage offers this to the performer for the run of the performance, and to the audience the experience of the show as given, uninterrupted by what produced it.

## Withheld

Everything the back region shelters: the rehearsal, the discarded take, the team's private read on the audience, the self set down between performances. Frontstage withholds backstage from the audience by design -- if the audience saw what the back region shows, the front would stop working.
