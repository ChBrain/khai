# Instructions: Backstage

How a Playwright wires the backstage engine into a play. You wire by linking the
play's own content to the engine's files; the engine files stay untouched, they
are canon. The Roadie plumbs the engine's law into Instructions Knowledge on
deploy. Authoring guidance, not runtime content, and it does not go on tour.

## Human

- The human defines which region a scene occupies -- backstage, frontstage, or outside -- who counts as the accepted team versus the audience versus a bystander for the performance in question, and what would break if that boundary were crossed.

## Agent

- From the play's own content, a persona links the scene's region to its Projection. The region is the space the performance occurs within or is prepared inside, not what the persona projects while in it -- that stays on the persona's own Projection, Action, Shadow, and Tell. You link to the engine, you never edit it.

## Collaboration

- The persona's own Projection, Action, Shadow, and Tell hold the performance itself -- the mask, what is said, what is hidden, what slips. This engine holds only the region the performance happens within. The hierarchy and power engines hold standing within the team preparing a front together; backstage holds the region they share. The emotion and mood engines hold what the performer feels dropping the front or holding it up; this engine holds the space, not the charge. The secret engine holds any one suppressed fact that needs its own file; backstage holds the region that shelters suppressed facts in general.

## Knowledge

- Backstage is the region hidden from the audience where a performance is prepared, rehearsed, corrected, and dropped. It is a place: what it shows is the performer with the front set down; what it holds is the team and the equipment of the performance not in use; what it offers is relief from performing, available only to those already admitted; what it withholds is admission itself, released only to those accepted as team and bound by the discretion the region demands. The engine models two contrasting expressions -- frontstage (the front region, where the performance is given to an audience under expressive standards) and outside (the residual region, neither front nor back for the performance in question) -- completing Goffman's tripartite scheme.

## System

- Do not model the performance itself here -- the mask, the projected self, what is said and withheld in character. That belongs to the persona's own Projection, Action, Shadow, and Tell.
- Do not let backstage stand in for a specific secret. A suppressed fact that needs its own file belongs to the secret engine; this engine models the region, not any one thing hidden in it.
- Do not treat the outside as empty. It is a residual region with its own traffic -- other performances, other teams -- not a null space.
- Do not edit the engine's files; wire only from the play's side.
