# Process: Deception

## Taxonomy

A process (TO IDLE). How a persona deliberately creates a false belief in a target's mind without the target's consent, across the phases of constructing the false impression, delivering it, and maintaining it under scrutiny. Root of the deception process tree.

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: deception

## Initiated by

The persona's decision to conceal a truth and install a different belief in the target. Deception is initiated when the persona knows something, wants the target not to know it, and acts to produce a false belief in the target that the target has not consented to hold. What distinguishes deception from other communicative acts is the intent: the deceiver knows the truth and is deliberately creating a divergence between what they know and what the target comes to believe.

## Direction

From concealment through construction to the target's false belief and its perpetuation. The deception process moves through three phases that are not strictly sequential: the construction of the false impression may continue during delivery, and maintenance may require re-construction when the original deception is challenged. The direction is toward the installation and sustainment of a false belief in the target that serves the persona's purpose.

## Lever

The target's epistemic trust: their tendency to accept what they are told, to interpret ambiguous signals in the direction the persona steers them, and to rely on their normal truth-detection heuristics. DePaulo's research shows that truth detection is poor: people correctly identify deception at rates barely above chance in controlled conditions. The lever is the target's default trust in the persona, the situation, and their own perception.

## Echo

The persona's cognitive load and the deception's accumulated fragility. Maintaining a false belief in someone who can ask questions, observe behavior, and compare accounts requires the persona to track the deception's coherence over time -- what the target knows, what they have been told, and what new information might contradict it. The echo of deception is this ongoing management burden, which grows as the deception extends and the target's information accumulates.
