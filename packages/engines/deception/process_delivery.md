# Process: Delivery

## Taxonomy

[Deception](process_deception.md), the phase in which the persona presents the false impression to the target. Delivery is the communicative act that installs the false belief: what the persona says, does, omits, or signals in the target's presence.

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: deception

## Initiated by

The persona's contact with the target in the context where the false impression is to be installed. Delivery begins when the persona performs the act that is designed to create the false belief -- the statement, the silence, the performance of a false state (feigning emotion, simulating ignorance), or the staging of misleading information. The delivery is the point at which the deception becomes an interpersonal event rather than an internal design.

## Direction

From the persona's act to the target's belief. Delivery moves the intended false impression from inside the persona's design to inside the target's understanding. The target's acceptance of the false impression is the criterion of successful delivery; the persona who has stated something false to a target who has not believed it has delivered but not succeeded.

## Lever

The persona's management of the cues that accompany deception. DePaulo's research on deception cues identifies a wide range of behaviors -- vocal hesitation, gaze aversion, over-controlled facial expression, narrative inconsistency -- that leak the deception and allow the target to detect it. The lever is the persona's ability to control these cues: either suppressing them (strategic self-control) or explaining them (pre-emptive framing that makes the cues seem irrelevant).

## Echo

The target's changed belief and the persona's awareness of what the target now believes. Delivery leaves a specific epistemic state in the target that the persona has produced -- this is what must be tracked and maintained. The echo is the persona's responsibility for the false belief: having installed it, the persona is now in the maintenance position.
