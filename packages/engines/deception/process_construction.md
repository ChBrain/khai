# Process: Construction

## Taxonomy

[Deception](process_deception.md), the phase in which the persona fabricates the false impression: deciding what to conceal, what to substitute, and how to make the false belief available to the target.

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: deception

## Initiated by

The persona's recognition that the target's current or impending knowledge threatens something the persona wants to protect or achieve, combined with the decision to manipulate that knowledge rather than manage the underlying situation. Construction begins when the persona starts designing the false impression: what the target will believe, what the persona will do or say to install that belief, and what inconsistencies need to be pre-empted.

## Direction

From the truth the persona holds to the false belief they intend to create. Construction moves the persona from knowing the truth to having a designed alternative: a specific false impression, with the specific acts, omissions, or statements that will produce it. The construction phase is more cognitively demanding than spontaneous truth-telling; it requires the persona to simultaneously hold the truth and the alternative while planning how to make the alternative available.

## Lever

The choice of mechanism: falsification (substituting a false account for the true one), concealment (omitting true information without substituting a false one), or misdirection (steering the target's attention away from the truth without stating anything false). Buller and Burgoon identify these as the primary forms of deception, each with different demands on the deceiver and different vulnerabilities to detection.

## Echo

The designed false impression and the persona's pre-commitment to it. Construction leaves the persona committed to a specific account -- what they have decided the target will believe -- and to the specific acts that will install it. This pre-commitment constrains the delivery phase: the persona must be consistent with what they have constructed.
