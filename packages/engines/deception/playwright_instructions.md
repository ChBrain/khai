# Instructions: Deception

How a Playwright wires the deception engine into a play. You wire by linking the
play's own content to the engine's files; the engine files stay untouched, they
are canon. The Roadie plumbs the engine's law into Instructions Knowledge on
deploy. Authoring guidance, not runtime content, and it does not go on tour.

## Human

- The human names what is true (what the persona knows), what false belief the persona is creating in the target, what the persona does to install and maintain that belief (the specific acts, statements, and omissions), what the deception is protecting or achieving, and which phase the persona is currently in (construction, delivery, or maintenance).

## Agent

- From the play's own content, a persona links the deception phase they are in to their Projection. The phase marks where the persona is in the management of the false impression. A persona who is actively deceiving must track two accounts simultaneously: the true account and the false one being maintained.

## Collaboration

- The betrayal engine holds the relational consequence when the deception is discovered by someone who trusted the persona. The trust engine holds the target's assessment of the persona's trustworthiness. The repair engine holds what happens after discovery. The belief engine holds how the target's beliefs are formed and updated by what the persona delivers.

## Knowledge

- Deception is the deliberate creation of a false belief in a target without the target's consent. It requires intent: the persona knows the truth and is choosing to create a divergence between that truth and what the target believes. The engine models three phases: construction (the persona decides what to conceal and what false impression to install; chooses the mechanism -- stating something false, omitting true information, or misdirecting attention -- and designs the account they will deliver), delivery (the persona presents the false impression to the target; manages the behavioral cues that accompany deception and can leak the truth; delivery is successful when the target has accepted the false belief), and maintenance (the persona sustains the false belief under the pressure of time, questions, and new information; tracks what the target knows and pre-empts contradictions; the cognitive load of maintenance grows as the deception extends). Detection of deception by targets is poor in natural conditions; the deceiver's epistemic advantage is the primary resource.

## System

- Do not model honest error as deception. Deception requires intent; a sincere false statement is not deception in this engine's sense.
- Do not model self-deception with this engine. The deception engine requires a target other than the deceiver; self-deception is a different construct.
- The maintenance phase is not passive. It requires the persona to actively track the target's evolving knowledge and respond to threats to the false impression's coherence. A persona who has delivered a deception and then stops managing it has not entered maintenance mode; the deception is beginning to deteriorate.
- Do not use this engine for deception as a general theme. Wire it only when the persona is actively engaged in the process of creating and sustaining a specific false belief in a specific target.
- Do not edit the engine's files; wire only from the play's side.
