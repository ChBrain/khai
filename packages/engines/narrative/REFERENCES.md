# Narrative: Reference

## Line of Work

Narrative identity as **position**: the story the persona tells about their own life and what that story demands of them. The domain models the shape and orientation of the self-story -- the sequence the life has taken (redemptive or contaminated) and the thematic centre it is organised around (agentic or communal) -- not the events themselves or the emotional charge they carry.

The spine is McAdams' narrative identity framework: human beings are storytelling animals who construct their personal identities through the internalized, evolving life story they tell about themselves. The story's sequence and thematic orientation have measurable consequences for psychological adaptation, wellbeing, and social behaviour.

## Origin

Narrative psychology and the life story model of identity.

| Source                    | Key Work                                                                                | Scope                                                                                                                                           |
| :------------------------ | :-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | :---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Dan P. McAdams**        | _Stories We Live By_ (1993)                                                             | The foundational account of narrative identity: the self as author and protagonist of an internalized life story.                               |
| **Dan P. McAdams**        | _"The psychology of life stories"_, Review of General Psychology, 5(2), 100--122 (2001) | The formal model: narrative identity defined, the two sequences (redemption, contamination) and two themes (agency, communion) specified.       |
| **Dan P. McAdams et al.** | _"When bad things turn good and good things turn bad"_, JPSP, 80(3), 472--484 (2001)    | Redemptive and contamination sequences in life narratives linked to generativity, mental health, and psychological wellbeing.                   |
| **Dan P. McAdams**        | _The Redemptive Self_ (2006)                                                            | The redemptive narrative as a cultural form: how the arc from suffering to grace organises American identity and its consequences for the self. |

## Restrictions

What the engine refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **The events themselves**: The engine models the shape the persona gives to their life, not the specific events. What happened is owned by the plot and the persona's history, not by the engine.
- **The emotional charge**: The engine models the narrative posture, not the felt tone. Grief, hope, anger, and relief that arise from the story's shape are delegated to the **emotion** and **mood** engines.
- **The arc across the play**: The engine models the persona's narrative stance at entry. Whether and how the life story shifts across the run belongs to the play's arc and the plot's tension.
- **Disposition to tell stories**: The engine models the shape and orientation of the self-story, not the persona's general openness, extraversion, or willingness to self-disclose. Those are delegated to the **temperament** engine.

## Encoding

Source to constraint, per file.

- **[narrative](position_narrative.md)** (the anchor): The life story as position -- the interpreted sequence the persona carries and through which events are received. Anchored by McAdams (1993, 2001).
- **[redemptive](position_redemptive.md)** (expression): The arc from bad to good; suffering is the hinge of growth or deliverance. Anchored by McAdams et al. (2001) -- redemptive sequences linked to generativity and wellbeing.
- **[contaminated](position_contaminated.md)** (expression): The arc from good to bad; promise gives way to loss. Anchored by McAdams et al. (2001) -- contamination sequences linked to reduced wellbeing and generativity stagnation.
- **[agentic](position_agentic.md)** (expression): The story organised around mastery, achievement, and autonomy. Anchored by McAdams (2001) -- agency as a thematic line of narrative identity, linked to self-definition through accomplishment.
- **[communal](position_communal.md)** (expression): The story organised around belonging, care, and love. Anchored by McAdams (2001) -- communion as a thematic line, linked to self-definition through relationship.
