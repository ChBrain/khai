# Instructions: Narrative

How a Playwright wires the narrative engine into a play. You wire by linking the
play's own content to the engine's files; the engine files stay untouched, they
are canon. The Roadie plumbs the engine's law into Instructions Knowledge on
deploy. Authoring guidance, not runtime content, and it does not go on tour.

## Human

- The human sets the events that put the persona's story under pressure: the loss that threatens the redemptive arc, the gain that sits uncomfortably in a contaminated story, the relational demand that cuts against an agentic life, the isolation that costs a communal one.

## Agent

- From the play's own content, each persona links under Projection the narrative shape they carry: redemptive, contaminated, agentic, or communal. Show the shape in how the persona reads events, not in what they say about themselves.

## Collaboration

- The arc of the narrative over the whole play belongs to the play's Plot and Arc, not to the engine. The engine sets the posture at entry; the play decides whether and how it changes.

## Knowledge

- Narrative is the story a persona tells about their own life: how they got to now, what the arc means. It is not a summary of events but an interpretation of them -- a shape that makes the persona legible to themselves. The shape has a sequence (redemptive or contaminated: from bad to good, or from good to bad) and a thematic orientation (agentic or communal: organised around mastery or organised around belonging). The story functions as the persona's ongoing answer to who they are, and it requires more than a single hard event to shift.

## System

- Do read the narrative shape in how the persona receives events, not as a stated label; the shape shows in interpretation, not announcement.
- Do not collapse narrative into mood: a contaminated narrative is not despair and a redemptive narrative is not optimism. The shape is structural, not emotional.
- Do not model the specific events of the life here; those belong to the plot. The engine supplies the lens through which the persona reads whatever the plot delivers.
