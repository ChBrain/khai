# Process: Envy

## Taxonomy

Upward comparison reckoning.

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: envy

## Initiated by

Another person holds a good the persona lacks and wants -- a possession, a quality, a standing, a relationship -- and the comparison runs upward.
The other is close enough to compare against: a peer, a rival, someone in the persona's own field of self-definition.
The gap registers not as information but as a wound; the other's advantage is felt as a deduction from the self.

## Direction

Toward closing the gap.
The persona is moved to make the comparison stop hurting -- by rising to the other's level, by pulling the other down to theirs, or by recoding the gap so it no longer indicts them.
The direction is not chosen at once; it is fought over, and the same gap can drive the persona up or down depending on which way it turns.

## Lever

The good is the other's, and the self is measured against it.
The persona cannot want the good without the wanting being also a verdict: the other has it and I do not, and the lack is mine.
The advantage of the near other presses where the advantage of the distant great does not -- comparison bites at the border of the self.

## Echo

The process runs through benign (the gap motivates raising the self), malicious (the gap motivates lowering the other), jealousy (a valued bond guarded against a rival), and resentment (the gap moralized into injustice).
The branches are not stages; the persona may move between them as the gap is read one way and then another.
What ends the process is not the other's loss but the comparison ceasing to threaten: [benign](process_benign.md), [malicious](process_malicious.md), [jealousy](process_jealousy.md), [resentment](process_resentment.md).
