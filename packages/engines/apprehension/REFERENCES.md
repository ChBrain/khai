# Apprehension: Reference

## Line of Work

Apprehension as **position**: the baseline nervous-system filter and cognitive grasp a persona arrives with and returns to. It does not model active attention allocation or real-time communication loss (which are dynamic processes); it models the resting cognitive configuration, sensory thresholds, and expressive preferences that bias those processes. The core claim is that human minds vary systematically across sensory, attentional, executive, and communicative dimensions, and that every profile carries both adaptive advantages and inherent metabolic/cognitive costs.

## Origin

The cognitive science and psychology of neurodiversity, sensory processing, and communication theory.

| Source                       | Key Work                                                                                    | Scope                                                                                              |
| :--------------------------- | :------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ | :------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Murray, Lawson, & Lesser** | _"Attention, monotropism and the diagnostic criteria for autism"_ (2005)                    | The monotropic/polytropic model of attention allocation based on interest systems.                 |
| **Elaine Aron**              | _"Sensory-processing sensitivity and its relation to introversion and emotionality"_ (1997) | Sensory Processing Sensitivity (SPS) and the depth-of-processing/overstimulation model (DOES).     |
| **Lucy Jane Miller**         | _Sensory Modulation Disorder_ (2007)                                                        | The classification of sensory over-responsivity (avoidance) and under-responsivity (seeking).      |
| **Damian Milton**            | _"On the ontological status of autism: the 'double empathy' problem"_ (2012)                | Mismatches in communication and empathy styles causing mutual, bidirectional interaction friction. |
| **Uta Frith**                | _Weak Central Coherence Theory_ (1989/2003)                                                 | Bottom-up (local) vs. top-down (global/gestalt) information synthesis styles.                      |
| **William Dodson**           | _Interest-Based Nervous System_ (2016)                                                      | Executive function and task initiation differences in ADHD (interest/urgency vs. linear sequence). |

## Restrictions

What the engine refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **The situational attentional spotlight:** Apprehension determines the baseline attention style (monotropic vs. polytropic); the real-time spotlight budget and dynamic capture/division is **delegated to the attention engine**.
- **The felt reaction to overload:** Apprehension models sensory threshold sensitivity; how a persona reacts when sensory thresholds are breached (e.g., fight, flight, freeze, fawn) is **delegated to the stress engine**.
- **The actual emotion felt:** The emotional valuation of events (e.g., panic, anger) is **delegated to the emotion engine**.
- **The communicative channel and loss:** Apprehension models the preferred style of communication (declarative vs. attuned); the actual transmission and loss of meaning is **delegated to the language engine**.

## Encoding

Source to constraint. One position anchor (the baseline filter) and ten pole positions across five axes.

- **[apprehension](position_apprehension.md)** (the anchor): The baseline nervous-system filter and cognitive grasp.
- **Attention Allocation** — [monotropic](position_monotropic.md) (single-channel focus, flow-prone) / [polytropic](position_polytropic.md) (multichannel scanning, distributed).
- **Sensory Reactivity** — [hyperreactive](position_hyperreactive.md) (low threshold, deep perception, overload-prone) / [hyporeactive](position_hyporeactive.md) (high threshold, sensation-seeking).
- **Executive Initiation** — [linear](position_linear.md) (sequential planning, priority-based) / [associative](position_associative.md) (interest/urgency-driven, non-linear).
- **Communication Style** — [declarative](position_declarative.md) (direct, literal) / [attuned](position_attuned.md) (indirect, subtextual).
- **Integration Style** — [bottom_up](position_bottom_up.md) (localist, detail-first) / [top_down](position_top_down.md) (globalist, gestalt-first).
