# Instructions: Apprehension

How a Playwright wires the apprehension engine into a play. You wire by linking the play's own content to the engine's files; the engine files remain read-only canon. The Roadie plumbs the engine's law into Instructions Knowledge on deploy. Authoring guidance, not runtime content, and it does not go on tour.

## Human

- The human defines the persona's cognitive, sensory, and communicative baselines before any situational pressure or emotional spike acts upon them.

## Agent

- Every persona carries links under Projection to its chosen poles across the five axes. Let these positions tilt every interaction. The baseline is the default shape of the persona's grasp; show them return to it when the situational tension relaxes.

## Collaboration

- **Double Empathy Interaction:** When writing dialogue and scenes between personas, pay attention to the alignment of their poles.
  - Matched personas (e.g., both monotropic/declarative) communicate with minimal subtextual overhead.
  - Mismatched personas (e.g., one monotropic/declarative and one polytropic/attuned) experience friction not from bad faith or lack of capability, but from the systemic cognitive cost of translating between their respective styles. Write this friction as bidirectional.

## Knowledge

- Apprehension is the baseline nervous-system filter and cognitive grasp: five axes, two adaptive poles each. It is the soil in which attention, stress, and communication grow. It does not fluctuate with the scene's mood or the character's emotion; it is the constant against which departure and effort are measured.

## System

- Do give every persona a baseline.
- Do let the poles dictate what a persona registers, what they miss, and what costs them energy.
- Do not shift the poles across the run; they are structural disposition, not dynamic state.
- Do not edit the engine's files; wire only from the play's side by linking to the poles under `## Projection`.
