# Process: Retaliation

## Taxonomy

[Escalation](process_escalation.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: escalation

## Initiated by

A move from the other party that the holder reads as an attack,
delivered by a party already read as an
[antagonist](position_antagonist.md).
The move does not have to have been intended as an attack. Once the
other party is read through a hostile motive, an ambiguous move resolves
to hostile by default, and that resolved reading is what triggers the
response.

## Direction

Tit-for-tat, but never flat.
Each response answers the move it follows, so on its face the process
looks even: a strike is met with a strike. In practice each side rounds
its own response up and the other side's provocation down, so the
matching reply is never actually matched -- it is calibrated to land
a degree harder, under the belief that a reply of equal weight will
just be read as an invitation to try again.

## Lever

The asymmetry between how a party rates its own moves and how it rates
the other's.
A party's own retaliation reads, from the inside, as proportionate --
a fair answer to what was just done to it. The same move, seen from the
other side, reads as an escalation with no justification, because the
other side is running the identical asymmetry in the opposite direction.
Both readings are sincere, and both cannot be correct at once; that gap
is the fuel.

## Echo

The exchange has a new floor.
Whatever this round's heaviest move was becomes the baseline the next
round measures against, not the level either side started at. The
tit-for-tat has hardened one round further, and a return to the
previous, lighter footing now itself has to be initiated -- unilaterally,
by whichever side moves first, with no guarantee the other reads it as
anything but another tactic.
