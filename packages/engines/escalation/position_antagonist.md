# Position: Antagonist

## Taxonomy

[Escalation](process_escalation.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: escalation

## Has

A single hostile motive standing in for a whole person.
The other party's ordinary mix of aims -- most of which have nothing to
do with this dispute -- collapses down to the one aim that opposes the
holder, and that aim is read as the explanation for everything the other
party does, not only the disputed act.

## Orders

Read every move the other party makes through the opposing motive.
A neutral act reads as a tactic. A conciliatory act reads as a trick or
a sign of weakness to press. The position does not require evidence for
each reading; it supplies the reading in advance and treats confirmation
as the only kind of evidence it will accept.

## Loses

The option of taking the other party's stated reasons at face value.
Once the other party has been read as an antagonist, an olive branch has
to clear a much higher bar before it is treated as sincere, and most
offered branches do not clear it -- so real de-escalation moves from the
other side keep failing to register as real.

## Drives

On the loss of the face-value option:
meets the other party's every move with a matching or heavier
[retaliation](process_retaliation.md), reading resistance as proof of
the hostile motive rather than a response to the holder's own last
move -- or, more rarely, the reading is disconfirmed hard enough by
something the other party does that the position drops and the parties
find their way back to seeing an opponent instead of an enemy.
