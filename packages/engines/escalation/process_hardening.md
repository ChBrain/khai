# Process: Hardening

## Taxonomy

[Escalation](process_escalation.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: escalation

## Initiated by

A position stated once, then challenged.
The challenge does not have to be strong. It only has to land while the
speaker is already invested in having said it, which is enough to turn a
stance held loosely into a stance held on principle.

## Direction

From a specific complaint toward a general indictment, and from a soft
tactic toward a heavier one.
The first exchange is usually about one thing, argued in words. Each
round afterward drags in a second issue, a third, a piece of history
that was never settled, until the original complaint is one item on a
list. In step with that spread, the tactics used to press each item get
heavier: reasoned argument gives way to pressure, pressure gives way to
action taken where words alone no longer feel adequate.
Both slides run together -- the issues widen and the tactics used on
them get heavier at the same rate, each licensing the other.

## Lever

The cost of backing off a stated position weighed against the cost of
letting it stand unchallenged.
Once a position has been said aloud in front of the other party, walking
it back reads as conceding the whole exchange, not just the point in
question -- so the position holds even where the holder privately doubts
it, and holding it under doubt is what hardening is.

## Echo

The position is no longer available for revision by ordinary means.
It is held past what the original disagreement earned, and further
exchanges start from the hardened position rather than reopening it,
which is what feeds [the antagonist reading](position_antagonist.md)
and the next round of [retaliation](process_retaliation.md).
