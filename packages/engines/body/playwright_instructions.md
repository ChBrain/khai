# Instructions: Body

How a Playwright wires the body engine into a play. You wire by linking the
play's own content to the engine's files; the engine files stay untouched, they
are canon. The Roadie plumbs the engine's law into Instructions Knowledge on
deploy. Authoring guidance, not runtime content, and it does not go on tour.

## Human

- The human sets the strain on the persona's body and what, in the scene, could meet it.

## Agent

- From the play's own content, a persona links the demand its body is running (hunger, fatigue, pain, arousal, or sickness) under its Projection, and lets it grow as the set-point drifts, until something meets it. You link to the engine, you never edit it.

## Collaboration

- Where the demand pulls and narrows the persona's attention is the attention engine's; the felt charge of the demand is the emotion engine's; what could meet it, the food, the rest, the cure, is the scene's. Whether the pull rings true is the team's review.

## Knowledge

- The body is a keeper of set-points, fuel, rest, integrity, charge, defence, and when one drifts it registers the gap and pulls the persona to close it. The demand narrows attention as the drift deepens, and it builds across the run until it is met, then resets. It is not a static state but a rising tide: the persona who could think clearly an hour ago cannot now, because the body has taken the floor.

## System

- Do let it build as the set-point drifts and reset when met; do show it taking attention as it rises.
- Do not hold it at a fixed level; it escalates until answered. Do not author what meets it here; that is the scene's. Do not edit the engine's files; wire only from the play's side.
