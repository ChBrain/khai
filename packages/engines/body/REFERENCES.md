# Body: Reference

## Line of Work

Body as **process**: how a persona's physical state makes its demands felt and pulls on everything else. The model is otherwise all mind and social read; this is the physical substrate it all runs on. The domain does not model the feeling _about_ the bodily state, nor the social read of the body, nor the decision to act on the demand; it models the demand itself -- a set-point drifting out of range, registered as a gap, pulling the persona to close it and narrowing what else can be reached until it is met.

The spine is homeostasis and interoception: the body keeps a handful of regulated levels -- fuel, rest, integrity, charge, defence -- and senses its own internal state directly. When a level drifts, the drift is felt as a demand with a direction, and the demand competes with the task and the world that would defer it. Each of the five is a different set-point come due, with its own pull and its own price for being ignored.

## Origin

Homeostasis, interoception, and the drive theory of motivation, applied directly as process constraints.

| Source             | Key Work                                 | Scope                                                                                                 |
| :----------------- | :--------------------------------------- | :---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Walter Cannon**  | _The Wisdom of the Body_ (1932)          | Homeostasis: the body holds its internal set-points within a range and acts to correct a drift.       |
| **A. D. Craig**    | _"How do you feel?"_ (2002)              | Interoception: the body senses its own physiological condition, and that sense is the felt demand.    |
| **Melzack & Wall** | _"Pain mechanisms: a new theory"_ (1965) | Gate-control: pain is a regulated signal that seizes attention to a breach of bodily integrity.       |
| **Clark Hull**     | _Principles of Behavior_ (1943)          | Drive reduction: a deficit creates a drive, and the drive pushes the organism to act until it is met. |

## Restrictions

What the engine refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Autonomic survival surge**: The engine models the steady demand of a drifting set-point, not the involuntary nervous-system mobilisation under acute threat. The fight, flight, freeze, and fawn surge is **delegated to the stress engine**. A persona may run both at once -- stress routes the survival response, this engine names the bodily demand underneath.
- **The feeling about the state**: The engine models the bodily demand, not the appraised emotion the persona has _about_ it -- the dread of the pain, the shame of the hunger, the relief of rest. The discrete felt emotion is **delegated to the emotion engine**.
- **The social read of the body**: The engine models the demand as it presses from within, not how the body is read from outside -- the gendered, aged, judged surface the room sees. The social read of the body, and age-as-read, is **delegated to the gender engine**.
- **The decision to act**: The engine models the pull, not the persona's choice to obey, defer, or override it. Whether and how the demand is acted on -- the regulation of the drive into conduct -- is **delegated to the motivation engine**.
- **The long arc**: The engine models the demand in the scene, the acute state come due now. Chronic illness, injury that does not heal, and the body's aging across the play are delegated to the play's arc design.

## Encoding

Source to constraint, per file.

- **[body](process_body.md)** (the root): The organism as a keeper of set-points -- fuel, rest, integrity, charge, defence -- that registers a drift as a gap and pulls to close it. Anchored by Walter Cannon (homeostasis) and A. D. Craig (interoception).
- **[hunger](process_hunger.md)** (expression): The fuel set-point drifts low; the pull is toward food, focus narrows, and patience and warmth thin. Anchored by Clark Hull (drive reduction from a deficit).
- **[fatigue](process_fatigue.md)** (expression): The rest set-point drifts low; the pull is toward stopping, attention frays, judgment slows, the body heavy, and micro-lapses open. Anchored by Walter Cannon (the reserve held within a range).
- **[pain](process_pain.md)** (expression): The integrity set-point is breached; the alarm of damage seizes attention to the site, the body guards, and everything else recedes. Anchored by Melzack & Wall (gate-control).
- **[arousal](process_arousal.md)** (expression): The charge set-point drifts high; the body is keyed up by exertion, adrenaline, or physical desire, and the energy seeks discharge. Anchored by A. D. Craig (interoception of activation).
- **[sickness](process_sickness.md)** (expression): The defence set-point is engaged; the body fights itself, withdraws, conserves, the world made distant, the pull to lie still and wait it out. Anchored by Walter Cannon (the body's defence of its own internal order).
