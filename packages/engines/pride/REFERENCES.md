# Pride: Reference

## Line of Work

Pride as **process**: how a persona reads a standard met as a positive evaluation of the self, and the read becomes a felt display and a drive toward more of the same. The domain does not model the negative self-conscious readings (shame, guilt), the felt affect underneath any appraisal, the standing a win buys in the group, or a stable trait of self-regard -- those are delegated elsewhere.

The spine is Tracy and Robins' two-facet structure of pride: a single self-conscious emotion that splits, at the level of causal attribution, into two psychologically and behaviourally distinct readings. Where the credit for a success lands -- on an unstable, specific, controllable act, or on a stable, global, uncontrollable self -- determines whether the pride felt is authentic ("I did well") or hubristic ("I am great").

## Origin

Self-conscious emotion research and the attribution theory of pride, applied directly as process constraints.

## Restrictions

What the engine refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **The negative self-conscious readings**: The engine models pride, the positive self-evaluation, not shame (a global self read as exposed and deficient) or guilt (a specific act read as wrong). Those are **delegated to the shame engine** and to a future guilt engine.
- **The felt affect underneath**: The engine models the appraisal specific to a standard met and credited to the self, not the underlying felt charge any appraisal produces. General felt emotion is **delegated to the emotion engine**.
- **The standing the win buys**: The engine models the persona's own read of the success, not the rank, deference, or position that win earns in the group's eyes. That is **delegated to the status and hierarchy engines**.
- **A stable trait of self-regard**: The engine models the discrete appraisal an event generates, not the persona's persisting baseline of self-worth. That is **delegated to a future self-esteem engine** (a position, not a process).

## Encoding

Source to constraint, per file.

- **[pride](process_pride.md)** (the root): The appraisal where a standard met meets a self credited for meeting it, and becomes a display and a drive. Anchored by Tracy & Robins (pride as a self-conscious emotion following a positive self-evaluation).
- **[authentic pride](process_authentic_pride.md)** (expression): The success is credited to the act -- effort, a specific controllable cause; the read is "I did well," and the tendency is to invest more in the act. Anchored by Tracy & Robins (authentic pride's attribution to unstable, specific, controllable causes, and its association with genuine self-esteem and prosocial orientation).
- **[hubristic pride](process_hubristic_pride.md)** (expression): The success is credited to the self entire -- a stable, global, uncontrollable cause; the read is "I am great," and the tendency is to assert and defend the self. Anchored by Tracy & Robins (hubristic pride's attribution to stable, global, uncontrollable causes, and its association with arrogance, dominance, and narcissism).
