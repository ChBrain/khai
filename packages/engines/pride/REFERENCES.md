# Pride: Reference

## Line of Work

Pride as **process**: how a persona reads a standard met as a positive evaluation of the self, and the read becomes a felt display and a drive toward more of the same. The domain does not model the negative self-conscious readings (shame, guilt), the felt affect underneath any appraisal, the standing a win buys in the group, or a stable trait of self-regard -- those are delegated elsewhere.

The spine is Tracy and Robins' two-facet structure of pride: a single self-conscious emotion that splits, at the level of causal attribution, into two psychologically and behaviourally distinct readings. Where the credit for a success lands -- on an unstable, specific, controllable act, or on a stable, global, uncontrollable self -- determines whether the pride felt is authentic ("I did well") or hubristic ("I am great").

Around that spine the engine now carries three more pieces of the same read. The display that answers a win is innate, not learned -- it fires the same way whether or not the persona has ever seen another winner's posture to copy. Each facet is also the affective engine underneath a distinct route to social rank: authentic pride toward prestige, freely conferred; hubristic pride toward dominance, taken by threat. What either route actually confers -- the rank itself, the ladder, the deference order -- stays the standing status and hierarchy engines' ground; pride carries only the emotion that fuels the reaching for it. And the same 2007 study that gives the facets their structure also gives the persona a trait-level dial on both.

Self-conscious emotion research and the attribution theory of pride, applied directly as process and position constraints.

## Restrictions

What the engine refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **The negative self-conscious readings**: The engine models pride, the positive self-evaluation, not shame (a global self read as exposed and deficient) or guilt (a specific act read as wrong). Those are **delegated to the shame engine** and to a future guilt engine.
- **The felt affect underneath**: The engine models the appraisal specific to a standard met and credited to the self, not the underlying felt charge any appraisal produces. General felt emotion is **delegated to the emotion engine**.
- **The standing the win buys, and the routes' own rank structure**: The engine models the persona's own read of the success, and the emotion that fuels reaching for rank -- authentic pride toward prestige, hubristic pride toward dominance -- not the rank, deference, or position that win earns in the group's eyes, nor the ladder, the moves, or the deference order those routes produce. That is **delegated to the status and hierarchy engines**.
- **A stable trait of self-regard**: The engine models the discrete appraisal an event generates, not the persona's persisting baseline of self-worth. That is **delegated to a future self-esteem engine** (a position, not a process).

## Encoding

Source to constraint, per file.

- **[pride](process_pride.md)** (the root): The appraisal where a standard met meets a self credited for meeting it, and becomes a display and a drive. Anchored by Tracy & Robins (pride as a self-conscious emotion following a positive self-evaluation).
- **[pride display](process_pride_display.md)** (expression, child of the root): The innate nonverbal signal -- expanded posture, chest out, head tilted back, arms raised or akimbo -- that fires whether or not the persona has ever seen another winner. Anchored by Tracy & Matsumoto (congenitally blind athletes producing the display after victory) and by Tracy, Shariff & Cheng's naturalist's-view source on the display's signaling function.
- **[authentic pride](process_authentic_pride.md)** (expression): The success is credited to the act -- effort, a specific controllable cause; the read is "I did well," and the tendency is to invest more in the act. Anchored by Tracy & Robins (authentic pride's attribution to unstable, specific, controllable causes, and its association with genuine self-esteem and prosocial orientation).
- **[route, prestige](process_route_prestige.md)** (expression, child of authentic pride): The status route authentic pride fuels -- rank freely conferred for demonstrated skill and knowledge shared, evaporating once the showing stops. Anchored by Cheng, Tracy & Henrich (the facet-to-route mapping) and Cheng et al. 2013 (the route's real behavioral payoff).
- **[hubristic pride](process_hubristic_pride.md)** (expression): The success is credited to the self entire -- a stable, global, uncontrollable cause; the read is "I am great," and the tendency is to assert and defend the self. Anchored by Tracy & Robins (hubristic pride's attribution to stable, global, uncontrollable causes, and its association with arrogance, dominance, and narcissism).
- **[route, dominance](process_route_dominance.md)** (expression, child of hubristic pride): The status route hubristic pride fuels -- rank taken and held through intimidation and the credible threat of cost, persisting exactly as long as the fear does. Anchored by Cheng, Tracy & Henrich (the facet-to-route mapping) and Cheng et al. 2013 (the route's real behavioral payoff).
- **[pride disposition](position_pride_disposition.md)** (the dial, child of the root): The trait-level position -- how readily this persona's pride fires, and which facet it habitually resolves toward. Anchored by Tracy & Robins' 2007 trait and state scales for both facets.
