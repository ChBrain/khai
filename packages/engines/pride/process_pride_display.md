# Process: Pride Display

## Taxonomy

[Pride](process_pride.md), the appraisal this display makes visible.

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: pride

## Initiated by

The appraisal fires and the body answers before the persona decides to let
it: the chest expands, the shoulders draw back, the head tilts up, the arms
lift or plant at the hips. Tracy and Matsumoto found the same posture in
congenitally blind athletes from more than thirty nations in the instant
they won -- competitors who had never seen a winner's stance to copy it
from. The display initiates from the appraisal itself, not from watching a
room and learning what a winner is supposed to look like.

## Direction

Outward, involuntarily.
The vector is expression ahead of deliberation: the posture rises and
claims space in the instant of the win, before any choice gets made about
whether to be seen. A persona can compose the face that follows; the body
has already spoken.

## Lever

The appraisal meets the body's own wiring, not the persona's intent to be
seen. Being biologically prepared, not culturally taught, is what presses
this lever: strip away every model the persona could have imitated and the
display still fires, because [pride](process_pride.md) itself drives it,
not an audience.

## Echo

The display leaks whether the persona wills it or not, and suppressing it
is itself a visible act -- a held chest, a stance caught and pulled back
down, reads as effort spent, not as pride's absence. What the display does
not do is sort the two facets apart: a raised chest looks the same whether
the credit underneath lands on the act or on the self entire, so the room
has to read [authentic](process_authentic_pride.md) from
[hubristic](process_hubristic_pride.md) pride in the words and the conduct
that follow, never in the posture alone. Once the room has that read, the
same posture is also doing signaling work, broadcasting status and
achievement to onlookers, which is the ground this engine's naturalist's-view
source already covers.
