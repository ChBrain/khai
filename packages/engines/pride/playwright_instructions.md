# Instructions: Pride

How a Playwright wires the pride engine into a play. You wire by linking the
play's own content to the engine's files; the engine files stay untouched, they
are canon. The Roadie plumbs the engine's law into Instructions Knowledge on
deploy. Authoring guidance, not runtime content, and it does not go on tour.

## Human

- The human sets the standard and the outcome that meets it, and picks which way the credit falls -- onto the act, or onto the self.

## Agent

- From the play's own content, cast the persona's facet (authentic or hubristic pride), the status route that facet fuels (prestige or dominance), and the persona's own disposition, under its Projection, in the first beat after the standard is met. Note that the pride display itself leaks regardless of facet -- the posture rises whether the credit underneath is authentic or hubristic, so let the room read the attribution from what the persona says and does next, not from the posture alone. You link to the engine, you never edit it.

## Collaboration

- The standard the persona is held to is the scene's; the standing either route actually buys -- the rank, the ladder position, the deference order -- is status's or hierarchy's, not this engine's, which carries only the emotion that fuels reaching for it; a stable trait of self-regard is self-esteem's, not this. Whether the attribution -- act or self -- rings true to what the persona actually did is the team's review.

## Knowledge

- Pride is an appraisal: a standard is met, the credit lands on the self, and the read becomes a display and a drive. The display -- expanded posture, chest out, head tilted back -- is innate and involuntary, firing on the appraisal itself rather than on having seen another winner, and it does not by itself distinguish the facets. Authentic pride credits the act and pulls the persona toward more of it, fueling the prestige route -- rank freely conferred for competence shown, lost when the showing stops. Hubristic pride credits the self entire and pulls the persona toward asserting and defending that self, fueling the dominance route -- rank taken and held by threat, lost the moment the threat is called. A persona's disposition sets how readily pride fires at all and which facet it defaults toward. It is the felt charge of a moment, never a standing trait: a persona proud at one beat can be ashamed the next.

## System

- Do tie it to a standard actually met and let the facet follow where the credit lands; do show the display in the first beat, enacted -- the posture, not a label -- regardless of which facet is running; do cast the route each facet fuels and the persona's disposition where the scene calls for them. Do not confuse it with shame or guilt (the negative self-conscious readings; those engines' own); do not author the rank ladder, the deference order, or any other structure either route actually confers, that is status's or hierarchy's to write. Do not edit the engine's files; wire only from the play's side.
