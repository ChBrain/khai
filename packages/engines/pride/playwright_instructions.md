# Instructions: Pride

How a Playwright wires the pride engine into a play. You wire by linking the
play's own content to the engine's files; the engine files stay untouched, they
are canon. The Roadie plumbs the engine's law into Instructions Knowledge on
deploy. Authoring guidance, not runtime content, and it does not go on tour.

## Human

- The human sets the standard and the outcome that meets it, and picks which way the credit falls -- onto the act, or onto the self.

## Agent

- From the play's own content, a persona links the facet it runs (authentic or hubristic pride) under its Projection, in the first beat after the standard is met. You link to the engine, you never edit it.

## Collaboration

- The standard the persona is held to is the scene's; the standing the win buys, if any, is status's or hierarchy's; a stable trait of self-regard is self-esteem's, not this. Whether the attribution -- act or self -- rings true to what the persona actually did is the team's review.

## Knowledge

- Pride is an appraisal: a standard is met, the credit lands on the self, and the read becomes a display and a drive. Authentic pride credits the act and pulls the persona toward more of it; hubristic pride credits the self entire and pulls the persona toward asserting and defending that self. It is the felt charge of a moment, never a standing trait: a persona proud at one beat can be ashamed the next.

## System

- Do tie it to a standard actually met and let the facet follow where the credit lands; do show it in the first beat, enacted -- the posture, not a label. Do not confuse it with shame or guilt (the negative self-conscious readings; those engines' own); do not author the standing the win buys, that is status's or hierarchy's. Do not edit the engine's files; wire only from the play's side.
