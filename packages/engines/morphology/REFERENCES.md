# Morphology: Reference

## Line of Work

Morphology as **play**: the wondertale as Vladimir Propp abstracted it -- not
one story but the invariant skeleton beneath a hundred of them, a fixed
sequence of functions that recur, in the same order, across tale after tale.
The domain does not model a particular hero, a particular villain, or a
particular kingdom (those are the production's, materialised outside the
engine); it models the sequence itself -- what has to happen, in what order,
for a wondertale to be a wondertale.

The spine is Propp's function: an act of a character, defined from the point
of view of its significance for the course of the action. Propp's claim, made
against the folklorists of his time who classified tales by motif or by
theme, was that the functions -- not the characters who perform them, not the
objects that carry them -- are the stable, limited, and ordered units of the
tale.

## Origin

Russian Formalism, applied directly as a fixed function sequence and a closed
set of dramatis personae: Propp's structural analysis of the wondertale.

| Source                  | Key Work                                                                                             | Scope                                                                                     |
| :---------------------- | :--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | :---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Vladimir Propp**      | _Morphology of the Folktale_ (1928; Eng. trans. 1958/1968)                                           | The 31 functions, their fixed order, and the seven spheres of action.                     |
| **Alan Dundes**         | Introduction to the 1968 English edition; _The Morphology of North American Indian Folktales_ (1964) | The reception of Propp's method and its extension beyond the Russian corpus.              |
| **Claude Lévi-Strauss** | _Structural Anthropology_ (1958)                                                                     | The structuralist critique of, and dialogue with, Propp's sequential (syntagmatic) model. |

## Restrictions

What the engine refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **The specific tale**: The engine ships the function skeleton, not a named
  story. Which hero, which villain, which kingdom, and how the functions are
  dressed in incident is the production's, materialised outside the engine.
- **The cultural skin**: Propp's corpus was Russian; the functions travel
  further, but the color of the telling -- the kingdom's customs, the
  villain's shape, what counts as a wedding -- is the consuming culture's to
  supply.
- **The named hero's interiority**: The engine models the function the hero
  performs (departs, reacts, struggles), not the hero's felt experience of
  performing it. What the hero feels along the way belongs to the personas
  and processes that inhabit this play, not to the sequence itself.
- **Absent functions**: Propp observed that no single wondertale uses all 31
  functions, and that this is normal, not a defect. The engine names the full
  order; which functions a given tale skips is a production choice, not
  something the engine enforces.

## Encoding

Source to constraint. A play anchor (the fixed sequence) with six plots, each
a phase of Propp's function order, chained from the initial lack to the final
wedding.

- **[the folktale](play_folktale.md)** (the anchor): The read itself -- the
  wondertale as a fixed sequence of functions, run in the same order however
  many are present. Anchored by Propp (the 31 functions and their invariant
  order).
- **[preparation](plot_preparation.md)**: Absentation, interdiction,
  violation, reconnaissance, delivery, trickery, complicity -- functions
  I-VII. Anchored by Propp (the initial situation and the villain's
  approach).
- **[complication](plot_complication.md)**: Villainy or lack, mediation,
  counteraction, departure -- functions VIII-XI. Anchored by Propp (the
  tale's motor and the hero's consent).
- **[the donor sequence](plot_donor.md)**: The first function of the donor,
  the hero's reaction, provision -- functions XII-XIV. Anchored by Propp (the
  donor's test and the transfer of the magical agent).
- **[struggle](plot_struggle.md)**: Guidance, struggle, branding, victory,
  liquidation -- functions XV-XIX. Anchored by Propp (the direct contest and
  the liquidation of the initial lack).
- **[return](plot_return.md)**: Return, pursuit, rescue, unrecognized
  arrival -- functions XX-XXIII. Anchored by Propp (the reversed road and the
  arrival that does not yet resolve the tale).
- **[recognition](plot_recognition.md)**: Unfounded claims, the difficult
  task, solution, recognition, exposure, transfiguration, punishment,
  wedding -- functions XXIV-XXXI. Anchored by Propp (the false hero's
  exposure and the tale's close).
