# Instructions: Morphology

How a Playwright wires the morphology engine into a play. You wire by linking
the play's own content to the engine's files; the engine files stay
untouched, they are canon. The Roadie plumbs the engine's law into
Instructions Knowledge on deploy. Authoring guidance, not runtime content,
and it does not go on tour.

## Human

- The human sets which wondertale is being told: the specific lack, the
  specific villain, the specific hero -- the dressing the fixed sequence
  wears in this telling.

## Agent

- From the play's own content, give the play its function sequence under
  Arc: link the phases it runs (preparation, complication, the donor
  sequence, struggle, return, recognition) in Propp's fixed order. A given
  tale may skip a phase's functions, but never reorders what it keeps. You
  link to the engine, you never edit it.

## Collaboration

- The named hero's felt experience of departing, testing, and struggling is
  identity's and emotion's; the standing the wedding or ascension confers is
  hierarchy's; the "we" the tale closes into is group's; the specific field
  each plot plays out in is the scene's. Whether the sequence is true to
  Propp's order is the team's review.

## Knowledge

- Morphology is Propp's function: an act of a character defined by its
  significance for the course of the action, not by who performs it or what
  object carries it. Read the wondertale as a fixed sequence of these
  functions, grouped into six chained phases -- an initial lack fires
  departure, a donor's test gates the magical agent, a struggle's victory
  liquidates the lack, a return sets up recognition. Functions may be absent
  from a given tale, but the order among the functions present never
  changes.

## System

- Do anchor the sequence under Arc as a fixed, chained order; do let a
  given tale skip functions rather than force all 31; do keep the seven
  spheres of action (villain, donor, helper, princess and father,
  dispatcher, hero, false hero) as the only cast a plot draws from.
- Do not author the named hero's interiority here; that is identity's and
  emotion's. Do not reorder the phases to suit a scene; that breaks the
  morphology. Do not edit the engine's files; wire only from the play's
  side.
