# Plot: Preparation

## Taxonomy

[The Folktale](play_folktale.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: morphology

## Cue

The tale opens on an ordinary household at rest -- Propp's initial
situation, the members of the family enumerated before anything happens to
them. Beneath the calm, an absence is already scheduled: someone is about
to leave.

## Action

Absentation opens the sequence: a family member leaves the security of home
(the elder generation departs for work, or the younger generation is
allowed out). Interdiction follows -- the hero is given a rule ("do not go
there," "do not open this"). Violation reverses it: the interdiction is
broken, and the villain, until now offstage, enters the tale. Reconnaissance
has the villain seek information (about a child, about valuables); delivery
has the villain receive it, often from the very person questioned. Trickery
and complicity close the group: the villain, in disguise, attempts to
deceive the victim to take possession of them or their belongings, and the
victim, unwittingly, helps the enemy by complying.

## Stage

No pieces cross the threshold yet -- this is the household before
departure, its rooms and its rule. What is staged is the family itself: the
ones who leave, the one who stays, the interdiction spoken between them,
and the villain's disguise once reconnaissance turns to trickery.

## Tension

The interdiction is broken without the warning a reader gets: the hero
rarely knows the rule was load-bearing until it is too late. The villain's
disguise is the plot's contraband outright -- deception dressed as
helpfulness -- and the victim's compliance with it is the unfulfilled test:
the household's rule asked for exactly the caution this plot spends its
whole action failing to keep.
