# Plot: Recognition

## Taxonomy

[The Folktale](play_folktale.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: morphology

## Cue

Two claimants stand where the tale only has room for one. The false hero's
account has had the run of the room since Return closed; the true hero,
still unrecognized, has said nothing yet.

## Action

Unfounded claims open the sequence: the false hero presses claims to the
feat the true hero actually accomplished. A difficult task is set -- often
by the princess's father, to sort the claim -- and solved, by the true hero
alone, using the very mark or agent that branding and provision left in
their keeping. Recognition follows: the true hero is identified, by the
mark, by the solved task, by the magical agent no false claimant can
produce. Exposure names the false hero's claim for what it is.
Transfiguration gives the hero a new appearance -- washed, dressed, restored
to a form the tale can finally reward. Punishment lands on the villain or
the false hero. Wedding, or ascension to a throne, closes both the sequence
and the play.

## Stage

Princess and father cross the threshold together here, the ones who set
and judge the difficult task; false hero and true hero are staged in direct
contrast for the first time in the tale. The mark from branding and the
agent from provision are produced as evidence -- pieces carried the whole
distance for exactly this scene.

## Tension

The difficult task is staged as a fair test, but its real work is forcing
the mark into the light -- without it, the tale has no way to arbitrate
between two claimants who otherwise tell the same story. Exposure is the
plot's payoff on every earlier plot's contraband at once: the interdiction
broken in Preparation, the lack opened in Complication, the disguise the
villain wore, the claim the false hero pressed -- all of it collapses the
moment the true hero is recognized, and the wedding is the tale's only way
of saying so cannot be undone.
