# Plot: Struggle

## Taxonomy

[The Folktale](play_folktale.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: morphology

## Cue

The magical agent is in hand; the road narrows from wandering to aim.
Guidance delivers the hero to where the tale has been pointing since
villainy first struck.

## Action

Guidance (or transference) is the function that gets the hero to the
location of the object of search -- flown, led, or carried there, often by
the very helper the donor sequence supplied. Struggle sets hero and villain
into direct combat or contest -- fight, duel, competition of wit. Branding
marks the hero in the course of it: a wound, a mark on the body, a ring or
a scarf received -- a physical sign no later imposter can produce on
demand. Victory defeats the villain outright. Liquidation closes the
group: the initial misfortune or lack, standing since Complication, is made
good -- the object of search obtained, the abducted returned, the spell
broken.

## Stage

Villain and hero meet at last, on ground the guidance function chose --
rarely home, usually the villain's own territory. The mark is staged onto
the hero's body directly, a piece that cannot be handed off or
counterfeited. The object of search, once liquidation lands, crosses back
into the hero's keeping.

## Tension

Victory looks like the tale's climax and reads as its resolution, but the
plot's contraband is that liquidation only settles the _initial_ lack -- it
answers Complication, not the tale's ending. The mark from branding sits
inert here, unclaimed evidence waiting for a use no one on stage
anticipates: it means nothing until a false hero forces the question of who
really earned it.
