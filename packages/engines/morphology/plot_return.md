# Plot: Return

## Taxonomy

[The Folktale](play_folktale.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: morphology

## Cue

The struggle is won and the object of search is in hand; by every measure
the tale could end here. Instead, the road that carried the hero out
doubles back, and what was won proves easier to carry than to keep.

## Action

Return simply reverses departure: the hero starts back over the same route
by which they arrived. Pursuit follows close behind: the villain, or what
the villain leaves in its place, gives chase -- transformed, flying,
pursuing at speed. Rescue answers it: the hero is saved from pursuit -- by
obstacles thrown up, by transformation, by the helper's intervention.
Unrecognized arrival closes the group: the hero reaches home or another
country unrecognized, often the same day a false hero arrives claiming the
very feat just accomplished.

## Stage

The helper is on stage again here, doing the work rescue needs; the magical
agent, if it can turn the road behind the hero into an obstacle, is spent
partly in this plot rather than saved whole for later. The false hero
crosses the threshold at the close, arriving at the same door the hero is
about to reach unseen.

## Tension

Unrecognized arrival is the plot's contraband made structural: the hero who
did everything is, for now, invisible, while a false hero's unearned claim
is the only account anyone on stage can hear. The gap between what
happened (staged across every prior plot) and what is known (nothing, yet)
is exactly the lie that Recognition exists to close.
