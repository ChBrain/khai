# Plot: Complication

## Taxonomy

[The Folktale](play_folktale.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: morphology

## Cue

Preparation's compliance has done its work; the household's guard is down.
The tale turns from waiting to injury: something is about to be taken, or
is already known to be missing.

## Action

Villainy is the function that gives the tale its motor: the villain causes
harm or injury to a member of the family (abduction, theft, banishment, a
spell cast). Where no villain acts, a lack stands in its place -- something
is simply wanted (a bride, a magical object) and its absence is felt.
Mediation is the misfortune made known: the hero learns of it, and is
dispatched or allowed to go. Counteraction is the hero's consent -- the
decision to act, the seeker agreeing to depart. Departure closes the group:
the hero physically leaves home.

## Stage

The dispatcher crosses into view here -- the figure who announces the lack
or hands the hero their charge -- alongside the hero, now named as such by
the act of consenting. No magical piece is carried yet; the hero departs
empty-handed, which is exactly what the donor sequence exists to correct.

## Tension

Villainy and lack are two faces of the same absence, and the plot never
quite says which one is truly driving the tale until later functions
resolve it. The hero's counteraction reads as free choice, but mediation
has already narrowed it to one: the unspoken pressure of a household that
cannot supply its own remedy is what counteraction "agrees" to.
