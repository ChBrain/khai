# Plot: The Donor Sequence

## Taxonomy

[The Folktale](play_folktale.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: morphology

## Cue

The road has already replaced the household; the hero travels without the
resource the tale still owes them. Somewhere on it, a figure is waiting who
has no stake in the villainy but every power to change its odds.

## Action

The first donor function is the test: the hero is confronted, interrogated,
attacked, or petitioned by a figure who will hand over a magical agent -- if
answered rightly. The hero's reaction follows: withstands or fails the
test, responds to the greeting, releases the captive, divides the food
fairly -- the coin the donor is actually pricing. Provision, the receipt of
the magical agent, closes the sequence: an object passed directly, or the
means to acquire or manufacture one, or a helper who binds itself to the
hero's service from this point on.

## Stage

The donor crosses the threshold first, met on the road rather than at
home; the (magical) helper crosses second, often as what the donor's gift
turns out to be. The piece proper -- the magical agent, whether object or
ally -- changes hands here and travels with the hero through every plot
that follows.

## Tension

The test rarely announces itself as a test; the donor's request looks like
an ordinary courtesy or an ordinary threat, and the hero's true examination
is failing to notice they are being examined at all. What the hero cannot
see is that the whole donor sequence exists to gate the struggle to come:
no reaction, no agent; no agent, and the villain downstream is simply
stronger than the hero can be alone.
