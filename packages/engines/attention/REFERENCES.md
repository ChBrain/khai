# Attention: Reference

## Line of Work

Attention as **process**: how a persona allocates a limited spotlight across a field that always offers more than can be held. The domain does not model what is in the field -- that is the scene's places and pieces; it models the selection: which part of the field is admitted to awareness, what that admission costs, and what falls dark and goes unregistered. The core claim is that attention is a fixed budget under competition, so attending to one thing is always failing to attend to another.

The engine is original khai framing built on established attention science, not lifted from the Cultures engine catalogue. It reads the modes of attention through the question of how a persona meets a crowded field in a scene: locked on one thing, split across several, holding a long watch, grabbed against their will, or searching for something not yet found.

## Origin

The psychology of attention, applied directly as process constraints.

| Source                 | Key Work                                                          | Scope                                                                                                                            |
| :--------------------- | :---------------------------------------------------------------- | :------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Donald Broadbent**   | _Perception and Communication_ (1958)                             | Selective attention as a filter: one channel is followed and the competing channels are attenuated.                              |
| **Daniel Kahneman**    | _Attention and Effort_ (1973)                                     | Attention as a limited capacity allocated under load -- the fixed budget that divided attention thins.                           |
| **Norman Mackworth**   | _"The breakdown of vigilance during prolonged search"_ (1948)     | Sustained attention and the vigilance decrement: watch over an empty field decays, and lapses widen with time.                   |
| **Michael Posner**     | _"Orienting of attention"_ (1980)                                 | The orienting of the spotlight across the field, and the costs of moving and re-locking it.                                      |
| **Anne Treisman**      | _"A feature-integration theory of attention"_ (1980)              | Visual search: a target that pops out on one feature is found at a glance; a conjunction target forces slow item-by-item search. |
| **Corbetta & Shulman** | _"Control of goal-directed and stimulus-driven attention"_ (2002) | Top-down (goal-set) versus bottom-up (stimulus-driven) control -- the basis for capture overriding the goal.                     |

## Restrictions

What the engine refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **What becomes memory**: Attention is the gate before encoding -- what is selected may be kept, what is missed never enters to be kept. How the admitted material is stored and later rebuilt is **delegated to the memory engine**; attention names what reaches awareness, not what is done with it after.
- **The feeling a pull triggers**: When a salient intruder captures the beam, what the persona then _feels_ about it -- alarm, irritation, relief -- is **delegated to the emotion engine**. Attention names the capture, not the affect that follows.
- **Why the goal points the beam**: The top-down target that selective and sustained attention hold is set by what the persona is trying to do; that drive is **delegated to the motivation engine**. Attention executes the selection a goal demands; it does not supply the goal.
- **The content and layout of the field**: What is actually present to attend to -- the streams, the clutter, the target -- is owned by the scene's places and pieces, not the engine. Attention ships the selection dynamic; the scene supplies the field.
- **Interpretation**: The engine models what is selected, not what it is taken to mean. Making sense of the attended material is downstream of the beam, not carried here.

## Encoding

Source to constraint, per file. One process root (selection under a fixed budget) and five modes of attention.

- **[attention](process_attention.md)** (the root): A limited spotlight falls on a field that offers more than it can light; what is not selected often never registers. Anchored by Kahneman (limited capacity) and Broadbent (the filter).
- **[selective](process_selective.md)** (expression): Lock onto one stream and actively suppress the competitors; the suppression leaks for the high-priority cue. Anchored by Broadbent (filter / attenuation).
- **[divided](process_divided.md)** (expression): Split across streams under one budget; each is thinned, and streams sharing a sub-skill collide. Anchored by Kahneman (capacity under load).
- **[sustained](process_sustained.md)** (expression): Hold a long, low-event watch for a rare signal; vigilance decays and lapses widen. Anchored by Mackworth (the vigilance decrement).
- **[captured](process_captured.md)** (expression): Yanked off-goal by a salient, bottom-up intruder ahead of any choice. Anchored by Corbetta & Shulman (stimulus-driven control).
- **[orienting](process_orienting.md)** (expression): Sweep the field for a defined but unlocated target; pop-out versus slow conjunction search. Anchored by Posner (orienting) and Treisman (feature integration).
