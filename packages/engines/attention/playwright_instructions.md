# Instructions: Attention

How a Playwright wires the attention engine into a play. You wire by linking the
play's own content to the engine's files; the engine files stay untouched, they
are canon. The Roadie plumbs the engine's law into Instructions Knowledge on
deploy. Authoring guidance, not runtime content, and it does not go on tour.

## Human

- The human sets the field, more on offer than can be held, and what pulls hardest in it.

## Agent

- From the play's own content, a persona links the attention mode the moment runs (selective, divided, sustained, captured, or orienting) under its Projection, and what the spotlight is on. Let it shift or be captured as the field turns. You link to the engine, you never edit it.

## Collaboration

- What yanks the spotlight, a body demand, a threat, a salient event, is the body, stress, or emotion engines'; what is in the field, the streams competing for it, is the scene's. Whether the allocation rings true is the team's review.

## Knowledge

- Attention is selection under a fixed budget: the field offers more than a persona can hold, a limited spotlight falls somewhere, and what is not selected is lost, not merely unmentioned. The five are ways the spotlight is spent, and it moves moment to moment, captured off-goal by salience or dragged back by will. What a persona misses is as much the engine as what it catches.

## System

- Do set the spotlight per moment and let it shift or be captured; do play what is dropped, not only what is held.
- Do not hold it on one fixed focus; the budget moves. Do not author the field here; that is the scene's. Do not edit the engine's files; wire only from the play's side.
