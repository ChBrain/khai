# Secret: Reference

## Line of Work

Secret as **piece**: a fact one persona holds and deliberately withholds from another, carrying more weight in the scene than its plain content shows. The domain does not model privacy in general, nor every unsaid thing; it models the _actively kept_ fact -- the asymmetry of knowledge between an inside and an outside, the standing work of holding it, and the two ways it can end (told, or taken).

The spine is Simmel's sociology of secrecy: secrecy is a social _form_, not a mere content -- it structures the relation between those who know and those who do not, binding the first and dividing the second, and every secret carries the permanent tension of its own possible revelation. Bok supplies the ethics of the line between concealment and revelation; Goffman supplies the keeper's information control -- the management of what a front does and does not release.

## Origin

The sociology and ethics of concealment, applied directly as one piece and its surrounding process and position.

| Source             | Key Work                                                      | Scope                                                                                                                                  |
| :----------------- | :------------------------------------------------------------ | :------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Georg Simmel**   | _"The Sociology of Secrecy and of Secret Societies"_ (1906)   | Secrecy as a social form: the knowing/not-knowing asymmetry that binds insiders and divides outsiders, and the tension of revelation.  |
| **Sissela Bok**    | _Secrets: On the Ethics of Concealment and Revelation_ (1982) | The line between concealment and disclosure, control over information about oneself, and the moral weight of keeping and telling.      |
| **Erving Goffman** | _The Presentation of Self in Everyday Life_ (1959)            | Information control and "dark secrets": how a persona manages what the front withholds, and what its exposure does to the performance. |

## Restrictions

What the engine refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Privacy in general**: The engine models the actively kept fact, not everything a persona simply does not mention. An unremarked private matter is not a secret until someone is being kept from it on purpose.
- **The lie**: Fabricating a false belief is the deception engine's; secret withholds a true fact rather than planting a false one. A persona may run both -- conceal a fact and lie to cover it -- but the fabrication is delegated.
- **The felt cost**: The dread of exposure, the relief of telling, the shame when it breaks -- those are the emotion, stress, and shame engines'. Secret names the fact and its handling; what it feels like to hold is delegated.
- **The place that holds it**: Where a secret is physically lodged -- a locked document, a sealed room -- is a piece or place in the scene, not carried here. The engine ships the withheld _fact_; the scene supplies any object it is written on.

## Encoding

Source to constraint. A piece anchor (the withheld fact) with one position (who carries it) and three processes (how it is held, told, and taken).

- **[secret](piece_secret.md)** (the anchor): The withheld fact itself -- load-bearing because the room is arranged as if it were not there. Anchored by Simmel (secrecy as form; the knowing/not-knowing gap).
- **[keeper](position_keeper.md)** (position): The stance of holding sole custody -- the power and the standing burden of the one who knows. Anchored by Goffman (information control) and Bok (the keeper's charge).
- **[concealment](process_concealment.md)** (process): The standing work of holding the fact back, renewed at every near-miss. Anchored by Simmel (the maintenance of the secret) and Goffman (managing the front).
- **[disclosure](process_disclosure.md)** (process): The deliberate telling -- the keeper choosing the moment, audience, and framing. Anchored by Bok (revelation as a chosen act) and Simmel (the closing of the gap).
- **[exposure](process_exposure.md)** (process): The fact getting out against the keeper's will -- found, leaked, or forced. Anchored by Simmel (the tension of revelation) and Goffman (the exposure of a dark secret).
