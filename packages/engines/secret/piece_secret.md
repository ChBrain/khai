# Piece: Secret

## Taxonomy

Information asymmetry / the sociology of secrecy (no khai file of its own yet).

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: secret

## Place

Not in a drawer -- in the gap between who knows and who does not.
A secret sits inside one persona and is withheld from another, and
that asymmetry is where it lives. It travels with the keeper into
every room, present in what they do not say. When a second persona
comes to know it, the secret does not move so much as the gap
closes, and the tie between the two changes shape around it.

## Load Bearing

Remove it and a relationship loses the thing it was quietly built
on: the trust that assumed full disclosure, the intimacy that was
really only partial, the alliance that held because one side did not
know. A secret is load-bearing precisely because the persons around
it have arranged themselves as if it were not there. What it holds
up is invisible until it fails.

## Apparent

On the surface, nothing -- and that is the point. A secret shows as
an absence: a subject the keeper steers around, a question answered
a beat too smoothly, a room the keeper will not fully enter. What is
visible is the shape of the withholding, never the fact. The better
the keeping, the less there is to see, until the seam under load.

## Yearbook

Every secret carries the history of its own keeping. It was made the
moment one persona decided another must not know; it accumulated
weight with every occasion it was nearly told and was not; it bound
whoever was let in and divided whoever was kept out. The longer it
is held, the more the world is arranged around it, and the more its
eventual telling -- or escape -- costs. A secret's age is measured
in the decisions built on top of it.
