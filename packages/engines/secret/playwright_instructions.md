# Instructions: Secret

How a Playwright wires the secret engine into a play. You wire by linking the
play's own content to the engine's files; the engine files stay untouched, they
are canon. The Roadie plumbs the engine's law into Instructions Knowledge on
deploy. Authoring guidance, not runtime content, and it does not go on tour.

## Human

- The human sets what is being kept, who holds it, and who is kept outside it.

## Agent

- From the play's own content, place the secret: link the keeper stance under the holding persona's Projection, and link the process the secret is in -- concealment while it holds, disclosure where the keeper chooses to tell, exposure where it breaks. Keep the fact itself in the play's content, not in the engine. Let the asymmetry do the work across plots: the persons arranged as if the fact were not there, until the seam gives. You link to the engine, you never edit it.

## Collaboration

- The dread of exposure and the relief of telling are emotion's and stress'; the shame when it breaks is shame's; a fabrication built to cover it is deception's; the standing the fact would cost is hierarchy's; the object it is written on is a piece or place in the scene. Whether the secret truly bears the weight the plot needs is the team's review.

## Knowledge

- A secret is a fact one persona holds and withholds from another on purpose. It is load-bearing: the persons around it have arranged themselves as if it were not there, so it holds up a trust, an intimacy, or an alliance that its telling -- or its escape -- would change. It binds whoever is let in and divides whoever is kept out. Read it as an absence the keeper steers around, and read its two ends: disclosure, where the keeper chooses the moment; exposure, where the world does.

## System

- Do link the keeper under Projection and name the process the secret is in; do let the arrangement built over the gap show before the seam gives; do let exposure land on whatever the concealment was holding up.
- Do not fabricate a false belief here; that is deception's. Do not author the felt cost; that is emotion, stress, and shame's. Do not edit the engine's files; wire only from the play's side.
