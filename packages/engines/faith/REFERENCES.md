# Faith: Reference

## Line of Work

Faith as **position**: the persona's standing orientation toward a sacred or
transcendent order. The domain does not model a single doctrine held true, nor
the epistemic work of testing a claim against evidence; it models the resting
stance a persona carries toward an order it takes to be there, unseen, and
toward its own good as bound up in a harmonious relation to that order. The
orientation is prior to any tenet: it is what a persona leans on before, and
independent of, whatever it can state and defend.

The spine is the psychology of religiosity as a lived, orienting stance rather
than a set of propositions. William James read faith as the sense of an unseen
order and of the self's supreme good as lying in a harmonious adjustment
thereto -- a total reaction upon life, not an argument won. Kenneth Pargament
read religion and spirituality as an ongoing search for significance in ways
tied to the sacred, a coping and orienting resource a person returns to under
strain, not a claim settled once. Faith, in this domain, is that standing
search and that sense of order, carried as a position -- something a persona
holds and is held by, not something it is currently proving.

## Origin

The psychology of religious experience and religious coping, read for the
standing orientation rather than any doctrinal content.

| Source                | Key Work                                       | Scope                                                                                                                                   |

## Restrictions

What the engine refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Holding a claim true and revising it under disconfirmation**: Faith is the
  standing orientation, not the epistemic process of testing a proposition
  against evidence and updating, defending, or abandoning it. That settlement
  -- update, rationalize, deny, convert, suspend -- is delegated to the
  **belief** engine. A persona may hold faith toward the order and still run a
  belief process on any single doctrinal claim about it.
- **The sacred object**: Faith is the orientation toward the order, not the
  concrete thing that carries its charge -- the relic, the site, the object set
  apart and protected. That is delegated to the **totem** engine.
- **The collective rite**: Faith is a standing, carried stance, not the
  bounded, threshold-crossing collective action that enacts or renews it. The
  shared ritual is delegated to the **ritual** engine.
- **Whether life matters**: Faith orients toward a sacred order; whether the
  persona's life itself is felt as mattering, searched for, or adrift is
  delegated to the **meaning** engine. Faith can anchor a sense of meaning, but
  the two are not the same position.
- **The response to vastness**: The accommodation a persona makes when it
  meets something that exceeds its current frame -- a vastness that does not
  yet fit -- is delegated to the **awe** engine. Faith is the settled
  orientation that can follow such an encounter, not the encounter itself.
