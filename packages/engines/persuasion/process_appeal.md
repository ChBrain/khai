# Process: Appeal

## Taxonomy

[Persuasion](process_persuasion.md), the process this phase opens.

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: persuasion

## Initiated by

The persuader's decision to act on their intention. The appeal is the persuader's move: the message they construct, the route they aim for, the cues they deploy alongside the argument.

## Direction

Toward the target's attention. An appeal that is not attended to cannot persuade. The persuader selects what to say, how to frame it, which principles of influence to activate -- reciprocity, commitment and consistency, social proof, authority, liking, scarcity -- and whether to lead with argument quality (central route) or with peripheral cues.

## Lever

Source credibility and likeability. A persuader the target trusts and likes activates peripheral processing even when the target is capable of deep processing. A persuader with low credibility must depend on argument quality alone, because cue-based processing will discount the message regardless. The wise persuader reads the target's motivation and ability before choosing the route.

## Echo

A message is in front of the target. What the target does with it belongs to reception and processing.
