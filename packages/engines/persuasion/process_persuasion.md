# Process: Persuasion

## Taxonomy

A process. How a persona attempts to change what another believes, values, or does. Root of the persuasion process tree.

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: persuasion

## Initiated by

The persuader's intention to change something in the target: a belief, an attitude, a value, or a behavior. Persuasion is distinct from coercion in that the target is addressed as someone capable of being moved by reasons, appeals, or cues.

## Direction

Toward attitude or behavior change in the target. Whether change is achieved depends on what the target processes and how. What the persuader controls is the appeal; what happens next is the target's.

## Lever

The processing route. A target who is motivated and able to think carefully about the message will process centrally: argument quality determines the outcome. A target with low motivation or ability will process peripherally: cues, heuristics, and source characteristics determine the outcome. Central-route change is more durable and resistant to counter-persuasion than peripheral-route change.

## Echo

The target's belief, attitude, or behavior has shifted -- or has not. If change occurred via the central route, it is more likely to persist and guide future action. If via the peripheral route, it is more susceptible to decay and to reversal by a competing message.
