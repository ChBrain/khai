# Persuasion: Reference

## Line of Work

Persuasion as **process**: how a persona attempts to change what another believes, values, or does. The domain models four phases -- appeal (the persuader constructs and delivers a message aimed at a route), reception (the target's motivation and ability determine which processing route engages), processing (the target evaluates argument quality or relies on heuristics), outcome (the change, or its absence, settles into belief or behavior) -- and what governs each phase.

The spine is dual-process theory of persuasion: attitude change is not one thing but two, depending on how much cognitive work the target invests. Petty and Cacioppo supply the Elaboration Likelihood Model (ELM) -- the central route (argument quality) versus the peripheral route (cues and heuristics); change via the central route is more durable. Chaiken supplies the Heuristic-Systematic Model (HSM) -- parallel architecture, same insight: systematic processing produces more durable change than heuristic processing. Cialdini supplies the six principles of influence that function as peripheral cues -- reciprocity, commitment and consistency, social proof, authority, liking, scarcity -- and explains why they work by exploiting the heuristics of everyday judgment.

## Restrictions

What the engine refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **The emotional state of the target or persuader during the attempt**: How it feels to be persuaded against one's resistance, or to be believed -- these belong to the **emotion** and **mood** engines.
- **The negotiation frame**: When persuasion occurs inside a negotiation, the overarching process belongs to the **negotiation** engine. Persuasion is one move within the invention and exploration phases of negotiation.
- **Power-backed directives**: When the persuader can compel compliance (coercion, legitimate authority with enforcement), the move is not persuasion in the ELM sense -- the target is not being addressed as someone capable of being moved by reasons. Power dynamics belong to the **power** engine.
- **How the persuader's social standing amplifies or undercuts source credibility**: The persuader's authority and likeability are peripheral cues, but what grants those cues -- status, expertise, relational trust -- belongs to the **status**, **capital**, and **trust** engines.
