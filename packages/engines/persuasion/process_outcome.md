# Process: Outcome

## Taxonomy

[Persuasion](process_persuasion.md), the process this phase closes.

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: persuasion

## Initiated by

The target's processing verdict settling into an attitude or behavior. Outcome is not the persuader's move; it is what the target carries forward.

## Direction

Toward a changed or unchanged belief, attitude, or behavior. Change achieved via the central route is more durable: the target has done the cognitive work and the change is integrated into their existing beliefs. Change via the peripheral route is more fragile: it can decay with time or reverse when a competing message arrives with a stronger cue.

## Lever

Behavioral commitment. A target who acts on a new attitude -- even a small act -- is more likely to hold the attitude. Commitment locks in the change and activates consistency motivation: the target's sense of themselves as someone who holds this belief. The persuader who can elicit early behavioral commitment converts a fragile peripheral change into something more durable.

## Echo

The persuasion has run. The target believes, values, or does something differently -- or does not. The durability of what changed depends on the route it arrived by.
