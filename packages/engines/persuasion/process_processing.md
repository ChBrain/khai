# Process: Processing

## Taxonomy

[Persuasion](process_persuasion.md), the process this phase constitutes.

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: persuasion

## Initiated by

The target's engagement with the message beyond initial reception. Processing is the work of evaluation: is the argument strong, are the cues trustworthy, does the message fit what the target already believes?

## Direction

Toward a verdict. On the central route, the target evaluates argument quality: are the reasons good, is the evidence credible, does the conclusion follow? On the peripheral route, the target relies on heuristics: is the source an expert, do other people agree, does the message feel right?

## Lever

Counterargument. A target processing centrally will generate their own objections to a weak argument; a persuader who cannot anticipate and answer those objections will fail even with a well-crafted appeal. A target processing peripherally does not generate counterarguments spontaneously -- they accept or reject on cue. But peripheral acceptance is fragile: a competing cue can reverse it without any argument at all.

## Echo

The target has evaluated the message. Their verdict -- positive, negative, ambivalent -- feeds the outcome. What they carry out of processing is not yet the changed attitude; that settles in the outcome phase.
