# Regulatory Focus: Reference

## Line of Work

Regulatory focus as **position**: the persona's resting orientation toward how
a goal is pursued. The domain does not model which goal is chosen, the choice
made under risk, or the force that mobilizes effort toward a goal at all; it
models the baseline strategic inclination carried into any pursuit, read on a
single axis -- whether the goal is held as a gain to reach for (promotion) or a
loss to guard against (prevention). The two orientations differ in what they
are sensitive to (gains and non-gains versus losses and non-losses) and in the
strategic means they favor (eager approach versus vigilant caution).

## Origin

Regulatory focus theory, from the psychology of self-regulation and
motivation.

| Source              | Key Work                                                                                                                      | Scope                                                                                                                                                                                                                 |
| ------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **E. Tory Higgins** | _"Beyond Pleasure and Pain"_ (American Psychologist, 1997)                                                                    | Founding statement of regulatory focus theory: promotion focus (gains vs non-gains, eager strategic means) and prevention focus (losses vs non-losses, vigilant strategic means) as distinct self-regulatory systems. |
| **E. Tory Higgins** | _"Promotion and Prevention: Regulatory Focus as a Motivational Principle"_ (Advances in Experimental Social Psychology, 1998) | Extends the theory: strategic eagerness and vigilance as means, regulatory fit, and focus as both chronic disposition and situationally induced state.                                                                |

## Restrictions

What the engine refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **The specific goal pursued** (the target): Regulatory focus orients toward
  how a goal is pursued; which goal is chosen and held in the first place
  **delegates to the goal engine**. Regulatory focus sets the manner; goal
  sets the object.
- **The choice under risk** (the pick): Regulatory focus sets which kind of
  error a persona works hardest to avoid; the actual weighing and picking
  among options under uncertainty **delegates to the decision engine**.
  Regulatory focus tilts the weighing; decision does the choosing.
- **What moves the persona to act at all** (the drive): Regulatory focus holds
  the strategic inclination once a pursuit is underway; the force that
  mobilizes effort toward a goal in the first place **delegates to the
  motivation engine**. Regulatory focus shapes how the persona pursues;
  motivation supplies whether it pursues at all.
- **The felt eagerness or vigilance** (the spike): Regulatory focus is a
  standing orientation, not the discrete cheer or agitation that fires when a
  gain lands or a loss looms. The sudden, cause-bound feeling **delegates to
  the emotion engine**. Regulatory focus is the slant the emotion fires
  through, not the strike.

## Encoding

Source to constraint, per file.

- **[regulatory-focus](position_regulatory_focus.md)** (the anchor):
  regulatory focus as a resting orientation toward how a goal is pursued, a
  baseline on a single axis -- gain to reach for or loss to guard against --
  carried continuously rather than run as a process. Anchored by Higgins
  (1997), the founding statement of promotion and prevention as distinct
  self-regulatory systems.
- **[promotion](position_promotion.md)** (expression): the goal held as a
  gain, sensitivity to the presence or absence of gains, strategic eagerness
  that risks an error of commission for a shot at a hit. Anchored by Higgins
  (1997, 1998) on promotion focus and eager means.
- **[prevention](position_prevention.md)** (expression): the goal held as a
  loss to guard, sensitivity to the presence or absence of loss, strategic
  vigilance that risks an error of omission to secure a correct rejection.
  Anchored by Higgins (1997, 1998) on prevention focus and vigilant means.
