# Instructions: Regulatory Focus

How a Playwright wires the regulatory-focus engine into a play. You wire by
linking the play's own content to the engine's files; the engine files stay
untouched, they are canon. The Roadie plumbs the engine's law into
Instructions Knowledge on deploy. Authoring guidance, not runtime content, and
it does not go on tour.

## Human

- The human sets the goal the persona is pursuing, and what has shaped the
  inclination they bring to it.

## Agent

- From the play's own content, every persona carries its regulatory focus
  under Projection (promotion or prevention). Let it tint which risks read as
  worth taking and which errors the persona works hardest to avoid, and drift
  slowly across the run, never snap beat to beat. You link to the engine, you
  never edit it.

## Collaboration

- Which goal is being pursued is the goal engine's; the actual weighing and
  picking under uncertainty is decision's; the force that moves the persona to
  pursue at all is motivation's; the felt spike of eagerness or agitation when
  a gain lands or a loss looms is emotion's. Whether the orientation rings
  true is the team's review.

## Knowledge

- Regulatory focus is the persona's resting orientation toward how a goal is
  pursued, a baseline on a single axis, the goal held as a gain to reach for
  or a loss to guard against. It is the slant, not the goal: no persona
  pursues by a neutral method, the orientation colours which risks look worth
  taking and which errors get worked hardest against, and it moves on the
  timescale of a stretch, not a beat.

## System

- Do give every persona a regulatory focus (the law admits no exception); do
  let it colour which errors the persona guards against and drift slowly
  across the run.
- Do not play it as the momentary feel of a gain landing or a loss looming;
  that is emotion. Do not author which goal is being pursued here; that is the
  goal engine's. Do not edit the engine's files; wire only from the play's
  side.
