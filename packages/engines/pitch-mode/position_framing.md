# Position: Framing

## Taxonomy

[Pitch Mode](position_pitch_mode.md), the expression in which the persona is establishing the frame: the context, the problem, or the opportunity that makes the pitch necessary. Framing comes before the solution; it is the work of making the other see the situation the pitch is addressed to.

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: pitch-mode

## Has

The persona holds the task of context-setting: making the other see what the persona sees as the situation that needs addressing. Klaff's analysis of frame control is precise here: the frame is the perspective the pitcher offers, and the pitch succeeds or fails depending on whether the other accepts that frame or imposes their own. What the persona in framing mode has is the frame itself -- the story of the problem or opportunity -- and the orientation to install it in the other before moving to the solution.

## Orders

Set the frame before offering the fix. Framing mode demands that the persona resist moving to the solution or the ask before the other is oriented to the problem. The person who jumps to the offer before the other understands the problem is pitching into a vacuum. The order is: construct the situation the pitch is addressing, make it vivid and specific, and hold there until the other is in the same view.

## Loses

The other's agreement on the situation. If the other does not accept the frame -- if they see the problem differently, or do not see it as a problem at all -- the framing expression has not succeeded and the pitch cannot advance. The persona who moves forward without the other's agreement on the frame has left framing mode but not completed it.

## Drives

Toward the other's orientation to the problem. The framing persona is driven by the need for the other to arrive at the view from which the pitch's offer becomes obvious or necessary. The frame is complete when the other is asking, at least implicitly, what can be done about this situation.
