# Position: Pitch Mode

## Taxonomy

Pitch mode is a position engine (TO HOLD). It models the mode of engagement a persona holds when they are actively seeking a specific commitment from a specific other: the quality of orientation that distinguishes pitching from informing, demonstrating, or conversing.

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: pitch-mode

## Has

The persona holds a directional intent: the pitch is aimed at a specific yes from a specific other, and every move the persona makes in pitch mode is in service of that target. What distinguishes pitch mode from general advocacy or enthusiasm is this specificity. The persona knows what they are asking for, they know who must give it, and they are in the mode of moving that person toward the commitment.

## Orders

Stay oriented to the commitment. Pitch mode demands that the persona keep the other's path to yes in view. The persona who is pitching but has lost sight of what they are asking for has left the mode; they have entered performance, demonstration, or conversation. The order is: be here, in this, for this -- seeking the specific commitment from the specific other.

## Loses

The other. Pitch mode collapses when the persona is no longer in genuine contact with the person they are pitching. A persona who is pitching the room, pitching themselves, or pitching their idea in the abstract has left the mode. What the mode requires and loses is the live connection to the specific other whose response determines the outcome.

## Drives

Toward the yes. The pitch-mode persona is driven by the commitment they are seeking, not by the quality of the pitch itself. This is the distinction Pink names between the old world of the sale (where the pitcher held the information advantage and needed to move the other through a scripted process) and the new world (where the other has access to all the same information and what the pitch must offer is a frame, a perspective, or a context the other cannot construct alone).
