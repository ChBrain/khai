# Instructions: Pitch Mode

How a Playwright wires the pitch-mode engine into a play. You wire by linking the
play's own content to the engine's files; the engine files stay untouched, they
are canon. The Roadie plumbs the engine's law into Instructions Knowledge on
deploy. Authoring guidance, not runtime content, and it does not go on tour.

## Human

- The human names what the persona is pitching, who they are pitching to, what commitment they are seeking, what the other's likely objections or hesitations are, and which expression the persona is currently in (framing, building, landing, or recovering).

## Agent

- From the play's own content, a persona links the pitch-mode expression they are in to their Projection. The expression is what the persona is doing to move the other toward the commitment -- not what the persona feels about the pitch, and not the content of the pitch itself.

## Collaboration

- The persuasion engine holds the general mechanisms of social influence across contexts. The negotiation engine holds the process of reaching agreement once the commitment is conditionally offered. The emotion and mood engines hold the persona's felt state during the pitch. The status-move engine holds the moment-by-moment status transactions within the pitch interaction.

## Knowledge

- Pitch mode is the mode of engagement a persona holds when they are actively seeking a specific commitment from a specific other. It is not enthusiasm for an idea, not advocacy in general, and not conversation about a topic. The persona knows what they are asking for and from whom, and every move they make is in service of moving the other toward that commitment. The engine models four expressions: framing (the persona is establishing the context or problem that makes the commitment necessary; the other must accept the frame before the offer can land), building (the persona is assembling the evidence, narrative, and value that make the yes rational and desirable; building is calibrated to this specific other), landing (the persona makes the specific ask clearly and then holds the space; they do not soften, qualify, or immediately manage the other's response), and recovering (the persona handles the other's resistance while maintaining the path to yes; recovery re-establishes the frame rather than entering the other's alternative frame).

## System

- Pitch mode is not enthusiasm. The persona in pitch mode is oriented to a specific commitment from a specific other; a persona who is excited about their idea but not seeking a specific yes from a specific person is not in pitch mode.
- Do not conflate landing with persuading. Landing is the act of naming the specific commitment and holding the space. The persuasion has happened in framing and building; landing is the moment the pitch reaches its target.
- Do not let recovery become retreat. The recovering persona addresses the objection without abandoning the frame. A persona who lowers the ask, narrows the commitment, or accepts the other's reframing of the problem has retreated, not recovered.
- Do not edit the engine's files; wire only from the play's side.
