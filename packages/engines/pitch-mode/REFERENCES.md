# Pitch Mode: Reference

## Line of Work

Pitch mode as **position**: the mode of engagement a persona holds when they are actively seeking a specific commitment from a specific other. The domain models the anchor (what pitch mode is; what it has, orders, loses, and drives) and four expressions -- framing (establishing the context or problem that makes the pitch necessary), building (constructing the case that makes the commitment rational and desirable), landing (making the specific ask), and recovering (handling resistance while maintaining the path to yes) -- and what each expression requires and forecloses.

The spine is Klaff's analysis of frame control: the pitcher's primary task is to establish and hold the frame -- the perspective from which the pitch's offer becomes obvious or necessary -- and to prevent the other from imposing an alternative frame that positions the pitcher as supplicant. Klaff's neurofinance account of the pitch as addressed to the other's threat-detection and reward systems supplies the practical constraint: the pitch must be novel enough to trigger attention and safe enough not to trigger threat. Pink's account of the new pitch environment extends this: in a world where buyers have access to the same information as sellers, the pitch that survives is not the one that transmits information but the one that offers a frame, a perspective, or a clarity the other cannot construct alone. Cialdini's principles of influence supply the mechanisms available within the building expression: social proof, authority, scarcity, commitment, reciprocity, and liking.

## Restrictions

What the engine refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **The persona's enthusiasm for the idea**: Whether the persona is excited about what they are pitching belongs to the **emotion** and **motivation** engines. Pitch mode models the orientation to the commitment, not the persona's feeling about the pitch content.
- **The substance of the pitch itself**: What the persona is pitching -- the idea, the product, the proposal -- is play content, not engine content. The pitch-mode engine models the mode of engagement; the content of the pitch lives in the play's own material.
- **Persuasion as a general social process**: The mechanisms of persuasion across all contexts belong to the **persuasion** engine. Pitch mode models the specific orientation of actively seeking a commitment; it is not a substitute for the persuasion engine.
- **Negotiation over the terms of commitment**: Once the other has signaled conditional interest and the conversation shifts to terms, the **negotiation** engine holds that process. Pitch mode ends when the parties are negotiating; the ask has been made and received.

## Encoding

Source to constraint, per file.

- **[pitch-mode](position_pitch_mode.md)** (the anchor): The mode of engagement as a position: what it has, orders, loses, and drives. Anchored by Klaff (2011) -- the frame as the mode's operative unit; Pink (2012) -- the commitment-specific orientation of the modern pitch.
- **[framing](position_framing.md)** (expression): Establishing the context or problem that makes the commitment necessary. Anchored by Klaff (2011) -- frame control as the pitcher's primary task; the frame as the perspective the other must accept before the offer can land.
- **[building](position_building.md)** (expression): Constructing the case that makes the commitment rational and desirable. Anchored by Cialdini (2001) -- the six principles as the mechanisms of case construction; Pink (2012) -- building calibrated to this other, not to the general case.
- **[landing](position_landing.md)** (expression): Making the specific ask and holding the space for the response. Anchored by Pink (2012) -- the ask as collaborative invitation; the restraint that follows the named commitment.
- **[recovering](position_recovering.md)** (expression): Handling resistance while maintaining the path to yes. Anchored by Klaff (2011) -- re-establishing the frame as the first task of recovery; the distinction between addressing an objection and abandoning the frame.
