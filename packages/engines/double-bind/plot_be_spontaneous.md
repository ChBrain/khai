# Plot: Be Spontaneous

## Taxonomy

[Double Bind](plot_double_bind.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: double-bind

## Cue

The binder wants a feeling demonstrated, not a task performed: affection offered freely, desire that looks unprompted, obedience that reads as choice. The room carries the ordinary ask of any intimate bond, that the other party want this too, tightened past what asking can honestly produce.

## Action

The binder utters an injunction whose content is unbidden action itself ("be spontaneous," "want what I want, freely," "love me because you choose to"). The victim, hearing an order, moves to satisfy it, and produces a gesture aimed at spontaneity. Bateson's point sits exactly here: the command specifies a class of act (the unbidden) at a logical level that a following-of-the-command can never occupy, so the visible compliance and the content commanded are different logical types colliding in the same act.

## Stage

The binder stands over the exchange, watching for the quality the order named; the victim performs the requested gesture, hand extended, word spoken, alone in producing something that must look sourceless while everyone present can trace its source to the command just given.

## Tension

Whatever the victim shows is read against the injunction that produced it: a demonstrably obedient act is disqualified from being the spontaneous one the binder asked for, so satisfying the order voids it in the same motion. The victim cannot point at the paradox, since naming it is itself an act taken on instruction, and equally not the spontaneous thing demanded, so metacommunication about the injunction is foreclosed by the very injunction it would comment on.
