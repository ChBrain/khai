# Plot: Double Bind

## Taxonomy

A communicational trap (Bateson).

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: double-bind

## Cue

An intense relationship the victim cannot leave: parent and child, lovers, patient and institution, any bond whose stakes are survival, not preference. The tie is old enough and close enough that walking away is not a live option; the air in the room is the ordinary weight of that dependency, not yet a trap.

## Action

The binder issues a primary injunction, an order at one logical level ("come here," "obey," "love me"), and the victim moves to comply. Underneath it runs a secondary injunction, contradicting the first at a more abstract level (the frame, the tone, the relationship itself signals the opposite of the words), so satisfying one defies the other. Every visible move the victim makes, complying, refusing, asking which order to follow, collides with a level the words never named.

## Stage

[Be Spontaneous](plot_be_spontaneous.md) and [Disqualification](plot_disqualification.md) cross into the room as the binder's two faces, one spoken, one carried in the frame; the victim stands alone between them, holding no ally who could read both levels at once. Any witness present who could name the pattern from outside is bound by [No Metacomment](plot_no_metacomment.md) as tightly as the victim is.

## Tension

A tertiary injunction, never spoken, forbids the victim from leaving the field and forbids naming or asking about the contradiction itself. That silence is the bind's engine: not the conflict of orders alone, which a question could dissolve, but the ban on the question. Every response the victim can make, comply, refuse, comment, reads as a further move inside the trap, so the contradiction that produces the pain stays permanently off the record, and no reply can be right.
