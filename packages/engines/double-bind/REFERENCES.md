# Double Bind: Reference

## Line of Work

The double bind as **plot**: Gregory Bateson's communicational trap, cast as the casting of forces that makes a collision inevitable before a word is spoken. The domain does not model a diagnosis (that is a clinician's read of a person over time); it models the structure of the trap itself, the relationship, the injunctions, and the prohibition, that leaves every response wrong.

The spine is Bateson's theory of logical types applied to communication: a message and a message about that message (metacommunication) occupy different levels, and a system that forces those levels into open contradiction, while forbidding comment on the contradiction, produces a bind no correct response can escape.

## Origin

Communication theory and cybernetics, applied directly as the plot's Action and Tension: Bateson's double bind and the paradoxical-communication tradition it founded.

## Restrictions

What the engine refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Clinical diagnosis**: The engine models the communicational structure of the trap, not a pathology claim about a person. Whether a scene's bind produces or reflects a clinical condition is delegated entirely; the engine stops at the trap's shape.
- **The specific relationship**: The engine ships the trap's irreducible ingredients, not a named family, couple, or institution. Which relationship carries the bind, and its cultural skin, is the production's to cast.
- **Resolution**: The engine does not model how a bind is broken (therapy, reframing, a witness who finally speaks). It models why, from inside the trap, no response wins; escape is a different plot the production supplies.

## Encoding

Source to constraint. A plot anchor (the bind as a whole) with three ingredient plots, each casting one of the necessary elements that makes the trap inescapable.

- **[Double Bind](plot_double_bind.md)** (the anchor): The trap as such, an intense relationship the victim cannot leave, holding a primary injunction, a contradicting secondary injunction, and a tertiary injunction against escape or comment. Anchored by Bateson, Jackson, Haley & Weakland (1956).
- **[Be Spontaneous](plot_be_spontaneous.md)**: The paradox of a command whose content demands unbidden action, so obeying it disqualifies the very thing commanded. Anchored by Watzlawick, Beavin & Jackson (1967).
- **[Disqualification](plot_disqualification.md)**: The conflict of levels, words carrying one message while tone, posture, or frame carry the opposite, so no reading of the message is safe. Anchored by Bateson (1972), logical types and metacommunication.
- **[No Metacomment](plot_no_metacomment.md)**: The prohibition on naming or leaving the contradiction, the ingredient that turns a paradox into a trap. Anchored by Bateson, Jackson, Haley & Weakland (1956), the tertiary injunction.
