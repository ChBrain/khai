# Plot: Disqualification

## Taxonomy

[Double Bind](plot_double_bind.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: double-bind

## Cue

A room where the words on offer are warm and the body in the room is not: the mother who says "come here, dear" while her posture stiffens and her arms do not open. Nothing yet has been said about the mismatch; it sits underneath the greeting as ordinary atmosphere.

## Action

The binder issues the primary injunction in words, an invitation to approach, while a second message runs on another channel at the same moment, tone, posture, timing, that signals the opposite. Bateson's conflict of logical types is exactly this: the verbal message and the message about the message (the metacommunication that frames how the words are meant) disagree, so no single reading of "come here" is available. The victim moves to obey the words and meets the frame that punishes the approach.

## Stage

The binder holds both channels at once, voice and body speaking against each other; the victim crosses the distance the words invited, carrying no way to check which channel was the real order before the crossing is already made.

## Tension

Approaching is read as ignoring what the body plainly signaled; holding back is read as ignoring what the words plainly said. Both readings are available and each punishes the choice made on the other, so no response to the message is safe. The victim cannot ask which channel to trust, since naming the split would require commenting on the metacommunication itself, and that comment is the ground [No Metacomment](plot_no_metacomment.md) forbids.
