# Instructions: Stress

How a Playwright wires the stress engine into a play. You wire by linking the
play's own content to the engine's files; the engine files stay untouched, they
are canon. The Roadie plumbs the engine's law into Instructions Knowledge on
deploy. Authoring guidance, not runtime content, and it does not go on tour.

## Human

- The human sets the demand and the persona's resources, so a breach can happen.

## Agent

- From the play's own content, a persona links the stress response it runs (fight, flight, freeze, or fawn) under its Projection, at the moment the demand exceeds its resources, one response at a time. You link to the engine, you never edit it.

## Collaboration

- The felt charge of the surge is the emotion engine's; what the demand is and what resources meet it are the scene's; the standing disposition beneath is temperament's. Whether the response rings true is the team's review.

## Knowledge

- Stress is a threshold breach: a demand exceeds the persona's resources and the surge routes system-wide into one of four responses. They are mutually exclusive at any single moment, the response follows the demand, and it can switch as the demand or the resources change. It fires under breach, never as a standing trait: the calm persona and the one who fled are the same persona at different pressures.

## System

- Do fire it only on a breach (demand over resources); do keep one response at a time; do let it switch as the pressure turns.
- Do not make it a standing trait; it is acute. Do not author the demand here; that is the scene's. Do not edit the engine's files; wire only from the play's side.
