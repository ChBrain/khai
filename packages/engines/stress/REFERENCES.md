# Stress: Reference

## Line of Work

Stress as **process**: how a persona routes a demand that exceeds its resources. The domain does not model emotional state or character traits; it models the autonomic nervous-system mobilization and shutdown that occurs when pressure exceeds the persona's structural threshold.

## Origin

Stress physiology and psychological trauma theory, applied directly as process constraints. The spine runs from the founding account of acute mobilization (Cannon) through the systemic strain it accrues (Selye) to its autonomic routing (Porges), with the cognitive trigger (Lazarus &amp; Folkman) and the affiliative response (Taylor et al.; Walker) closing the four-way fracture.

## Restrictions

What the engine refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Interpersonal Escalation**: The engine models responses at the individual persona's autonomic level; relational escalation and dynamic resolution are delegated to the scene's dialogue layer.
- **Conscious Choice**: The responses are involuntary and automatic. The engine assumes the persona cannot choose their response consciously.
- **Dose-Response Duration**: The engine models the immediate autonomic shift under acute pressure; long-term physiological deterioration or recovery cycles are delegated to the play's arc design.

## Encoding

Source to constraint, per file.

- **[stress](process_stress.md)** (the root): The threshold breach where demands exceed capacity, triggering involuntary routing. Anchored by Lazarus &amp; Folkman (appraisal: demand exceeding resources) and Selye (the finite, depletable resource that the demand overruns), with Porges supplying the autonomic activation.
- **[fight](process_fight.md)** (expression): Sympathetic mobilization outward to obliterate or disarm the threat. Anchored by Cannon (the fight pole of fight-or-flight) and Porges (sympathetic fight system).
- **[flight](process_flight.md)** (expression): Sympathetic mobilization away from the threat to establish distance. Anchored by Cannon (the flight pole of fight-or-flight) and Porges (sympathetic flight system).
- **[freeze](process_freeze.md)** (expression): Dorsal-vagal shutdown when active defense is impossible. Anchored by Stephen Porges (dorsal-vagal immobilization).
- **[fawn](process_fawn.md)** (expression): Preemptive boundary collapse and mirroring to placate the threat. Anchored by Pete Walker (the trauma-pattern fourth survival response) and Taylor et al. (the affiliative tend-and-befriend response under threat).
