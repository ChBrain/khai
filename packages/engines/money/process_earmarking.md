# Process: Earmarking

## Taxonomy

[Money](piece_money.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: money

## Initiated by

People refuse money's own logic and mark a sum as special. The
threshold is the moment a quantity stops being interchangeable in
practice -- this is the rent money, that is the gift, this is not to be
touched, that is dirty and cannot be kept -- even though every unit is
physically identical.

## Direction

Toward meaning. Where conversion strips quality into quantity,
earmarking runs the other way: it dresses a fungible sum back in
significance, tying it to its source (wages, winnings, inheritance,
charity) and its destiny (the child's schooling, the church, the
funeral) so that spending it wrongly feels like a betrayal, not a
mistake.

## Lever

The abstract fungibility of money meeting the social need to make it
mean. The force is people's insistence that not all dollars are the
same dollar; the resistance is that, to the market, they are. Remove
the marking and money is pure quantity again; remove the fungibility
and it was never money, only a specific gift.

## Echo

A sum earmarked carries a rule about itself into every later decision:
it may be spent, saved, or given only in certain ways without cost to
the spender's standing or conscience. The next process inherits money
that is quantity and meaning at once -- the market treating it as
interchangeable, the people holding it treating it as anything but.
