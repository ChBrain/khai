# Money: Reference

## Line of Work

Money as **piece**: the general equivalent -- a token that converts any value into any other and stores it as pure quantity, carrying a power out of proportion to its material. The domain does not model debt (the owing that binds two parties -- that is the debt engine's) or the felt greed and security of wealth (emotion's and desire's); it models the _token_ -- what holding it confers, and the two opposed pulls of making value abstract and making a sum special.

The spine is the money-form: Marx's universal equivalent, the commodity that all others measure themselves against, and Simmel's account of money as the perfect means that dissolves qualities into quantity and becomes an end in itself. Against that abstraction Zelizer sets the social meaning of money: in practice people refuse fungibility, earmarking sums by source and destination so that not all dollars are the same dollar.

## Origin

The philosophy, economics, and sociology of money, applied directly as one piece with its holder and its two pulls.

| Source              | Key Work                                   | Scope                                                                                                                                   |
| :------------------ | :----------------------------------------- | :-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Georg Simmel**    | _The Philosophy of Money_ (1900)           | Money as the pure means become an end; the abstraction and quantification of value, and what a fully monetised world does to qualities. |
| **Viviana Zelizer** | _The Social Meaning of Money_ (1994)       | Earmarking: how people mark fungible money as special by its source and destination, defeating its own logic of interchangeability.     |
| **Karl Marx**       | _Capital, Volume I_ (1867), the money-form | The general (universal) equivalent: the commodity in which all others express their value, and money's fetish character.                |

## Restrictions

What the engine refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Debt**: The owing that binds a creditor to a debtor is the debt engine's. Money is the token and its liquidity; a claim that one party owes another is delegated, though money is usually what it is owed in.
- **The felt charge**: Greed, security, humiliation, the thrill of a windfall -- those are the emotion and desire engines'. Money names the token and what it does; what wanting or losing it feels like is delegated.
- **Field position by capital**: Standing bought by economic capital, and its conversion to cultural or social capital, are the capital and heritage engines'. Money models the liquid token; the durable position it can buy is delegated.
- **The system**: Prices, markets, banking, and how a whole economy of money runs are the scene's and the culture's. The engine models one sum and its handling, not the monetary system around it.

## Encoding

Source to constraint. A piece anchor (the general equivalent) with one position (the holder) and two opposed processes (its abstract and its special pulls).

- **[money](piece_money.md)** (the anchor): The general equivalent -- liquid value that can become anything, carrying power beyond its material. Anchored by Marx (the universal equivalent) and Simmel (money as pure means).
- **[holder](position_holder.md)** (position): The stance of holding liquid claim -- freedom, command, and the pull to price what one might have simply valued. Anchored by Simmel (what money confers and costs).
- **[conversion](process_conversion.md)** (process): Money dissolving qualities into quantity, rendering unlike things exchangeable. Anchored by Simmel (abstraction) and Marx (the money-form).
- **[earmarking](process_earmarking.md)** (process): The same sum made special and moral by source and destination, against money's own fungibility. Anchored by Zelizer (the social meaning of money).
