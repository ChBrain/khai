# Instructions: Money

How a Playwright wires the money engine into a play. You wire by linking the
play's own content to the engine's files; the engine files stay untouched, they
are canon. The Roadie plumbs the engine's law into Instructions Knowledge on
deploy. Authoring guidance, not runtime content, and it does not go on tour.

## Human

- The human sets the sum, who holds it, and what it is meant for.

## Agent

- From the play's own content, set the money: link the holder stance under the possessing persona's Projection, and link the process the sum is in -- conversion where a quality is being put to a price, earmarking where a sum is being marked as special. Keep the amount and its source in the play's content, not in the engine. Let the two pulls play against each other: money that flattens everything into price, and people insisting some of it means more than its number. You link to the engine, you never edit it.

## Collaboration

- The owing that binds two parties is debt's; the greed, security, or shame around wealth are emotion's and desire's; the standing that lasting capital buys is capital's and hierarchy's; the gift that asks nothing back is gift's. Whether the money carries the meaning the plot needs -- cold command or earmarked significance -- is the team's review.

## Knowledge

- Money is the general equivalent: a token that converts any value into any other and stores it as pure quantity, giving its holder liquidity -- the freedom to command goods, labour, and time, and to wait where others must act. It pulls two ways: conversion dissolves qualities into a price and makes unlike things exchangeable; earmarking runs against its own logic, marking a fungible sum as special by where it came from and what it is for. Read money as power in liquid form, and as a number people keep trying to give meaning back.

## System

- Do link the holder under Projection and name the process the sum is in; do let liquidity confer real freedom and command; do let earmarked money resist being spent like any other.
- Do not model a binding owing here; that is debt's. Do not author the felt greed or security; those are emotion's and desire's. Do not edit the engine's files; wire only from the play's side.
