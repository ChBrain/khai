# Process: Conversion

## Taxonomy

[Money](piece_money.md)

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: money

## Initiated by

A quality is put to a price. The threshold is the moment something not
made to be sold -- an hour, a skill, a harm, a kindness -- is set equal
to a sum, and so made exchangeable for anything else a sum can buy. The
world's unlike things are rendered in one measure.

## Direction

Toward abstraction. Conversion dissolves the particular into the
general: what mattered as itself -- this labour, this loss, this object
-- becomes a quantity that can be added, compared, and swapped for an
unrelated quantity. Money is the universal solvent, and its direction
is always toward the comparable.

## Lever

The reach of the measure meeting the resistance of the incommensurable.
Money's force is that it can price nearly anything; its resistance is
that some things are diminished by being priced -- a friendship, a
vote, a life. Remove the measure and there is no trade across unlike
things; remove the resistance and everything is merely for sale.

## Echo

Once priced, a thing does not easily return to being priceless: the
number sticks, and the quality it stood for is now known to have a
figure. The next process inherits a world one conversion more
quantified -- more able to exchange, and a little less able to hold
some things as beyond exchange.
