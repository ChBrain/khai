# Piece: Money

## Taxonomy

The general equivalent / the social meaning of money (no khai file of its own yet).

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: money

## Place

Everywhere and nowhere in particular. Money's place is defined by its
lack of one: unlike other pieces it is made to move, to be held only
in order to be released, to sit in a pocket, an account, a promise as
readily as in a hand. What fixes it is not where it rests but who can
draw on it -- its place is a claim, not a location.

## Load Bearing

Remove it and an exchange loses the bridge that let unlike things
trade: the labour for the bread, the hour for the wage, the harm for
the settlement. Money holds up the possibility of converting any value
into any other without barter's double coincidence. It also holds up a
standing of its own -- for the one who has it, options that the one
without it does not; take it away and both the trade and the freedom
collapse.

## Apparent

On the surface, a neutral token -- a coin, a note, a number on a screen,
worth only what it says. What the neutrality hides is that it is never
quite neutral: the same sum is wages here, a gift there, blood money
elsewhere, and people treat those sums as different things. The figure
looks like pure quantity; the meaning it carries is smuggled in the
side.

## Yearbook

Every sum carries the history of how it was got and what it is meant
for. It began as a promise a community would honour; it grew into the
universal solvent that could price anything and buy anything; it
acquired, against its own logic, a moral life -- money earmarked as
sacred or dirty, saved or squandered, one's own or the household's.
The longer a token circulates the more it erases the qualities of what
it passed through -- and the harder people work to mark some of it back
into meaning.
