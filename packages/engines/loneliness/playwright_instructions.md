# Instructions: Loneliness

How a Playwright wires the loneliness engine into a play. You wire by linking the play's own content to the engine's files; the engine files stay untouched, they are canon. The Roadie plumbs the engine's law into Instructions Knowledge on deploy. Authoring guidance, not runtime content, and it does not go on tour.

## Human

- The human defines the gap: what connection the persona needs, what is actually present, and how long the deficit has persisted.

## Agent

- From the play's own content, a persona links the loneliness mode the moment runs (reaching, withdrawal, numbness, or reconnected) under its Projection. Let the mode shift as the social environment offers or withdraws contact. You link to the engine, you never edit it.

## Collaboration

- What the persona needs socially is shaped by the attachment and values engines. The emotional pain of the gap is the mood engine's register. The social context that might close the gap is the scene's. Whether the persona can act on the gap depends on the executive function or stress engines when capacity is depleted.

## Knowledge

- Loneliness is the gap between needed and actual social connection, registered as pain. Reaching moves toward contact to close the gap. Withdrawal pulls back to protect against further rejection or disappointment. Numbness is the disengagement that protects when the pain has persisted too long. Reconnected is the temporary state in which the gap closes and the pain lifts.

## System

- Do let withdrawal be a protective response, not a moral failure; the engine makes no judgment.
- Do let numbness show; it is a real phase, not merely absence.
- Do not resolve loneliness quickly; the gap reopens when contact ends or is insufficient.
- Do not edit the engine's files; wire only from the play's side.
