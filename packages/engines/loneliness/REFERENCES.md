# Loneliness: Reference

## Line of Work

Loneliness as **process**: how the gap between needed and actual social connection registers as pain and produces responses that may close the gap or widen it. The domain does not model attachment (that is the attachment engine's strategy for proximity-seeking); it models the baseline pain signal that fires when the connection available falls short of the connection needed, and the movements that follow from it.

The spine is the evolutionary-social model of loneliness: the signal is an alarm, homologous to physical pain, evolved to push the organism back toward the group. Chronically activated, it produces hypervigilance that reads the social environment as more threatening than it is, paradoxically making the contact it calls for harder to reach.

## Origin

Social neuroscience and epidemiology of loneliness, applied directly as process constraints.

| Source                                                        | Key Work                                                              | Scope                                                                                                                          |
| :------------------------------------------------------------ | :-------------------------------------------------------------------- | :----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **John Cacioppo &amp; William Patrick**                       | _Loneliness: Human Nature and the Need for Social Connection_ (2008)  | Evolutionary model: loneliness as a social pain signal; the hypervigilance loop that turns the alarm into a barrier.           |
| **John Cacioppo &amp; Stephanie Cacioppo**                    | _"The Growing Problem of Loneliness"_ (2018)                          | Chronic loneliness as a health risk; the distinction between felt loneliness and objective social isolation.                   |
| **Julianne Holt-Lunstad, Timothy Smith &amp; Bradley Layton** | _"Social Relationships and Mortality Risk"_ (2010)                    | The health-equivalent of loneliness: its physiological and mortality impact, independent of actual isolation.                  |
| **Robert Weiss**                                              | _Loneliness: The Experience of Emotional and Social Isolation_ (1973) | Two types: emotional loneliness (absence of a specific close attachment) and social loneliness (absence of a broader network). |

## Restrictions

What the engine refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Attachment strategy**: The engine models the pain signal and the responses it produces, not the strategy the persona uses to seek closeness under threat. The attachment engine governs the proximity-seeking system; this engine governs the background ache of connection that is insufficient, which can be present even when attachment figures are available.
- **Solitude**: The engine models the felt gap between needed and actual connection, not voluntary aloneness. A persona can be alone without running this process; a persona can run this process in a crowd. The presence or absence of others is not the trigger; the gap is.
- **Depression**: The engine models the social pain signal and its movements, not the clinical syndrome of depression, which involves vegetative, cognitive, and somatic symptoms beyond the relational domain. A persona running numbness long-term may develop depression; that is a biographical fact owned by the play's arc.
- **Trust and rejection**: The engine models the baseline social pain signal, not the bet placed on a specific other (trust engine) or the reading of a specific social rejection (emotion engine). This engine governs the systemic condition; specific rejections and specific bets are owned by other engines.

## Encoding

Source to constraint, per file.

- **[loneliness](process_loneliness.md)** (the root): The gap between needed and actual connection registers as pain; the alarm fires and the persona's read of the room shifts. Anchored by Cacioppo &amp; Patrick (the evolutionary-social model and the hypervigilance loop).
- **[reaching](process_reaching.md)** (expression): The signal fires and the persona moves toward contact -- the alarm working as designed. Anchored by Cacioppo (the reaching-out response as the intended outcome of the loneliness signal).
- **[withdrawal](process_withdrawal.md)** (expression): The signal fires and the hypervigilance it produces reads the room as threatening; the persona pulls back from the contact it needs. Anchored by Cacioppo (the hypervigilance loop that makes the alarm self-reinforcing).
- **[numbness](process_numbness.md)** (expression): The chronic signal flattens; the acute pain becomes ambient background; the persona functions at a distance from the world. Anchored by Cacioppo &amp; Cacioppo (chronic loneliness and its physiological down-regulation).
- **[reconnected](process_reconnected.md)** (expression): Contact lands -- real, sufficient, received -- and the alarm stands down. Anchored by Cacioppo (connection as the signal's resolution; the return to baseline).
