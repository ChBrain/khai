# Flow: Reference

## Line of Work

Flow as **process**: total absorption arising when a clear-goaled, immediately-feedbacked activity holds a balance between challenge and skill -- both pulled high and matched. The domain does not model how attention is allocated moment to moment (that is the attention engine's), whether the activity is read as play (that is play-mode's), what drives the persona to take the activity up (that is motivation's), or the felt enjoyment the run produces (that is emotion's). It models the entry condition, the running merge, and the autotelic stance in between.

The spine is Csikszentmihalyi's flow: a state entered when the demands of a clear-goaled, immediately-feedbacked task sit level with the doer's own skill, both stretched high rather than left slack. Inside the match, action and awareness merge, self-consciousness and the ordinary sense of time recede, and the activity comes to be worth doing for its own sake -- autotelic, not instrumental to some outside reward.

## Origin

The psychology of optimal experience, applied directly as process constraints.

| Source                                        | Key Work                                            | Scope                                                                                                                                     |
| :-------------------------------------------- | :-------------------------------------------------- | :---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Mihaly Csikszentmihalyi**                   | _Flow: The Psychology of Optimal Experience_ (1990) | The challenge-skill channel, the merging of action and awareness, loss of self-consciousness, distorted time, and the autotelic activity. |
| **Jeanne Nakamura & Mihaly Csikszentmihalyi** | _"The Concept of Flow"_ (2002)                      | A condensed restatement of the flow model and its component dimensions, used to cross-check the encoding below.                           |

## Restrictions

What the engine refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **The attentional spotlight itself**: The engine models the condition under which attention locks onto one activity and holds, not the moment-to-moment allocation of the spotlight. That mechanism belongs to the **attention engine**; flow is one of the states attention can settle into, not the allocator.
- **Treating the activity as play**: The engine models absorption in an activity of any kind -- work, craft, sport, art -- not the framing that marks an activity as play in the first place. That framing is **play-mode's**; flow can run inside or outside a play frame.
- **What drives the persona to the activity**: The engine models the state once the activity is taken up and matched, not why the persona reached for that activity over another. The pull toward it is **motivation's**.
- **The felt enjoyment**: The engine models the structural conditions (match, merge, autotelic stance) and their echo, not the felt pleasure the run produces. The read of what is felt is **emotion's**; flow and joy often co-occur but are not the same file.

## Encoding

Source to constraint, per file.

- **[flow](process_flow.md)** (the root): The entry gated by match -- a clear-goaled, immediately-feedbacked activity taken up the moment challenge and skill sit level, both pulled high. Anchored by Csikszentmihalyi (the flow channel).
- **[challenge-skill balance](process_challenge_skill_balance.md)** (entry condition): The gate itself -- too much challenge over skill tips to anxiety, too little slackens to boredom, matched it opens onto flow. Anchored by Csikszentmihalyi (the challenge-skill channel model).
- **[absorbed](position_absorbed.md)** (stance): The autotelic position held while inside the match -- the activity worth doing for its own sake, self-consciousness and clock time lost. Anchored by Csikszentmihalyi (the autotelic experience; loss of self-consciousness).
- **[immersion](process_immersion.md)** (running state): The merge of action and awareness the immediate feedback loop sustains for as long as the match holds. Anchored by Csikszentmihalyi (the merging of action and awareness) and Nakamura & Csikszentmihalyi (the condensed restatement of the same dimension).
