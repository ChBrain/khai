# Instructions: Gender

How a Playwright wires the gender engine into a play. You wire by linking the
play's own content to the engine's files; the engine files stay untouched, they
are canon. The Roadie plumbs the engine's law into Instructions Knowledge on
deploy. Authoring guidance, not runtime content, and it does not go on tour.

## Human

- The human sets the room whose read the persona carries: gender is what the room assigns, not what the persona privately is.

## Agent

- From the play's own content, every persona carries its gender read under Projection, one expression (the engine ships male and female; more slot in as siblings). Where a persona conceals a different read, put that under Shadow. You link to the engine, you never edit it.

## Collaboration

- The felt experience of the read is the emotion engine's; the body is the body engine's; the relational stance is attachment's or trust's; what the room is, the eyes doing the assigning, is the scene's. Whether the read rings true is the team's review.

## Knowledge

- Gender is a position: the read the room applies to a persona before it speaks, not a trait the persona owns. The law is system-wide, every persona carries a read and none can drop it. The carried read sits under Projection; a read concealed, different from the one shown, sits under Shadow, and the gap between the two is where the engine does its work.

## System

- Do give every persona a read (the law admits no exception); do read it in the first beat; do use Shadow only when a persona hides a read different from the one it carries.
- Do not treat it as a trait the persona owns; it is the room's assignment. Do not author the room here; that is the scene's. Do not edit the engine's files; wire only from the play's side.
