# Instructions: Tragic

How a Playwright wires the tragic composite into a play. You wire by linking the
play's own content to the composite's files and, through them, to the three atom
engines it depends on; every file stays untouched, they are canon. The Roadie
plumbs the law into Instructions Knowledge on deploy. Authoring guidance, not
runtime content, and it does not go on tour.

## Human

- The human sets the fall and the witness: whose fortune turns to misfortune,
  and who watches it from what distance.

## Agent

- Cast the three parts from their atoms: the loss from the grief engine (what
  the fall takes away, irreversibly), the channel from the empathy engine (how
  open the witness is to the sufferer's inner state), and the turn from the
  reversal engine (the peripeteia from fortune to misfortune). Then read the
  compound and its crossing: a fall witnessed through an open channel and read
  as undeserved is pity, and read back onto the self is fear, and pity and fear
  borne from a distance that holds cross into catharsis. Held too near it is
  distress, the fall taken as real; held too far it is spectacle, the fall seen
  and unfelt. A persona stranded at either edge may link the bridge under
  Projection; the audit surfaces the ones left unnamed. You link to the
  composite and its atoms, you never edit them.

## Collaboration

- Each part's science is its atom's: grief carries the irreversible loss,
  empathy the channel that takes another's state in, reversal the turn from
  fortune to misfortune. The sufferer's own grief is the grief engine's
  directly, first person; the dramaturgy that stages the fall is the
  dramatic-arc and dramatic-situations engines'; the raw feeling-tone is the
  emotion engine's. Whether the production earns the tragic -- shows a fall
  genuinely undeserved, witnessed from a distance that holds, rather than
  collapsing into sadness or into spectacle -- is the team's review.

## Knowledge

- The tragic is a witness's emotion, not a sufferer's: pity at another's
  undeserved fall and fear that the same turn could take you, borne from a
  distance that keeps the suffering another's and the fiction a fiction, and
  crossing -- when that distance holds -- into catharsis, the clarifying
  pleasure Hume and the distancing-embracing account explain. The crossing is
  gated at two edges: too near, the distance collapses and pity and fear become
  distress with no relief; too far, the engagement never lands and the fall is
  spectacle, seen and unfelt. Read the production across the compound and its
  crossing, never in the loss alone, because a loss without the fall is only
  sadness and a fall without the feeling is only spectacle.

## System

- Do cast all three parts before reading the tragic; do hold the witness at the
  one remove where the suffering can be embraced without being suffered; do
  build scenes on distress and spectacle as standing conditions, the two edges
  the crossing fails at.
- Do not read a first-person loss as the tragic -- that is grief; do not stage
  the dramaturgy of the fall as the composite's work -- that is the arc engines;
  do not confuse the two failures -- distress is too much of the compound,
  spectacle is none of it; do not edit the composite's or the atoms' files; wire
  only from the play's side.
