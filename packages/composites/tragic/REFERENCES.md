# Tragic: Reference

## Line of Work

The tragic as **composite**: the emotion of witnessing an undeserved fall, read
whole across the atoms it spans. Aristotle named the response tragedy is built
to produce, the catharsis of pity and fear, and this composite reads the three
its atoms hold: the grief engine's irreversible loss (what the fall inflicts),
the empathy engine's channel (through which the witness takes that loss in as
pity), and the reversal engine's peripeteia (the turn from fortune to
misfortune that makes the loss tragic rather than merely sad). This package
authors none of those readings again. It declares the three engines as
dependencies, wires their members by hard package links, and adds only what no
atom holds: the compound the witnessing produces, pity fused with fear at a fall
that could be anyone's, and the crossing that compound makes across the
aesthetic distance into catharsis, or fails to make at either edge.

The warrant is accordingly integrative, not primary. The tragic emotion is not
a fourth atom beside grief, empathy, and reversal; it is what those three
compose when a fall is witnessed rather than undergone, and the pleasure that
compound can yield is the paradox Hume posed and the distancing-embracing model
explains. The sources below are the literature of that paradox and its
resolution; each atom's own REFERENCES carries the primary science of its part.

## Restrictions

What the composite refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Each part of the compound**: The irreversible loss and its adaptation is
  the grief engine's; the channel that takes another's inner state in, its
  openness and cost, is the empathy engine's; the turn from fortune to
  misfortune, the peripeteia, is the reversal engine's. The composite restates
  none of them; a bridge links, it never re-authors.
- **The sufferer's own grief**: The tragic is the witness's emotion, not the
  faller's. What the persona who actually suffers the loss undergoes is the
  grief engine's directly, the first-person adaptation to absence; the
  composite reads the fall watched, not the fall lived.
- **The plot that stages the fall**: The five-act shape of the descent is the
  dramatic-arc engine's, the bounded set of collisions the
  dramatic-situations engine's, the acknowledgment of who a persona is the
  recognition engine's. The composite reads the emotion the staged fall
  produces in a witness, not the dramaturgy that stages it.
- **The felt charge along the way**: The raw affective register beneath the
  pity and the fear is the emotion engine's; the composite reads the tragic
  compound and its crossing, not the moment-to-moment feeling-tone it rides on.

## Encoding

Source to constraint. A root (the tragic crossing) with two bridge states, each
an edge where the crossing fails, wired by hard package links into the atoms it
spans.

- **[tragic](process_tragic.md)** (the root): a loss witnessed (grief) and
  taken in (empathy) and shaped by a fall (reversal), the pity and fear that
  compound answers with, and the crossing that compound makes into catharsis
  when the aesthetic distance holds. Anchored by Aristotle (the catharsis of
  pity and fear) and Hume (the conversion of the disagreeable passions to
  pleasure).
- **[tragic, distress](position_tragic_distress.md)** (bridge state, the near
  edge): the distance collapses and the fall is taken as real, so pity and fear
  arrive without the remove that would convert them and the crossing does not
  run. Anchored by Menninghaus et al. (the embracing mechanism firing with the
  distancing mechanism failed) over the empathy, grief, and reversal members it
  links.
- **[tragic, spectacle](position_tragic_spectacle.md)** (bridge state, the far
  edge): the engagement never lands and the fall is watched cold, so the
  reversal is seen and no pity or fear is felt. Anchored by Menninghaus et al.
  (the distancing mechanism holding with the embracing mechanism failed) over
  the reversal, empathy, and grief members it links.
