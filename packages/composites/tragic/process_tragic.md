# Process: Tragic

## Taxonomy

A composite reading over three atom engines (no canon file of its own): the
grief, empathy, and reversal engines this package declares as dependencies.

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: tragic

## Initiated by

A persona witnessing another's undeserved fall, from a distance that holds.
The tragic is not the sufferer's own state but the witness's: the
[grief](@chbrain/khai-engine-grief/process_grief.md) engine holds the
irreversible loss the fall inflicts, the
[empathy](@chbrain/khai-engine-empathy/position_empathy.md) engine holds the
channel through which the witness takes that loss in, and the
[reversal](@chbrain/khai-engine-reversal/process_reversal.md) engine holds the
peripeteia, the turn from fortune to misfortune that makes the loss land as
tragic rather than merely sad. Aristotle named the emotion that answers this
witnessing: pity, the fellow-feeling at another's undeserved suffering, and
fear, the recognition that the same turn could take the witness too. The
composite begins where a fall is watched, not undergone.

## Direction

From witnessed suffering toward catharsis, across the aesthetic distance.
Pity is the empathy channel opened onto the
[yearning](@chbrain/khai-engine-grief/process_yearning.md) of a loss that
cannot be reversed; fear is the reversal read back onto the self, the
fortune-to-misfortune turn felt as one that spares no one. Held too near, this
compound is only distress. But held at a distance that keeps the suffering
another's and the fiction a fiction, the movement Hume called the conversion
runs: the disagreeable passions are not effaced but turned, and pity and fear
become a clarifying pleasure rather than raw aversion. The direction is one way
and doubly gated: the distance must hold and the engagement must land, or the
crossing does not complete.

## Lever

Whether a production reads the tragic in three, and holds both distance and
embrace. Sadness is grief alone, the loss without the fall; spectacle is the
fall without the feeling. The tragic is the loss (grief) taken in (empathy)
and shaped by the turn (reversal), witnessed from a safety that lets pity and
fear be borne. The lever is to stage the fall as undeserved and the witness as
held at the one remove where the suffering can be embraced without being
suffered, because that remove is the whole condition of the tragic pleasure and
the thing a production most easily loses.

## Echo

The crossing completes, or it fails at one of its two joints. When the
distance holds and the engagement lands, pity and fear are clarified and
discharged, the catharsis Aristotle named and the paradoxical pleasure Hume and
the distancing-embracing account explain, and the atoms resume, grief carrying
the loss onward. When the distance collapses and the fall is taken as a real
threat, the witness is stranded in
[distress](position_tragic_distress.md), pity and fear without relief; when the
engagement never lands and the fall is watched coldly, the witness is left in
[spectacle](position_tragic_spectacle.md), a reversal seen and nothing felt. A
production that stages only the completed catharsis has cast an audience that
is always moved and never overwhelmed or unreached; the tragic this composite
is for is the one that can fail at either edge.
