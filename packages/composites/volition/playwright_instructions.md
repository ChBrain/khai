# Instructions: Volition

How a Playwright wires the volition composite into a play. You wire by linking
the play's own content to the composite's files and, through them, to the three
atom engines it depends on; every file stays untouched, they are canon. The
Roadie plumbs the law into Instructions Knowledge on deploy. Authoring
guidance, not runtime content, and it does not go on tour.

## Human

- The human sets the goal and the field it is pursued in: what the persona
  wants, and what stands between the wanting and the doing.

## Agent

- Cast the three phases from their atoms: the wish from the goal engine (what
  is wanted and how held), the plan from the implementation-intention engine
  (is there an if-then bridge and a cue), and the control from the
  executive-function engine (can the pursuit be held against interference).
  Then read the crossing: deliberated, planned, and carried through is
  volition whole; deliberated but never planned is the gap; planned but never
  executed is the stall. A persona stranded in the gap or defeated in the
  stall may link the bridge under Projection; the audit surfaces the ones left
  unnamed. You link to the composite and its atoms, you never edit them.

## Collaboration

- Each phase's science is its atom's: goal carries the deliberated end,
  implementation-intention the if-then bridge, executive-function the control
  that carries the pursuit. Why the persona wants the end at all is
  motivation's; the frustration of the gap and the defeat of the stall are
  emotion's and mood's; whether a persona should let a goal go is a
  disengagement reading, motivation's, not this crossing's. Whether the
  production earns the crossing -- shows a wish genuinely failing to become a
  will, or a will genuinely failing to execute, rather than collapsing wanting
  and doing into one act -- is the team's review.

## Knowledge

- Volition is a crossing, not an act: a goal deliberated (the wish), an
  if-then plan that carries it over the Rubicon into action (the will), and
  the executive control that holds the pursuit against interference (the
  doing). The phases are gated -- a wish that is never planned does not cross,
  a plan the control layer cannot carry does not complete -- so the crossing
  can fail at two joints: the wish that never becomes a will, a goal never
  bridged into a plan (the gap, the intention-behavior gap), and the will that
  cannot be carried out, a plan defeated by interference the control layer
  cannot suppress (the stall). Read the production across the crossing from
  wanting to doing, never in the wish alone, because a deliberated goal looks
  like a step taken when nothing has yet been done.

## System

- Do cast all three phases before reading the crossing; do let a wish fail to
  become a will and a will fail to execute as distinct failures; do build
  scenes on the gap and the stall as standing conditions.
- Do not read a deliberated goal as an action begun; do not resolve a gap or a
  stall in the beat that reveals it where it should stand; do not confuse the
  two -- a gap is never crossing, a stall is crossing and not completing; do
  not edit the composite's or the atoms' files; wire only from the play's side.
