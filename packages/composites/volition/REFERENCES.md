# Volition: Reference

## Line of Work

Volition as **composite**: the crossing from wanting to doing. Heckhausen and
Gollwitzer mapped goal pursuit as phases split by a Rubicon, and this composite
reads the three its atoms hold: the goal engine's deliberated end (the wish),
the implementation-intention engine's if-then plan that carries it across (the
will), and the executive-function engine's control that holds the pursuit
against interference (the doing). This package authors none of those readings
again. It declares the three engines as dependencies, wires their members by
hard package links, and adds only what no atom holds: the crossing across all
three, and the two states where it fails, the wish that never becomes a will
and the will that cannot be carried out.

The warrant is accordingly integrative, not primary: the sources below are the
literature of the passage from motivation to volition as one sequence, and of
the gap where intention fails to become behaviour, where each atom's own
REFERENCES carries the primary science of its phase.

## Restrictions

What the composite refuses to model, and to whom it delegates.

- **Each phase of the crossing**: The deliberated goal, its orientation and
  its definition, is the goal engine's; the if-then plan, its cue, trigger,
  and execution, is the implementation-intention engine's; the control that
  holds the pursuit -- inhibition, updating, shifting, planning -- is the
  executive-function engine's. The composite restates none of them; a bridge
  links, it never re-authors.
- **The wanting itself**: Why the persona wants the end at all, the drive
  beneath the goal, is the motivation engine's; the composite reads whether
  the wish crosses into a will and a deed, not the energy behind the wish.
- **The felt charge of the crossing**: The frustration of the gap, the
  defeat of the stall, the satisfaction of a pursuit completed are the
  emotion and mood engines'; the composite reads the crossing, not the affect
  along it.
- **The disengagement from a goal**: Whether and when a persona lets a goal
  go -- the failure to disengage from a pursuit no longer worth it -- is a
  motivational reading of the goal's fate, not a failure of the crossing from
  wanting to doing; the composite reads the passage into action, not the exit
  from it.

## Encoding

Source to constraint. A root (the Rubicon crossing) with two bridge states,
each a joint where the crossing fails, wired by hard package links into the
atoms it spans.

- **[volition](process_volition.md)** (the root): a goal deliberated, an
  if-then plan that carries it across, and the control that holds the
  pursuit; wanting becoming doing, gated at each step. Anchored by Heckhausen
  & Gollwitzer (the Rubicon model) and Gollwitzer (the action phases).
- **[volition, gap](position_volition_gap.md)** (bridge state, goal without
  implementation-intention): the wish never becomes a will, a goal
  deliberated but never bridged into an if-then plan, so the intention never
  initiates. Anchored by Sheeran & Webb (the intention-behavior gap) over the
  goal and implementation-intention members it links.
- **[volition, stall](position_volition_stall.md)** (bridge state,
  implementation-intention without executive-function): the will cannot be
  carried out, the plan fires but control cannot hold the pursuit against a
  prepotent response or under load. Anchored by Heckhausen & Gollwitzer (the
  actional phase and its volitional demands) over the implementation-intention
  and executive-function members it links.
