# Position: Volition, Gap

## Taxonomy

[Volition](process_volition.md), the state where the wish never becomes a
will.

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: volition

## Has

A deliberated goal and no bridge across. The goal engine's end is fully
formed, held as [approach](@chbrain/khai-engine-goal/position_approach.md)
or [avoidance](@chbrain/khai-engine-goal/position_avoidance.md), wanted and
even resolved upon, but it is never bridged into an if-then
[plan](@chbrain/khai-engine-implementation-intention/process_planning.md):
no cue is specified, no when-and-where committed, so the
implementation-intention engine's
[trigger](@chbrain/khai-engine-implementation-intention/process_triggering.md)
never has anything to fire. What the persona has is the intention-behavior
gap in its pure form, a genuine wish on the wrong side of the Rubicon, the
resolution that stays a resolution because nothing was ever set to carry it
into action.

## Orders

Keep wanting, and mistake the wanting for progress. The gap demands nothing
of the persona but the goal itself, which is its trap: a deliberated goal
feels like a step taken, so the persona re-wants, re-resolves, renews the
intention, and reads that renewal as movement. The order is to hold the wish
warm without the one thing that would cross it, the specified if-then that
would hand initiation to a cue and stop requiring the persona to notice the
moment and decide again each time.

## Loses

The action the wish was for. Sheeran and Webb's gap is exactly this loss,
the well-documented distance between what people intend and what they do; the
persona in it forfeits not the goal but its enactment, and keeps the goal
precisely because keeping it costs nothing the gap makes them pay. They lose,
too, the diagnosis: because the goal is real and strongly held, the failure
reads as weak will or bad character rather than a missing structural bridge,
and the persona reaches for more resolve where a specified plan is what was
absent.

## Drives

Toward a plan that crosses, or a wish worn smooth by renewal. The persona
forms the if-then link, specifies the cue, and the gap closes as the wish
becomes a committed course; or the goal is renewed indefinitely, each fresh
resolution standing in for the crossing and none of them making it; or the
wanting itself fatigues and the goal is abandoned unattempted, never having
reached the line. A production stages the gap as a persona genuinely wanting
and genuinely not acting, and reads the drama in whether a bridge is ever
built or the wish is renewed until it dies.
