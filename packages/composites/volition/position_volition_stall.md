# Position: Volition, Stall

## Taxonomy

[Volition](process_volition.md), the state where the will cannot be carried
out.

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: volition

## Has

A crossed intention and a control layer that cannot hold it. The Rubicon is
behind the persona: the goal was bridged into an if-then plan and the cue has
[triggered](@chbrain/khai-engine-implementation-intention/process_triggering.md),
the action initiated, but
[executing](@chbrain/khai-engine-implementation-intention/process_executing.md)
it to completion needs the executive engine to
[inhibit](@chbrain/khai-engine-executive-function/process_inhibition.md) a
prepotent competing response and hold the goal active across the sequence,
and that capacity is not there or not enough. What the persona has is a
genuine will defeated in the doing: the plan fired, the intention was real
and committed, and the pursuit still fails against interference, load, or the
pull of the response the plan was meant to override.

## Orders

Carry a will the control layer keeps dropping. The stall demands the persona
hold the pursuit through exactly the interference their executive capacity
cannot suppress, so each attempt begins on the far side of the crossing and
fails in the same place, at the competing response inhibition should have
blocked. The order is to keep executing against a layer that will not hold,
to have decided and committed and still be unable to make the doing complete,
which is a different failure from never having crossed.

## Loses

The completion the crossing promised. A will that cannot be executed loses
the one thing volition was for, the deed at the end, and loses it after the
costly part, the deliberation and the commitment, is already spent; the
persona has paid to cross and cannot collect. It forecloses, too, the
gap's excuse: the persona cannot say they did not really intend, because the
plan fired and the attempt was made, so the failure sits visibly in the
carrying-out and not in the wanting.

## Drives

Toward control shored up, or a will spent against the wall. The persona
strengthens the executive layer or lowers the interference, an easier cue, a
cleared field, a smaller step, and the stall resolves into the pursuit
completing; or the will is spent in repeated defeated attempts, each crossing
paid for and each execution failing at the same joint, until the goal is
abandoned not for lack of wanting but for lack of carrying; or the failure
routes back and the persona mistakes a stall for a gap, concluding they never
really wanted it when in fact they could not do it. A production stages the
stall as a will that keeps failing in the doing, and reads the drama in
whether the control to carry it is ever found.
