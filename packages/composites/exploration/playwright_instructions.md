# Instructions: Exploration

How a Playwright wires the exploration composite into a play. You wire by
linking the play's own content to the composite's files and, through them, to
the two atom engines it depends on; every file stays untouched, they are
canon. The Roadie plumbs the law into Instructions Knowledge on deploy.
Authoring guidance, not runtime content, and it does not go on tour.

## Human

- The human sets the unknown and the stakes: what novel thing the persona
  meets, and what it would mean to investigate it and to play with it.

## Agent

- Cast the two stances from their atoms: the pull from the curiosity engine
  (which dimension draws the persona to the unknown) and the mode from the
  play-mode engine (how they engage once inside the frame). Then read the
  transition: investigation giving way to play is the passage working;
  investigation that never releases is arrested; play with no investigation
  under it is rote. A persona held short of play or playing by rote may link
  the bridge under Projection; the audit surfaces the ones left unnamed. You
  link to the composite and its atoms, you never edit them.

## Collaboration

- Each stance's science is its atom's: curiosity carries the disposition
  toward the unknown and its dimensions, play-mode carries the modes of the
  play frame. The pleasure or flatness across the transition is emotion's and
  mood's; what draws the persona to the thing, or keeps them at it, is
  motivation's. Whether the production earns the crossing -- shows
  investigation genuinely giving way to play, or genuinely failing to, rather
  than collapsing curiosity and play into one undifferentiated interest -- is
  the team's review.

## Knowledge

- Exploration is a transition, not a stance: the curiosity-driven
  investigation of the novel ("what does this do?") gives way, once the thing
  is understood, to the play made of it ("what can I do with this?"). The
  crossing is gated on understanding -- what is still unknown can only be
  investigated, not yet played with -- so it can stall in two ways: the
  investigation that never crosses into play, holding the unknown as a
  permanent problem (arrested), and the play run over ground never
  investigated, the motions of discovery with the pull gone dead (rote). Read
  the production in the movement between curiosity and play, never in either
  stance alone.

## System

- Do cast both stances before reading the transition; do hold curiosity and
  play apart so the crossing is visible; do build scenes on the arrested and
  the rote states as standing conditions.
- Do not collapse investigation and play into one undifferentiated interest;
  do not resolve an arrested or rote state in the beat that reveals it where
  it should stand; do not edit the composite's or the atoms' files; wire only
  from the play's side.
