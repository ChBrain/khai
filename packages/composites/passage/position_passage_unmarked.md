# Position: Passage, Unmarked

## Taxonomy

[Passage](process_passage.md), the state where the crossing has run ahead
of the rite.

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: passage

## Has

A completed crossing and no ceremony to confer it. The individual clock
has reached [emergence](@chbrain/khai-engine-liminality/place_emergence.md),
the persona has genuinely become what the passage was toward, while the
collective clock never ran: no
[rite](@chbrain/khai-engine-ritual/process_ritual.md) separated them, no
[incorporation](@chbrain/khai-engine-ritual/process_incorporation.md)
announced the arrival, no room witnessed the turn. Turner marked this as
the liminoid, the transition that falls outside the obligatory collective
rite, individual and optional; Grimes marked its cost, the modern life sped
through its intersections unmarked, births and departures and becomings
that no ceremony gathers. What the persona has is a real change with no
public record of it, a self that has moved while their standing in every
room stayed where the last ceremony left it.

## Orders

Hold a crossing the world has not registered. The unmarked passage demands
the persona carry an emergence no one conferred, and so no one is obliged
to honour: they are changed and must act changed against rooms that still
read the old status. The order is to bear the asymmetry, to know the
crossing is real while every collective clock around them shows an earlier
time, and to decide in each room whether to assert the unwitnessed change
or let it pass unclaimed. The self has arrived; the social world has no
entry for it.

## Loses

The force a ceremony carries. A crossing conferred by rite arrives with
witnesses, a date, a room that will vouch for it; the unmarked crossing
has none, so it can be doubted, by others and by the persona, in a way a
witnessed one cannot. It loses ratification: what no ceremony marked, the
persona must re-assert privately every time it is questioned, holding a
transformation that has to keep proving itself because nothing external
ever certified it once and for all.

## Drives

Toward marking, or toward permanence unmarked. The persona builds the rite
that was missing, the deliberately re-invented ceremony Grimes argued the
modern passage needs, gathering witnesses after the fact to confer what
the crossing already earned; or accepts the passage as permanently
private, a change real enough not to need the room's confirmation; or
presses the unmarked change on rooms that keep the old reading and pays
for asserting a status no ceremony backs. A production stages the unmarked
passage as a standing condition, the emergence that outran every clock but
the persona's own, and reads the drama in whether the world is ever made
to catch up.
