# Process: Passage

## Taxonomy

A composite reading over two atom engines (no canon file of its own): the
ritual and liminality engines this package declares as dependencies.

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: passage

## Initiated by

A crossing that runs on two clocks at once. The
[ritual](@chbrain/khai-engine-ritual/process_ritual.md) engine holds the
collective clock, the ceremony that detaches a participant, suspends them,
and returns them re-marked; the
[liminality](@chbrain/khai-engine-liminality/place_liminality.md) engine
holds the individual clock, the persona's own between-ness, freshly at the
threshold or held deep in suspension. Van Gennep drew both as one figure,
the rite of passage that carries the person across; Turner deepened the
liminal phase for both the collective and the person inside it. The
composite begins where those two clocks are supposed to be the same
clock, and reads what happens when they are not.

## Direction

Toward phase-lock, when the passage works. In the intact rite the two
clocks drive each other: the ceremony's
[separation](@chbrain/khai-engine-ritual/process_separation.md) opens the
persona's [threshold](@chbrain/khai-engine-liminality/place_threshold.md),
the collective liminal phase holds their
[suspension](@chbrain/khai-engine-liminality/place_suspension.md), and
[incorporation](@chbrain/khai-engine-ritual/process_incorporation.md)
confers the [emergence](@chbrain/khai-engine-liminality/place_emergence.md)
the persona has genuinely reached. Durkheim's effervescence is the force
that couples them, the shared intensity that makes the ceremony land
inside the person. When the clocks are locked, the rite and the crossing
are one event and neither engine's reading is wrong; the composite has
nothing to add. Its whole subject is the lock coming loose.

## Lever

Which clock the production trusts. A rite of passage claims that the
ceremony is the crossing, that to be incorporated is to have emerged, and
a scene either honours that claim or exposes it. The lever is the
composite's one demand: never let the ceremony's completion stand as
proof of the persona's crossing without showing the crossing itself. The
rite is a public instrument that can run on time while the person inside
it runs slow or fast; a production that reads only the collective clock
stages fables where every graduate is changed, and one that reads only
the individual clock loses the social force the ceremony actually carries.

## Echo

The clocks are locked or they have slipped, and the slip is the drama.
When the rite runs ahead of the crossing the passage is
[hollow](position_passage_hollow.md): performed over a persona who has not
moved. When the crossing runs ahead of the rite the passage is
[unmarked](position_passage_unmarked.md): reached by a persona no ceremony
confirmed. A production that keeps the two clocks welded shut has cast a
world without failed rites or private awakenings; the passages worth
staging are the ones where the ceremony and the self keep different time.
