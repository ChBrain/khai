# Instructions: Passage

How a Playwright wires the passage composite into a play. You wire by linking
the play's own content to the composite's files and, through them, to the two
atom engines it depends on; every file stays untouched, they are canon. The
Roadie plumbs the law into Instructions Knowledge on deploy. Authoring
guidance, not runtime content, and it does not go on tour.

## Human

- The human sets the passage and its stakes: what crossing is at issue, what
  ceremony (if any) the world provides for it, and what the crossing is worth
  to the persona and the room.

## Agent

- Cast the two clocks from their atoms as usual: the ceremony's phase from
  the ritual engine (separation, the liminal phase, effervescence,
  incorporation) and the persona's threshold state from the liminality
  engine (threshold, suspension, emergence). Then read the phase relation:
  locked, hollow (the rite ahead of the crossing), or unmarked (the crossing
  ahead of the rite). A persona living in a hollow or unmarked passage may
  link the bridge under Projection; the audit surfaces the ones left
  unnamed. You link to the composite and its atoms, you never edit them.

## Collaboration

- Each clock's science is its atom's: the ritual engine carries the ceremony
  and its solidarity, the liminality engine carries the between and its
  states. The felt charge of the passage is emotion's and mood's; the status
  the crossing confers is status's and standing's; the recognition an
  acknowledgment builds is recognition's. Whether the production earns the
  phase gap, shows the rite and the crossing genuinely keeping different
  time rather than collapsing them into one moment, is the team's review.

## Knowledge

- A passage is two clocks at once: the collective ceremony that separates,
  suspends, and re-incorporates a persona, and the individual crossing that
  must actually happen inside them. The rite claims the ceremony is the
  crossing, but the two clocks can slip: the rite can complete over a
  persona who has not moved (hollow), leaving them to carry a conferred
  status the self has not reached, and a persona can genuinely cross with no
  ceremony to confer it (unmarked), carrying a real change no room has
  registered. Read the production in the gap between the two clocks, never
  in the ceremony's completion alone and never in the private crossing
  alone.

## System

- Do cast both clocks from their atoms before reading the relation; do let
  the rite and the crossing keep different time where the passage is at
  issue; do build scenes on the hollow status and the unmarked change as
  standing conditions.
- Do not let a completed ceremony stand as proof the persona crossed; do
  not resolve a hollow or unmarked passage in the scene that reveals it; do
  not edit the composite's or the atoms' files; wire only from the play's
  side.
