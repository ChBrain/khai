# Position: Passage, Hollow

## Taxonomy

[Passage](process_passage.md), the state where the rite has run ahead of
the crossing.

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: passage

## Has

A completed ceremony and an unmoved self. The collective clock has reached
[incorporation](@chbrain/khai-engine-ritual/process_incorporation.md), the
room has declared the persona changed and treats them as changed, while
the individual clock is still at
[threshold](@chbrain/khai-engine-liminality/place_threshold.md) or held in
[suspension](@chbrain/khai-engine-liminality/place_suspension.md): the
crossing the rite announced has not happened inside. Grimes named the
failure precisely, the rite that is ineffectual or insincere, performed in
correct form yet producing no change in the person it was done to. What
the persona has is a public status their inner state does not back, and a
ceremony they cannot ask to be repeated without unsaying it.

## Orders

Carry the conferred status without the crossing to support it. The hollow
passage demands the persona hold a position the room believes they have
earned inwardly, the graduate expected to feel qualified, the widow the
funeral was supposed to release, the initiate the rite declared an adult.
The order is to perform incorporation's part while privately still in the
between, because the collective clock does not run backward: the ceremony
is spent, the witnesses have dispersed, and the status is now a fact the
persona must inhabit whether or not the self has caught up.

## Loses

The rite as an available instrument. A ceremony spends itself in one
performance; having been incorporated, the persona cannot be separated and
carried across again by the same rite without confessing it failed. They
lose, too, the room's help: because the collective clock reads them as
arrived, the suspension they are actually in becomes invisible and
unsupported, a private between-ness inside a public after. The one crossing
the rite was built to confer is now the one crossing no rite is left to
confer.

## Drives

Toward a reckoning between the clocks. The persona lets the hollow stand
and lives the status as costume, growing the gap between what the room
sees and what they are; or forces the crossing by other means, seeking
outside the spent ceremony the change it failed to make; or exposes the
rite, refusing the conferred status and reopening the between the
ceremony tried to close. A production stages the hollow passage to be
lived in, not resolved in the scene that reveals it: the emptiness under a
completed rite is a standing condition, and the drama is how long the
persona can hold a status the self has not reached.
