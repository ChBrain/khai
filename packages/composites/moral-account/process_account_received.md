# Process: Account, Received

## Taxonomy

[Moral Account](process_moral_account.md), the bridge where the dyad's
ledger crosses to the wronged party's.

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: moral-account

## Initiated by

An account has crossed the doorstep, and now it lands on ground the
account-giver does not control. What decides its reception is nothing in
the account itself: the dyad's stored regard
([sentiment override](@chbrain/khai-engine-repair/position_sentiment_override.md))
reads a clumsy offer generously or a skilled one as manipulation; the
wronged party's
[stance](@chbrain/khai-engine-forgiveness/position_stance.md) has already
set what any words can reach; and where the wrong cut a trusted bond, the
wronged party's
[blindness](@chbrain/khai-engine-betrayal/position_blindness.md) decides
whether the account informs them or detonates what they could not afford
to see.

## Direction

Through three filters in the receiver, not one.
Lazare's anatomy of the failed apology is the anatomy of this bridge: the
same words heal or insult depending on what they land against. An account
received by a
[vengeful stance](@chbrain/khai-engine-forgiveness/position_stance_vengeful.md)
is evidence for the prosecution; the same account received by a
[benevolent stance](@chbrain/khai-engine-forgiveness/position_stance_benevolent.md)
is the occasion it was waiting for. A high-blindness receiver may be
hearing, inside the account, the first news of the
[betrayal](@chbrain/khai-engine-betrayal/process_betrayal.md) itself, so
the offer and the discovery arrive as one blow.

## Lever

The receiver's filters are invisible from the doorstep. The account-giver
calibrates to the harm as they price it, in their own ledger, and cannot
see the stance, the stored regard, or the blindness the offer is about to
meet. McCullough's empathy finding names the one force that moves the
filters themselves: the account that induces empathy shifts the stance,
where the account that merely states the facts leaves every filter as it
found it. The lever is not the size of the offer but what it does to the
receiver's reading of the offerer.

## Echo

The account now exists in two versions that will never fully reconcile:
as given and as received. The dyad's arc proceeds on the received version,
toward
[restitution](@chbrain/khai-engine-repair/process_restitution.md) or back
into rupture; the wronged party's ledger reprices on the same version,
toward release, or deeper into the
[reckoning](@chbrain/khai-engine-betrayal/process_reckoning.md). The
account-giver, holding the given version, frequently cannot say which of
the two just happened.
