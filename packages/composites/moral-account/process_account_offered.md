# Process: Account, Offered

## Taxonomy

[Moral Account](process_moral_account.md), the bridge where the
transgressor's ledger crosses to the dyad's.

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: moral-account

## Initiated by

The transgressor's exit road reaches the dyad's door, or turns away from
it. The guilt engine ends where the pull toward
[reparation](@chbrain/khai-engine-guilt/process_reparation.md) delivers the
persona to the door; the repair engine begins where an
[account](@chbrain/khai-engine-repair/process_accounting.md) is given and
received. This bridge is the doorstep itself: the handoff neither engine
stages, where what the transgressor has been doing inwardly becomes the
form the dyad actually hears.

## Direction

From exit road to account form, predictably.
The road chosen inside guilt shapes the form that arrives in repair: the
paid road tends to arrive as
[concession](@chbrain/khai-engine-repair/process_accounting_concession.md),
the account that owns it whole; a
[denial](@chbrain/khai-engine-guilt/process_refusal_denial.md) that walks
to the door at all tends to arrive as
[excuse](@chbrain/khai-engine-repair/process_accounting_excuse.md), the
agency already argued down; a
[condemnation](@chbrain/khai-engine-guilt/process_refusal_condemnation.md)
arrives, when it arrives, as
[justification](@chbrain/khai-engine-repair/process_accounting_justification.md),
the injury disputed in the standard's own vocabulary. The bridge makes the
prediction castable: name the persona's inner road and the scene knows what
lands on the doorstep.

## Lever

The account that never arrives. Two of guilt's roads produce payment
without delivery:
[self-punishment](@chbrain/khai-engine-guilt/process_reparation_self_punishment.md)
pays the standard and sends nothing to the door, and a refusal that hardens
sends nothing anywhere. The dyad's ledger registers only what crosses the
doorstep, so a transgressor can be paying ruinously in their own ledger
while the dyad's reads, accurately, that no account was ever offered. That
gap, invisible to both parties, is this bridge's whole dramatic charge.

## Echo

Something has crossed, or conspicuously has not, and the dyad's arc takes
over from here. What was offered is now out of the transgressor's hands:
the same concession lands as closure in one dyad and as too-little in
another, which is the next bridge's business. What did not cross remains
the transgressor's alone, compounding in the grade the guilt engine
carries, and the door stays where it was.
