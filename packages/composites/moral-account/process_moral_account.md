# Process: Moral Account

## Taxonomy

A composite reading over four atom engines (no canon file of its own): the
guilt, repair, forgiveness, and betrayal engines this package declares as
dependencies.

## Owner

- Project: khai
- Engine: moral-account

## Initiated by

A wrong inside a bond opens one account, and three parties begin keeping it
at once, each in a ledger the others cannot read. The transgressor's ledger
opens as felt debt, the crossing and its weight
([guilt](@chbrain/khai-engine-guilt/process_guilt.md)). The dyad's ledger
opens as a rupture awaiting an account
([repair](@chbrain/khai-engine-repair/process_repair.md)). The wronged
party's ledger opens twice over: as a grievance they are owed
([forgiveness](@chbrain/khai-engine-forgiveness/process_forgiveness.md))
and, where the wrong cut a trusted bond, as a violation to be reckoned with
([betrayal](@chbrain/khai-engine-betrayal/process_betrayal.md)). No single
engine sees the whole account; that is what this composite is for.

## Direction

Around the triangle, never in a line.
The account moves from the transgressor toward the dyad (something is
offered, or withheld), through the dyad toward the wronged party (the offer
lands, or fails to), and back from the wronged party toward the transgressor
(release, revenge, or silence, each re-pricing the original debt). Tavuchis
read apology as exactly this circuit: a social exchange that no party can
complete alone, in which the saying is the doing.

## Lever

The refusal of the three ledgers to agree. The transgressor's felt debt is
not the size of the harm the wronged party carries; the account the dyad
hears is not the account the transgressor thinks they gave; the release the
wronged party grants or withholds answers their own ledger, not either of
the others. Every mismatch is pressure: a persona who has paid heavily in
their own ledger and nothing in anyone else's, a wronged party released of a
grievance the transgressor never knew they held. Where the ledgers happen to
agree, the account closes quietly and no play is needed.

## Echo

The account closes only ledger by ledger, and almost never all at once. Paid
but never received, received but never released, released but never repaired:
each part-closed state is stable, common, and a standing engine for the next
scene. A production that closes all three ledgers in one gesture has written
a fable; the composite's read is that the gesture which closes one ledger
routinely reopens another, and the bridges beneath this root name the three
places it happens.
