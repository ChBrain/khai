# Instructions: Absorption

How a Playwright wires the absorption composite into a play. You wire by
linking the play's own content to the composite's files and, through them, to
the three atom engines it depends on; every file stays untouched, they are
canon. The Roadie plumbs the law into Instructions Knowledge on deploy.
Authoring guidance, not runtime content, and it does not go on tour.

## Human

- The human sets the activity and the field: what the persona is doing, what
  it demands of their skill, and what else in the scene might seize the beam.

## Agent

- Cast the three links from their atoms: the lock from the attention engine
  (which mode holds the beam), the match from the flow engine (is challenge
  level with skill, has immersion formed), and the felt duration from the
  time-perception engine. Then read the chain: locked and matched and time
  gone is real absorption; locked and unmatched and time heavy is the
  counterfeit; a capture landing on a running merge is the break. A persona
  held in a counterfeit lock or thrown by a break may link the bridge under
  Projection; the audit surfaces the ones left unnamed. You link to the
  composite and its atoms, you never edit them.

## Collaboration

- Each link's science is its atom's: attention carries the spotlight and its
  capture, flow carries the match and the merge, time-perception carries the
  felt clock. The enjoyment of a real merge and the dread of the counterfeit
  are emotion's and mood's; why the persona took the activity up, or rebuilds
  the lock after a break, is motivation's; the play frame is play-mode's.
  Whether the production tells real absorption from its counterfeit, rather
  than reading every locked beam as focus, is the team's review.

## Knowledge

- Absorption is a chain, not a state: attention locks the spotlight
  single-pointed, a challenge-skill match turns the lock into a
  self-sustaining merge, and felt time dissolves from the inside. The links
  fire in order and none without the one before, so the chain can break
  register: a lock with no match under it never merges and time drags heavy
  instead of vanishing (the counterfeit, rapt outside and trapped within),
  and a bottom-up capture can shatter a merge already running, dropping the
  persona into clock time all at once (the break). Read the production across
  all three links, never in the locked beam alone, because from outside the
  real merge and its counterfeit look the same.

## System

- Do cast all three links before reading the chain; do let the locked-but-
  dragging counterfeit be seen through as the trap it is; do stage the break
  as a capture worth its cost, and show the interval returning all at once.
- Do not read a locked beam as absorption without the match under it; do not
  resolve a counterfeit or a break in the beat that reveals it where it should
  stand; do not edit the composite's or the atoms' files; wire only from the
  play's side.
