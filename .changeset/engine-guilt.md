Add the guilt engine: guilt as process -- how a persona appraises a specific act against a standard it holds as its own, and the appraisal becomes a reparative pull rather than a verdict on the whole self. A process anchor (guilt) with one process naming the crossing (transgression), one position naming the stance it leaves behind (culpable), and one process naming how it resolves (reparation). Grounded in Tangney & Dearing's guilt/shame distinction, with Lewis's earlier framing. Guilt's sibling on main, the shame engine, keeps the whole-self condemnation and hiding this engine delegates away.
