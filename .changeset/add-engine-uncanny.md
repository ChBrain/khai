Cut the uncanny engine: a process tree wired directly to Freud's 1919 catalogue in "The Uncanny." The root reads the boundary between the known-and-put-away and the strange-and-present blurring, and five species fall out of Freud's own examples: the double (Rank's insurance against death reversed into its harbinger), recurrence (involuntary repetition read as design), animism (the surmounted belief that thought moves the world, briefly confirmed), animate (the animate/inanimate sort left undecidable, folding in Jentsch's intellectual-uncertainty account and Freud's castration-complex reading of dismemberment), and revenant (the surmounted belief that the dead are not wholly gone). Delegates the felt fright to the emotion and mood engines, anticipatory dread to the emotion/dread domain, and the persona's own finitude to the mortality engine.
